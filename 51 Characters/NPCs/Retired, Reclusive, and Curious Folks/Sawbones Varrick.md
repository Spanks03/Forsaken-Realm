_Physical Description:_ A gaunt, gray-haired man with a weather-beaten face and a perpetual slump in his shoulders. His hands are nicked with scars, fingers stained faintly with old herbs and ink, though they still move with a surgeon’s steady precision—when not shaking from the drink. He wears a patched old army coat, the insignia long since torn off, with flask-sized bulges in nearly every pocket. His left leg drags slightly from an old injury, making his gait uneven and tired.

_Character:_ Varrick is a retired field medic who drinks to drown the screams of the battlefield but finds they always surface with the next swallow. He’s not officially part of Oar’s Break’s clinic, but when the healer there is overwhelmed or a farmhand comes in with a nasty gash, Varrick can be found lurching in, smelling of brandy and pipe smoke. Once his hands steady, he works miracles with crude tools and a hard bedside manner. Patients often hear him muttering half to himself, half to the ghosts of soldiers past: “I’ve stitched worse… hold still, you’ll live.”

He’s both respected and pitied by the townsfolk. Some avoid him, unable to stomach his haunted eyes and blunt words, while others buy him drinks in exchange for grim stories of the war. Kids dare each other to approach him, but to those brave enough to ask, he’ll sometimes give lessons on binding wounds or mixing poultices—usually with a warning about how medicine “never saves enough.”
