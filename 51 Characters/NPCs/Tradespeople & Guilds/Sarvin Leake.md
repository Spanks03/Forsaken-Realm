_Physical Description:_ Sarvin Leake is a wiry man in his late fifties, with a lean frame that speaks less of labor and more of years hunched over ledgers. His hair, once jet black, has thinned and gone mostly silver, swept neatly back with a dab of oil. His skin is pale with the faint sallowness of someone who spends more time indoors than under the sun, though the fine cut of his clothes offsets any impression of frailty. He favors tailored vests of deep navy or burgundy with brass buttons, always kept spotless, and a signet ring bearing the guild’s crest rests on his right hand. His sharp gray eyes seem to miss nothing, flicking across rooms and faces with a calculating swiftness.

_Character:_ Sarvin is a meticulous man who measures worth in coin, contracts, and reliability. Though not unfriendly, his warmth feels transactional — his smiles reserved for those who bring profit to the guild. He is an able negotiator, preferring compromise over conflict, but always ensures the scales tip in his favor. Despite this, Sarvin has earned the respect of Oar’s Break for his consistency; merchants know he will protect their interests, and townsfolk know he will keep trade flowing. He holds himself with quiet authority, speaking in a measured tone that makes even his subtlest words carry weight. Though some call him calculating, others see him as the stabilizing balance that keeps the markets honest — or at least, predictably corrupt.
