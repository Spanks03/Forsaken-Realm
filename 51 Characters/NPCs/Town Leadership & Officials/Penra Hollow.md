_Physical Description:_ A lean half-elf with angular features and dark hair kept pulled into a severe bun. Her pale green eyes rarely soften, and her sharp brows seem forever arched in judgment. She dresses in tidy gray-blue coats with pressed collars and brass buttons, the ink stains on her fingers the only mark of her long hours spent with quills and ledgers. A thin silver chain with a timepiece hangs at her waist, which she checks often with audible clicks.

_Character:_ Penra serves as **Oar’s Break’s town treasurer and vault clerk**, and she carries herself with an air of rigid discipline. Her speech is clipped and exact, her handwriting immaculate, and her records flawless. To Penra, numbers make more sense than people; they follow rules, do not complain, and cannot lie. She holds little patience for disorder or casualness, preferring neat stacks of ledgers over messy conversations.

Despite this, she is not heartless—only stern. She takes her duty of safeguarding the town’s wealth with obsessive seriousness, seeing herself as the invisible wall between Oar’s Break and financial ruin. She is rarely seen without the iron-banded keys to the vault at her belt, which jangle softly when she walks the cobbled streets. Though respected, few townsfolk would call her a friend—Penra seems to prefer it that way.
