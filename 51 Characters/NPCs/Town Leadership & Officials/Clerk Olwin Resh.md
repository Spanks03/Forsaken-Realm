_Physical Description:_ Olwin Resh is a small, wiry gnome whose hands never seem still. Ink stains his fingertips and the cuffs of his sleeves, and the faint smell of parchment and wax follows him wherever he goes. His sandy-brown hair is an untidy thatch, pushed back with a perpetually ink-smudged forehead, and his spectacles are often perched on the end of his nose or propped up in his hair. His hazel eyes gleam with a restless sharpness, darting quickly between documents, faces, and quills, as though everything must be taken in at once. He wears layered waistcoats with far too many pockets, many of which contain rolled-up scraps of parchment, seals, or half-broken quills.

_Character:_ Olwin speaks in a rapid-fire staccato, often finishing one thought while already beginning another, leaving listeners scrambling to keep up. He thrives on order, rules, and the strange comfort of obscure bylaws no one else remembers. He can quote clauses, sub-clauses, and exceptions at lightning speed, and he takes great joy in reminding others of regulations long forgotten. Despite his obsession with details, Olwin has a friendly—if overwhelming—energy, and those who can endure his chatter find him surprisingly helpful. Some in Oar’s Break see him as eccentric but harmless; others quietly suspect that he knows far too many loopholes for his own good.
