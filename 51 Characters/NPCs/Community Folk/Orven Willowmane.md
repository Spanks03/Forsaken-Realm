_Physical Description:_ A wiry elf with wind-tossed chestnut hair and sharp cheekbones, Orven always carries the smell of hay and leather. His hands are strong and calloused from years of handling both hooves and hides, though his fingernails are trimmed neatly, and his clothes—while simple—are brushed clean each morning. His hazel eyes are keen, often lingering on the details of a horse’s gait or the stitching of a boot, as if both reveal truths others might miss.

_Character:_ Orven serves as **stablehand for Oar’s Break**, tending horses with patience and quiet dignity. Though he spends his days mucking stalls and brushing coats, his true passion lies in **fine cobbling**. A back corner of the stable is his little sanctuary—an orderly bench with awls, lasts, and scraps of high-quality leather. Here he repairs worn boots for travelers, or—when time allows—crafts elegant shoes and boots with subtle artistry, far beyond what most in the town would expect.

He has a refined eye for fashion, quietly critical of shoddy stitching or poor leatherwork, though he rarely voices his opinions unless pressed. Those who do wear a pair of his custom shoes often find them both practical and striking, a touch of elven grace in every step. Though Orven is no merchant, word of his craft spreads through whispers: _“The stablehand makes boots finer than any lord could wear.”_
