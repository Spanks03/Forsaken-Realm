*Lesser God - Child of Cassieriel - Keeper of Night/Darkness*

Lunala, the Keeper of Night and Darkness, is a divine Lunar Dragon who drapes the world in rest and reflection. Where her brother Sol ignites the day with radiant breath, it is Lunala who draws the veil across the Celestial Ring, ushering in the quiet majesty of night. Her wings, vast and argent-black, unfold like a curtain over the world, bringing dreams, endings, and silence. Hers is the realm of mystery—not to confuse, but to reveal slowly.

Lunala is revered as the custodian of the unseen, the keeper of final breaths, and the gentle hand that ends the day. Her presence is not absence—it is preservation. In darkness, secrets can be kept safe. In sleep, the soul repairs. Under her stars, truths once burned away in daylight may be discovered.

### Origins of Worship

Worship of Lunala began among stargazers, death sages, and the kinless—those who found peace in silence, truth in mystery, and beauty in sorrow. In early myths, she is described as a great silver dragon who rose to catch falling shards of the Celestial Ring and shield the world from blinding radiance. Her act created the first night, the first shadow, and the first dream.

Some legends claim that Lunala was the one who wove the ring from soul fiber itself, binding echoes of ancient beings into crystalline form. She gave the ring to her brother Sol, not to dominate, but to share—a cycle of balance and reverence. While Sol breathes into the ring to bring day, it is Lunala who quiets it, letting it rest and absorb the soul-fiber's deeper resonance.

Her temples began as moonlit sanctuaries for mourners and dreamers—places where no torches burned, but silver pools reflected the stars above. Her faithful believe night is sacred, not for hiding, but for healing.

### Lunala's Influence

The **Umbral Veil**, a silent and secretive order of oracles, soul-guides, and grief-tenders. They officiate funerals, interpret dreams, and aid in quiet passages—literal and spiritual. Their sigil is a crescent dragon wing shading a single star. They wear flowing black and violet robes laced with silver thread.

The **Twilight Weavers**, artists, philosophers, and arcane scholars who study the subtle magic that awakens in darkness—divination, shadowcraft, memory magic, and the mind’s weave. They believe Lunala whispers truths only heard in silence. Their emblem is an hourglass pierced by a crescent moon.

The **Children of the Last Light**, wanderers, protectors, and saboteurs who operate under starlight. They guard travelers in the dark and act when the daylight world fails to. Some are spies or peacekeepers. Others serve as night-blessed rangers or wardens. Their mark is a silver lantern cupped in a claw.

>   **Sigil of Lunala**
> 
> - **Central Motif:** A sinuous Lunar Dragon coiled in the shape of a crescent, its wings spread downward like a falling curtain. The crescent cradles a constellation of five stars—representing dreams, death, secrets, night, and magic.
> - **Veiled Ring:** Behind the dragon is a translucent arc—the Celestial Ring, half-shrouded in violet shadow. Fine silver lines trace through it like a spider’s web, symbolizing the pathways of unseen fate.
> - **Outer Band:** Runic glyphs of reversal, reflection, and rest glow faintly in the dark. The border glimmers under starlight or moonlight but fades completely in full daylight.

### Temples of Lunala

The **Eclipse Cloister**, a stone sanctum hidden in a canyon where no direct sunlight ever falls. The cloister is roofless, open to the stars, and filled with reflective pools used for moon rites and dream divination. Priests walk barefoot through ink-black water to perform blessings for the dying or lost.

The **Archive of Still Pages**, a silent, underground library said to house every dream that has ever been remembered. Only whispers are allowed, and many of the books write themselves in moonlight. Seekers come here for forbidden truths, prophecies, or the last words of the dead.

The **Dreamtide Sanctuary**, built on the shores of a silver sea, this temple functions as both hospice and dream-ward. Patients nearing the end of life are brought here to die peacefully under Lunala’s gaze. Dreamwalkers trained at the sanctuary can enter the unconscious mind to offer solace, answers, or closure.

### Religious Hierarchy

**Moonbearers** – High oracles and dreamseers who oversee rites of passing, moon phases, and prophetic interpretation. They are seen as calm and untouchable—bridges between night and the unknowable.

**Nightwards** – Temple keepers, hospice clerics, and wandering guides who act as both grief counselors and shadow mages. They bring quiet clarity to those in turmoil or fear.

**Starhands** – Novices and caretakers who guide nightly prayers, manage rituals of silence, and light sacred lanterns at dusk. Many are chosen from orphans, mourners, or the forgotten.

### Sacred Practices

The **Last Ember Rite**, performed when someone is near death or has experienced great loss. A candle is lit in a bowl of water, reflecting their final hopes into the sky. As the flame extinguishes, the memory is released to Lunala’s veil.

The **Dreamwalk**, conducted on nights when the stars align across a known fracture of the Celestial Ring. Participants engage in trance-meditation, guided by a Nightward, to seek answers in dreams or commune with echoes of past lives.

The **Moonveil Procession**, a monthly walk at dusk, where the faithful move silently through town bearing veiled lanterns. It honors both those who rest and the quiet powers that protect while the world sleeps.

### Core Tenets of Lunala

##### Darkness is Not Emptiness
Night is not the absence of light—it is the cradle of stillness, healing, and hidden wisdom. Do not fear the dark. Respect it.

##### What Ends is Sacred
Death, dreams, silence, and secrets are part of the cycle. Tend to them. Witness them. Preserve their dignity.

##### Not All Truth Shouts
Some revelations come only in whispers. Seek with patience. Honor the veiled knowledge that cannot bear the sun.

##### Rest is Resistance
The world does not always reward stillness, but rest is sacred. Protect the weary. Offer pause. Heal quietly.
