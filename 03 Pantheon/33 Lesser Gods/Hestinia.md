## Hestinia: Keeper of Hearth/Hospitality

Hestinia, the Keeper of Hearth and Hospitality, is a divine Gold Dragon revered not for conquest or dominion, but for her unwavering guardianship of warmth, welcome, and the sacred power of home. Her scales shimmer like sunlit brass, and her voice is said to echo with the crackle of firewood and the clink of shared cups. She is the matron of inns, the protector of the lost, and the spiritual guardian of hearths both humble and grand.

Hestinia is most revered in times of need—by the poor, the weary, and the orphaned. Her faithful say she is present in every open door, every shared meal, and every hand extended in kindness. While her divine presence is gentle, her wrath is reserved for those who violate sanctuary or exploit the vulnerable. In her eyes, to shelter someone in need is to perform the highest act of worship.

### Origins of Worship

The worship of Hestinia began in the aftermath of a great winter storm that decimated trade routes and isolated entire regions. One surviving tale tells of a lone traveler who found an abandoned tavern in the snow, its hearth mysteriously alight and stew still warm. From this miracle, the traveler survived—and spread word of the “Golden Flame” that would never let a soul freeze or starve alone again.

Over generations, this legend birthed the Hearthkeeper’s Path, a religious tradition that spread not through war or edict, but through stories, generosity, and countless rescued lives. Today, Hestinia’s presence is felt in every welcoming inn, every warm fire offered to a stranger, and every orphaned child taken into loving arms.

### Hestinia's Influence

Among the followers of Hestinia, various groups embody her teaching in unique ways. The **Hearthwardens** are protectors that travel the world defending places of refuge and offering their blades to those who cannot protect their homes. They are equally likely to fortify a crumbling village inn as they are to stand between a mother and a charging beast. Their emblem is a glowing flame cradled by a shield.

The **Gilded Table** are composed of chefs, caretakers, and community leaders, the Gilded Table emphasizes the sacredness of shared meals and hospitality. They run traveling kitchens, host public feasts, and act as social mediators. Their symbol is a golden plate surrounded by ten empty chairs—always room for more.

The **Golden Nest** is an order of caretakers dedicates itself to orphans, runaways, and children without homes. They build sanctuaries, organize education, and form adoptive networks for those who have been left behind. Their robes are stitched with a simple image of a hearth with a child curled beside it. Many of them were orphans once, now devoted to passing on the care they once received.

### Temples of Hestinia

The **Emberhold Hall** is built atop ancient volcanic rock, this grand temple is equal parts sanctuary and communal feast hall. It is said the central hearth was lit by Hestinia herself and has burned without pause for centuries. No soul is ever turned away from Emberhold, and its walls bear thousands of names etched into brass plates—each one a person saved or sheltered there.

The **Golden Hearths**, found in villages, ports, and city slums, these are small but sacred temples open day and night. Each houses a sacred hearthfire that offers literal warmth and symbolic welcome. They double as orphanages, soup kitchens, and emergency shelters, each managed by a Hearthkeeper and several Kindling Hands.

The **Lantern Refuge**, a mountaintop sanctuary where orphans are brought for healing and education, the Lantern Refuge is illuminated at all hours by floating gold lanterns. Each lantern represents a life that has been given a second chance. Visitors often leave tokens here—blankets, toys, or children's stories—to be used by those most in need.

### Religious Hierarchy

**Matron Flame / Patron Flame** – High priests who oversee entire regions and preside over seasonal feasts and rites of hospitality. They are often consulted in times of political or humanitarian crisis.

**Hearthkeepers** – Local clerics who manage temples, cook for communities, and offer shelter to the displaced. They are the lifeblood of Hestinia’s faith.

**Kindling Hands** – Acolytes who perform humble tasks and accompany traveling priests. They are often seen mending clothes, gathering firewood, or playing with children to offer comfort and care.

### Sacred Practices

The **Rite of First Flame**, performed when someone begins a new home, adopts a child, or opens a shelter. A sacred fire is lit with three herbs—sage, sweetroot, and golden thorn—each symbolizing peace, sweetness, and protection.

The **Open Table**, a holy feast held at least once per month in every temple. All are welcome, and titles are forgotten. Elders and orphans, lords and laborers, eat side-by-side.

The **Vigil of Lanterns**, held on the longest night of the year. Followers light lanterns and leave them outside for travelers, spirits of the lost, or those seeking hope. Orphans and their caretakers often share stories of their journeys as part of this ceremony.

### Core Tenets of Hestinia

##### Hospitality is Sacred

Every guest, regardless of origin, deserves kindness, nourishment, and shelter. To provide this is to act in divine accord with the Keeper of Hearths.

##### No Child Left Cold

Orphans, the lost, and the abandoned are sacred charges. Their care is a direct covenant with Hestinia, and their safety is paramount.

##### The Hearth is a Sanctuary

Homes must be protected—be they a castle, a cottage, or a campfire in the wilderness. Violence that violates hospitality is among the gravest sins.

##### Generosity is Strength

True wealth is found not in what one keeps, but in what one shares. Food, space, and time given in kindness ripple across generations.
