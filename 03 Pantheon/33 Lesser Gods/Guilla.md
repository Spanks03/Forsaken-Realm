## Guilla: Keeper of the Hunt

Guilla, the Keeper of the Hunt, is a divine White Dragon whose presence stirs primal fear and reverence in equal measure. Among the pantheon, he is the most animalistic, the most silent, and the most unrelenting. Guilla does not speak. He does not teach with words or prophecy. His lessons come in clawed wounds, breathless chases, and narrow escapes. His divinity lies not in thought, but in motion—in the pure, sacred instinct of predator and prey.

He is the living hunger of the frost, the patience behind the snow-covered stillness, the breath caught in the throat just before the kill. Guilla is worshipped not as a teacher, but as a force of nature—neither merciful nor cruel, only necessary.

To encounter Guilla is to stand on the edge of being hunted, judged only by your ability to survive.

### Origins of Worship

Guilla’s worship did not begin with sermons—it began with survival. Isolated tribes living in brutal tundras came to understand that the wild was not theirs to tame, only to endure. The stories passed down were not parables, but sightings: claw marks in ancient trees, the carcasses of apex beasts killed cleanly and left behind, eyes gleaming like ice in the storm.

They spoke of a white blur, silent but watching. A predator that did not kill out of need, but out of judgment. Those who survived its gaze were considered worthy. Those who didn't were never found.

Over time, hunters, rangers, and wild druids began leaving offerings—bone carvings, antlers, and the breath of their kills—hoping to appease the Hunt-Mother. And sometimes, in return, they found themselves guided through blizzards by invisible tracks, or spared by beasts who suddenly turned away.

### Guilla's influence

The **Fangs of Frost**, warriors who see Guilla as the ultimate test of fitness. They hunt monstrosities and apex predators to prove their strength. They wear trophies of their own hunts and believe Guilla grants silent approval through close calls and near-death moments.

The **Hollow Tracks**, druids and rangers who live entirely within Guilla’s path—eschewing cities, speech, and excess. They walk the wilds barefoot, masked, and often mute, believing words are distractions from survival. They teach through gesture and demonstration alone.

The **Stillborn Cry**, a grim but compassionate order that performs mercy hunts. Where suffering festers and cannot be healed, they end it with clean strikes. Many consider this act the most sacred aspect of Guilla’s nature: ending pain with silence and grace.

> ### Sigil of Guilla
> 
> - **Central Motif:** A white dragon in mid-pounce, lean and streamlined, more beast than god. Its head is low, eyes narrowed—teeth bared in total silence.
> - **Bone Halo:** A thin ring of antlers, tusks, and claws encircles the dragon. There are no runes, no glyphs—only symbols of prey and predator etched in weathered relief.
> - **Silent Snow:** The background is a textured field of snowfall, untouched by track or wind, evoking Guilla’s presence as a stillness before motion.
> - **Blood Detail:** A faint red crescent cuts across the lower third of the sigil—representing sacrifice and the thin line between life and death.

### Temples of Guilla

Guilla has no grand temples. Instead, his followers mark holy ground with kills left untouched, cairns made of beast bones, or claw carvings into stone or tree. Silence is the only liturgy.

The **Shrine of Scars**, a frozen cave of ritual offerings. Its walls are scratched with personal marks from every hunter who has survived a hunt. It is not lit or guarded—only those who can find it are deemed worthy.

The **Cradle of Snowbone**, the ribcage of a massive prehistoric beast turned into a sacred hunting ground. Offerings must be made before entering, and no kill can be taken unless Guilla’s sign—a lone white feather, claw, or breath-cloud—is seen.

The **Echoing Wind**, a solitary peak where wind never ceases. Those who climb it in silence and survive the night are said to return changed. The mountain is considered Guilla’s resting place, and his breath whistles through its crags.

### Religious Hierarchy

Guilla’s followers do not use titles of command. Their hierarchy is earned, not declared, and based entirely on survival, scars, and deeds. Names are often shed.

**Claw-Wardens** – Those who have survived a direct encounter with a divine beast or Guilla himself. They rarely speak. Their authority comes from endurance.

**Stalk-Brethren** – Seasoned hunters and druids who mentor through demonstration. They guide mercy hunts, balance ecosystems, and teach reading nature’s voice.

**Frost-Pups** – Initiates who must live in the wild for one season with no aid. They are not named until they return—and some never do.

### Sacred Practices

The **Blooding Trail**, a trial of self-earned rite. The hunter chooses their quarry, tracks it alone, and must kill without aid, boast, or waste. They leave part of the kill behind for Guilla.

The **White Vigil**, held on the longest night of the year. Followers wait motionless in snow or wild terrain, without food, shelter, or speech, to prove their oneness with the wild.

The **Mercy Fang**, a sacred rite to end suffering—either of beast or mortal—cleanly and without pain. It is considered the truest act of compassion and alignment with Guilla’s will.

### Core Tenets of Guilla

##### The Hunt is the Law

All life is predator or prey. This is not cruelty—it is truth. You live because you earn your place in the cycle.

##### Words are Wind

Strength is proven in action, not argument. Let your scars and skill speak for you.

##### Silence Honors the Kill

To kill is a sacred act. Never boast, never waste, never revel. End it cleanly. Mourn in silence.

##### Only the Worthy Endure

Survival is Guilla’s approval. If you are alive, it is because the Hunt has not yet claimed you.
