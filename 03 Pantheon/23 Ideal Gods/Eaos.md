## The God of Logic: Eaos

_"Ideas come according to their logic. That logic is not rational. It's not linear. We may get to the middle before we get to the end. We may get to the end before we get to the beginning. Be ready for this. Don't resist it."_  
  

Logic is not tied to any emotions or heartstrings. Eaos is the true essence of making reasonable action according to a strict principle of validity. The problem with Eaos is that he sees all valid principles and will happily show opposing reasoning to watch one's principles crumble; He wants to know if one's principles change once they break. A God that rarely makes decisions, but when he does, he removes the fundamentals from the realm.

### Eaos' History

As stated in Elianora V. Scolenruh's transcription of Sage Wynne's tellings of the birth of Terra, Eaos was the first god molded by the Elder God Nosduh. Logic was a significant characteristic of Nosduh's personality that contributed to his ascension into godhood and seemed such a natural creation.

Once birthed into existence, Eaos fell into his role of the eldest son and the tasks needed from his father. As his siblings were birthed, Eaos tried to share his core values, but he saw them ignore him and create a life of their own. Eaos did not try to correct his siblings, but he thought he could develop children who would understand him and make logical sense of the world. With that thought process, Eaos was overzealous and created the Fire, Water, Earth, and Air Genasi, the Dwarves, the Elves, and the Loxodon.

As the life of his children spread across Terra with his siblings' children, Eaos saw corrections that could be made to his creations and other beings. He walked among the people of Terra, as the other gods did, but his all-knowing logical outcomes may have seen all of Terra's creatures, but they did not see the obvious mistakes he made and what was to come until just a moment before.

Eaos used the moment before the cataclysm to warn Cassierrel. As Cassierrel laid down his life for the people of Terra, the Cataclysm of the gods unfolded. Due to the events in the cataclysm, Eaos' imprisonment as the Omniscient was his punishment, as was protecting the Weave from the same people they created.

### Eaos' Influence

There are a few groups that follow Eaos. **The Rational Thinkers** seek to understand every side of thought processes. They are the essences of 'devil's-advocate'; they try to make even the most profound emotions make sense in a logical thought process. Members of the Rational Thinkers worship Eaos because their principles have flipped, and they need understanding.

**The Cerebral** is a different group that worships him for his all-knowingness. They stretch to understand all logical outcomes to know events before they happen. Babbling Antoinette Siltwiggle, a half-deranged yet mildly civilized hag is the current High Oracle of the Savantic Order, a nigh-on monastic organization located halfway between Malmsbury and Sage Wynne. They are followers of Eaos, whose intellectual prowess has developed into precognition and clairvoyance, allowing them to more or less become oracles and soothsayers. It's known that they are contracted to act as advisors within various governmental bodies. Eaos, before the disappearance, was known for making the simplest of changes to a realm that snowballs into massive events because he knew all logical options.

### Eaos' Temples

Most of Eaos' locations of worship have not changed in thousands of years. These ancient locations are predominantly known as a source of a mass amount of knowledge, the most known location gives off a library feel of organized ancient knowledge with a faithful community that takes care of it. Some of the most known locations of worship are the White Grove, Sanctum of Fate, and Sanctuary of Pozdarr.

The White Grove is a small and mostly abandoned source of knowledge located under the ruins of a church in the forgotten city of Todazur now known as Old Cliff. The information of future destruction and people shutting out the Gods were sealed underneath the young city of Todazur and a church of spiritual ancestors was built above it, leaving the knowledge forgotten. When the prophecy unfolded and history eroded the city into Old Cliff, a group of nomads discovered the ruins which are known to be a safe space for Eaos worship. Still, there have been some whispers that they may have even more information on Terra's history.

The Sanctum of Fate is run by Babbling Antoinette Siltwiggle who was mentioned earlier in this section. The library is supported by the Children of the Savantic Order who usually will not let any outsiders into the library without a donation of knowledge. Rumors say that this is Sage Wynne's personnel collection that was stolen sometime after his death but what is known is that it is one of, if not, the largest collection of knowledge in one location.

The Sanctuary of Pozdarr is a collection of people that are living in a cutout of the Spine with the Tecumheim Depths and Pozdarr Falls hiding it unless you are looking for it. Named after the waterfall and a logical general in the War of the Broken Mountain, this temple does not have a collection of artifacts but a collection of people with different points of view coming together for safety and Eaos.

### Eaos' Artifact

When Eaos roamed Terra, he create an pair of rings called _The Grateful Twins_. Eaos spoke of a mortal being or a pair of beings were going to have to raise in front of a great darkness like his creators did in their past. Eaos carved the first ring from his own bone and sealed it with his blood. This ring he gave to his good friend Casseirrel to wear on a chain around his neck to be protected until the right wielder came along. The second ring he shaped from his breath and harden it with his mind alone. This ring he gave to the first Genasi he made, a Water Genasi named Kailu Tecumheim.

After millenium passing, and even the history of the cataclysm was eroded from man mouth, the Grateful Twins are still await Eaos' champion. Legend states that first ring is found somewhere on Glasminster's Peak, where Casseirel's clavicle rested. Their are rumors that the first ring is protected by a distant descendant of Casseirel. Tales state that the second ring is in Tecumheim Depths and has drifted into the Oceri Trench. The Trench are inhabited by the fallen Caeril, beings that have succumb to the Siren blood curse.

The item card for this artifact will be in appendix A.

### Eaos' Children

Eaos found his burden heavy and saw that a couple child was need to help him. Eaos found the most intelligent being and she gave birth to Wynne, Sage of Fate. A seemingly elf-like figure, Sage Wynne became a open figure to the world of Terra but when the city that was named after him was leveled by a group of furys from the Damned Pass, Wynne has been more anonymous. He even gave the title of Sage Wynne to another to continue his job of fate.

Documents described him as a mocha colored elf with silver beard and bald head. With large prayer bead around his neck and thin light colored robes. A large light-grey hood drapped over that most of the time obscured his face. Milky eyes of a blind man but seem to have no trouble in seeing all.

#### Eaos' Champions

**Alignment:** Mostly lawful, often neutral  
**Suggested Classes:** Artificer, Cleric, Monk, Unforsaken, Wizard  
**Suggested Cleric Domains:** Knowledge, Light, Order  
**Suggested Background:** Acolyte, Guild Artisan, Sage  
Most champions of Eaos are brilliant and logical beings, either completely devoid of emotion or full of it. The followers are generally scholars, always looking to understand or learn a new way of thinking. It's a commonplace to find these astute people in oracular or strategic positions.

#### Earning and Losing Piety

You increase your piety to Eaos when you honor him or the rational thinking processes through an act such as these:

- Studying different cultures and the way they think and understand the world
- Solving a challenging riddle or puzzle
- Smiting the unwise and foolish in Eaos' name, whether it be physically, mentally, or verbally
- Helping strategize in a battle

---

Your piety score to Eaos decreases if you fall prey to ignorance and emotion through acts such as these:

- Jeopardizing others through rash or foolish actions
- Willingly subverting or impeding a wise course of action
- Failing to plan appropriately for a challenge

#### Eaos' Devotee

_Piety 3+ Eaos trait_  
  
You've begun the intense mental training required to be a devotee of Eaos. You gain proficiency in Arcana, History, Insight, and Investigation.

#### Eaos' Votary

_Piety 10+ Eaos trait_  
  
Your ability to quickly and logically think of solutions and wisely plan a course of action in response to a sudden event knows no bounds and far exceeds the standard. Your initiative modifier is no longer equal to your Dexterity modifier; instead, it's the sum of your Intelligence and Wisdom modifiers.

#### Eaos' Disciple

_Piety 25+ Eaos trait_  
  
Your constant study of the world around you and your increased mental capacity have made you particularly observant, with eidetic memory to boot. You receive the _Keen Mind_ and _Observant_ feats. In addition, because of your newfound memorization capabilities and your quick thinking has allowed you to have slight precognition. You receive one portent die, as from the Wizard's School of Divination. Follow the restrictions presented in the subclass.

#### Champion of Validity

_Piety 50+ Eaos trait_  
Your comprehension skills are unrivaled, bar the divine. Your aptitude and resourcefulness are unprecedented. Due to your perseverance in understanding anything and everything that takes place, you can affect the world, bending it in favor of your whims because you know all the outcomes. At the beginning of combat, you may choose to replace a creature's initiative with a number of your choice. Alternatively, suppose you opt not to change a creature's initiative at the beginning of combat. In that case, at the beginning of each of your turns, you can either reverse the initiative order or make one ally (including yourself) be first in the initiative order for the round, as your expeditious calculating allows you to either fool an enemy into making an illogical decision or telling an ally to move at the perfect time.
