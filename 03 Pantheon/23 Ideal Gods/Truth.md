## The God of Truth: Truth

_"The divine representation of veracity, facts, and authenticity; a god with a power so great it can only be expressed with the word of the divine, Truth."_  
  

Due to the nature of being a god, Truth knows no bounds. Anything Truth says must become factual; however, there are some limitations to his power; any being of equal or greater power is unaffected by the word of Truth, and only the present and the future are influenced by the truth, not the past. Lastly, statements of Truth may only be reverted by a wish granted by **The Elder Gods** or the **Omniscient**.

### Truth's History

Eaos was the first god that the Elder God Nosduh sculpted. Quickly following suit with Nosduh, Ogul's first son was of the personality he not only held himself but also what he thought a new world would need; Truth.

Once put into history, Truth was more like his creator than was expected; he was the holder of the truth and had his father's humor in creating it. The first truth he made was the existence of the Tortle, the Tabaxi, the Firbolg, and the Kalashtar.

Truth found his words were more infectious than he thought. He always tried to emulate his older brother but did not understand everything he did and couldn't help acting on his urges. Truth's following became prominent, and he had fun, but the Cataclysm snuck up on him. Due to the events in the Cataclysm, Truth was also imprisoned as the Omniscient as his punishment, as was protecting the Weave from the same people that they created.

### Truth's Influence

Truth has two groups of followers, each driven by two distinct interpretations of the god. Firstly, **The Seekers**; seek whatever version of their truth they believe is somewhere; these followers typically have a question to an answer they've yet to discover. The other group of followers is known as **Revisers**; they are driven by the power to influence change. These followers typically have some sort of 'wrong' to make 'right again'. During the times when gods would meddle in the affairs of mortals, Truth was known to be a trickster of sorts. He would take on many forms, such as a young Kalashtar child or an owl. Still, most notably, it is said that when one knowingly denies the facts presented to them, Truth will appear to this person in the form of a moon-sized eye and reveal to them the truth of the universe, driving anyone who listens mad. Truth does not disgrace those who lie or have lied; he simply lacks the tolerance for those who look at the facts, validity, or authenticity of something and deny the evidence of truth.

### Truth's Temples

Most of Truth's locations of worship are hard to find and even hard to get to, needing to be sought out to find the real truth. These locations vary in uses: from a place for vision quests to be taken place to find truth from within, to a place to scry across Terra to find truth, and even possibly a place to change a truth to a lie, a lie to a truth, or someone's truth to another's truth. Not may locations are known but some that have been seeking Truth may be familiar to the Temple of Truth, Shrine of the Looking Glass, and the Mosque of Isolation.

The Temple of Truth is known by most and is the easiest to access. Like all gods, Truth too has a temple on the train. Truth's Temple is located in Pompus Place of Veriwind while sharing a thin wall in the same cart of the Temple of Eaos, and while it is a little bit more generic then the other temples on the train, it still gets quite a bit of foot traffic. An half container with religious decoration and seating meant for small groups to talk amongst themselves. Once entering the Temple of Truth all beings are in a zone of truth spell that cannot be prevented in anyway, even with magical means. This area is used by many for normal conversation, job deals, and even subtle interrogations.

The Shrine of the Looking Glass is a location of scrying. Known by the few but if researched they would find that it is found a 12 day ride into the Todazur Flats from Wolf's Pit. A tall thin tower with no doors, windows, or visual entrances of any kind. Through research, the person may find the combination of stones to press on the tower to open an entrance in a boulder 2 days ride northwest from the tower. When walking through underground maze back to the tower, you enter the shrine. The Shrine of the Looking Glass has a crystal ball that can scry to any person, place, or thing in any realm or plane of existence.

The Mosque of Isolation is a place for visions. If looking for it, the mosque is found somewhere in the Alesund Valley's sulfur ponds in a small region locally known as Omeadahre. In a place where a tribe of people called the Yupreica can only live due to their full body suits and breathing apparatus's. The air in the area is toxic not only to breath but also to make skin contact with. The Yupreica made a village in a dome in the middle of Omeadahre for protection. In the center of the village is a white bark tree with purple and pink blooms that makes mass amounts of clean air called the Yupreica tree. The Yupreica people have dug a small tunnel under to the roots of the tree where there is small traces of toxic gas that it is taking in and others. This dark hole is the Mosque of Isolation, where people can go and commune with ancestral spirits or Truth himself to find all inner truths.

### Truth's Artifact

Truth created an artifact for people to find truth named _The Snare of Truth_. Truth found that some when displayed the truth would still not believe and thought of a way to punish the ignorant while still helping the people that seek truth. Truth created a glass bottle with the sands of time and spoke nothing but truth inside of it with the inscription:

>[!quote|mark] Truth
>  _**Ask me some Questions, <br> but more than just One. <br> Become a Reflection, <br> if asking for Fun.**_  

As the sun rised and set through the millennium, the Snare of Truth passed many hands. Some say it is resting in one of Truth's temples, others say it is in the college of Eriwall in the Head Master's office as to protect anyone from falling into the snare. You may hear some rumors of Ominous, one of Truth's children, having the snare to find the right people to fall into or use the glass bottle properly.

The item card for this artifact will be in appendix A.

### Truth's Children

Truth did seek out to have children in his spare time but seem to create one inadvertently. With is ability to create truth into lies and lies into truths, Truth accidentally created a being to fill the gaps of his truths. Ominous, his son who was birthed by a Kalashtar, become the Vessel of Lies and some may know him as the Duke of Nightmares.

Ominous is discribed as a shadow or monster to some, they are most likely missing up one of his creations, the Nalabel genie. His is slender figure in black robes, most of the time with a hood up making him look similar to a reaper. His thin lipped mouth raised out of the shadow of the hood when he talk to show a indescribable thin tattoo tribe on his lower jaw when the soft words echo beyond his sharpen teeth.

#### Truth's Champions

**Alignment:** Mostly neutral  
**Suggested Classes:** Cleric, Druid, Monk, Paladin, Wizard  
**Suggested Cleric Domains:** Arcana, Knowledge, Light, Nature, Trickery  
**Suggested Background:** Acolyte, Guild Artisan, Investigator, Sage  
Most champions of Truth are steadfast and firm in their beliefs. These followers have unlocked an ability that sees past the falsehoods within reality, gazing upon the veracity of the natural world. Be careful not to garner these Speakers' ire, for with a thought, they will flip the truth of your world on its head.

#### Earning and Losing Piety

You increase your piety to Truth when you honor him or the finding the truth through an act such as these:

- Changing a truth
- Teaching the ignorant
- Seeking out new truths

---

Your piety score to Truth decrease if you fall into ignorance of truth through acts such as these:

- Willingly being ignorant of something
- Denying the facts of something true
- Creating rumors to make people believe something false, subverting the truth
#### Truth's Devotee

_Piety 3+ Truth trait_  
  
As you follow in the ways of Truth, you learn to mimic the most minute amount of his power. You can cast _command_ with this trait, requiring no material components once per long rest. Due to your piety, the _command_ spell evolves for you, regardless if you cast it with this trait or spell slots, becoming a way to magically breathe life into your own truths. The spell's duration becomes a minute, you can speak 1 short and direct sentence instead of a word, and the spell affects the target(s) regardless of if they are undead or can't understand you as you briefly speak a new truth into existence. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for this spell.

#### Truth's Votary

_Piety 10+ Truth trait_  
  
Your exertion in changing the world around you grows. You gain expertise in Persuasion or a +5 bonus if you already have it. Also, if you speak a truth at a creature, that creature must make a Charisma saving throw (DC equal to 8 + your Charisma modifier + your proficiency bonus) or be charmed by you.

#### Truth's Disciple

_Piety 25+ Truth trait_  
  
You further enhance your mimicry of the god you worship, making you able to speak more truths at once. As a bonus action, you can cast _command_ without expending a spell slot, and a third eye appears on your forehead for 10 minutes or until your concentration ends (as if you were concentrating on a spell). During this time, you can cast _command_ as a bonus action on each of your turns without expending a spell slot.  
  
Any creature charmed by you automatically fails its saving throw against the _command_ you cast with this feature, and _command_ can't be countered when cast this way. Once you use this feature, you can only use it again after you finish a long rest.

#### Champion of Truth

_Piety 50+ Truth trait_  
  
You've become a living embodiment of the values of Truth. You are a Speaker, a being who can change the present or future on a whim with a single word. You gain access to a new exclusive ritual only available to people who have genuinely inherited and adopted the ideals of Truth through strict and diligent reverence. If you spend 24 hours reflecting on the axiom of your life and being, you can truly articulate into the fabric of the universe. Decide on a truth that your DM approves of. For 1 minute, your truth becomes a reality. Your truth can't affect gods, demigods, or creatures with a challenge rating higher than a third of your piety score, rounded down (to a maximum challenge rating of 30). You can only use this ability once every 1d4 days.
