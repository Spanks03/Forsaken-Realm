## The God of Rage: Temp

_“There's an anger in all of us. Some hidden rage that you keep at bay is the hunger that you need.”_  
  

The God of rage is a god that fuels his followers toward their goals. The rage has a way of corrupting the weak minded by burning away their mind but if you fuel the flame enough and are willing to let it burn, you will be in fast track in becoming a champion of Temp.

### Temp's History

Springing from the fury in his father's heart, Temp was born from Ogul's chest: the fire that was sparked in his competition with Nosduh. Ogul did not mean to bring Temp into existence, however, his need to compete with Qhathos resulted in the god's birth. As the 4th born, Temp's constant rage caused concern amongst his siblings and creators, and so they sent him away to discover a way to calm himself. In his exile and isolation, his wrath only grew, causing him to lash out. His outburst led to the creation of some of the most feared and infamous monstrous races: the Minotaur, Orc, and Centaur.

Realizing the power that comes from his creations, Temp set out to enflame the sparks of fury and rage that reside in the hearts of all the created beings. Wreaking havoc across the planet, Temp would purposefully cause strife in the lives of the mortals: causing jilted lovers to act on their impulses, make those who've been cheated seek out revenge, or purposefully creating battles and skirmishes. Temp worked hand and hand with Vimnera on several occasions, giving those with revenge in their heart the push they need to enact it.

During the cataclysm, Temp did nothing but make the situation worse by enraging everyone, mortals and divine alike. For his reprehensible actions, he was the second god imprisoned as the Apex Predator as his punishment, causing him to take up the task of destroying the Weave so he can be released.

### Temp's Influence

Temp isn't necessarily followed by any particular organized groups, however, several of the nomadic and Hypnotic Wild tribes worship various tribal forms that they have created that all represent Temp, especially the lycanthropic ones. The feline and partially lycanthropic **Ashen Spearbloods** and the bloodthirsty **Wanderers of Greed** are two such groups.

The **Ashen Spearbloods** The Ashen Spearbloods is a feline-centric tribe of people who live within the Hypnotic Wilds. Separated from the more traditional tribes due to their acceptance of lycanthropic people and use of mysterious and unknown magic, the Spearbloods thrive in their individuality and isolation. The tribe is home to many several clans and peoples from all walks of life, who all have one thing in common: in some way, they are cats. The Leonin clans all fall under the leadership of Great Warrior Amnez Windmask, a large Leonin who towers over most Minotaurs and Goliaths. The Tabaxi clans are lorded over by a thin and lithe Tabaxi male named Hidden Flame of the Thrill,

a heavily disfigured tabby who uses his Aberrant sorceries to mystically protect the Spearbloods and serve as the presiding body over any trials and punishments that happen within the Spearbloods. Everyone else within the tribe are various forms of werecats or feline shifters who don’t necessarily have their own clans, but they fall under the wise leadership and grace of Nightwalker Amayeta, a werepanther centaur whose curse has permanently changed her bottom half into that of a panther. The tribe follows a form of Temp called the Ancestral Spirit Gishilam that grants the ability to become a werecat to any that prove their worth. The clan shows its dedication to rage when they go on large hunting raids, led by Amnez, called Prey Pursuits where 3 parties of 20 hunters receive speed-enhancing and rage-inducing tonics that cause them to go absolutely feral as they rapidly prowl through the Wilds, eagerly ripping apart any prey they find to bring back to the Spearbloods in order to have a large feast called the Indulgence.

The **Wanderers of Greed** are a multiracial nomadic tribe that are simply nothing more than mauraders and raiders who travel throughout Togrulyia pillaging and destroying anything they can find. They also have a sect that roves within the Eastern side of Eophoris. Their leadership isn't static as their is always a new leader coming and going as only the strongest one amongst them can lead after various fights to the death. One of the few things that unite the various sects, other than name and philosophy, is their worship of Grumnarsh, a entity believed to have the lower body of a lion, the torso of a Orc, and large horns that would make any Minotaur jealous. Grumnarsh is another aspect of Temp and encourges their tribe to commit horrible atrocities on the so called "civilized" folk in the world, the ones who are resisting their base primal urges.

### Temp's Blood Curses

Temp's meddling also resulted in the creation of the various blood curse forms. The blood curses tortured his father's creations and almost mirrored his eldest brother's creation and the Elder Gods standing forms. His first blood curses were the intrusion in mortal life caused him to merge an angry hunter, named Balan Marou, with his pet wolf creating the first werewolf and merging a hunted soldier, named Dyrk Armanci, with a bat creating the first vampire. At first a spiteful act toward his creators watching forms and their creations called the Harmonians, but after witnessing the beastial rage that consumed the cursed souls and the havoc they created, Temp set out to create four more forms of blood curses and made their curse contagious so they can spread his rage for him.

The other four blood curses after lycanthropy and vampirism is thalassothropy, aerothropy, catathropy, pyrothopy. The thalassothrope was made next after the first werewolf. Made from a cursed fisherman maiden named Isadora Salantine, Temp made this curse to torture the Tritons, his fathers creation, and to stain his brother Eaos' first creation of water. These weresiren [Siren] are drawn to the water's heartbeat and uncontrollably change when submerging and are even known to walk in direction of water when getting wet by some means. When the change is complete, the being is a alluring humanoid from the waist up and a strong shark-like tail.

The Siren is known for is beautiful song to allure sailors and people alike to jump in the water and follow the siren to the deeps of the sea. The curse seems to be transferred to victims that survive a bite from a full formed Siren and their first change is always their swim upwards to safety.

The third curse is the aerothrope, a wereharpy [Harpy] are drawn to altitude. These being constantly looking upwards feeling like they are suffocating on the ground but the thinner air or even a giant gust of wind can trigger their change. Temp saw how incredible his alluring siren worked on the people near the water and wanted have something similar inland. The Harpy in it full form is an alluring bird person that song hypnotizes people to climb up to them and step off to their deaths. This curse seems to be transferred to victims that survive a bite from a full formed Harpy and their first change is always their flight from that fall.

The fourth curse is catathropy, a werebilesse [Liar] are drawn to underground caverns and dungeons. A hard curse for an average adventure to handle due to its hardest to recover from the change. The change is slow almost convincing themselves and others that down is the direction of travel through tremendous deceit and fully changing from the earth in the air and the darkness around them. Liars are shape shifters and will take any shape that will convince its victims to get lost in the depth of the dungeon, from a wounded adventure/maiden to a party member that stepped away from the group. Liars are very selfish and rarely attack a strong person instead of waiting for them to sleep or past out to feed. This curse seems to be transferred to the victims that survive a bite from a full formed Liar and usually start the slow change after severely wounding or killing the Liar.

The last curse is pyrothopy, a werekivego [Fury] are drawn to the flicker of flames. Hypnotized by any flame, it is hard to tell that a fury has transformed other than a subtle trail of smoke coming out of their nose and mouth. The fury searches is area with is thermal vision for the largest gathering of people and walks towards it. When the fury finds the group that it wants, it will walk up to it and without warning explode doing catastrophic damage and splattering lava everywhere in the area. The fury will be hurt from the explosion but will awake after all of the flames are put out, which are hard to manage. The curse seems to be transferred to survivors of the explosion or rescuers that are burned by the flames when trying to put out the flames.

### Temp's Temples

Temp's temples are traditionally animalistic and primal in nature, usually tucked away deep in the various forests and wilderness of the world. Someone can easily discover that they're near a temple to Temp if they spot several werewolfs and Minotaurs patrolling and guarding a certain area. The temples are adorned with the carcasses of sacrifices given in the name of Temp and are little more than large shrines.

It's not uncommon for someone who manages to kill all of the guards of the Temple and step inside of it, to undergo a painful process and become a werewolf themself or embracing his blood curses as Temp praises them in their wrath and helps them further embrace it. Some of the most known locations of worship are the Harbinger Slab, the Putrid Yew, and the Animus Hot Springs.

The Harbinger Slab is large sacrificial flat stone that is found midway between Sage Wynne and Los Almohodas along the Damned Pass. This place of worship is mostly used by the people of Los Almohodas that make the pilgrimage to Sage Wynne to find answers but through the travels are enraged by the rigorous travels. The Damned Pass is infested by blood cursed individuals and this slab is usually used for saving a loved one or self from suffering through a curse that they make have contracted by sacrifice, going through their blood curse transformation for the first time, or even embracing the change permanently.

The Putrid Willow is a gigantic rotting willow tree in Mahosard Thicket that is a mushroom forest in the Vontrie Snow Plains about a two day ride east from Netherfield. The drooping branched make a sanctuary from the horrible effects of the forest. Spores from the mushrooms snow on the people that enter the thicket causing them to slowly think that everyone is a foe that needs to be killed whilst a simmering rage becomes to boil. These spores are brushed off by the drooping branches and this smelly sanctuary becomes a calm realization of the world around them. All realize the anger that needs to be embraced to leave the thicket and some find a rare form of worship of Temp through calm tranquility.

The Animus Hot Springs are a cluster of hot springs on the Vakkail's Tail close to the western edge of the Vontrie Snow Plains. This safe haven is nearly a death sentence for anyone trying to reach it by foot. These Springs are home to blood cursed beings that have full embraced the transformation and have made an exclusive community called the Pack, to escape from hunting men. The springs are known for teleporting submerged beings across Terra to the person they hold the most hatred for. The Pack call this ritual Temp's Call.

### Temp's Artifact

When Temp raged through Terra, he created a weapon named _Anger's Release_. Temp felt the need to release the built up anger across Terra and found many outlets to due so and one was this artifact. Temp battled the Elder Gods favorite creature, Casseirrel, in what was later called The Titans Fray. This dual was not to the death but Temp wound Casseirrel by removing one of his ribs. Shape out of Temps hatred and the rib of Casseirrel, this weapon was forged into existence and thrusted back into the dragon were it remained until his passing many years later.

After ages blew by, Anger's Release was stumbled upon by a dwarf explorer named Eris Ruthgorr on the submit of Coaltona across the Spine. This weapon had many wielders and due to the nature of it, it is hard to track down. The last known location was in the Movin Desert on the continent of Osse. Some say it sank into the ocean of sand dunes never to be found and others say a beast called the Sand Serpent guards it.

### Temp's Children

Both Qhathos and Temp found love in a tiefling woman named Florence Khan. Florence was a member of the Benediction who also worshipped Temp and used her position as an escort to kill hundreds of men in a bloodthirsty rage that stemmed from mistreatment she received as a child. Qhathos fell in love with her love of the hunt for more challenging targets and Temp fell in love with her rage. With Temp she had Ildros, the Beserker of Destruction. Ildros is a monstrous shapeshifter molded in the image of his father's favorite creations. In his normal form, he is a massive minotaur with towering horns and a giant build. When he decides to change form, he becomes an even more monstrous flaming werewolf that retains his horns. When angered, especially in his werewolf form, Ildros is prone to randomly exploding, causing massive explosions of lava to erupt from his body, dousing anyone and everything in a 200ft radius with the burning liquid. Ildros uses this ability to utterly destroy anything around him. He can not be reasoned or negotiated with as he is nothing short of a mindless beast acting as a force of nature.

#### Temp's Champions

**Alignment:** Mostly neutral, often evil  
**Suggested Classes:** Barbarian, Blood Hunter, Cleric, Forsaken, Sorcerer, Warlock  
**Suggested Cleric Domains:** Death, Tempest, War **Suggested Background:** Criminal, Hermit, Haunted One  
Most champions of Temp are mindless storms of destruction. They are simply beacons of hate and harbingers of extermination and annihilation. Those who have reached this level of madness are rarely coherent enough to talk to, let alone reason with. They only know of the fiery sensation in their soul, and they readily embrace it, wiping out entire city blocks and massacring enemies and innocents alike when they fight.

#### Earning and Losing Piety

You increase your piety to Temp when you ~ through an act such as these:

- Wanton destruction
- Constantly succumbing to Bloodlust
- Using your rage at least once a day

---

Your piety score to Temp decreases ~ through acts such as these:

- Meditation in order to calm your rage
- Not succumbing to Bloodlust at least once a week
- Refusing to give in to your inner rage

#### Temp's Devotee

_Piety 3+ Temp trait_  
  
As you begin your journey into the endless abyss of rage, you learn to tap into the burning fire deep in your soul, releasing it. You gain the Untamed Rage and Bloodlust features from the Barbarian's Path of Untamed Rage. You have a number of rages equal to your Constitution modifier, and these rages are controllable for a number of rounds also equal to your Constitution modifier. If you already have these features, for the duration of your rage, you can make a single melee weapon attack as a bonus action on each of your turns after this one. In addition, if you have the spellcasting feature, you can cast cantrips while raging. This does not allow you to use spells given to you through features such as the _Shadow Touched_ feat.

#### Temp's Votary

_Piety 10+ Temp trait_  
  
You've begun to master your anger, not only using it as a source of power but as a source of vitality. If a spell, such as _raise dead_, has the sole effect of restoring you to life (but not undeath), the caster doesn't need material components to cast the spell on you. Also, you can cast a modified _wrathful smite_, without using a spell slot with this trait (DC equals 8 + your primary ability modifier + your proficiency bonus). When you cast _wrathful smite_ this way, you can expend a rage to increase its damage by 1d6 for each rage you expend, and you gain the extra damage on any attack, not just weapon attacks. If you have the spellcasting feature, you can cast another spell on your turn after you cast _wrathful smite_, so long as it requires an attack roll and only targets and damages one creature. You can cast _wrathful smite_ this way even while raging. Constitution is your spellcasting ability for this spell.

#### Temp's Disciple

_Piety 25+ Temp trait_  
  
Your mastery over of your own rage has allowed you to excite and enflame the rage of others around you. If you hit an enemy with an attack while you are raging, there's a chance for your rage to start their own. Once per turn, you may have a creature you've hit with an attack make a Wisdom saving throw (DC 17) or become overcome with rage. If the target fails, they fall into a deep rage, for a number of turns equal to their proficiency bonus, where each turn they must attack as many creatures as possible, using every action and all of their movement. AFter a creature's rage ends, they are immune to this ability for the rest of the combat.

#### Champion of Wrath

_Piety 50+ Temp trait_  
  
You have reached the absolute pinnacle of wrath, to the point where it's become a vehement mania. Your indulgence in your explosive temper has paved the way for you to alter yourself into the embodiment of anger. As an action, you can let your anger break free, totally consuming you. You transform into an _Angry Sorrowsworn_ for 1 minute. While changed this way, you are always under the effects of Untamed Rage and in Bloodlust, with the damage bonus from your rage starting at 1, regardless of if you were raging before transforming. If you reach 0 hit points as an _Angry Sorrowsworn_ or the minute ends, you revert to your standard form with 4 points of exhaustion, and you lose any remaining rages you have left. You can only use this ability again once you no longer have exhaustion and you have rages to consume.
