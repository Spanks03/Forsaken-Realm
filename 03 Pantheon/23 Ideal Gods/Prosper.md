## The God of Luck: Prosper

_“Luck is when skill and opportunity come together. The only sure thing about luck is that it will change and when it does, you never know what worse luck your bad luck has saved you from.”_  
  

Luck isn't always good and it isn't always bad. Luck is completely neutral and fair, with the only thing that can change it is skill. Prosper enjoys injecting luck, good and bad, into any and every situation just to see how people will react to sudden changes.

### Prosper's History

The god of luck, Prosper, was the last born of the children and was born and used as a sort of backhanded olive branch between the elder gods. On one hand, Ogul used him as a peace offering to Nosduh to try to quell the flames of their competition , while on the other hand, Ogul used it as a way to horde prosperity and luck for himself, leaving only suffering for Nosduh.

As the youngest, the lack of oversight allowed Prosper to easily and naturally fall into his role of a very mischevious god. Prosper loved sticking his nose in the business of mortals and influencing their lives in unpredictable ways. In his frivolous meddling, he decided he wanted to follow in the footsteps of his siblings and create mortal life, but he wanted his to fun and whimsical like all the wildlife that populated Terra, so Prosper created the Jergal, Kenku, Grung, and Harengon. Prosper loved subverting everyone's expectations, divine and mortal alike, for profit by either failing something he shouldn't or succeeding at something they didn't think he could. This constant risk taking and exchanging of goods and currency eventually became gambling as the mortals took what Prosper did and added their own rules and traditions to it.

Prosper never missed a chance to make something more chaotic and hectic, which is why his actions during the cataclysm almost had him trapped in the Void with his other misbehaving siblings. It was only due to his ever-present luckiness helping Eaos and Truth get the upper hand during the incident that caused the Elder Gods to add Prosper to the Omniscient.

### Prosper's Influence

There are only a few groups that are dedicated to Prosper as he's one of the gods that is usually casually worshipped by most of the commonfolk.

The most prolific followers belong to the church of Prosper called the **Silver Vaety**. While all of these followers are gambling addicts, they have a fierce loyalty and devotion to ensuring freedom and fairness in everything. This surprisingly small order is equally surprisingly powerful. The current Vicar Prosper, Jebbek, and her Hands of Prosper steamrolled a potential coup by the 3 family heads and 8 pirate captains of Chang-Chi-Lou, even though they were outnumbered 10:1. Unbeknown at the time, Jebbek is a powerful cleric who's power is strongest within cities, and even stronger in Chang-Chi-Lou, the city of Gambling.

Another group of followers of Prosper is actually a group that occasionally also follows Qhathos and is an underground fighting ring called **The Citadel**. This fighting ring is almost unknown to the normal populace, due to it being invite only, but its invites can come from anywhere and anyone. Recruiters for the Citadel are known to travel to Osse and Togrulyia in order to find more successful and strong fighters. The organization is ran by a man named Markus "Meatbag" Bourkia. Despite being a tall, fat, and rotund human, Meatbag is incredibly strong and his strength is only rivaled by his intelligence. It's rumored that Tal Honcese fought and won in The Citadel once.

Most non-religious being of Terra practice a ritual/superstition call Prosper's Largesse to increase their luck. It is a practice of hanging a easy accessible coin purse from ones hip with an offering and a single trinket inside. This coin purse is meant to be stole. This practice distributes luck both parties by reducing violent crimes and helping out people of need.

### Prosper's Temples

Prosper is one of the few gods to have temples that serve purposes other than religious ones. Several of his churches also function as gambling houses and are famously known to be incredibly fair, both the House and the patrons winning and losing equally. These churches have strict anti-cheating policies that are physically and magically enforced by the clergy and staff. The Indulgent Cathedral is the most well known, and largest, of these churches. Located in Chang-Chi-Lou, patrons are well protected from the chaos of the city and the Cathedral itself is strictly neutral in family and pirate matters. Over the lifetime of the city, there has only been one time where the families and pirates challenged the church, and the resulting battled ended in the church being in charge of the city for a whole week while the family heads and pirate captains were essentially in time out. The Cathedral has an underground sister church named the Bright Ring in Osse.

The Bright Ring is a church in a gamble hall named The Lucky Blossom in the city of Ba'Cre. Originally ran by a halfling named Maurice Ravenblossom until he left to join the Resistance, the Lucky Blossom is a run of the mill gambling hall for the scum of Ba'Cre to cheat, steal, and lose their money. The Bright Ring is held underneath the Blossom as a fair fated place for lost of the dog-eat-dog city. Ran by a family friend of Maurice named Flynn Boggenlow, the Bright Ring is a place for people that are down on their luck or lost to turn their luck around. This shelter's location is known only by the few and people are only brought here by the church's clerics.

### Prosper's Artifact

When Prosper laughed through Terra, he create a compounding cluster of artifacts called _The Five Pieces of Luck_. Prosper saw his siblings create items for champions and he want greatness to be able to fall onto anyone's hands, with a little bit of Luck. Through his time with the people of Terra, Prosper saw the people make powerfully meaningfull things out of meaningless items and he thought he would do the same.

Out of a piece of copper that his first born, Linmir, gave him, he shaped into a copper coin with Linmirs face on a side for the first piece of luck. He gave it back to his son and he made it into inspiration for currency in the world. This piece could be anywhere and across Terra but legend says it could be the random copper that is on the street or in the wishing fountain.

Out of a stone Prosper shaped the second piece of luck into a twenty sided die. This perfectly weighted die he dropped in one of the first gambling taverns in Terra named The Waiting Snapdragon which ended up being bought by a jackpot winner named Hao Chang and rumored to be the start of the Chang's fortune.

Out of a elven wine bottle cork, Prosper made his third piece of luck. This bottle was the drank at his wedding day and was made to make sure others have better luck than he had with love. This half stained cork has not only blessed many bottles but also have used as a charm from saving the Resistance from the Legion hundreds of years later.

Out of a marble he was given by a child, Prosper made his fourth piece of luck. Prosper shot the marble back into the world as fast as he made it. Legend states that the glass eye of the goblin kingpin, Baeek the Master of the Marsh, is this marble.

Out of a chipped rose colored button from Matilda de Mowbray, a tiefling women that Prosper fell in love with and married, he made the last piece of luck. After be killed by his siblings, Prosper made this piece stronger then the rest because it means the most to him. Legend says that this piece was the only reason Prosper had the luck in the cataclysm to be on the right side. Rumors say that this ragged button was sewn on a blouse and have brought many people to greatness but when they have enough money to get a nicer shirt, pass it on with their luck.

### Prosper's Children

Prosper had a bushel of children across time and realm. Prosper's marriage to Matilda produced a son named Linmir. Linmir, with his piece of luck, became the Merchant of Fortune and was the creator of currency system and the blessings of fortune.

Linmir looks like a regal merchant in gold, silver, and gems all over. A golden half mask covers his face with sapphire eyes glimmering behind it. He skin seemly painted copper but seems to be flawless.

#### Prosper's Champions

**Alignment:** Mostly chaotic, often neutral  
**Suggested Classes:** Bard, Cleric, Druid, Rogue, UnForsaken  
**Suggested Cleric Domains:** Trickery, Nature  
**Suggested Background:** Criminal, Gambler, Guild Merchant, Hermit, Inheritor, Urchin  
Most champions of Prosper are brilliant and logical beings, usually confused as being followers of Eaos but easily distinguishable by their more laid-back attitude that Eaos' champions don't have. These gamblers of fate usually will think their actions through but still rely on their propensity towards risk-taking. Be careful when battling one of them, for when things seem to be going your way, the whole world will flip, and suddenly you'll be hitting rock bottom.

#### Earning and Losing Piety

You increase your piety to Prosper when you bring prosperity to yourself and others through an act such as these:

- Win a risky gamble
- Win something when the odds are heavily stacked against you
- Set up a system to help keep a starving village fed

---

Your piety score to Prosper decreases when ~ through acts such as these:

- Cheating instead of letting luck handle everything
- Supporting or playing in a lopsided system, where one side has more probability of winning over another
- Refusing to settle a deal over a game of luck when offered.

#### Prosper's Devotee

_Piety 3+ Prosper trait_  
  
You can cast _fortunes favor_ with this trait, requiring no material components, a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum of once). Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for this spell. Also, whenever a creature that you can see within 60 feet of you rolls a 1 or 20 on the d20 for an attack roll ability check, or saving throw, you can use your reaction to invoke your god's power. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier (a minimum of once). Each time you use this feature, you choose one of the following effects:

- Blessing. Either you or the creature (your choice) has advantage on one attack rolL ability check, or saving throw of its choice that it makes before the end of its next turn. It chooses to use the blessing before it makes a rolL not after. Once used, the blessing vanishes.
- Fortune. Either you or the creature regains hit points equal to 2 + half your character level (your choice)
- Fumble. The creature must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw (DC equals 8 + your primary ability modifier + your proficiency bonus), or else it is knocked prone, drops what it is holding, and is unable to take reactions until the start of its next turn.

---

You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

#### Prosper's Votary

_Piety 10+ Prosper trait_  
  
Due to your innate prosperity, you learn how to recognize the sensation of approaching bad fortune. You have advantage on saving throws against traps and on initiative rolls, and you can't be surprised In addition, when you are critically hit by an attack, you reduce the damage you take from the attack by an amount equal to half your piety score (to a maximum of 25).

#### Prospers's Disciple

_Piety 25+ Prosper trait_  
  
Your natural luck never seems to run out and it's prone to bursting out of you at any moment. You gain the _Lucky_ feat with 4 luck points instead of 3, or an additional luck point if you already have it. In addition, you've learned that some times you just need to leave things to chance. As a bonus action, you pray for your never-ending luck to continue. Until the end of your next turn, when you make an attack, you can briefly close your eyes and choose to use the blessing. You have advantage on the attack roll and ignore disadvantage for being unable to see the target. If you hit with the attack, the blessing is used up, and if the attack deals any damage, you increase the damage dealt to one target by 2d6 + your character level.

#### Champion of Luckiness

_Piety 50+ Prosper trait_  
  
You are able to control your lucky streaks with perfect precision. It seems like nothing can ever go truly wrong for you. At the start of any of your turns you can choose to enter into a lucky trance. If you do, roll 1d6. For the next number of rounds equal to that number, you have advantage and ignore disadvantage on all attacks, ability checks, and saving throws, and creatures have disadvantage on saving throws against spells you cast. In addition, every time you make an attack roll, roll 1d100. If the number is equal to or lower than your piety score (maximum is 75%), your attack is a critical hit, regardless of what you rolled.  
Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you take a long rest.
