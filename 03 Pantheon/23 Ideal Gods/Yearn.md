## The God of Desires: Yearn

_“Desire knows no boundary. It is simply the very nature of the universe itself. Desire is the very root of the universe.”_  
  

Desire is more than a simple ‘want’, it is the motivation behind every decision that has been or will be made. Yearn does not have any qualms regarding one's endeavors, nor does she care of the nature of your desire. Yearn has never been truly depicted or seen in her natural form. She is always viewed as the person of which you most desire, and for those who claim the desire no one, Yearn will know deeply in your heart that which you desire most. Do not fight your true desires in the presence of Yearn, for doing so may summon the wrath of her twin brother Datarr.

### Yearn's History

As the cataclysm came to a close, the Elder Gods Nosduh and Ogul came together and released the only things he had left into the realm, twins. Nosduh shape their suffering into Datarr to show Terra the one thing that he thought was missing and Ogul shaped their desires into Yearn.

As her twin brother struggled to get followers, Yearn did not. Her beliefs swayed with most being's natural instincts. Yearn saw all the beings that were made and loved all, so she made a race called the changlings that could choose to look like whichever race fit their desires at that moment. Then she made the satyrs, a race that could not help but follow their desires. Yearn realized that this urge may be a bit strong for most creatures so she created a hard to locate island named after herself to prevent the satyrs from invading upon others desires.

### Yearn's Influence

Yearn’s “**Free-Spirits**” follow what they truly desire, though their judgment tends to be clouded by their heart. Free-Spirits tend to be content with the choices they've made. Yearn has the most followers of the gods due to her accepting nature, but with the outstanding quantity, comes a lackluster quality. Free-Spirits do not typically care of the feelings of others and tend to ignore most of their own problems, choosing instead to drink, party, and do whatever makes one content with their own existence; but the power of Yearn herself is not one to scoff at. Yearn is sometimes referred to as the “Wish queen”, due to her ability to grant “wishes” to those who follow her. These “wishes” are not of the spoken variety and are granted when she sees fit; what you truly desire is the wish you may be granted.

### Yearn's Temples

There are many Yearn locations of worship across Terra; most large cities have a Yearn temple in or right outside of their walls. These locations mostly are place of healing or pleasure, this is the reason some are not built directly in the middle of the city. People travel to the doors to save loved ones from aliments, seek cures to horrible diseases, their final hope; Other location of worship people go to forget about the stresses of the world and drown in pleasures. There are some that are more well known than others like the Vitality Pool, Pleasure's Oasis, and the Primal Island.

The Vitality Pool is a large healing pool in a temple located directly in-between the western edge of 'The Wood' and the Tecumheim Depths. This marble temple has a small village built around it to take care the longer cases. This knee high crystal clear pool is full of Yearn and Amra clerics to help heal and comfort people in need. There is a pit in the center of the pool where they send their fully devout follows in and if the waters sense their pureness, they are reborn a cleric. They also drift their dead in the depths of the pit.

Pleasure's Oasis is middle of the Todazur Flats where many say that you must spend thirteen days in the heat mirages and others say you will find it you hope of survival is gone and dehydration has come and gone. This beautiful Oasis saves poeple from death crossing the Shubae Wastes in the Boiling Badlands. This magical place has all the pleasures one could think of: incredible food, cold water, and gorgeous people.

The Primal Island or the Island of Yearn is somewhere in the Unresting Sea south of Eophoris. This island is made by Yearn herself and a sanctuary for the saytrs and others that found a way upon the island that is described in text as "Heaven on Terra".

### Yearn's Artifact

When Yearn skipped through Terra, she created an artifact called _Bag of Want & Need_. Yearn, like most, gained tired of moral beings asking for material things. She created this bag out her own womb and once this bag was made, refused to humor any desires that were material object rather than experiences, feelings, and heartfelt meaning. This bag was given to one of Yearn's first follow that wanted to be a great adventure, a dwarf named Hardouck Grumbletank.

As time passed by and dreams became memories, the Bag of Want & Need is still awaiting anyone that is seeking material greatness or adventuring convenience. Legends say that the bag was lost on Mahoneau Island in the Gaullinet Islands south of Eophoris. These moving and orbiting islands not only move but float out of the water and submerge underwater all orbiting an island moves but stay on the Sea's surface called Mahoneau. These island are a great feat to traverse for the adventurers of Terra and many say that they are riddled with incredible equipment and loot from adventurers that did not survive their travels. If there is a place where the Bag of Want & Need would be needed it and a place that is guarded from people that want it would be this place.

The item card for this artifact will be in appendix A.

### Yearn's Children

Yearn has had countless children across Terra. Yearn struggled to trace the fathers to the children but she does have a few well known children. Amara, the lunar twin to Nyrella, is half-Saytr that became the Lady of Life.

Amara looks like a bright glowing female Saytr with beautiful flowers and ivy in her amber hair. As she walks, life follows in the form of plant growth, light, and even birth. She wears a thin white robes that layer delicately covering her.

#### Yearn's Champions

**Alignment:** Mostly Chaotic, often neutral  
**Suggested Classes:** Bard, Cleric, Forsaken, Interstellar, Paladin, Warlock, Wizard  
**Suggested Cleric Domains:** Knowledge, Life, Peace **Suggested Background:** Criminal, Entertainer, Far traveler, Sage  
Most champions of Yearn will be the happiest people you've ever seen. They'll seem unnaturally ecstatic and blissful, as their power stems from simply indulging in the things they love most. Usually, the life of any party they're in attendance, these champions will make even the most stoic and cold-hearted enemies turn into mindless adoring fans.

#### Earning and Losing Piety

You increase your piety to Yearn when you honor her or chasing your desires through act such as these:

- Doing what you please, despite your inhibitions.
- Convincing someone to chase their dreams
- Follow your heart

---

Your piety score to Yearn decrease if you put your desires on hold through acts such as these:

- Suppressing your desires
- Punishing those who act on their desires
- Dissuading those who wish to act on their desires

#### Yearn's Devotee

_Piety 3+ Yearn trait_  
  
You've become more empathetic with desires those around you as a result of you fulfilling your own. You can cast _detect thoughts_, requiring no material components, a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum of once). You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for this spell. The desires of any creature who's thoughts you read are revealed to you. After discovering these desires, you can use your action to conjure up an inanimate object in your hand or on the ground in an unoccupied space that you can see within 10 feet of you that coincides with what the creature wants. This object can be no larger than 3 feet on a side and weigh no more than 10 pounds, and its form must be that of a nonmagical object that you have seen. The object disappears after 1 hour, when you use this feature again, or if it takes or deals any damage.

#### Yearn's Votary

_Piety 10+ Yearn trait_  
  
Just as you've learned to divine what people wish for, you've also learned that you can exploit it for your own needs. You can cast _incite greed_ with this trait, requiring no material components. Once you cast the spell in this way, you can’t do so again until you finish a long rest. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for this spell.

#### Yearn's Disciple

_Piety 25+ Yearn trait_  
  
You've become the thing people want most. They want your status, your power, and your connections. Your appearance permanently gains an otherworldly aspect that makes you look more lovely and fierce. As an action, choose a number of humanoids within 60 feet of you, up to a number equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum of one). Each target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC equals 8 + your primary ability modifier + your proficiency bonus) or be charmed by you. While charmed in this way, the target idolizes you, it speaks glowingly of you to anyone who talks to it, and it hinders anyone who opposes you, although it avoids violence unless it was already inclined to fight on your behalf. This effect ends on a target after 1 hour, if it takes any damage, if you attack it, or if it witnesses you attacking or damaging any of its allies. If a target succeeds on its saving throw, the target has no hint that you tried to charm it. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a short or long rest.

#### Champion of Desire

_Piety 50+ Yearn trait_  
  
You've reached the point where you can simply tell people what you want them to desire. You've become a master of cravings and obsessions and you can change them or indulge in them as much as you want. As a bonus action, you can assume a magically majestic presence for 1 minute or until you are incapacitated. For the duration, whenever any creature tries to attack you for the first time on a turn, the attacker must make a Charisma saving throw (DC equals 8 + your primary ability modifier + your proficiency bonus). On a failed save, it can’t attack you on this turn, and you choose a new target for its attack or the attack is wasted. On a successful save, it can attack you on this turn, but it has disadvantage on any saving throw you make it roll on your next turn. Additionally, as a bonus action you can make a creature roll a Wisdom saving throw (DC equals 8 + your primary ability modifier + your proficiency bonus), becoming stunned as you fill their mind with thoughts of their greatest desire on a failure. Once you assume this majestic presence, you can’t do so again until you finish a short or long rest.
