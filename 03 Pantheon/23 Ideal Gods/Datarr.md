## The God of Suffering: Datarr

_“Life IS Suffering. Out of suffering have emerged the strongest souls; the most massive characters are seared with scars.”_  
  

The God of Suffering not only causes suffering to others, but he is also suffering himself. Datarr has struggled with strength due to the not-so-appealing nature of his mantra. Just because Datarr has the smallest following does not make him a being to underestimate. He overuses his power to the levels of suffering to make his followers inherently stronger than most, but not without suffering themselves.

### Datarr's History

As the cataclysm came to a close, the Elder Gods Nosduh and Ogul came together and released the only things he had left into the realm, twins. Nosduh shape their suffering into Datarr to show Terra the one thing that he thought was missing and Ogul shaped their desires into Yearn.

Datarr stepped into the world with major growth. Growing much faster than his twin at first due to the millions of being suffering from the cataclysm. But after the shock and scarring of that apocalyptic event, Datarr struggled to grow and show his purpose in the realm. He looked a saw this world full of races that were not connected to him, so he made the insectoids. He made a race that whole life cycle embodies suffering so he can stay alive. Datarr did his best to show others his purpose but ended up finding a place full of suffering and taking follower instead of waiting for them to find him.

### Datarr's Influence

Datarr's followers usually start very young, so they only remember or understand life with suffering. **The Chained** normally have their personal identity physically shredded and are maimed in some way but have the strength to move the life force around the battlefield. They are known to cause incredible suffering to themselves and their opposers, and win, despite the enduring experience. Their blood magic is devastating. They are led by a wingless Aarakocra named Rikkik, who carries around a Cat o' Nine Tails that he uses to whip both himself and other people on the battlefield. Followers are frequently found trying to stray away, even sometimes speaking of trying to destroy Datarr when they are strong enough, but after feeling the power that Datarr gives them, they turn into loyal servants. Datarr does not only reward the causing suffering to others but also the internal suffering of oneself to help others. This philosophy is best demonstrated by Dr. Marlowe Mandorci and his **Pillars**, followers of Datarr who view his teachings as a reason to bear the suffering of others, instead of increasing it. Members of the Pillars act as humanitarians and philanthropists, going through towns and cities and stealing the suffering of the people for themselves. They are frequently called to ease the passing of people in pain on battlefields or hospitals.

### Datarr's Temples

The locations of worship for Datarr are far and few between. When you would normally find the average practicing Datarr followers in the locations where suffering occurs to cause more or relieve it from others, the Temples are teaching locations to show the beauty of suffering. Most of these Temples are locked doored building and will not let you in unless you are willing to be locked in after. Some examples of famous Datarr Temples are the House of Sentiment, Zadicus's Orphanage, and the Temple of Datarr.

The House of Sentiment is an old large 'O' shaped building with a courtyard in the center that cannot be seen from the outside on the out skirts of Los Almohodas. The door is always locked and window are boarded but do not seem to have glass. This place is know to be a teach people what true suffering is. What happens inside is unknown but the people are a blessing to the city. At night, the homeless and freezing people of Los Almohodas's suffering is taken away by the barefoot, ragged clothed, blooded people that exit the house.

Zadicus's Orphanage is located outside of the Bastion of Ulfang the Black on a southern satellite island of Togruylia. Named after the solo victor in the the First Tournament, Zadicus the Relentless won his freedom from the Horde and opened up the orphanage. Word of history says that this orphanage is the only way Datarr stayed alive in his early years of godhood. This massive orphanage has turned into the location of where all lost and unwanted children are sent in Togruylia and are rumored to have a slim survival rate. Many of these children are refugees from The Tournament that is thrown for the 'Old Ones's pleasure. The children that survive this orphanage and age out, become sought out tools to shape cities.

The Temple of Datarr is famous for being the only temple of Datarr that does not have locked doors, other than the small shine in Eriwall for student use. This Temple is located in the capital city of Eophoris, Veriwind. Most people have forgotten the true name of the capital and just call it the Train. The Temple of Datarr on the Train is open for all but because its location on Skid Row, it is mostly visited by the lower class that is traveling. This is use as a hub of suffering relief to the lower class on the train and is very popular even though most do not dive into the worship.

### Datarr's Artifact

Once Datarr's strength grew after his intial struggle to gain follower, he created _The Savior's Chains_. Datarr sought out for lifetimes for man's understand that him and his twin are cut from the same cloth. Datarr forged this chain from his blood and tears that he shed for his followers and he placed it in the place where his strength began to grow, in Zadicus's Orphanage.

After history made the events of the Suffer's Children and the Damnation of Datarr into legend, the Savior's Chains still beg to be used. After given to a child that at first seemed to be the least deserving, he grew into the man called Marlowe Mandorci. Dr. Mandorci held this artifact for years but after 47 years of wielding it, made a pilgrimage to the orphanage to pass it on to the next child that is worthy. There are many rumors of where the chains may have ended up but the most popular are: Mandorci found a little girl in the orphanage named Daisy Painn and past it on to her and she traveled to Osse. Second is that so much time has past that one of the many chains that are use on the children is the artifact but will only latch on the child that truly embodies suffering. Third, that it was never Marlowe's to give away and it will appear to the true champion of Datarr.

The item card for this artifact will be in appendix A.

### Datarr's Children

Datarr only had a few bastards that bleed across Terra and the realm. Datarr fell in love with a terminally ill half-elf that even with his power could not reduce all her pain but could keep her alive for many years longer than she was suppose to live. They had a child named Ova, the Maiden of Pain.

Ova looks like a wet black haired women covered in cuts and blood. A constant slow stream of tears rolls down her cheeks but blends in with the blood. Wearing a chainmail dress that is repaired with barbed wire, her voice is surprisingly soft and calm. Legends say that anyone that touchs Ova will forever be in pain until their demise.

#### Datarr's Champions

**Alignment:** Mostly lawful, often evil  
**Suggested Classes:** Blood Hunter, Cleric, Forsaken, Monk, Warlock  
**Suggested Cleric Domains:** Death, Life, Trickery, Twilight **Suggested Background:** Criminal, Gladiator, Hermit, Knight, Urchin, Outlander, Sage  
Most champions of Datarr are covered in scars. You'll find these beings consistently suffering in one way or another: whether from them helping others or hindering others; these blood users are a force to be reckoned with. Be wary of the Datarr follower who seems to be on their last legs, for that's when they're at their strongest.

#### Earning and Losing Piety

You increase your piety to Datarr when you honor him or suffering for yourself or others through act such as these:

- Bringing on additional suffering, whether it be your own or someone else's
- Bearing someone else's suffering and/or pain
- Prolonging suffering longer than it could have gone without your imposition

---

Your piety score to Datarr decrease if you runaway from opportunities to reduce others suffering through acts such as these:

- Turning a blind eye to someone clearly suffering
- Putting someone out of their misery
- To not bear any suffering/pain

#### Datarr's Devotee

_Piety 3+ Datarr trait_  
  
As you begin the rigorous and painful process of being a follower of Datarr, you are gifted with insight on how to control, reduce, or abuse the suffering in others or yourself. You gain a number of Blood Points equal to your half piety score. As a free action, you can sacrifice any amount of hit points you have; for every 5 hit points that you lose, you gain 1 Blood Point. These Blood Points can manifest the various Blood Pooling abilities from the Way of the Bleeding Heart Monk. You can use your Blood Points in place of ki points to pay for the cost of the abilities. After a successful long rest, you regain all of your Blood Points. If you are a Way of the Bleeding Heart Monk, instead your ki point pool is increased by a number of ki points equal to your proficiency modifier.
#### Datarr's Votary

_Piety 10+ Datarr trait_  
  
As you learn more about your blood related abilities, you learn to invoke a rite that infuses your weapon strikes with extra elemental energy and recovers your magical energy. Choose one of the three Crimson Rites that don't require a prerequisite from the Blood Hunter class. As a bonus action, you can activate any rite you know on one weapon you’re holding. The effect of the rite lasts until you finish a short or long rest. When you activate a rite, you take necrotic damage equal to one roll of your hit die. This damage can’t be reduced in any way. While the rite is in effect, attacks you make with this weapon are magical, and deal extra damage equal to your hit die of the type determined by the chosen rite. A weapon can hold only one active rite at a time. Other creatures can’t gain the benefit of your rite.

If you have the spellcasting feature, you may instead sacrifice any number of your Hit Dice, regaining a number of spell slots who's total level are equal to or lower than half of the number of Hit DIce you lost. In addition, you may use your body as an Arcane Focus, as long as you do not have your maximum amount of health. Also, you may take 1d10 damage to replicate 50 gp worth of material for a spell. If this brings you to 0 health, the spell will not be cast. However, you get to keep the spell slot.

#### Datarr's Disciple

_Piety 25+ Datarr trait_  
  
You can channel your life force into the power of your attacks. When a creature you can see within 60 feet of you fails a saving throw against a spell that deals damage, you can expend a number of Hit Dice equal to the level of the spell. Roll the expended Hit Dice and add them together. The damage that the creature takes increases by an amount equal to that total.

#### Champion of Suffering

_Piety 50+ Datarr trait_  
  
Through the constant enlightenment that's been written in and on your body in the form of various scars, broken bones, and pain filled days, you've managed to grasp at and understand what true suffering is. You've reached the point that not many have, and most don't want to, and you can now truly embody the suffering around you. As a bonus action, you can become one of the two embodiments of suffering: The Leper or The Savior. Depending on which you choose, you transform into a variation of yourself and you use the statblock of the chosen type. You retain your Charisma, Intelligence, and Wisdom scores while transformed. The transformation lasts for a minute or until you hit 0 points, and when it ends, you lose all remaining Blood Points and drop to 1 hit point. You can only use this ability again after 1d4 days.
