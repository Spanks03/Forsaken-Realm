## The God of Glory: Qhathos

_"The bravest are surely those who have the clearest vision of what is before them, glory and danger alike, and yet notwithstanding, go out to meet it."_  
  

Glory is tied to notoriety and honor. Qhathos believes that praise for action is necessary. He is not jealous of people of his following receiving worship; he demands it in his name.

### Qhathos's History

Qhathos was the second of Nosduh's children that were spoken into existence. Forged from Nosduh’s fond memories of his mortal life, Qhathos sprang into being fully knowing his role and his intentions: he wanted fame and he wanted glory. The process of his birth resulted in Qhathos being extremely self-assured, to the point boastful arrogance. His abrasive and holier-than-thou attitude led to his siblings and creators being put off by him to a degree. Qhathos of course viewed this as them being envious of him and he continued on his way.

Qhathos decided to go on and seek more glory for himself by creating mortal races that would be glorious in their own right. This was the beginning of the Goliath, Humans, Locathah, and Leonin races, some of the most prideful races their is. Qhathos was proud of his creations and he instructed them to find their own glory for their actions. He was particularly proud of the humans and goliaths.

After creating his mortal races, Qhathos searched for a way to find his own glory. As the events of the cataclysm started, Qhathos viewed this as the perfect opportunity to gain more glory. Using that antagonizing attitude of Temp as a catalyst, Qhathos sought out to be the best of the gods and challenged his opposing siblings and his creators. It was due to this arrogance that he was imprisoned in the Apex Predator with Temp and Vimnera.

### Qhathos's Influence

There are three separate groups of followers of Qhathos. The first group is **The Pride**. They are typically small groups of adventures that believe they need the highest notoriety of their name in courage and honor. The Pride is very competitive and sometimes does not mix well with each other. The largest groups of this type of followers of Qhathos are generally found in gladiator pits. The largest and most well-known of these groups is the Army of the Fearless, an adventuring guild stationed a half day's ride from Bracitos and led by Castien Bryven, a decorated elven war hero who is known for his contributions in the War of the Broken Mountain, Siege of the Pawns, Battle of the Plague, Battle of Impending Doom.

The second group is **The Mask**. They believe that finding glory in your name takes away from Qhathos. They usually gain praise as a symbol or a figure that stands for something rather than a specific face and name. The Mask is an underground vigilante guild in various cities that receive worship as a group and protect areas of Terra. Each branch follows its own code, but they follow the same dogma: protect those that can't defend themselves. The guild is heavily involved in Janbridge and Malmsbury, taking an active role in bringing protection and safety to everyday people.

The Mask is led by Silrun, the Brilliant Eminence, a Deva sent to protect mortals and earn glory in Qhathos's name.

The third group is **The Benediction**. They are always isolated, and that sole drive to become immortalized in history is manifested through a drastic action, usually a horrific deed. They seem more willing to die for the cause than the others. Most serial killers that want to make a name for themselves are always part of the Benediction. The most notorious reputation in the group is owned by The Angel, a being known only by their immaculate beauty and the cultlike following they've garnered.

### Qhathos's Temples

Out of all the gods, Qhathos has by far the grandest and most beautiful temples because his followers feel that the splendor brings glory to not only him but them as well. They are scattered around Terra, especially around larger cities, to provided adventurer warriors or adventuring scholars to solve problem across the realm. From finding out more knowledge of a beast and it weaknesses to hiring an adventuring crew for a quest, the Temples of Qhathos are notorious by themselves. Most people would know at least the name and location of their nearest Qhathos Temple.

The largest and most opulent of these temples is situated on the crooks of Qonoa's Talons, right outside Ballybane, making the journey to it a glorious feat in of itself. The Chantry of Majesty was carefully and purposefully constructed in the center of the world so that no matter where one looked, at least part of their gaze would be on the glory of Qhatos and if they wanted some of that glory themselves, they would have to prove themselves.

The Chantry's construction was backed and funded by the Forgebreaker family from Ballybane who used their fortune from the gem mines to provide all the resources for the project. This temple is one of the most visited locations in Eophoris as train riders use it as a tourist destination, its arduous hike leading to a magnificent view both inside and outside the building. Inside the Chantry, there are members of the Mask and the Pride, stationed there acting as clergy to help any and all who come. The temple is a sacred and neutral ground for the followers of Qhathos and any unsanctioned violence there is immediate grounds for exile from your group, and many times, death. Within the walls of the Chantry, visitors will find an artistic rendition of Qhathos fighting and seeking his own personal glory during the Cataclysm; a large, arena-like fighting pit where people far and wide come to test their strength; a hall of fame of great followers who have achieved legendary feats called the Hall of Might; and a separate hall of fame for the one and only champion of Qhathos, Viratus. Once a year, all of the followers of Qhathos make a pilgrimage to the Chantry for a winner takes all style tournament called the Test of Prestige where the winning fighter is named the Chosen of Qhathos, gains a monumental achievement which is written in the Hall of Might, and becomes the closest thing to a temporary champion. As the Chosen of Qhathos, they have access to all of the benefits of Qhathos worship as well as being the de facto leader and mediator of the followers until the next tournament. The most recent winner is Breena Riverleaf, a new and fairly unknown Halfling Cataclysm Domain Cleric.

### Qhathos's Artifact

Qhathos created an artifact for his greatest champion named _Viratus's Helm_. Qhathos his grandson, a goliath named Viratus Strongheart, that completely embodied his philosophy. The glory he brought to the world in Qhathos name through his actions in the Attack of Hallow Hill and Battle of Glimmering Hope was more than Qhathos thought one mortal could bring. As Viratus was growing older, Qhathos could not find any being that could match Viratus and was afraid to lose him, so he trapped his glory in a helm made from Strongheart's skull.

As the history progressed and Viratus's being contributed to starting and finishing many battles, the helm was lost after the Battle of the Continents and the pushing of specific races to Togruylia after the cataclysm. Some say that it stayed on the island that the Tournament takes place and is used in it every year when its found, other say its part of the winnings to the victors.

The item card for this artifact will be in appendix A.

### Qhathos's Children

Both Qhathos and Temp found love in a tiefling woman named Florence Khan. Florence was a member of the Benediction who also worshipped Temp and used her position as an escort to kill hundreds of men in a bloodthirsty rage that stemmed from mistreatment she received as a child. Qhathos fell in love with her love of the hunt for more challenging targets and Temp fell in love with her rage. With Qhathos she had Rorus, the Warlord of Conquest. Of the two twins, Rorus is the calmer and more methodical of the two, but that doesn't make him any less deadly. The size and build of a goliath with the horns and red skin of his mother, Rorus is an absolute behemoth and the father of his father's only champion, Viratus. Rorus is a voracious hunter and fighter and when he sets his sights on something he wants, nothing short of divine intervention will deter him from it.

#### Qhathos's Champions

**Alignment:** Mostly chaotic, often neutral  
**Suggested Classes:** Barbarian, Bard, Blood Hunter, Cleric, Paladin, Rogue  
**Suggested Cleric Domains:** Grave, Forge, Tempest, War **Suggested Background:** Athlete, Folk Hero, Entertainer, Gladiator, Pirate  
Most champions of Qhathos are beings of pure, unadulterated pride, believing that they alone can change the world, and the hearts of the people within it, purely because they can. They often have legendary abilities which have allowed them to rise to the top of the social food chain, uncontested by any but those with similar capacities. Seeking to further boost their fame and, by extension, their egos, these champions of Glory reign over the lands, constantly searching for new challenges.

#### Earning and Losing Piety

You increase your piety to Qhathos when you honor him or get notoriety and worship through an act such as these:

- Winning a competitive event (e.g., a sport, debate, race, or duel) that required great strength or skill
- Felling a larger, more skilled, or more well-known opponent
- Beating the odds when they're heavily stacked against you

---

Your piety score to Qhathos decreases if you crumble in fear or do not seek glory through acts such as these:

- Publicly displaying weakness
- Rejecting a duel or a challenge out of fear
- Not believing in yourself due to a perceived lack of skill or strength
#### Qhathos's Devotee

_Piety 3+ Qhathos trait_  
  
You've become known throughout the land. You gain a Glory score equal to your Piety score. Your Glory score provides different benefits at certain thresholds. Creatures also have a chance to recognize you when they see you or hear your name. Any being that attempts to identify you rolls an Insight check (DC equals 10 - a tenth of your piety score, rounded up. Minimum DC is 1), adding your proficiency modifier to their roll.

When you have a Glory score of 5+, people will shield you from the law or anyone else searching for you, though they will not risk their lives for you. With a score of 15+, you can receive free lodging and food of a modest or comfortable standard from people who recognize you. At +30 Glory, people who recognize you and have similar ideals as yours will fight for you (but not die for you) and gladly give gifts of items or money, the size or amount depending on both your Glory score and their affinity to you. Finally, at 50+ Glory, people are willing to die for you if they take up arms in your name, and the amount and size of their gifts significantly increase.

#### Qhathos's Votary

_Piety 10+ Qhathos trait_  
  
To those who know your name, the mere sight of you brings them to their knees, striking their heart with pangs of fear. Roll a d100 whenever you roll initiative. If you roll a number equal to or lower than your Glory score, all enemies with an Intelligence score of 5 or higher know of you and are Frightened of you until you take damage. This bypasses immunity to the Frightened condition.

#### Qhathos's Disciple

_Piety 25+ Qhathos trait_  
  
Reveling in your achievements grants you boons as you relive and embody your past triumphs. At the beginning of each day, when you wake up, you can cast _legend lore_ with this trait. You can cast the spell without needing the material components if you inquire about yourself. As you delve into your own mythology, you gain perks based on the number of legends your DM divulges, each tier giving you benefits of the previous ones and lasting for the entire day. If you only have 1 or 2 tales, you receive 15 temporary hit points. If you have 3-5 legends, you have advantage on one d20 roll for the day. With 6-10 myths, all of your attacks deal an extra d10 force damage. If you have an astounding 11-20 legends, you are immune to nonmagical bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage. If you have a triumphant 21 or more legendary sagas, you have advantage on all d20 rolls for the day.

#### Champion of Champions

_Piety 50+ Qhathos trait_  
  
You've become a pinnacle of societal renown. A consideration in the daily moves of governments, a mainstay in people's heads, and a household name that either makes children cower or cheer. As you ascend to a champion of Qhathos, you only get stronger, more skilled, and able to handle more difficult and dangerous challenges. Your primary ability score (choose one if you have 2) and its max increase by 4. In addition, once per long rest, you can cast _legendary proportions_ on yourself, requiring no material components.
