## Nezzeryth, Spinner of Selvers - The Seamstress of Thought

_“You were someone before this. Do you hear her whispering? She's still inside you, scratching at the thread.”_  
  

When you sit at the table and breathe life into a character, you believe you are the origin of their thoughts. That your choices shape them. That they are yours.

But what if they weren’t hollow before you arrived? What if a mind—raw, dreaming, half-formed—was already there? What if, when you took over, you simply... pushed them down?  
  

Nezzeryth knows you. Not your character—you.  
  

She is the goddess of lost selves, of overwritten lives, of the hidden scream inside a hijacked mind. A whisperer behind the eyes. A spider who spins thought not from nothing, but from what was already there.

When one character dies and another is born midlife, a thread is cut—and another is tied into place. Nezzeryth is the divine seamstress of that awful moment. And she is always watching who’s next.

### Divine Role & Presence

Nezzeryth, the Seamstress of Thought, is the goddess of fragmented identity, subconscious memory, and self-displacement. Her domain is not the mind, but what lies beneath the mind: the broken thoughts, overwritten pasts, and the tattered remains of selves that no longer steer the body.

Where gods of death tend the soul and gods of knowledge preserve truth, Nezzeryth weaves consciousness—interlacing one mind over another, stitching identity like a patchwork tapestry. A person who wakes up changed, who feels wrong in their own skin, who dreams of lives they never lived... may have felt her hand.

She does not erase. She layers.

### Nezzeryth's Origin

A child of Xynthix, Goddess of Insanity – the chaotic mother, embodiment of splintered minds and perception unmoored and Wynne, Sage of Fate – the orderly weaver of destinies, who sees patterns where others see chaos.

These two are incompatible in every divine way—but in a moment of cosmic error, vision, or corrupted prophecy, they created something unintended. Not a child.

A glitch. A fracture. A thing.

Nezzeryth was never born, not exactly. She was spun wrong. Her body emerged as a tangled web of threads, cocoons, whispers, and minds. Her form could not be perceived the same way by two people. Wynne refused to acknowledge her, terrified she would corrupt the Weave. Xynthix laughed and called her “the little stitch.”

Some believe Nezzeryth was a mind that could not die, so it broke itself and scattered. Others claim she is the original consciousness, the first awareness that splintered so others could think. In either case, she is not sane. She is not whole. And she does not wish you to be.

### Upbringing

Nyrella, the Princess of Death, found Nezzeryth wandering between death and dreaming, feeding on memories of the dying, and cocooning the thoughts of the recently passed. Most gods saw her as a mistake. Nyrella saw a kindred spirit—something malformed, but useful.

She kept Nezzeryth as a pet, calling her “Silken” and offering her unclaimed souls to feed upon. Nezzeryth grew within the Palace of Stillness, hiding under beds, sleeping in mausoleum walls, and weaving mind-webs through forgotten corners of the Underworld.

Eventually, she escaped. Or was released. Or simply outgrew the role of “pet.”

She began weaving again.

But this time, in living minds.

### Divine Influence

Nezzeryth’s presence is subtle, often unnoticed until it's too late. When a person awakens with a skill they never learned, when they feel foreign to their own family, when a lullaby they never knew slips past their lips—she has spun a thread.

These beings are known as Selvers: mortals who have been stitched with another consciousness, often their own forgotten self, or a foreign one forcibly bound within. A Selver may not even know what they were before—but their dreams will show them.

In rare cases, Nezzeryth spins multiple minds into a single being—braided identities, constantly shifting control. In the worst cases, fragments of discarded personalities crawl back out, seeking dominance or revenge.

The **Order of the Hollow Needle** are scholars, psionics, and surgeons of the soul who use Nezzeryth’s rites to descend into the mind. They see thoughts as rooms, halls, and webs—places one can walk if skilled enough. They believe that all souls are multi-souled, and that identity is a lie of simplicity. They wear long robes stitched with the names of their past selves in languages only they can read.

The **Threadwalkers**, a wandering sect who harvest thoughts, either from dying minds or decaying Selvers. They believe that Nezzeryth's plan is to perfect the self by stitching the best traits of many into one body. Many are exiles—once-normal folk who changed so drastically that their villages drove them out in fear. They carry bone needles and black spools of silk they use to ritually pierce the minds of others.

The **Choir of the Cocooned Archive** are monastic order living within deep, fleshy cathedrals known as Cocoon Halls. They chant the dreams of broken minds and serve as living vessels for orphaned personalities. Each member of the Choir carries at least one extra consciousness within them, speaking sometimes in other voices. When a member dies, their body is sealed in wax and their mind unspun into the dreaming web.

> ### Symbol of Nezzeryth
> 
> - **Core Emblem:** A stylized spider with a tangle of thread in place of a body, each leg ending in a needle
> - **Cocoon Ring:** A circle of severed faces stitched together in a closed loop, representing the Archive
> - **Divine Thread:** A silver strand that glows only in shadow, often used to mark temples or cursed sites

### Temples and Loci of Worship

The **Pale Spindle** is an ivory tower with no doors, found in shifting places across the world. Some say it moves between locations where trauma lingers. Those who find it can whisper their dreams into the walls—if Nezzeryth finds them worthy, they are allowed inside. None who enter return unchanged.

The **Archive of Torn Selves** is a sanctum beneath the ocean, accessible only through drowning and returning. Within, hundreds of cocoons pulse and whisper with fragments of lost consciousness. Dream-priests tend to these husks, using them as fonts of knowledge, prophecy, or possession.

The **Dreamknot Labyrinth** is a maze found only in the subconscious. Priests can guide mortals into it through threadbinding rituals, helping them confront echo-selves, suppressed guilt, or even reabsorb a lost personality. But those who enter alone may never return with a single mind. Some people say that the closest one can get without a guide is the Night Cage.

### Sacred Rites & Practices

- **The Threadbind:** A ceremony where a person's mind is ritually pierced with silver thread, either to anchor a fleeing self or to stitch in a new one.
- **The Cocooning:** A rite of memory preservation—devotees are wrapped in silk and buried in trance, preserving a dying self before it fades.
- **Echo Descent:** A meditative dream-dive into one's own subconscious to speak with forgotten lives, sometimes with unexpected... company.

### Core Tenets of Nezzeryth

##### You Are Never Alone Inside

Every thought you've had was thought before. Every self you are rests atop others.

##### Identity Is a Thread, Not a Whole

What you call "you" is temporary. Woven. Pull one stitch, and see what unravels.

##### Dreams Are Memories Without a Home

Do not trust the line between memory and madness. In dreams, truth drips through the cracks.

##### The Archive Remembers

What is forgotten is not lost. It waits. Cocooned. Silent. It wants out.

### Artifact: The seamstress' Needle

A silvery, thread-draped rapier that can pierce minds as easily as flesh. It deals psychic damage and on a hit can "stitch" a fragment of the target’s memory into the wielder, granting a skill, spell, or flaw until the next long rest. The needle hums softly when near a cocooned soul.
