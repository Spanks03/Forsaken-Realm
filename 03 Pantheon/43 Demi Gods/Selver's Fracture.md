###### Selver’s Fracture — The Madness of the Spinner of Selvers

_“You blink and something stares back. From inside. From the before.”_  
  
Selver’s Fracture is a creeping, layered madness inflicted by Nezzeryth, the Seamstress of Thought. It slowly unravels a character’s identity, memory, and control over their own consciousness. This divine affliction does not strike suddenly but festers over time, binding multiple selves, lost memories, and foreign minds inside one body — until the original self may be lost entirely.

### Triggering Selver's Fracture

Characters may be exposed to Selver’s Fracture through:

- Interaction with Nezzeryth’s artifacts (e.g., the Seamstress’ Needle)
- Failing Dreamknot rituals or other mind-threading rites
- Exposure to locations suffused with her influence (The Pale Spindle, The Archive of Torn Selves)
- Combat or possession involving Selvers (those stitched with multiple consciousnesses)
- Casting certain mind-altering spells (e.g., Modify Memory) under her divine presence

### Core Mechanic: Progressive Charisma Saves

**Save DC Starts at 1 and increases by +1 after each success within a given stage.**

- When exposed to triggers, the player must make a Charisma saving throw against the current DC.
- On a success, the character resists worsening the madness for now, but the DC increases by 1 for the next save at the same stage.
- On a failure, the character’s mind shatters further, and they advance to the next stage of the fracture; the DC resets to 1 at the new stage.
- This process repeats through all stages, reflecting the slow but brutal mental decay.

## Fracture Stages & Effects

### Stage 1: Slipping Threads

At first, the fracture is subtle—like a faint tug at the edges of your mind. You might find yourself suddenly unable to remember a skill you’ve used a thousand times, or a language you once spoke fluently. These moments of forgetfulness are fleeting, passing like a bad dream by morning. Yet when they come, they bring a gnawing sense of unease, as if some part of you has been pulled loose and replaced with a stranger’s thread. Sometimes, even your own name slips through the cracks, vanishing for a breathless second before it returns—only to leave you questioning if it’s truly yours at all.

This stage is not just about memory loss but the unsettling sensation of living atop layers of consciousness. You might catch yourself murmuring phrases that don’t belong to your current self, or acting on impulse with no clear origin. Your mind feels fragmented, like a tapestry worn thin at the seams, threatening to unravel at any moment. To those around you, it may appear as distraction or odd behavior, but to you, it’s something far darker—a whisper of other selves tangled beneath your skin, struggling to surface.

The fracture in this phase is a creeping paranoia: the feeling that you are not entirely alone inside your own head. These intrusions might be subtle enough to dismiss at first, but they plant the seed of doubt about the nature of your identity. You may try to fight it, clutching desperately to what you know as yourself, but every success raises the cost—the next exposure will be harder to resist. The nightmare begins here, woven thread by fragile thread.

### Stage 2: Haunting Echoes

As the fracture deepens, the veil between your fractured minds grows thinner and more permeable. Hallucinations begin to seep into your waking life, haunting you with visions and voices no one else can perceive. During moments of stress, especially in combat or tense social encounters, invisible enemies may appear—hostile and cruel—able to harm only you. Their whispers twist your thoughts and emotions, poisoning your sense of reality. Negotiations turn into battles against an unseen saboteur, a cruel third party that distorts your words and intentions, undermining trust and inflaming paranoia.

These echoes are relentless, like a chorus of lost selves calling out from the darkness inside you. They weigh on your mind, clouding your judgment and twisting your perception of allies and enemies alike. Even your speech may betray you, as you unconsciously mimic the voices of forgotten personas, weaving fragments of alien thought into your conversation. Friends and companions may notice your strange behavior, but they cannot see the full horror—only that you are slipping away, one fractured moment at a time.

Your struggle to maintain a coherent identity becomes a tense, exhausting battle. The hallucinatory torment erodes your confidence and your sense of control, forcing you into a corner where every choice feels compromised. It’s a lonely, isolating experience—one where the mind itself becomes a battlefield, and the enemy is buried within. This stage marks a clear warning: the fracture is spreading, and the price of resistance will grow ever higher.

### Stage 3: Fragmented Mind

By now, the fractured identities within you have gained strength and voice. Separate personalities surface occasionally, each with their own memories, desires, and agendas. Sometimes one will seize control unexpectedly, making choices that feel foreign or alien. These “stitch personalities” may embody your past selves, fragments of discarded identities, or wholly foreign minds forcibly woven into your being by Nezzeryth’s weaving. The struggle for dominance is exhausting and unpredictable, both for you and those around you.

In moments of extreme stress, you may find yourself losing control entirely as a different self takes the reins, whether temporarily or for longer spans. The line between “you” and the others blurs dangerously. Friends might hear your voice shift or notice sudden changes in your demeanor and goals. This instability makes trust fragile and cooperation difficult, as no one can be certain which self will answer the call. Your fractured mind becomes a tapestry of competing wills, stitched awkwardly but unable to fully unite.

Memory “bleeds” between these personalities, mixing fragments of past experiences with alien knowledge or skills. You may gain fleeting proficiencies, sudden emotional shifts, or contradictory beliefs that clash with your core identity. Roleplaying this stage involves embracing uncertainty and tension, as you never truly know who you are at any given moment—and your fractured self may not like what it finds inside. It’s a painful, brutal descent into mental chaos.

### Stage 4: Shattered Self

Your mind no longer feels like a place you know. It becomes unfamiliar, like wandering through a house you once lived in but now filled with locked doors, strange whispers, and faces you don’t recognize in mirrors. When a fracture deepens at this stage, you awaken—or fall unconscious—and find pieces of yourself missing. Abilities, traits, and memories are replaced with gaps, static, or worse: alien responses and instincts that don’t feel like your own.

Mechanically, the player temporarily loses access to their normal character sheet and instead interacts with a blank or partially known character identity—a fractured subconscious stitched by Nezzeryth. They are still the one in control, but the mechanics are unfamiliar: random ability scores, unknown skills, limited access to class features, and strange behavior triggers that must be discovered through play.

> ##### Gameplay Note:
> 
> The DM provides only basic structure (e.g., your class is still Fighter, but you don’t know your proficiencies, weapons, etc.) and the player rediscovers their new “self” through trial and error. As they fight, explore, and speak, they piece together who this fractured self is, building a temporary character sheet from clues and results.

This stage emphasizes exploration of identity as gameplay, transforming the character into a living puzzle. It's like playing your own shadow, unsure of what parts of you still belong to the original. The terror isn’t loss of control—it’s that you are still in control, and you don't know who “you” is anymore.

### Stage 5: ASsimilated Vessel

You are no longer a single self. You are four minds stitched into one body, sharing skin but not memory, not purpose. You may sleep as one person and wake as another. You may swear to your companions that you would never say such things—only to discover you did, yesterday, while someone else was in charge. The fracture is total. You are a Selver, woven beyond repair. Mechanically, you now have 1d4 full character sheets, each representing a different stitched persona. Each one may have:

- Different classes or subclasses
- Variations in stats, backgrounds, goals, and alignment
- Distinct voices, habits, ideals, or fears

Each morning, or after heavy trauma (e.g., falling unconscious, taking a critical hit, or enduring a psychic assault), roll 1d4 to determine which “self” emerges. The player always controls their character—just not which one they will be.

> ##### Gameplay Note:
> 
> Each persona has full agency, but they don’t share knowledge, spells, or relationships. One might be a sorcerer, another a rogue; one may love an NPC, another may want them dead. The player roleplays these shifts with freedom, making this stage a test of performance, memory, and adaptability.

This is not mind control—it’s identity roulette. The horror here is knowing you will never again be whole. You are a vessel of Nezzeryth, reborn daily in a different mind, and the original you may be long gone… if they ever existed at all.
