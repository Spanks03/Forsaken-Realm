## Echoes Beneath Still Water

_Compiled in the Third Age of Currents by the Archivist-Keeper, Seluin of the Coral Spire._

---
> [!quote|mark] Fragment of a Grung proverb <br> origin lost
> _“The sea does not forgive. It only delays.”_

Centuries have passed since the First Leviathan War — though to the deep, centuries are as seconds.  
In that stillness, new coral has grown over the bones of empires.  
Cities have been rebuilt, and children of a generation unscarred now swim through the same trenches where their ancestors drowned.  
They call it peace. The scholars of the Coral Spire call it **forgetting**.

The treaties that ended the Tide War — the **Oath of Nine Currents** — still shimmer in the archives, each signed by the hands or fins of every surviving race. Its central vow reads:

> _“Should the Sea itself rise again in hunger, we shall remember what unity costs.”_

Yet even that promise has faded like light through deep water.

---

### **The Signs Return**

The first whispers came not from prophets or seers, but from **fishermen**.  
Catch dwindled. Whales changed their migratory songs.  
The moon’s reflection on the sea began to tremble — as though the water below were breathing.

Sirens have reappeared along the sunken faultlines of the **Tecumheim Deep**, their melodies soft, almost apologetic, luring no one yet.  
Triton priests who once served in silence now speak in unison during their dreams.  
And in the black archives beneath Auraluun’s ruins, diviners claim to hear a slow, steady heartbeat, echoing against the vaults of memory.

The Locathah refuse to dredge near the old seal sites. The Grung have abandoned their tide-casinos, saying the dice only show drowning eyes.  
Even the Tidecoil Syndicate — eternal profiteers — have closed their currents.  
Something unseen presses at the edge of perception.

---

### **Theories of the Learned**

The current generation of sea-scholars is divided into **three schools of thought**:

**1. The Resonance Theory** – that Ur-Vorath’s imprisonment was imperfect, and its dreams have begun to seep through the weakened seals.

**2. The Sirenic Conduit Hypothesis** – that the Siren curse, being divine in origin, forms an unbroken tether between Temp’s bloodline and the Leviathan’s hunger. The reawakening of one inevitably stirs the other.

**3. The Godfall Premise** – that no Leviathan stirs at all, but that one of the **Eidolon’s siblings**, the lost entity called **Mirath the Hollow Wave**, has begun to rise in its absence — a reflection, not a return.

None can agree. All can feel it.

---

### **A New Fear in the Deep**

Even in still water, there is movement.  
The **Sea Elves of the Fathom Choir** have ceased their moonlit rituals, and the glow from their trench-cities dims more each year.  
The **Triton legions** drill once again beneath silent banners — not for conquest, but for remembrance.  
And across the marshes, Grung elders whisper of a number rolled thrice — the same number that appeared before the last awakening.

Only the Sirens sing without fear. Their tone has changed: mournful, almost reverent.  
To some, it is an omen. To others, it is the sea rehearsing its next dirge.

> [!quote|mark] Siren matron Aelune to a triton war-priest:  
> _“You hear us as warning. We are not warning. We are memory trying to be mercy.”_

---

### **Conclusion of the Archivist**

If Ur-Vorath truly dreams, then perhaps it dreams of what it once saw — a world at war with itself, united only by terror.  
Perhaps this is why the tides seem to breathe again.  
For though we sealed the Leviathan, we did not unmake its truth:  
that all things born of the sea share one pulse.

And when that pulse stirs, so too will the tide.

Until then, we live in the hush before another hymn.  
The surface glimmers bright, the depths are quiet, and the sea holds its breath once more.

> [!quote|mark] Final entry, Seluin of the Coral Spire  
> _“Peace is a tide. And the tide always returns.”_
