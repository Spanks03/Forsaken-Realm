## The First Leviathan War 

> [!quote|mark] Fragment from the drowned codex of Ithis-Mer <br> recovered from the Abyssal Shelf  
> _“No kingdom remembers how the Tide War began. Only how it ended — in silence and light.”_

---

When the banners of Auraluun lay drowned and the Selachi Empire bled into myth, the seas did not rest.  
Peace in the deep is a fragile illusion — and when gods fall quiet, old things listen.

The first songs of the Tide War rose not from conquest, but from fear.  
The tides had grown erratic. Whirlpools opened where none had been. In the ruins of drowned temples, lights moved like heartbeat pulses.  
And across the seabed, the **Seal of the Eidolon** — a vault older than creation’s first breath — began to crack.

### **The Uneasy Dawn**

The first to sense it were the **Tritons**, heirs of the lost city Auraluun. Their prophets spoke of a “pressure beneath thought,” a mind stirring in the trenches. The **Sea Elves** called it _the undertide whisper_, a dream that pulled them into sleep for weeks at a time.  
Even the **Grung** felt it — the water in their marshes turned still, their gods’ dice rolled nothing but zeroes.

For a generation, the races of the sea watched one another with suspicion.  
Tritons blamed the Locathah for awakening something in their endless dredging.  
Locathah blamed the Sea Elves and their secret rites in the moonlit trenches.  
The Grung accused everyone.  
And from the blood-slick ruins of the Selachi empire, something began to gather its teeth again.

It was not war yet. It was **the holding of breath before the scream.**

---

## **The Breaking of the Seal**

In the 11th Tide of the Second Era, an expedition of **Ironcurrent Locathah** miners uncovered what they believed was a buried temple beneath the **Tecumheim Deep**. It was not.  
They had found the **outer gate** of the Eidolon Seal — a prison forged by the Elder Gods themselves to bind one of their errant children:  
**Ur-Vorath, the Leviathan of Memory.**

When the seal cracked, no one noticed at first. The water merely grew warmer, then heavier, as though the ocean itself remembered its own weight.  
But within weeks, the dreams began — shared visions across every race.  
Voices calling from the deep.  
Songs that no one remembered learning.  
Shadows that moved with the slow certainty of continents.

> [!quote|mark] Locathah Tidekeeper Nuar-Shaal, final entry  
> _“It is not evil. It is remembrance. The sea remembers us, and it is angry.”_

The **Sirens** were the first to answer the call.  
Once mortal, now cursed daughters of Temp’s wrath, they sang as if summoned by the ocean’s grief.  
Their melodies drew ships into whirlpools, armies into the deep. Many believed the Sirens were the heralds of Ur-Vorath, but in truth, they were its **echo** — their curse resonating with the Leviathan’s awakening hunger.

And so, as one race drowned the other’s blame, the sea itself rose to make war.

---

## **The War That Could Not Be Named**

Historians still argue if the Tide War was one conflict or many.  
To the Tritons, it was **The War of Remembrance** — a holy crusade to reclaim divine balance.  
To the Locathah, it was **The Drift War**, fought for survival against an ocean losing its mercy.  
To the Grung, it was **The Lucky Tide**, a storm of fate none could control.  
To the Sea Elves, it was simply **The First Leviathan War**.

Every current became a battlefield.

- Triton war-barges, powered by elementals bound from the earliest days of Auraluun, carved through Locathah shoals.
    
- The Tidecoil Syndicate armed the highest bidder, smuggling Grung poisons and Siren blood to both sides.
    
- Sea Elf tide-circles collapsed whole trenches with ritual tidesong, drowning armies in silence.
    
- Even the scattered Selachi remnants reemerged, guided by prophets who claimed to hear “the heart of the world” beneath the waves.
    

But the true enemy was never any of them.  
It was **the song beneath all songs** — the heartbeat of Ur-Vorath, growing louder with every drop of blood spilled.

---

## **The Siren’s Role**

> [!quote|mark] Recovered field log, Triton Warlord Saryen of Tecumheim  
> _“We thought them monsters. We were wrong. They sang not for us, but for what waited below.”_

The **Siren curse** spread rapidly during the early tides of war.  
First among coastal Tritons and Locathah raiders, then among entire settlements.  
Any who bled in seawater during the rising of the moon might awaken changed — drawn to song, to drowning, to the hunger of the deep.

Temp’s ancient curse had found fertile ground.  
Sirens became both weapon and warning — beautiful and grotesque, their songs opening fissures in the Seal itself.  
Some say Ur-Vorath whispered through them, learning the tongues of mortals. Others claim Temp himself guided them, laughing as his siblings’ creations tore each other apart.

For a brief, terrible moment, even gods looked away.

---

## **The Leviathan’s Rising**

When the Seal finally shattered, all the seas convulsed.  
Tides reversed. Coral bled. The sky cracked with auroral fire.

Ur-Vorath rose like a continent lifting from the deep — a mass of living ocean, scales of obsidian and pearl, eyes like moons drowning in themselves.  
Its voice was memory; to hear it was to recall every drowning that ever was.

The races of the sea, broken and furious, did the unthinkable — **they allied**.  
Triton battlemages, Locathah tideblades, Sea Elf seers, Grung alchemists, even Sirens turned their voices as weapons.  
For the first and only time, the peoples of the ocean sang the same song — a counter-harmony against the Leviathan’s call.

The war lasted a single night and a thousand years. Time had no meaning beneath Ur-Vorath’s gaze.

When it ended, the Leviathan did not die.  
It **slept** — bound once more within a seal of blood and song, a prison made not by gods, but by those it had nearly destroyed.  
The tides calmed.  
The ocean forgot.  
And peace returned — for a while.

---

## **The Era of Still Tides**

In the centuries that followed, the races of the sea rebuilt, haunted by dreams of the thing they had silenced.  
No empire rose again to match the Tritons of Auraluun or the fury of the Selachi.  
The Sirens retreated into myth, their songs faint but eternal.  
Grung dice rolled quietly in the mud, remembering nothing.  
Sea Elves watched from the dark, guarding a secret none dared name.  
And beneath them all, something enormous turned once in its sleep.

> [!quote|mark] Abyssal Sea Elf scholar Lurenth of the Drift  
> _“We are not survivors. We are witnesses. The tide has only paused.”_

---

### The Shadow Between Wars**

Now begins the **Era of the Still Tides** — an age of fragile peace, new gods, and quieter horrors.  
The scholars call it the **Silence of Ur-Vorath**, but those who dream of deep waters know better.  
They hear a faint pulse below the calm, a promise waiting to be kept.  
For the sea never forgets, and the First Leviathan was only the beginning.
