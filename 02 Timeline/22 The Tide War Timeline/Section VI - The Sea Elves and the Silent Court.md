## **The Return in Silver Silence**

When the last screams of the Blood Tides faded, the oceans lay still for an age. The currents slowed. Whales sang only dirges. The deep realms that once glittered with triton lamps now lay in twilight — until, one night, the stars beneath the waves began to move.

First seen by wreck divers along the Coral Reaches, the lights shimmered pale blue and white, drifting in patterns that mimicked constellations. But these were no will-o’-wisps. They were eyes — and from the trenches of Auraluun rose the **Sea Elves**, returned from extinction.

They came in silence, clad in translucent armor that seemed woven of moonlight and memory. Their cities — if they could be called that — were not built but _grown_: vaults of glass coral, fields of kelp shaped like cathedral spires. And at their heart sat the **Silent Court**, where no voice was raised, and every thought echoed like a ripple through the tide.

---

## **The Shattering and the Drowned Sleep**

No one knows how the Sea Elves survived Auraluun’s fall.  
Triton historians claim they were pulled beneath by their own magic — a city too perfect, drowned by its pride. Grung storytellers whisper that they bargained with death itself, binding their souls to the seabed.

Whatever truth lies buried, the Sea Elves returned changed. Their bodies were pale, hair drifting like seagrass, their eyes glowing with bioluminescent veils. They spoke not with tongues but through currents — telepathic murmurs that carried emotion instead of sound.

Each bore a shard of Auraluun’s last song embedded within their flesh, pulsing faintly with magic that neither healed nor faded. They called this affliction the **Drowned Sleep** — a half-death that preserved them through the centuries but severed them from mortal time. They remembered every pain as if it had happened yesterday.

To meet their gaze was to feel your lungs fill with saltwater and memory.

---

## **The Silent Court**

The Silent Court ruled not by decree but by resonance — an ancient telepathic choir of the oldest Sea Elves, their minds joined across leagues of dark water. Within their halls, judgment was passed by the weight of shared memory; guilt and grief alike were communal burdens.

Their queen, **Aevala Moondrift**, was said to have no shadow — her presence dimmed magic and silenced even the whales. Some claimed she could still hear Auraluun’s heart beating beneath the silt, waiting to rise again.

Under her reign, the Sea Elves became custodians of the forgotten: gathering relics from the drowned age, restoring mosaics from Auraluun’s wreck, and recovering the names of the dead. Yet there was no joy in their reclamation — only a ritualized grief.

Those who entered the Silent Court uninvited often returned mute, unable to speak of what they had seen. Those who refused to leave were never seen again.

---

## **The Deep Choirs**

If the tritons were order and the Grung chaos, the Sea Elves were memory given sound. They wove music not for celebration, but to hold the world together. These harmonics, called **The Deep Choirs**, resonated through coral, shell, and even leviathan bone, creating vast networks of magic that sustained their hidden realm.

It was said that when the Blood Tides raged, the Choirs went silent — every note absorbed by the carnage. Their return signaled balance reborn, but not peace. For with the songs came the dreams.

Sailors began to hear voices beneath the waves at night, whispering in forgotten tongues. Entire crews followed phantom choruses into fog and were lost. The Sea Elves denied involvement, yet their cities shone brightest on the nights these vanishings occurred.

---

## **The Mirror Truce**

The first contact after their return came through reflection, not voice. Triton ambassadors reported seeing figures mirroring their gestures from the other side of still water, answering questions before they were spoken. When one dared to breach the surface, his mind was flooded with visions — of empires drowned, gods sleeping, and a great spiral of blood turning beneath the moon.

This encounter birthed the **Mirror Truce**: an unspoken understanding between the Sea Elves and the surviving surface kingdoms.  
The elves would not interfere in mortal wars; mortals would not disturb the silent depths.  
For a time, both sides honored it.

But silence, like the sea, always hungers to be broken.

---

## **Legacy of the Silent Court**

Where the Selachi had ruled by fear and blood, the Sea Elves ruled by **remembrance**. Their dominion was subtle — the reclaiming of ruins, the rewriting of maps, the erasure of names.

To the living, they were ghosts; to the dead, shepherds.  
They bound the oceans not with chains, but with grief.

Even now, when the moon is drowned in cloud, mariners claim to see pale figures walking upon the seafloor, carrying lanterns of starlight. They say these are the **Court’s Heralds**, collecting memories that have sunk too deep — and that when the last memory is reclaimed, Auraluun will rise once more, not as a city, but as a tide.

## **The Drowned Lament**

> [!quote|mark] Anonymous entry, _The Ninth Tide Fragment_  
> _“When memory itself began to sing, not all who listened survived.”_

But the Choirs did not fade quietly.  
Their harmonics began to twist — songs turning to wails, remembrance curdling into despair. Those who heard them in dreams awoke weeping salt, their veins glowing faintly blue.

And then came the **Sirens**.

Born, some say, from the broken songs of the Sea Elves — others claim from Aevala’s own tears — their voices carried the weight of the Drowned Sleep, but without its mercy.

As the Silent Court withdrew deeper into shadow, the seas filled once more with song.  
This time, it promised not memory, but oblivion.
