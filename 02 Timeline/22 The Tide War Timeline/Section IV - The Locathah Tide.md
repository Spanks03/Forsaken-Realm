## The Clans and the Drift War

> [!quote|mark] From the **Song of Salt and Bone** <br> a Locathah tide-ballad passed down through generations  
> _“We remember the drift, for the drift remembers us.  
> We were born of motion — we live, we lose, we move.”_

---

## **The Salt-Born Aftermath**

The Grung’s laughter faded, but their poisons lingered.  
Tides thick with venom and rotting kelp forced countless peoples to flee their shallows and reefs. Among the displaced were the **Locathah**, a people neither wholly nomadic nor bound, who had once lived in the fertile lagoons between triton strongholds and coastal marshlands. When Auraluun fell and the Age of Poison spread its stain, their homes became brine tombs — and the Locathah learned to **live without land**.

They built flotillas from wrecks and driftwood, lashed hull to hull with sinew and faith. Children learned to swim before they walked. A thousand clans floated between horizons, carried wherever the currents pleased.  
Where the tritons mourned lost cities, the Locathah saw only endless road.

---

## **Rise of the Drift Fleets**

In time, survival became art.  
The Locathah transformed disaster into culture, crafting vast **drift-fleets** — living cities of coral, kelp, and scavenged hulls. These were not built but grown, singing to coral to bend its spines and weaving seaweed into sails that breathed. Every vessel was alive, and every clan carried its own reef.

No king ruled them. No god claimed them. Instead, the fleets bound themselves by the **Salt Oath**: _“None shall anchor forever, and none shall drift alone.”_

Each fleet was both home and nation — self-governed, fiercely independent, yet bound by shared routes and ritual meetings during the **Gathering of the Tides**, when hundreds of ships would lock together to trade, wed, and tell the news of the sea.

Outsiders saw chaos. The Locathah called it harmony.

---

## **The Drift War**

It began, as all sea wars do, with hunger.  
The poisoned currents of the south choked the fisheries, and the Locathah drift-fleets turned north seeking clean waters. Triton patrols saw them as raiders; Grung warbands saw them as trespassers. The Locathah saw themselves as neither — only survivors in motion.

When triton commanders demanded tolls for passage, some clans refused. The first **Skirmish at the Weeping Shoal** ended in blood and confusion, and soon the entire southern sea burned with **Driftfire** — the bright trails of tar and pitch the Locathah used to blind their enemies at night.

The Drift War was not a single conflict but a century of migrations, blockades, and small-scale battles fought between fleets, fortresses, and empires. The Locathah never sought conquest; they fought to keep moving, to keep living. But in that endless movement, they became something new — a people who could not be contained, whose map was the tide itself.

---

## **War Songs and Salt Myths**

> [!quote|mark] A verse from the Locathah Canticle of Currents  
> _“To still the tide is to kill it.  
> To anchor is to forget the sea’s dream.”_

Their poets turned survival into scripture. Every victory was sung, every ship lost remembered in tide-rhyme and bone drums. Locathah warriors marked their arms with ink made from the ashes of burned sails — reminders that loss was only another form of travel.

They worshiped not gods, but **motion** — the great Drift, which they said connected all lives between sea and sky. To them, death was not an end but a joining: the lost became new currents that carried their kin forward.

Even their weapons reflected this faith: spears tipped with coral that dissolved after striking true, nets that shimmered with bioluminescent plankton to confuse predators, and harpoons etched with personal journeys — maps of where they had fought and fled.

To fight a Locathah was to fight a story still being written.

---

## **The Gathering of the Nine Fleets**

As the Drift War wore on, nine great fleets emerged — each tracing its lineage to one of the first clans who had survived the Poison Years. Among them:

- **The Clan of the Bleeding Star**, wanderers of the deep night currents.
    
- **The Shellborn**, who rode armored turtles into battle.
    
- **The Vaskari**, whose name once adorned the reeds of drowned Vask-Tel.
    
- **The Hallowfins**, keepers of relics scavenged from Auraluun’s ruins.
    

For a brief generation, these nine came together under the **Pact of the Salt Path**, vowing to sail as one armada and carve safe passage for all free peoples. Their banners filled the horizon — coral blues, blood reds, and the white of salt.

But unity is an unnatural current.  
Pride and old wounds broke the pact before it could root. One fleet turned mercenary, another vanished chasing the promise of a floating paradise. The Locathah returned to what they had always been — scattered, strong, and ungoverned.

---

## **Legacy of the Drift**

When later historians speak of the Drift War, they call it aimless — a storm without center. Yet to the Locathah, it was the moment they found their soul.  
They learned that survival was not submission, that motion itself could be a creed. In time, their free fleets became both feared raiders and sought-after navigators, guiding others through the poisoned waters they had mastered.

Their maps — drawn in salt lines and fishbone runes — became sacred to traders and pirates alike. Even triton admirals later copied their drift routes, though they never admitted it.

As the centuries turned and the first tides of the great **Tide War** began to swell, the Locathah were already there — their banners fluttering between alliances, their oaths fluid as the sea. Neither conquerors nor conquered, they were the rhythm beneath the coming storm, the motion that never ceased.

> [!quote|mark] From the final line of the **Song of Salt and Bone**  
> _“When the gods sink, we will drift above them.”_
