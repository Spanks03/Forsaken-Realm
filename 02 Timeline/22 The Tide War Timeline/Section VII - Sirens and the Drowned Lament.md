## **The Unraveling of the Choirs**

In the centuries after the Silent Court withdrew into the deep, the Deep Choirs continued their haunting harmonies beneath the sea. But their songs, meant to hold memory and peace, began to sour. Chords bent where none had been, and in the currents, faint whispers answered them — not of grief, but of hunger.

Scholars once believed it was the echo of the dead.  
Now we know it was **Temp**.

The Shattered God, banished for his defiance, had long watched the creations of his divine kin — Tritons, Sea Elves, and mortals alike. Their harmony offended him; their ordered beauty, a reflection of what he had lost. And so, where the Silent Court’s melodies resonated through coral and shell, Temp’s rage seeped through the same channels, warping remembrance into desire, song into curse.

---

## **The Birth of the Thalassothrope**

The first Siren was **Isadora Salantine**, a mortal fisher-maiden of the Coral Reaches. Her song was once used to calm storms and guide lost vessels home — until the day she was caught in the aftershock of the Drowned Sleep, when the Choirs faltered and Temp’s whisper reached her heart.

He took pity twisted by spite, and in his rage against his father’s seas and Eaos’ waters, he bound her to the tides themselves. Her flesh flowed like waves; her lungs filled with salt water and song.  
When she next sang, the sailors she sought to save instead threw themselves into the sea.

Thus was born the first **Thalassothrope**, or _Siren_ — an alluring humanoid from the waist up and a strong shark-like tail, cursed to embody the ocean’s hunger and the god’s fury.

In daylight, they could walk among mortals, drawn always toward the sound of the tide. But when submerged or touched by rain, their bodies yielded to the curse — scales blossoming from skin, teeth lengthening, voice thickening into melody.

To hear their song was to feel the ache of drowning wrapped in ecstasy. Few resisted. None forgot.

---

## **The Drowned Lament**

As their numbers spread — carried by bites, by moonlit tides, by the sorrow of those who survived — the Sea Elves’ own Choirs began to fall silent.  
They could not separate their sacred harmonies from the Sirens’ alluring wails.  
The two frequencies intertwined, birthing a haunting music that could heal or destroy depending on who listened.

To mortals, it became known as the **Drowned Lament** — the song that called the sea to claim what it was owed. Whole coasts vanished under its spell. Ships steered toward phantom shores. Coral grew upon drowned temples as if the ocean itself wept.

Some whispered that Aevala Moondrift herself succumbed, walking into the dark to silence her own Choirs.  
Others say she became something else — the **First Mother of Sirens**, bound forever between memory and malice.

---

## **The Nature of the Curse**

Unlike the lycanthrope or vampire, whose transformation is born of fury or bloodlust, the Siren’s change is **melancholic** — an ache for the sea that drowns both mind and body.  
When water touches them, the call becomes irresistible.  
Their skin ripples, their heartbeat syncs to the tide, and their lungs beg for the deep.

Those who survive a Siren’s bite carry the curse forward — their first transformation always marked by the desperate swim upward toward light, only to realize it burns.

To cure a Thalassothrope is nearly impossible. Even if the flesh is healed, the song remains in the soul, humming faintly beneath the skin until it draws them back to the sea.

---

## **Sirens Among the Tides**

Sirens soon gathered into pods — haunting coastal ruins, coral grottos, and the shallows of sunken cities. They called themselves the **Lamented**, claiming they were neither mortal nor divine, but the sea’s truest children: born of creation’s cruelty and the ocean’s boundless memory.

In their courts, shells whisper prophecies and skeletons sing lullabies.  
Their queens rule not by strength but by voice — the one whose song can call the deepest whale or silence the loudest storm is obeyed.

Yet for all their savagery, the Sirens retain fragments of what they once were: poets of sorrow, archivists of pain. It is said that to hear a Siren’s song and survive is to know one’s truest grief — for their melodies reflect the listener’s soul, not the singer’s.

---

## **Legacy of Temp’s Curse**

Temp’s creation did not end with the Sirens. The blood curse would later find form in air, flame, and stone — the Harpy, the Fury, the Liar — each an echo of his defiance. But it was the **Thalassothrope** that most perfectly embodied his intent: a wound upon the divine sea, one that could not be healed.

The Sea Elves retreated entirely after their emergence, fearing that their songs could awaken new forms of the curse. Even the Tritons sealed their borders, warding their cities with silence.

The ocean, once home to a thousand harmonies, now throbbed with a single, haunting note — the Drowned Lament — echoing through every tide.
