## The Rise and Fall of Auraluun

> [!quote|mark] Scholar Ulwen Daekorr  
> _“The surface folk build walls to separate land from sea. We built currents to connect every tide to its sibling. Perhaps that was our first mistake.”_

---

### **The Age of Coral and Crown**

When the seas calmed after the first storms of creation, the tritons shaped their empire — not from conquest, but from harmony. This was the **Age of Coral and Crown**, when the ocean itself seemed to hum with memory.

Their cities were living things: coral-grown, shell-carved, illuminated by bioluminescent spires and the slow pulse of the sea’s heart. The tritons did not raise monuments _against_ the ocean; they coaxed the sea to grow their homes. The greatest of all these was **Auraluun**, built around the **Heart Coral**, a colossal living organism that thrummed in rhythm with the twin moons. It was said to sing through the currents — a pulse heard by every Thalassarchate across the deeps.

Auraluun was a beacon. Trade flowed from the reefs of Coliwe to the trenches of Iropon. The Drifter fleets of Tecumheim moored upon its outskirts, while Abyssal oracles brought prophecies from the silent depths. Every tide carried tribute, every current bore news. For the first and perhaps only time, **the seas of Terra moved as one mind**.

---

### **The Thalassarchates**

The empire of the tritons was never a single throne, but a constellation of them — the **Thalassarchates**, each bound by oath to the others.

|Thalassarchate|Domain|Virtue|Seat of Power|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Reefborn Courts**|Shallow reefs and coastal plateaus|Beauty, diplomacy, song|**Coliwe Reaches**|
|**Tidecaller Holds**|Deep open seas and stormfronts|Valor, law, tradition|**Beressomin Expanse**|
|**Abyssal Sanctuaries**|Deep trenches and void rifts|Knowledge, silence, prophecy|**Oceri Trench**|
|**Tecumheim Drifters**|Nomadic, upon living leviathans|Adaptability, trade, remembrance|**The Drifting Depths**|

Each realm was sovereign in its waters but swore allegiance to the **Nine Depth Vows**, a code etched into coral tablets within Auraluun’s Hall of Currents.

The Vows commanded:

- To share water before spilling blood
    
- To honor the memory of the drowned
    
- To carry no war into another’s tide
    
- And above all, to keep the **Heart Coral pulsing**, for its rhythm was the bond of the world
    

These were not divine decrees but **self-made oaths**, born of wisdom and necessity. The gods, distant in the firmament, did not intervene. The sea was theirs now — to nurture, or to ruin.

---

> [!quote|mark] Tidecaller Orsarr Vel-Kaen  
> _“We were children without parents, given the sea as inheritance. But even the sea can drown the ungrateful.”_

### **The Arts of the Tide**

In Auraluun’s courts, artistry and sorcery were inseparable.  
Every song was a spell. Every light, a memory.

The tritons practiced **Thalassomancy**, the art of channeling Weave through living water. Their rituals wove together sound, motion, and pattern. A hydroscribe could carve a rune into a droplet; a choir of tidepriests could calm an entire stormfront with a single held note. The sea remembered these acts — every current carried echoes of past songs.

- **The Reefborn** sang light into coral and crafted living architecture.
    
- **The Tidecallers** shaped pressure currents into weapons and shields.
    
- **The Abyssals** traced runes into silence itself, binding the minds of dreaming leviathans.
    
- **The Drifters** learned to sing across distance — a single chant that could be heard from one end of the ocean to another.
    

Magic was not faith, but **heritage** — a cultural practice as intimate as breath. Nosduh and Ogul were still venerated, but not obeyed. Their silence was interpreted as **trust**: the gods had stepped back so mortals might learn to rule the tides without divine hand.

---

### **The Drift of Pride**

Centuries passed, and Auraluun grew vast — a city of coral towers that glowed like sunrise beneath the waves. Yet the same diversity that had once united the Thalassarchates began to fray them.

Reefborn artisans accused Tidecallers of barbarism; Tidecallers derided Reefborn weakness. Abyssal seers spoke in metaphors no one could parse, warning of still waters and sleeping eyes. The Drifters, untethered and free, were accused of arrogance — trading knowledge as though it were theirs to sell.

And always, the Heart Coral pulsed slower.

Diplomatic councils grew more ceremonial, less genuine. Songs once sung in harmony turned competitive. Even the tides themselves began to shift unevenly, as if mirroring the discord above them. Auraluun’s High Conclave — led by the visionary Hydroscribe **Miraen Vaythar** — sought to restore balance through ritual.

---

### **The Chorale of Still Waters**

The Conclave believed that the Weave of the sea had grown _noisy_, frayed by centuries of overlapping spells. To restore it, they devised a ritual of immense scale: the **Chorale of Still Waters**, meant to synchronize every current, silence the chaos, and renew the Heart Coral’s pulse.

Each Thalassarchate contributed their own magic. Reefborn songweavers, Tidecaller stormpriests, Abyssal rune-keepers, and Drifter harmonists all participated. For the first time in generations, all tides sang as one.

And for a brief, impossible moment — the sea fell silent.

Every storm stilled. Every leviathan slept. The surface of the ocean became a perfect mirror.

But the stillness did not end.  
Currents froze. Coral ceased to grow. The Heart Coral’s glow dimmed.

The Auraluun Conclave tried to reverse the ritual, but their voices found no resonance — **sound itself could no longer move** through the deep. The city suffocated in perfect calm. Entire spires cracked under their own weight. The sea itself seemed to hold its breath until it broke.

When movement returned, Auraluun was gone.  
A dead reef, hollow and still, where even the light refused to linger.

---

> [!quote|mark] Abyssal Witness, recovered from Oceri  
> _“The silence came not as punishment, but as mercy. We had taught the sea our names — and it remembered them too well.”_

---

### **The Shattered Accord**

With the fall of Auraluun, the **Vessel of Depths** — symbol of the Thalassarchate alliance — cracked. The Nine Depth Vows lost their anchor. The confederation splintered, each realm turning inward to preserve what little remained.

- The **Reefborn Courts** sealed their waters, declaring isolation until the sea sang again.
    
- The **Tidecallers** took up arms, vowing to reclaim Auraluun’s ruins by force if necessary.
    
- The **Abyssals** vanished into their trenches, claiming the silence had “spoken to them.”
    
- The **Drifters** fled far beyond known currents, carrying fragments of the Heart Coral aboard their leviathan-cities.
    

Trade ceased. Magic fractured. Even the Weave of the sea felt thinner, weaker — as if some vital pulse had been permanently lost.

To the surface world, it was as if the ocean had fallen asleep.

---

### **The Last Coral Bloom**

Centuries later, divers from the Reefborn city of **Ishael** claimed to have seen a faint glow deep within the Beressomin — a single coral bloom pulsing where Auraluun once stood. They called it **the Last Heart**, and some Triton priests began pilgrimages to its depths, hoping to rekindle what was lost.

But the Abyssals warn that the Last Heart is not what it seems — that it beats in rhythm not with the moons, but with something **beneath** them.

---

> [!quote|mark] Reefborn Historian Sael Moraine  
> _“We remember Auraluun not for its fall, but for what it dared to be — proof that the sea could unite. Yet perhaps the ocean was never meant to speak with one voice.”_

---

### **Legacy**

The Fall of Auraluun marked the end of the divine era — when tritons still thought themselves the chosen of the tides.  
In its place rose the **Era of Drift**, a time of fractured empires, cultural suspicion, and whispered omens.  
The silence left by Auraluun’s fall would one day give rise to the first **Poison Wars**, the **Drift War**, and the **Tide War** itself.

But even now, in the quietest depths, some tritons claim to hear a heartbeat echoing through the black — faint, patient, waiting.

## **Interlude — The Waters Turn Bitter**

> [!quote|mark] Abyssal proverb <br> origin uncertain
> _“When the sea forgot its song, the silence filled with whispers.”_  

The fall of Auraluun did not end the tides — it poisoned them.

At first, the change was subtle. Currents grew colder. Fish schools swam in erratic spirals, as if fleeing an unseen current. Coral once pink with song grew gray and brittle. The Weave beneath the waves, no longer guided by harmony, began to twist in on itself. Magic curdled. Prayers sank unanswered.

Each Thalassarchate blamed the others. The Reefborn accused the Abyssals of hoarding knowledge. The Tidecallers saw omens of rebellion in the Drifters’ silence. In the void of Auraluun’s collapse, suspicion became law.

But worse than the silence was what grew _within_ it.  
From the stagnant trenches and the drowned gardens of coral rose strange blooms — bioluminescent and venomous, feeding upon the residue of failed spells. Scholars would later name this phenomenon **The Taint**, but in the beginning it was known simply as **the Bittering**.

Waters once sweet with Weave now ran with toxins — some natural, some conjured, some sentient. Creatures adapted or perished. Those who adapted did not remain unchanged.

In the black waters of the southern shallows, beneath jungled coasts, a new people stirred — born not of coral or current, but of **corruption and survival**.  
Small, swift, and cunning, they thrived where others drowned.  
They would one day call themselves the **Grung**.

And so began the **Age of Poison**, when the seas learned to bite.
