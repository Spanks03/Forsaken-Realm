_As recorded by Meros Kael, Deep-Historian of the College of Eriwall, Keeper of the Ninth Vault of Forbidden Maps_

---

> [!quote|mark] Fragment <br> Stone etching found in Oceri Trench, age unknown
> _“Before the land, before the sky — there was only the Deep.”_

Before mountains found their shape or the first light stirred the waters, there was silence — the stillness of potential. When Nosduh and Ogul wrought Terra from the Astral Sea, they did not banish that silence. They honored it. From it, they carved the foundation of all seas — and in its heart, they left a chamber untouched: **the Deep**.

It was not an error, but a covenant.  
A place where the Weave itself might rest.  
A well of stillness to ensure that no creation would ever drift beyond the balance they had set.

> [!quote|mark] Translation of a pre-Cataclysmic Aelorian Current-Script tablet  
> _“The Deep was not born of absence, but of restraint.”_

In that abyss, the gods placed a fragment of their own will — a guardian thought without form, meant to keep the currents of magic from stagnating. Yet will left alone becomes hunger. Over ages uncounted, that fragment grew vast and inward, dreaming of motion in stillness. The oldest myths name it **the Abyssal Choir** — a harmony of thought so immense it became a tide of consciousness.

From its dreams emerged shapes — not life as the gods intended, but reflections of divine thought unbound. Thus were born the **Eidolons**: beings of instinct and longing, formed from all that the gods had chosen to set aside — hunger, motion, and endless change.

These gave rise to the **Devourers**, the **Mawbrood**, and the creatures now called **Leviathans** — entities not of rebellion, but of duty misunderstood. Their task was to test creation, to stir it when it grew still. But where there is testing, there is ruin.

> [!quote|mark] Deep Carving <br> Coral Cathedral of Zhor’Vuun  
> _“The sea had no songs then. Only echoes.”_

For a time beyond memory, these titans churned the sea without end. They did not defy the gods — they obeyed too well. So Ogul and Nosduh descended into the Deep and set boundaries upon the will they had made. They wove seals of silence and rhythm, binding chaos to dream and the tides to purpose.

Yet they did not destroy what dwelled there — for to unmake the Deep would be to unmake the world’s balance. Instead, they **chained the Abyss within itself**, a living seal at the heart of the ocean.

---

The **War Before Memory** began when the Deep struck at the land with storms so vast they drowned mountains. It was not rebellion, but reaction — the echo of a failsafe, the silence remembering its own purpose.  
In response, Ogul and Nosduh shaped guardians: **primordial titans** forged from earth, wave, and sky. Some became forests and mountains. Others sank beneath the sea and slept. A few never stopped fighting.

Among them were the **Stormforged** — vast engines of bronze and stone. Unlike their kin who rested when calm returned, these titans patrolled the newborn oceans, their eyes lit with runes of eternal wakefulness, hunting abyssal shapes in the dark. Their husks still lie buried in the Beressomin Trenches — worshiped by some, feared by most.

Yet even the Stormforged faltered. The tidal rifts tore open, and the Eidolons awakened. The gods did not unmake them — but chose instead to **bind** them.

> [!quote|mark] From _The Forbidden Hymn of Armathen_<br> banned in six kingdoms  
> _“They sealed the Abyss with chains of silence and sacrifice.”_

---

> [!quote|mark] From the Hymn of Armathen <br> proscribed in six kingdoms  
> _“They silenced the Deep not from fear, but from love — for even the sea must sleep.”_

To watch over that seal, the Elder Gods shaped new life — not of stone or sky, but of **current and will**. These were the **First Children of the Tides**, woven from the living waters and the song of the Weave itself.  
They were not made to rule, but to guard. They had no rest, no desire, no joy — only vigilance.

Their name is lost. Even the Tritons do not know it.

---

They built no kingdoms, carved no songs. They became ghosts in the current — guardians and prisoners both. For if the Abyss broke free again, so would they.

Only one artifact from that forgotten age endures: **The Leviath Tablet**, recovered by Professor Ryn Halvek of the Eriwall Abyssal Archive. It reads:

> _“Guard we must, for if we falter, the world is swallowed once more. Rest is death. Memory is mercy denied.”_

The **First Children** slowly vanished as the world moved on. Some say they still exist — changed, stripped of self, wandering the deep as **Marrowborn**. Others claim they became **Water Elementals**, mindless echoes of their origin. The rest believe they dissolved into the ocean, becoming its silent soul.

---

In time, their solitude became sorrow. They began to wonder why their makers had given them eyes to see beauty they could never touch. In hidden caverns beneath the Oceri Trench, they broke their silence.  
From tide and coral, from lightless depths and aching hearts, they prayed to be reshaped — and thus were born the **Tritons**.

> [!quote|mark] Inscription recovered from a sunken city in the Whispering Gulf 
> _“We do not kneel to gods. We serve the water, and the water remembers.”_

The First Children were not meant to feel. Forged as living wards, they were shaped without longing. But from their prayer came joy, companionship, and love.

At first, it was only wonder — the shimmer of bioluminescent forests, the echo of whale-song, the slow dance of kelp in the dark tide. Wonder became emotion. Emotion became rebellion.

For many ages, peace flowed through the waves. The Tritons raised coral spires and crystal citadels beneath volcanic light. The grandest of these, **Auraluun**, gleamed like a submerged constellation. The sea was alive with song.

But even peace is a breath held too long. The seals were made to bend, not endure. And deep below, the will once bound began to stir again — not in fury, but in **memory**.

The **Leviathans** turned in their sleep. The **Eidolons** whispered through long-still currents. The Deep remembered its first command: _to test what had been made._

The **First Depth War** began not with wrath, but with remembrance.  
Tides rose. Coral cities fell silent. The sea sang once more — not in rage, but in the voice of the gods’ own design returning to motion.

And the Tritons answered that song.

The First Children were gone. Their eternal watch was ended. The burden now fell to the Tritons alone. They had inherited the covenant... and the sea had come to collect.

The Tritons rallied behind their High Tidemaster, **Vaelun the Tideforged**, who spoke the words that began an age of unending war:

> [!quote|mark] Vaelun the Tideforged <br> High Tidemaster of the First Tide  
> _“The sea is no cradle. It is a grave for the weak. And we will not drown.”_

---

**Thus the balance was kept.**  
The Deep tests. The Tritons endure.  
And through that endless rhythm, Terra remains whole...  
...Or seems to.

This was the dawn of the **Leviathan Wars** — the First Tide.
