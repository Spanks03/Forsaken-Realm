## The Blood Tides
#### _as recorded by Pelamur the Wander-Scribe, who survived the Fourth Nightfall_

> [!quote|mark] Fragment from the **Black Ledger of Oceri Trench**  
> _“Hope is a surface thing.  
> In the dark below, only hunger keeps its shape.”_

---

## **When the Deep Went Red**

There are tides that pass, tides that turn, and tides that take.  
The **Blood Tides** were the last.

Before their coming, the seas were fractured but living — Locathah drift-fleets roamed, Grung tribes whispered their bright poisons through the reeds, Tritons rebuilt in the shadows of lost Auraluun. Even the great leviathans slumbered, wounded but enduring.

Then the water warmed.  
Fish fled the trenches.  
The moonlight turned copper.  
And from the deepest fissures of the **Oceri Trench**, something began to move.

At first it was only missing scouts. Then entire reefs went silent. Then came the first survivors, babbling incoherently of “the many-fanged moon” and “the army with no eyes.”

The Selachi had awakened.  
And the sea remembered fear.

---

## **Origins of the Devourer Empire**

The Selachi claim to descend from **Droskhal**, the Devourer Below — not a god, not a spirit, but a being older than prayer, a hunger in the dark made flesh. Their mythology says he shed his scales to create them, granting a portion of his endless appetite.

Scholars of Eriwall reject this, of course.  
But the abyssal scars etched into their bones — the gill-slits shaped like runes of erasure — those are no accident.

Where tritons came from divine purpose, and Locathah from memory and motion, the Selachi emerged from **single-minded instinct**:  
**to conquer, consume, and darken all waters until nothing living remained that was not Selachi.**

Their empire rose in months.  
It fell in centuries.  
But for a time, their shadow blotted out the sea.

---

## **Anatomy of a Nightmare**

To understand the Blood Tides, one must understand the Selachi themselves:

- Lean, muscular bodies built for killing, not swimming
    
- Eyes solid-black, reflecting no light — no mercy
    
- Jaws that could unmake bone
    
- Claws that tore through coral like parchment
    
- Skin ridged with sensory folds that tasted fear in the water
    

And worst of all —  
**the Tidemothers**, enormous females capable of birthing hundreds in a single spawning frenzy, each brood more aggressive than the last.

A single Tidemother could drown a coastline in her children.

The empire was built on numbers.  
Numbers fed by blood.

---

## **The First Blood Tide**

It began quietly — an entire kelp forest stripped clean in a single night.  
Then a Locathah drift-fleet, hulls cracked open like oysters.  
Then a triton fortress, its gates melted by acidic bile and its defenders torn from the walls.

The Blood Tide was not a war.  
It was a **feeding event** stretched across seasons, led by Selachi war-priests who chanted through bubbling helmets of ichor and bone.

They swam not like soldiers but like madness given shape — precise, synchronized, relentless.

Triton records claim that the water itself **screamed**.

---

## **Empire of the Red Psalm**

The Selachi Empire was not a nation — it was a ritual.

Every conquest was a verse.  
Every kill a sacred offering.  
Every surviving foe a mistake to be corrected.

Their capital, **Thresk-Zhal**, was carved into the walls of the Oceri Trench using the bones of devoured leviathans. The city pulsed with crimson light — bioluminescent algae fed by blood and kept in motion by imprisoned elementals torn from their cores.

Here, the Selachi perfected the art of **Tide-Staining** — the ritual poisoning of waters with alchemical blood-mixtures that:

- Erased scent trails
    
- Paralyzed prey
    
- Drove leviathans into suicidal frenzy
    
- Corroded coral and shell
    
- Nullified healing magic
    

Thousands of miles of open water turned rust-red.

The sea became a wound.

---

## **The Red Harvest Years**

For nearly a century, every underwater people suffered:

- **Locathah drift-fleets** were harried until only the fastest could survive. Clans took to sailing without lanterns, afraid light would attract the Blood Tide.
    
- **Grung tribes** found their poisons useless — Selachi blood burned through toxin like sun through morning fog.
    
- **Sea elves** vanished from coastlines, leaving only drifting kelp circles where they had once stood.
    
- **Water genasi** were hunted for sport, their elemental cores used to fuel Selachi blood-engines.
    
- **Leviathans** were slaughtered and butchered, their great bones lashed into the ribs of war platforms.
    

Even triton war-chants admitted defeat:

> _“Against the Blood Tide, even oaths crack.”_

There were no victories — only delays against the inevitable.

---

## **The Night of Three Moons**

The turning point — the moment hope nearly ended — came during a rare celestial alignment. Under the triple-light of Ogul, Nosduh, and the Halo reflecting full strength, the Selachi unleashed their greatest horror:

**the Bloodwave.**

It was not a spell.  
It was not an army.  
It was the ocean itself weaponized.

A wave the color of fresh wounds rolled across the Beressomin Ocean, carrying with it schools of frenzied Selachi and the acidic remains of three leviathans that had been sacrificed to fuel the tide.

Entire Locathah fleets were erased.  
Triton cities sealed their gates and prayed for walls to hold.  
Sea elf sanctuaries drowned in silence deeper than grief.

For three nights, no ship lit a lantern.  
For three nights, no song was heard beneath the waves.  
For three nights, it seemed the sea had chosen one people and rejected all others.

---

## **The Empire That Devoured Itself**

But hunger has no wisdom.  
The Selachi spread too far, too fast, too violently.

The Tidemothers’ broods began fighting among themselves.  
Blood priests rebelled against their warlords.  
Their own tide-staining rituals corroded their breeding grounds.

And in the deepest part of Thresk-Zhal, the Devourer Below stirred —  
not in pride, but in rejection.

The Selachi had over-fed the sea, disrupting the very currents that carried their young.

The empire began to starve.

Cannibalism spread.  
Civil war erupted.  
Thresk-Zhal split open like a rotten shell.

By the time the other peoples realized the empire was collapsing, the Selachi were already devouring each other.

The Blood Tides faded.

Not because they were defeated —  
but because they **could not sustain the world they had made**.

---

## **Aftermath of the Red Days**

Though the empire died, its shadow remained:

- Poisoned waters that took centuries to cleanse
    
- Regions called “Dead Seas,” where nothing breathes
    
- Bone citadels still patrolled by feral Selachi remnants
    
- Cursed Leviathan Skeleton Reefs — gigantic reefs grown from the calcified bones of slaughtered behemoths
    
- Bloodwave scars found on coasts across three continents
    

Historians now call the Selachi Empire **“the empire that ended itself.”**  
But the Locathah have another name:

> _“The Sea’s Nightmare.”_

And the tritons whisper a darker warning:

> _“The Devourer dreams. And hunger always wakes.”_

## **Ebb of the Red Tide**

In the centuries that followed the collapse of Thresk-Zhal, the seas grew strangely quiet. No empire rose to fill the void. The waters were colder, emptier, and thick with memory — a silence not of peace, but of exhaustion.

The survivors — Locathah scavengers, wandering triton enclaves, the last Grung traders — spoke of hearing voices beneath the currents. Echoes of songs not sung in mortal tongues. Some said they were ghosts of those the Blood Tide had devoured. Others believed them the **songs of the Sea Elves**, returning after centuries of absence.

When the first silver lights appeared deep within the drowned ruins of Auraluun, no one dared investigate. Whole crews vanished, their ships found later adrift and barnacled, sails hanging like shrouds.

The Selachi Empire had died in blood.  
What came next wore silence as its crown.
