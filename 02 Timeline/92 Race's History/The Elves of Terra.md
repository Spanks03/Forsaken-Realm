The elves were brought into being by **Eaos, God of Logic**, sculpted with clarity of mind and ordered perfection. Where the dwarves sought power in stone, the elves sought permanence in memory. They were meant to be record-keepers, lawmakers, and visionaries who could guide a shattered world into balance. In the centuries after the Cataclysm, it was their hands that first reshaped civilization.

Cities like **Aelyndor**, **Val’Riyne**, and the **Crowned Crescent** became havens of learning when the world was still choking on ash. From their academies came the first written laws, from their towers the first enchantments that stabilized cities, and from their courts the first histories that gave structure to grief. These cities became the bedrock for later legacies — Eriwall, Janbridge, and Ichinoki — the cultural heartbeats of Terra’s modern empire.![[Elves.png|right lp|700]]

>[!quote|mark] Scholar of Eriwall 
> _“When the world was dust, the elves wrote upon it. When the sky was hollow, the elves painted stars. To live among them was to live at the edge of forever.”_

But elven unity was never eternal. What began as quiet disagreements among philosophers became open rifts of identity. Geography pulled them apart. Wars scarred them. And over the course of centuries, their unity dissolved into three great branches — the **High Elves**, who remained in the courts; the **Wood Elves**, who broke away into the wilderness; and the **Drow**, who were exiled beneath the earth.

---

#### The Aelari — High Elves

The first schism came not in war, but in philosophy. The **Aelari**, ancestors of the High Elves, clung to Eaos’ principles most rigidly. They built systems of hierarchy, councils of lineage, and bureaucracies of arcane oversight. To them, elves were not merely one race among many — they were stewards, rational caretakers placed above chaos to preserve the balance that mortals could not.

Their bloodlines became their power. Nobility was not only political, but mystical; family names carried arcane resonance, woven into enchantments that fortified cities and stabilized Weave-torn landscapes. Marriages were arranged for the strength of the whole, and purity of blood was equated with purity of reason. Over time, their devotion to order sharpened their features and shortened their ears, the result of subtle arcane shaping to match their ideals.

The Aelari claimed the **Crowned Crescent**, a city of marble bridges and radiant courts, where every law was carved in crystal and every noble house kept libraries thicker than their coffers. Even today, their legacy lingers — nobles in Ichinoki and Janbridge trace their ancestry to Aelari lines, and the codes of law they drafted remain the skeleton of Terra’s justice.

>[!quote|mark] A saying among humans captures both awe and resentment 
> _“If you wish to win a war, find a dwarf. If you wish to survive a century, find an elf.”_

---

#### The Ysilari — Wood Elves

Yet not all elves agreed with this ordered perfection. Many began to whisper that the Aelari had forgotten the wilds, the breath of Terra itself. To live only in towers, they argued, was to deny half their soul. These dissenters became the **Ysilari**, later known as Wood Elves.

Their exodus from the cities is shrouded in conflicting stories. Some say it was peaceful, a quiet parting of paths. Others whisper of the **Verdant Quiet** — an event when the Aelari, fearing rebellion, erased entire districts sympathetic to the Ysilari, silencing thousands beneath ivy and stone. Whatever the truth, those who left walked into the deep forests and rolling hills, swearing never again to be bound by councils.

They changed with the wilds. In the **Hypnotic Wild**, Ysilari ears lengthened and grew furred edges to detect predators. In the **Vontrie Snow Plains**, their skin paled and their bodies thickened to hold warmth. Along the **Whispering Expanse**, their voices softened, trained to carry across rivers without echo. Adaptation became their creed.

>[!quote|mark] Ysilari proverb  
> _“The tree does not ask the river how to flow. The river does not ask the mountain where to stand. We live because we do not ask — we become.”_

Their culture embraced druidic traditions, oral storytelling, and fierce bonds to land. Though often dismissed by High Elves as “fallen cousins,” Wood Elves became essential guides, rangers, and allies to frontier peoples. Where empire carved roads, it was the Ysilari who walked them first.

---

#### The Vaelari — Drow

The greatest fracture was not chosen, but inflicted. The **Vaelari**, who later became the Drow, once lived along the mountain borders, trading with dwarves, cutting stone, and guarding deep forests where light rarely reached. Pragmatic and hardy, they often worked closest with House Duergan during the War of the Dwarven Kings, maintaining neutral ties as dwarves tore each other apart.

When Duergan attempted to seize Khazaduum, their defeat dragged the Vaelari with them. Dwarves accused them of complicity, and the Kazak Barel demanded justice. To preserve elven diplomacy, the Aelari agreed to exile their kin.

What began as “temporary relocation” became eternal punishment. Cast into the **Underchasm**, the Vaelari adapted to eternal night. Skin blackened to obsidian, eyes grew pale and wide, ears narrowed to knife-edges. Their society hardened around survival and secrecy, until the name Vaelari was replaced with **Drow**.

The bitterness never faded. In the carved halls of **Zah’rien Vol**:

>[!quote|mark] Drow priest elder 
>_“The sun was not taken from us. It was stolen. And if it ever burns our skin again, may it burn theirs twice as deep.”_

#### Regional Variants

Though these three branches dominate history, the elven diaspora stretched across Terra, and their bodies changed with their environments.

In the **Vontrie Snow Plains**, elves grew shorter, broader, with pale eyes that reflected light like ice. Their hair was often white or silver, and their ears rounded slightly to preserve heat. Hunters here could track prey across storms, and their songs were low, steady rhythms meant to calm reindeer-like mounts.

In the **Alesund Valley**, where swamps bred toxins and mists, elves developed dusky skin mottled like bark, with green or amber eyes that could see through haze. Their bodies became resistant to poisons, and they often tattooed themselves in bright paints to ward off spirits. Outsiders called them “marsh ghosts.”

The **Todazur Flats** bred desert elves — tall, thin, with golden or russet skin and long lashes to guard against sandstorms. Their ears shortened drastically, and they wrapped themselves in layered silks that shimmered like mirages. Their culture prized water above all; to spill it without purpose was a crime akin to murder.

Along the **Whispering Expanse**, elves grew slender, with elongated necks and ears tuned to the flow of river currents. Their songs traveled over water, and their cities floated on barges chained together, drifting with the seasons.

In **Osse**, where the very air shimmered with magic, elves were saturated with arcane light. Their veins sometimes glowed faintly beneath their skin, and their children were born with streaks of luminous hair. While revered as oracles, many also suffered from Arcane Trauma, their lives cut short by brilliance too bright to sustain.

---

#### Legacy and Worship

Though fractured, elven worship remained tied to Eaos. High Elves honored him through law and scholarship; Wood Elves through ritual balance; Drow through whispered curses, seeing in him the logic of their exile. Over centuries, other Keepers and gods entered their pantheon, but Eaos’ shadow lingered in every debate.

The elves’ greatest contradiction is this: they once unified the world with order, yet could not keep themselves whole. Every city, every forest enclave, every Drow citadel remembers a different version of betrayal.

Yet their influence remains everywhere. The laws of Ichinoki echo Aelari codes. The rangers of Byhaven still tell Ysilari tales. And in the deep, the Drow still sharpen blades and stories alike, waiting for the day history listens again.

>[!quote|mark] Historian of Janbridge 
>_“The elves gave us order. The elves gave us stories. The elves gave us exile. In all these things, we are still their children, even if they are no longer one people.”_
