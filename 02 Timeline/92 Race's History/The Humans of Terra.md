The humans were brought into the world by **Qhathos, God of Glory**, who unlike the other gods, gave them no fixed destiny. To elves he gave clarity, to dwarves endurance, to orcs fury—but to humans, he gave **potential**. With no clear path, they were free to chase every path. That gift—glory—was not a crown, but a hunger. It burned in their veins like a fever, demanding they shape the world in their image, no matter the cost.

>[!quote|mark] High King Veyric of Eriwall
> *“The other races seek to become what they were meant to be. We alone must become what we dare.”*  

---

### After the Cataclysm

When the Cataclysm tore Terra asunder, the elves clung to their towers, and the dwarves buried themselves in mountains. But humans spilled across the wastes like fire. They were not the strongest, nor the wisest, nor the longest-lived, but they were the most **desperate**. Desperation breeds ambition, and ambition builds empires.

Where others mourned ruins, humans turned them into foundations. Where others prayed for gods to heal the world, humans plundered it. They scavenged steel from dragon-bones, raised cities on flooded plains, and forged kingdoms in the shadow of greater powers.![[Humans.png|right lp|700]]

### The Age of Ash and Sparks

Before the Cataclysm, humans had been little more than a servant race—scribes for elves, mercenaries for dwarves, slaves for dragons. But after, when divine silence choked the skies, they rose.

The first human kings were not born of noble blood, but of desperation. Bandit chiefs, war captains, and wandering priests declared themselves rulers, and their followers built the first banners of men. These were fragile, short-lived kingdoms, often lasting no more than a generation. But their collapse always left behind stones—and those stones became the next kingdom’s foundation.

>[!quote|mark] Dwarven saying, carved in the halls of Khazaduum
> *“The dwarves endure. The elves remember. The humans… forget. That is why they are always free.”*

### Regional Shaping

The sheer adaptability of humans fractured them into countless forms, their bodies and cultures molded by the regions they claimed.

- **Vontrie Snow Plains (tundra humans):** Pale or ruddy-skinned, stocky with barrel chests, hair often white or flaxen. They carve sagas into whale-bone, wear sealskin armor, and prize endurance above all.
    
- **Alesund Valley (swamp and toxic marsh humans):** Darker skin with mottled undertones, hair braided and coated with oils to resist pests. They are herbalists, poisoners, and shamans, known equally for cures and curses.
    
- **Todazur Flats (desert humans):** Bronze to deep brown skin, tall and wiry, faces veiled against the sun. Masters of oral tradition, caravan-keepers, and raiders. They tattoo themselves with reflective inks to ward off heat spirits.
    
- **Hypnotic Wild (forest humans):** Olive-skinned, long-limbed, with slightly elongated ears. They are hunters, druids, and trackers who live among beasts, feared for their “whispering tongues” that command animals.
    
- **Whispering Expanse (river humans):** Golden to light-brown skin, slender frames, eyes often green or hazel. Their culture thrives on trade, song, and diplomacy. They are builders of bridges, barges, and treaties.
    
- **Osse (arcane humans):** Pale, often scarred by magical burns or birthmarks. Their bloodlines tangled with sorcery, they are scholars, warlocks, and visionaries. Their cities glow with unstable brilliance.
    
- **Underdark (shadeborn humans):** Ash-grey skin, light-sensitive eyes, gaunt frames. Survivors of exile, they are scavengers, assassins, and heretics, despised but never broken.
    

>[!quote|mark] Wood elf proverb
> *“A man from the tundra cannot live in the desert, but a human will try, and in the trying, he will change.”*

### Religion of Many Faces

Though born of Qhathos, humans never bowed to one god alone. To them, the divine was a marketplace, and any who offered power was worthy of worship. Some prayed to Sol, Keeper of the Day, others to Yearn, goddess of harvest, others still to Myral, patron of bonds.

In many cities, cults flourished as quickly as they died. Saints rose, gods were invented, old gods reimagined. This chaos made humans both dangerously flexible and impossible to unify. Even today, no single church rules all humanity.

>[!quote|mark] Lenora Kilvenera <br> Priestess of Eaos
> *“They pray to everything that breathes, and when nothing breathes, they pray to the wind.”*

### The Human Curse

Their short lives drove them to **urgency**. What elves planned over centuries, humans attempted in decades. This made them innovators, explorers, and revolutionaries. But it also made them reckless, violent, and impatient. They rushed into wars, devoured forests, mined mountains hollow, and toppled empires before they could stabilize.

Every triumph was followed by collapse. Every empire by ashes. Every age by another rebirth.

---

### Human Wars and Empires

- **The Banner Wars** were the first great human conflicts—when a dozen kings claimed dominion over the same plains. These wars forged human heraldry, but also the first peace-accords.
    
- **The Siege of Janbridge** saw humans stand against both elves and dwarves, defending their independence with fire and blood.
    
- **The First Empire of Men** rose in Osse, ruled by sorcerer-kings whose blood was said to burn with magic. It collapsed when its rulers began sacrificing their own people to fuel their arcane towers.
    
- **The War of Hundred Faiths** fractured humans again, as cults and prophets fought for dominance. It was said more humans died by human hands in this war than in any elven or dwarven campaign.
    
- **The Age of Chains** followed, when humans enslaved their own kin and sold them to elves and dwarves. This shame is still whispered of in songs.
    

And yet—each collapse only set the stage for another rise.

### Reputation

To elves, humans are reckless children. To dwarves, unreliable allies. To Togruylia’s peoples, they are a plague. Yet humans endure.

They are common as peasants, feared as pirates, revered as kings. Their banners fly over mixed-race cities, not by tradition, but by cunning and force. They are both the **glue and the fracture** of the modern age.

>[!quote|mark] Drow saying
> *“Wherever change stirs, a human hand is on the wheel. Wherever fire burns, a human struck the flint.”*  
---

### Present Day

Today, humans can be found in every corner of Terra. They farm, they trade, they plot, they rebel. They are the backbone of armies and the spark of revolutions.

Most importantly, they believe in **glory**—not as immortality through stone or bloodline, but through action, name, and legacy. They rise and fall, but their stories are written in every ruin, every throne, every war.

For all their flaws, humans are Terra’s storm-bringers: always in motion, always changing, always dangerous.
