> [!quote|mark] Tide-Hymn of the Red Fathoms  
> _“The sea forgives nothing. Neither do we.”_

The selachi, also known as sharkfolk, were brought into being by **Vimnera, Goddess of Revenge**, not shaped in tenderness or logic but in fury. Vimnera tore them from the storming Deep — a people carved from teeth, hunger, and unrelenting instinct. She did not give them hearts; she gave them **predation**. She did not give them language; she gave them **blood-scent**. She did not give them purpose; she gave them **vengeance**.

In the earliest ages, before cities gleamed beneath the waves or coral knew the weight of crowns, the selachi hunted the trenches. Their ancestors stalked the black rifts with jaws that cracked the shells of ancient leviathans. When other races still crawled along the shores learning fear, selachi had already mastered its use.  
To them, fear is not emotion.  
It is **currency**.

Even today, surface folk repeat a simple warning:

> [!quote|mark] Fisherman’s proverb of Merrimarsh 
> _“Where the water turns silent, teeth follow.”_

Most never live long enough to learn how true that is.

## **The Birth of the Blood Tide**

The selachi have an origin soaked in brutality. When the first Leviathan bled during the War Before Memory, the Deep boiled with an emotion old as the gods themselves: **wrath without direction**.

Vimnera reached into that boiling rift, seized the churning hatred, and mixed it with fragments of bone-coral and the spines of trench-sharks. From this cauldron of fury she birthed her children — not individuals, but a **tide**.

They erupted into the world hungering for motion, for challenge, for blood. Their first act was to devour the carcass of the very Leviathan whose wound had created them.

Sages of the deep still whisper:

> [!quote|mark] Tablet of the Oceri Archive  
> _“Their cradle was a wound.”_

## **A Society Built on Teeth**

The selachi build no lasting kingdoms, carve no thrones, and enshrine no temples. Their authority is measured not in lineage or wisdom but in **the number of corpses one has left in the reef**.

Their settlements — if one can call them that — are maze-like warrens of bone and petrified cartilage. Each chamber is a battlefield waiting to happen. Every selachi is expected to challenge the one above them at least once per cycle of Ogulspire. Those who do not are gutted and fed to the broodlings.

Their “laws” are simple:

- **Kill cleanly.** A messy kill dishonors the hunt.
    
- **Eat first what you kill, share the rest with your clutch.**
    
- **Never speak a lie.** Lies confuse the blood-scent.
    
- **Never spill selachi blood without cause.** Without cause means “you lost the challenge.”
    
- **Obey the Queen, for she is the voice of Vimnera.**
    

Their speech is a razor-edged blend of clicks, hisses, and pressure pulses. Scholars call it primitive. Scholars also tend to die swimming too close to their reefs.

## **The Red Frenzy**

Where others are shaped by endurance or currency, selachi are ruled by **blood**.

The scent of it changes them — sharpens them. Their green-lit eyes narrow, their gills flare, and a slow cold calm overtakes them. Surface stories talk of “shark frenzy” as mindless madness. This is ignorance.

Selachi frenzy is not madness.  
It is **clarity**.

They think faster.  
Strike truer.  
Speak the language of battle with perfect fluency.

Triton soldiers call this state **the Red Prayer**, for selachi kill with ritualistic grace — each motion a litany of divine violence.

## **The Wars Beneath the Waves**

If dwarves are rivals of stone and goliaths are rivals of storm, then the selachi stand eternally opposed to **Tritons** — the old guardians of the Deep. What dwarves call holy war, selachi call **feeding**.

To them, Triton blood tastes like arrogance.

Their raids are infamous:

- Entire locathah villages erased in a night.
    
- Grung hunting parties dragged screaming into the surf.
    
- Ship crews found floating, untouched except for the missing hearts.
    
- Sea elf sanctuaries stripped bare; only quiet water remains.
    

Where the selachi hunt, the sea grows still.  
It is their only kindness.

## **Variants of the Selachi**

Though united by bloodlust, selachi diverge into numerous specialized breeds, each shaped by Trench, Tide, or divine whim.

---

### **✦ Trenchborn — Children of the Rift**

> [!quote|mark] Trenchborn creed  
> _“The abyss is mother. The abyss is maw.”_

Deep-black skin veined with crimson. Lantern-green eyes that pierce absolute darkness. Their bone talons are long as daggers, made to anchor into trench walls as they stalk prey in crushing pressure.

They are the priests of Vimnera, though “priest” is too soft a word. They carve runes into their own flesh to remember the wounds of the Leviathans. They alone hear “the pulse beneath the silence,” the heartbeat of the sealed Abyss.

---

### **✦ Reeftooth — The Shallow Sharks**

Ocean-green bodies patterned like reef shadows. Broad jaws capable of crushing coral. Sleek fins sharpened by evolution itself.

Where Trenchborn stalk and judge, Reeftooth **raid**.  
They strike from below, flipping boats or dragging victims through the seagrass. Many coastal towns have a saying carved into their docks:

> _“If the reef goes quiet, return to shore.”_

Reeftooth patrol the edges of selachi territory with near-military precision.

---

### **✦ Tidehowlers — Voices of the Storm**

Selachi born during undersea storms are marked by streaks of white lightning across blue-gray skin. Their frilled necks flare when they shriek — a sound capable of rupturing eardrums and startling even hardened triton veterans.

They predict storms with eerie accuracy, for the pressure of incoming weather swirls in their blood. Some sailors swear Tidehowlers **call** storms, inviting chaos.

---

### **✦ Broodmaws — The Queens’ Monsters**

Where dwarves banished traitors to darkness, selachi queens **breed** their monsters.

To forge a Broodmaw, a queen devours Leviathan marrow and gestates her clutch for a full cycle of Nosduhmere. What emerges is immense — twice the size of other selachi, plated in bone and crowned with a wormlike maw ringed in spiraling teeth.

They are biological siege engines, unleashed only upon cities, leviathans, or rival selachi tribes.

Broodmaws never speak.  
They obey.

---

### **✦ Moon-Drowned — The White Hunger**

Selachi abandoned in moonlit shallows undergo a horrifying metamorphosis. Their scales bleach to milk-white, their eyes cloud over, and their movements become eerily slow — until they strike.

Moon-Drowned can survive minutes on land, dragging themselves up beaches in search of prey. They leave behind trails of wet sand and silence.

Some Trenchborn whisper they are touched by **Nosduh**.  
Others say they worship something **in the light**.

---

## **The Red Communion — Faith Without Mercy**

Selachi worship **vengeance** and **the hunt**.  
Their rituals are as brutal as their nature.

The greatest rite is the **Red Communion**, performed when a Leviathan bleeds in a trench. Thousands gather to consume the tideblight. Those who survive become Trenchborn. Those who perish are considered blessed, carried into the afterlife on a surge of divine wrath.

Their holiest prayer is a simple phrase:

**“In blood, we rise.”**

---

## **Relations with Other Peoples of Terra**

- **Tritons:** eternal foes; selachi believe Triton flesh is “the true offering.”
    
- **Grung:** treated as food or bait.
    
- **Locathah:** hunted, enslaved, or used to flush out prey.
    
- **Sea Elves:** “the pretty thieves,” slain on sight for their songs.
    
- **Sirens:** dangerous rivals whose songs distort the blood-scent.
    
- **Water Genasi:** prized prisoners — their elemental power fuels brood rituals.
    
- **Surface folk:** meat, unless proven useful.
    

And yet… despite their savagery, selachi play a role in the great balance of the sea. Without their culling, certain predators would rise unchecked. Without their pressure, Triton kingdoms would grow complacent.

In their own cold logic, selachi see themselves as the sea’s executioners — the dark justice of Vimnera made manifest.

They are not misunderstood.  
They **understand perfectly**.

The world simply does not like the truth.
