**Origins and Nature**

The gnomes of Terra were never meant to exist. At least, not in the way that elves, dwarves, or orcs were meant. They were not forged by the hand of an Ideal God, nor sculpted from clay, blood, or ash. They were the children of circumstance: the offspring of elves and halflings in the fragile centuries after the Cataclysm. Where other races wore divine crowns, gnomes wore the quiet truth of accident and survival.

From their earliest generations, gnomes embodied paradox. They carried the nimble hands and hunger for comfort of halflings, but paired them with the sharp intellect and long-limbed grace of elves. They looked too small and fey for humans to take seriously, and too eccentric and restless for dwarves to trust. Yet what others dismissed as weakness became resilience. Where one door closed, gnomes built another.

>[!quote|mark] Sage Eriwall <br> 3RD Treatise on Mortal Bloodlines
>*“They were never given purpose, and in the absence of divine will, they chose one for themselves. To yearn.”*

It was no accident that when gnomes first sought gods to call their own, they turned not to Prosper or Eaos, but to **Yearn, the Desire Unending**. For in the restless hunger of that Ideal God, they saw themselves reflected.

**Folklore of Becoming**

Among the oldest gnome folktales is _The Lantern Without Oil_. It tells of the first gnome who stumbled through a storm carrying a lantern that would not light. Where an elf would despair and a halfling would huddle for safety, the gnome tore the lantern apart, rebuilt it with scraps, and coaxed fire from nothing.

>[!quote|mark] The end of the tale _The Lantern Without Oil_
> *“From then on, the little lantern-bearers were never content to keep a thing as it was, but always sought to make it brighter.”*

Such stories carried more than whimsy. They encoded a truth of gnomish life: they were never content with what they were handed, and never safe when they stood still.

**Regional Variants: The Surface Gnomes**

As gnomes spread across Terra, they adapted more than most races. Lacking a divine template to anchor them, their forms and cultures bent to the land.
![[Gnomes.png|right lp|575]]
- **Forest Gnomes** dwelled in the _Hypnotic Wild_, their ears long and their eyes bright as foxfire. Fey-touched, they whispered with spirits and wove illusions into leaves. Many became tricksters or guardians of hidden glades, often seen by travelers only as laughter in the trees.
    
- **River Gnomes** grew along the _Whispering Expanse_, where rivers carved lifelines between shattered civilizations. Shorter and wiry, their skin carried sun-warmed bronze tones, and they became ferrymen, smugglers, and water-mages.
    
- **City Gnomes** settled in human and dwarven strongholds. They became tinkers, locksmiths, and artificers—scrappy survivors in alley guilds and merchant stalls. Their sharp tongues and sharper minds earned them both ridicule and quiet influence.
    
- **Desert Gnomes** endured the _Todazur Flats_, their skin dark and their eyes lined against endless sand. Jewelcutters, sun-charmsmiths, and mirage-makers, they turned the desert’s cruelty into beauty.
    
- **Tundra Gnomes** clung to the _Vontrie Snow Plains_. Pale-skinned, with hair as red as flame or white as frost, they became keepers of fire and tale. Their hearth-songs carried not laughter but the steady drumbeat of endurance.
    

Each type of surface gnome was different, but each shared the same instinct: to adapt faster than the world could kill them.

**The Deep Gnomes**![[Deep Gnomes.png|left lp|550]]

The Cataclysm and the War of the Dwarven Kings pushed entire gnome enclaves underground. Fleeing with the disgraced Duergar, they tunneled into the _Underchasm_ and _Casseirrel’s Spine_.

There they became the **Svirfneblin**, or deep gnomes.

Two cultures grew in parallel. The first were pragmatic survivors: crafters, miners, and spies who traded in secret with drow and duergar. They were distrustful, but alive. The second were the _Ghost Clans_—pale, spectral gnomes who cut all ties to the surface and became myth even to other gnomes. Some say they can melt into stone, others that they whisper directly with the bones of Casseirrel herself.

>[!quote|mark] "Out in the Dark" <br> Dwarven Mining Song
> *“Beware the small eyes in the dark, for they see more than yours ever will.”*

**Suspicion and Prejudice**

For centuries, gnomes were treated as children, tricksters, or mistakes. Elves muttered of diluted blood. Halflings accused them of bringing trouble wherever they wandered. Humans used them as spies and scapegoats in equal measure.

This prejudice never vanished, but gnomes learned to turn it. If others thought them small and harmless, that was an advantage. If they were overlooked, they could see what others missed.

*“Never underestimate a gnome,”* said the human general **Tal Honcese** after a gnomish saboteur unraveled his siege engines in a single night. *“By the time you laugh, they’ve already stolen your crown.”*

**The Copperpot Legacy**

No tale shaped gnomish identity more than that of **Dayton Copperpot**. Born in the Age of Silence, Dayton began as a demolitionist and tinkerer, but his inventions soon shook empires. He crafted the first repeatable crossbow mechanism, the first arcane prosthetics, and even laid designs that later influenced the Veriwind Train. Copperpot made his self anew to be able to out run the touch of time and his maker's mark is likely found when thoroughly inspecting a device that could shape history.

Some say he stole dwarven secrets, others that he bargained with Yearn directly. Whatever the truth, Copperpot’s name became synonymous with gnomish brilliance.

*“Ask a dwarf for stone, an elf for song, a human for courage,”* one saying goes. *“But if you want the impossible—ask a Copperpot.”*

**Wars and Shadows**

Though rarely seen as warriors, gnomes have never been strangers to war. When battles came to their doorsteps, they did not meet them with brute force but with fire, wit, and invention.

The most feared of their kind were the **Cindercog Legion**, a gnomish warband whose craft lay in alchemy, clockworks, and controlled chaos. Armed with hand-held grenades, portable flamethrowers, and skittering machines the size of dogs, the Legion earned a reputation for turning the tide of sieges.

*“Where the Cindercogs march,”* wrote a terrified orc warlord, *“walls do not stand, and silence is never heard again.”*

They were not a formal army, but a loose confederation of clans and inventors bound together in times of need. Some fought for coin, others for vengeance, and some simply for the joy of seeing their creations unleashed. Their machines—nicknamed _gnashers_, _sparkskulls_, and _iron fleas_—were as likely to terrify allies as enemies, but in the chaos of battle, that terror became its own weapon.

One of the most infamous Cindercog assaults occurred during the **Siege of Bracitos**, when a dwarven host sought to strip gnome workshops of their secrets. The Legion responded not with open battle but with a night of fire: they unleashed swarms of clockwork hounds, collapsing the dwarves’ siege towers and filling the air with choking smoke. Survivors said the battlefield *“screamed like a thousand furnaces”* long after the fighting had ended.

The Cindercogs faded as an organized force after the Empire consolidated power, but echoes of their craft remain. Some say their descendants still work in hidden foundries, waiting for the day their machines are needed again. Others whisper that certain weapons of the Legion were never meant to be seen again—that they slumber in vaults too dangerous to be opened.

Whatever their fate, the Cindercog Legion proved one thing: never measure a gnome by their size, but by the weight of what they can carry in their pack.

**Religion and Yearn**

Most gnomes whisper their prayers to **Yearn**, for no other god embodies them so well. Desire to be more, to build more, to outgrow what they are. In Yearn’s hunger, they find validation.

But their worship is not always solemn. In taverns, a gnome may toast “To Yearn, who makes fools of us all!” while in workshops, inventors mutter “Yearn grant me one more spark.”

Some even say gnomes are Yearn’s truest children, for they were born not of divine plan, but of desire itself.

---

**Modern Gnomes**

Today, gnomes are found in every city, yet never in great numbers. They are jewelers in Todazur, tinkerers in Bracitos, ferrymen on the Whispering Expanse, and spies in Ichinoki. In the Spine, deep gnome clans still guard their secret tunnels.

They rarely rule, rarely command armies, rarely leave grand monuments. And yet, no history of Terra can be written without them.

For while others claim glory, gnomes quietly reshape the world.

>[!quote|mark] Archivist Wrynn of Eriwall
> *“Mark the gnome, for where one appears, change soon follows. They are the hinge on which doors swing.”*
