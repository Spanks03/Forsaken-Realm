The yuan-ti were born of **Vimnera, the Goddess of Revenge**. Unlike the rage of Temp or the glory of Qhathos, Vimnera’s gift was quiet, patient, venomous. Where others charged, she whispered; where others ruled through honor or law, she ruled through the certainty that betrayal would always be punished. Into her children she poured this venom of inevitability.

When the gods quarreled and the continents fractured, the yuan-ti were among those cast into **Togruylia**. The exile shaped them. Among the Horde’s rage and the Hive’s swarming hunger, the yuan-ti learned to coil, to watch, to strike unseen. They became the Nest—feared for their cunning, their sorcery, and their subtle hand that reached beyond the island’s borders.

But this is not the whole truth. For every brood that became monstrous in the Nest, another line of yuan-ti scattered into the world, carrying cunning without cruelty, patience without spite. In the diaspora of Eophoris, Osse, and the archipelagos, yuan-ti became **spymasters, oath-keepers, and survivors**. Some still whisper prayers to Vimnera, others quietly invoke Datarr’s suffering or Yearn’s desire, but what unites them is a deep code: _loyalty to kin, vengeance against betrayal._

>[!quote|mark] Serethis of the Marsh Brood
> _“They call us serpents. But a serpent is not evil—it is survival given form. We strike only when the foot comes down upon us.”_

## The Dual Legacy

Like all races that were divided in the Battle of the Continents, the yuan-ti exist in two faces:

- **The Togruylian Nest** — yuan-ti who remained in exile, their bodies reshaped by ritual and the monstrous tournaments. Their humanity withered, leaving serpentine monstrosities revered as Vimnera’s truest coils. They are warlords, priest-kings, and living weapons of vengeance.
    
- **The Diaspora** — yuan-ti who scattered after the **Assault of Kninad**, hiding in cities, coasts, and underworlds. They are more humanoid, more subtle, and often allied with other persecuted peoples. For them, loyalty is survival, and betrayal is death.
    

This duality means the yuan-ti are not simply “evil.” In one breath they are the terror of Togruylia’s arenas, in another, the shadowed hand that saved a rebellion from collapse.

## Regional Variants

Exile and adaptation split the yuan-ti into distinct broods:

- **Jungle Broods** – Green-scaled, sleek, and venomous. They build ruined step-temples deep in steaming canopies, where they rule with druidic cunning and illusions. Hunters whisper that their eyes glow like fireflies before the strike.
    
- **Marsh Serpents** – Pale, mottled hides with wide, mist-seeing eyes. Swamp rot fuels their necrotic magics. They are feared necromancers, but also keepers of forgotten funerary rites. Many diaspora yuan-ti trace kinship to Marsh Serpents who fled persecution.![[ChatGPT Image Sep 7, 2025, 06_59_11 PM.png|right lp|600]]
    
- **Sandfangs** – Gleaming bronze and gold scales, desert-born. Their venoms burn like acid, their illusions shimmer like mirages. Some are jewel-smiths, others assassins; all endure the cruelty of the Todazur Flats.
    
- **Deepfangs** – Driven into the Underdark after the War of the Dwarven Kings. Their scales gleam obsidian-black, their fangs phosphorescent. They whisper with drow Houses and duergar kings, trading secrets for survival.
    
- **Disguised Exiles** – Purebloods who blend into human and elven society. They wear glamours, but their serpentine eyes slip through when emotions burn. Many lead hidden guilds, rebellion cells, or serve as spymasters for nobles who never realize who they’ve hired.

## Hierarchies of the Serpent

The yuan-ti measure status by how much of humanity has been shed:

- **Anathemas** — rare, multi-headed abominations, worshiped as living fragments of Vimnera. Even broods outside Togruylia whisper their names with awe.
    
- **Priest-Kings and Sorcerer-Queens** — rulers who reshape flesh through venom and blood-ritual. They govern temples, arenas, and entire brood-cities.
    
- **Purebloods** — most human-like, often used as infiltrators. In the diaspora, many reject the Nest’s hierarchy, becoming leaders of their own cells.
    
- **Malfangs** — half-serpent warriors with fangs, tails, or coils. Enforcers of the Nest, or mercenaries in the diaspora.
    
- **Abominations** — hulking, twisted, bred for terror. To the diaspora, they are symbols of what they refuse to become.
    

Even among the diaspora, the memory of this hierarchy lingers. Some see it as a chain to be broken, others as a bond to heritage.

## Faith and Philosophy

Yuan-ti faith is not uniform. The Nest worships Vimnera in her most vengeful coil, demanding sacrifice and obedience. But outside Togruylia, yuan-ti faith fragments:

- In Osse, yuan-ti whisper prayers to **Yearn**, honoring desire and ambition.
    
- In Eophoris, some diaspora worship **Datarr**, seeing their suffering as proof of survival.
    
- Some even blend with local cults, adopting reverence for Prosper, Truth, or lesser gods of shadow.
    

But the truest yuan-ti creed is not worship—it is loyalty. A yuan-ti may betray a city, a kingdom, even a god, but never their kin.

>[!quote|mark] Oath of the Deepfangs
> _“We do not kneel for gods who forgot us. We coil around kin, and strike for them alone.”_

## The Yuan-ti Today

Yuan-ti remain feared and misunderstood. To some, they are nightmare cultists, hidden conspiracies, serpents waiting to strike. To others, they are invaluable allies—spies, saboteurs, oath-keepers whose memory for loyalty is as eternal as their memory for betrayal.

In **Togruylia**, they are monstrous, the masterminds of the Nest. In **Osse** and **Eophoris**, they are shadows, infiltrators, and quiet survivors. In both, they embody what Vimnera made them: the inevitability of vengeance, patient as a serpent in the grass.

>[!quote|mark] Common saying in Bracitos
> _“An orc will break your body. A goblin will rob you blind. But a yuan-ti? A yuan-ti will smile at you for ten years, wait until your child’s wedding, and strike when you finally think yourself safe.”_ 

The yuan-ti were not shaped gently, nor gifted freely. They were _wrought_—drawn from the coils of Vimnera, Goddess of Revenge, who spat her venom into the Weave and hissed her fury into flesh. Where Eaos made beings of logic, and Qhathos beings of potential, Vimnera gave her brood only hunger and memory. Her curse became their blessing: they would never forget an injury, and never forgive.
  
>[!quote|mark] Survivor account, Siege of Merrimarsh
> *“When a yuan-ti looks at you, you do not see eyes. You see judgment waiting.”*
