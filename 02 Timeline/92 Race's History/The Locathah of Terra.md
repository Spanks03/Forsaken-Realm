## Divine Origin

The locathah were not created in silence but _in applause_. Qhathos, the god of glory, forged them from the blood of warriors who fell into the sea during the First War of Ascension — mortals who had won no songs, earned no statues, and died unseen beneath the waves. Their valor, forgotten by land and sky alike, called out to the god who could not bear uncelebrated courage.

Thus, Qhathos shaped new flesh from their memory — scaled, finned, and armored with coral and bone — so that the sea itself would remember what the world above had forgotten. These became the **Locathah**, the “Eternal Champions,” bound to live and die for glory beneath the waves.

But as the ages turned, glory soured. The Locathah realized that Qhathos’s gift was not freedom but vanity — a curse to forever seek a stage that no one watched. In bitterness, some turned from the god of glory and began to serve **Underthar**, Keeper of the Deep Places, seeing in him not ambition but survival.

## The Age of the Drowned Banners

The Locathah once ruled the coasts of **Eophoris** and the shallow seas between **Togrulyia** and **Osse**. Their reef-cities, carved from living coral and luminous pearl, stood as proof that glory could thrive even beneath the waves. But when the **Battle of the Continents** poisoned the seas with blood and magic, the Locathah were dragged into the chaos.

The **Grung**, driven by their own divine parent Prosper, began to crawl from the swamps and deltas to stake their claim on the shallows. The Locathah, proud and unwilling to retreat, met them in centuries-long border wars known as the **Tide Wars** — a cycle of ambushes, drownings, and poisoned spears fought in silence beneath the murk.

Though neither side ever truly won, both races learned to hate each other with religious devotion. Even now, in the drowned marshes of Togruylia, Grung and Locathah shamans whisper curses into the same tide, each claiming to own it.

## Culture and Society

Locathah society prizes three things above all: **Memory, Honor, and Witness.** Every deed must be remembered, every victory honored, and every warrior must have someone who saw it — or else it might as well never have happened.

They paint their scales with **silt tattoos**, pigments mixed from sacred sands that record the story of each battle fought. These marks are washed away upon death, returned to the sea in ritual cremation known as _the Unmaking of Tide._ To die unwitnessed — without one to retell your last act — is the worst fate a Locathah can imagine.

Their hierarchies are built on _songs of remembrance_: the most famous warriors become “Tideblades,” their names woven into coral psalms sung by priests known as **the Wavekeepers**. The Wavekeepers tend to vast memory-reefs — living monuments of coral shaped to resemble faces, arms, and weapons — the very architecture of glory itself.

>[!quote|mark] High Wavekeeper Lath’aar of the Coral Reaches
> _“The sea is not cruel, only honest. It forgets what the land pretends to remember.”_

## The Tidecoil Syndicate

As the world above turned its back on them, many Locathah turned to less holy means of earning remembrance. The **Tidecoil Syndicate** rose from the drowned harbors of Eophoris and the trenches beneath Underbridge — a sprawling criminal network of Locathah smugglers, scavengers, and mercenaries.

They traffic in forbidden things: rare poisons from the Grung swamps, relics dredged from the ocean floor, and even **creatures stolen from Togruylia**, ferried across the deep in chains of coral and bone. Some whisper that the Syndicate has even begun to **trade in living genasi**, capturing them to fuel the underwater arcane engines that power their vessels — though none have lived to confirm it.

Despite their crimes, the Syndicate’s captains still adhere to an ancient code:  
_“Every deal is a duel, every duel a story, every story a monument.”_

In Underbridge’s black market, the smell of salt and rust always means Locathah are near — unseen, wet-eyed, and listening.

## Appearance and Variants

Locathah are sleek, muscular, and amphibious — standing between five and seven feet tall, with limbs adapted for both swimming and combat. Their scales shimmer like wet bronze or bone-white coral, often adorned with polished shells or shark teeth.

They breathe both air and water, though long periods out of the sea cause their skin to dry and crack, which is both painful and deeply shameful among their kind. Their eyes are round and luminous, reflecting light even in darkness — symbols of their divine call to _be seen._

Over the ages, different tribes of Locathah have adapted to distinct environments:

---

### **Coralguard Locathah** — _The Champions of Shallow Glory_

Brightly colored with blue-green scales and flowing fins, these Locathah dwell in reef cities and defend the sacred memory-reefs of Qhathos’s temples. They wield coral blades that hum with divine resonance when submerged.  
**Region:** Coastal shallows of western Eophoris and southern Osse.

---

### **Abyssal Locathah** — _The Drowned Remembered_

Pale and long-limbed with translucent fins, their bodies glow faintly in the dark. These Locathah abandoned Qhathos for Underthar, building cities in the black trenches. Their priests collect stories of drowned sailors, believing that each soul adds to their god’s strength.  
**Region:** Deep-sea trenches between Osse and Togruylia.

---

### **Mirefin Locathah** — _The Swamp Marauders_

Olive-green and broad-headed, with thick, mucus-covered scales. These Locathah live in the murky shallows where the Tide Wars never ended. Their spears are tipped with grung venom, and they adorn themselves with bones of their fallen rivals.  
**Region:** Border marshes of Togruylia and the Sunken Delta.

---

### **Ironcurrent Locathah** — _The Engineers of the Tidecoil_

Darker-scaled with metallic stripes along their spines, these are the Syndicate’s innovators — smugglers, shipwrights, and artificers. They build submersible vessels powered by alchemically bound elementals. Many are missing fins or eyes, replaced with mechanical grafts.  
**Region:** Underbridge’s flooded lower canals and hidden drydock caverns.

---

### **Frostscale Locathah** — _The Silent Scouts_

Albino or silver-scaled, these Locathah adapted to the frigid waters around Vontrie’s ice floes. Silent hunters and scouts, they act as intermediaries for the Syndicate’s northern routes. Their scales crackle faintly with frost when they breathe.  
**Region:** The Icerim Coast and frozen shallows north of Osse.

## The Locathah Creed

Every Locathah child learns the _Tide Oath_ before they can swim:

> “Glory is the current. Memory is the sea.  
> The world above forgets —  
> But we remember, forever drowning,  
> Forever seen.”
