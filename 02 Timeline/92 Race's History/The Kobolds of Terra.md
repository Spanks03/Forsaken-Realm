## Divine and Draconic Origin

The kobolds are proof that survival itself can birth divinity.  
When the Cataclysm tore apart the dragonkind, and Cassieriel’s blood fell upon the mortal world, a few droplets mingled with the halfling heart — humble, clever, and persistent. From that unlikely fusion, **the kobolds were born**: not as an intended race, but as an _accident of resilience_.

Though dragons did not claim them as kin, the kobolds worship the lost **First Ember**, a mythic spark said to be the final breath of Cassieriel herself. This ember, according to kobold shamans, sank deep into the tunnels beneath the world — and in the darkness, it whispered to them. Whether that whisper was Cassieriel, or **Underthar**, the Keeper of the Underdark, no one can say. But from that moment onward, the kobolds called themselves _the Children of the Last Fire._

## The Burrowed Ages

Kobolds first appeared in the **deep warrens beneath Eophoris**, carving out networks that would later merge into the vast **Underbridge** — a labyrinthine undercity sprawling beneath every continent. While humans and dwarves built skyward, kobolds tunneled inward, unseen and unthanked.

They learned to **bend their weakness into strength** — digging escape routes faster than armies could march, laying traps that made corridors into killing fields, and building complex societies where loyalty mattered more than strength. In their tongue, _“drath”_ means both _clan_ and _heartbeat_ — for to be kobold is to survive together or not at all.

During the **Battle of the Continents**, when monstrous races were driven into Togruylia, kobolds were forced upward and outward, clawing into swamps and mountain cracks. Those who joined **The Nest** became the faction’s eyes and knives — unseen, underestimated, and indispensable.

## Society and Culture

Kobold society runs on precision and purpose. Each clan, or **drath**, functions like a single organism — every member has a role. Younglings tend fires and watch for tremors; adults hunt, craft, or lay traps; elders record histories in charcoal murals that line the tunnels.

**Leadership** is not given to the strongest, but to the _cleverest survivor._ A leader who lives longest — not one who kills the most — earns divine favor. Their rulers are called **Scale Elders**, chosen by consensus through secret whisper-votes in the dark. Betrayal of this trust is the only true crime among kobolds, punished by being sealed alive behind the wall of your own warren — “buried in your failure.”

Religion among kobolds is a blend of draconic reverence and pragmatic superstition. They pray to **the First Ember**, but also whisper prayers to Underthar, Keeper of the Underdark, and occasionally to **Heselo**, Keeper of Greed — acknowledging the dual truth that fire both warms and consumes.

>[!quote|mark] Priestess Kli’sha of the Red Coil Clan
> _“To dig is to pray. To trap is to teach. To survive is to serve.” _

## The Underworld and the Tournament

In **Togruylia’s Nest**, kobolds form the foundation of an invisible network of spies, saboteurs, and sabbath engineers. Entire tournaments have turned when a kobold trap collapsed a warbeast’s staging pit or when a false tunnel led an enemy army into its own ambush.

To outsiders, kobolds appear as expendable pawns — the first to die, the last to be remembered. But among the monstrous races, they are feared for their unity. A single kobold may be weak, but a clan moves like living machinery, coordinated through echoes and signals known only to them.

It is said that when a clan declares **“Kava’reth”** — the Calling of the Ember — they become one mind, acting with uncanny coordination until every enemy is dead or every flame is snuffed.

---

## Relations and Trade

Kobolds trade in secrets, not gold. In **Underbridge**, they are the unseen engineers, fixing waterworks and rebuilding forgotten dwarven mechanisms for the right price. On the surface, they serve smugglers, alchemists, and assassins — always with a way to vanish when things go wrong.

Among the Nest, they often ally with **lizardfolk** for mutual defense and **yuan-ti** for access to poisons and relics. Yet no race fully trusts them, and perhaps that’s how kobolds prefer it — to be underestimated is the greatest armor of all.

## The Caste of Clans

Every kobold clan has a unique role within their society. Though they appear interchangeable to outsiders, their colorations, traditions, and territories are distinct — often reflecting the element or dragon they claim descent from.

---

### **Red Coil Clan** (Fire-Blooded, Togruylia)

Born of Cassieriel’s ember and her fiery progeny. Their scales glint rust-red or coal-black, and their eyes burn ember-orange. Masters of trapmaking, firebombs, and furnace-forges deep in the Nest’s volcanic tunnels. They maintain shrines to the First Ember lit by eternal flame — or so they claim.

**Region:** Volcanic fissures and lava-vent warrens of western Togruylia.

---

### **Mossback Clutch** (Earth-Blooded, Osse and Togruylia)

Broad-skulled and green-gray with mossy ridges, they move slower but hit harder. Known for building stone golems and subterranean siege engines powered by steam. Their tunnels are sculpted into vast honeycombs — part fortress, part garden.

**Region:** Coastal sinkholes and moss-laden caverns of southern Osse.

---

### **Hollowfang Clan** (Shadow-Blooded, Underbridge)

Their scales are a deep matte black, their eyes pale and milky. They revere **Underthar** above all, seeing him as the “One Who Hides Beneath the Ember.” Hollowfangs are assassins and whisperers, communicating through coded knocks on stone and flickers of shadowlight.

**Region:** The deepest layers of Underbridge, near forgotten vaults of the Drow.

---

### **Rustscale Burrow** (Metal-Blooded, Eophoris)

A strange mutation among kobolds — metallic sheen coats their scales like tarnished brass. Rustscale alchemists dissolve and reforge metal like clay, trading weapons and trinkets to smugglers. They are deeply pragmatic and only loyal to contracts sealed in blood.

**Region:** Ruined dwarven mines under the Spine Mountains.

---

### **Ashfoot Clutch** (Wind-Blooded, wandering)

Nomadic and rail-thin, their pale scales are dusted with gray, and their eyes glow faint yellow. They travel in groups across abandoned tunnels and canyon winds, often serving as couriers and scouts between drath. Their loyalty is to the message, not the sender.

**Region:** The open deserts of the Todazur Flats and wind-carved cliffs of Togruylia.

## The Kobold Creed

No matter the clan or color, all kobolds live by the same whispered creed, taught to each hatchling before their first dig:

> “The world forgets the small.  
> We live in their forgetting.  
> We burn where light cannot reach.  
> We endure, and in endurance — we are eternal.”
