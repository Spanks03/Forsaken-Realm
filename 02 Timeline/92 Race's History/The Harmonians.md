---

The Harmonians were the first children of Terra, crafted by the **Elder Gods** in an age before memory. They were not shaped like the races that followed; they were an experiment in _efficiency_, a shortcut to evolution. Rather than growing slowly through the struggles of nature, they were given a hive-mind connection—a colony of linked thoughts.

Each Harmonian was born into a chorus of voices, a collective memory. To one, a skill was taught; to all, it was known. If one survived, the colony endured. If one discovered fire, all could wield it. It was said that their first decade of life achieved more progress than centuries of any other race.![[Harmonian.png|right lp|700]]

Their appearance reflected their alien design. **Skin dark and grayish, mottled like stone beneath moonlight. Ears sharp and leathery, resembling the echo-chambers of bats. Eyes wide and predatory, catching the smallest trace of movement in the dark. And their mouths—lined with teeth fit not for meals of flesh, but for the taste of blood, where memory itself could be drunk.**

>[!quote|mark] Attributed to the god Syntara, Goddess of Ambition
> _“They are not as we are. They are not cousins, nor rivals, nor kin. They are predators of thought itself.”_  

---

At first, the Harmonians spread across Terra unhindered. They built no cities, but their colonies were everywhere—shifting, adapting, consuming. They believed themselves the _only true Terrarians_, and all else were intrusions, aberrations, or failures of creation. When the **lesser gods** birthed new races—elves, dwarves, men, beasts—the Harmonians declared war not only on the creations, but on the gods themselves.

This arrogance sparked the **Godwar.** The younger gods, fearful that their peoples would be exterminated, turned on the Elder Gods’ firstborn. Divine fire scoured colonies. The seas were salted with their blood. Across Terra, the Harmonians were hunted with a fury unmatched, their hive-minds unraveling as one after another perished.

In time, the Harmonians seemed extinct. The **younger gods triumphed**, but the Elder Gods were enraged. They had not commanded their children’s destruction. For this, on top of many other deviances, the Elder Gods **sealed away the Ideal Gods**, binding them as punishment. Terra was left fractured, its first race destroyed.

---

Or so it seemed.

### The Rebirth

The extinction of the ancients drove Harmonians into desperation. They could not reproduce naturally; their kind had been crafted sterile by the Elder Gods. But extinction was not something they would accept. They began to **experiment**.

They tried grafting Harmonian heads onto mortal bodies. The corpses twitched, then rotted. They tried feeding brains to mothers, hoping new Harmonian children would grow in their wombs. Nothing.

Until one ritual succeeded.

A woman was taken—some say priestess, some say peasant girl. She was with child. For nine months she was imprisoned, kept alive with nutrients and injections of Harmonian blood. The colony gathered around her constantly, pouring their combined will into her unborn child.

When the infant was born, the chamber echoed with their triumph. **The child’s eyes were already open, already knowing. She spoke—not in a baby’s cry, but in the language of the colony.**

This was the first of the **Reborn Harmonians.**

---

But the tale does not end there.

The mother escaped. Weakened, scarred, but alive. Years later, she bore another child. This time, the child was normal—or so it seemed. Yet as the boy grew, she realized she could _hear_ him. Not with her ears, but in her mind. They were connected, a colony of two.

Thus began the age of the **Broken Colonies.**

Some Reborn were perfect, growing as leaders of their own hives. Others were half-formed, twisted by mortal bloodlines, pulling only fragments of the old knowledge. Many were unstable, their colonies splintered, their voices at war with one another.

The Harmonians were not so easily erased. Their strength was not only in number, but in cunning. They learned to **disguise themselves**—not with shapeshifting, but by planting false images in the minds of those who looked upon them. A Harmonian might appear human, elf, or dwarf, though always with something slightly _wrong_. A detail you could not place. A shadow that stretched too long.

They learned to **drink blood for memory.** A mouthful of blood gave them a moment, a skill, a secret. A cupful might give them years. A man drained dry left behind not only a corpse, but an empty shell where once had been a story.

And so they survived, scattered, hiding in plain sight.

---

### The Matriarchs

Harmonians were linked in **colonies**, but one thing separated them from true unity: their daughters. When a Harmonian female was born, she was not connected to her mother’s colony. Instead, she became the seed of her own. Around her, a new hive would grow—brothers, cousins, children—all sharing her mind.

These **Matriarchs** were rare and sacred. Whole colonies lived and died for them. It was said that killing a Matriarch was the only true way to erase a colony, for without her the voices dissolved into madness.

>[!quote|mark] Anonymous survivor account <br> “The Black Book of Ballybane”
> _“I was taken once. Their Matriarch spoke through a child’s mouth. A child that was not a child at all. When she looked at me, I could hear her voice in my own thoughts, whispering that I was hers already.”_

### Abilities and Fears

The Reborn Harmonians retained abilities that made them objects of fear:

- **False Visage** — By temporarily detached from their colonies, they project illusions into the minds of others, making them appear as something more familiar.
    
- **Blood Memory** — By consuming blood, they relive the past of the one they fed upon. A drop might reveal a face. A mouthful, a secret. A life’s blood, an entire history.
    
- **Colony Knowledge** — Within a hive, any skill known by one can be used by another. A swordsman’s technique, a scholar’s script, a thief’s hand—all shared.
    
- **Mind Control** — With effort, a Harmonian may seize another’s will. At first only for moments, but with age and strength, they may hold it for hours, even days.

### Modern Harmonians

Today, Harmonians are known only in whispers. They are **rare**, but feared. In cities, they may serve as spies, assassins, or hidden advisors. In battlefields, they may appear as allies, only to vanish with the secrets of an army in their veins.

To most of Terra, they are _legends_. To those who have seen them, they are _nightmares_.

No other race inspires such paranoia. For a Harmonian does not need to be seen to be feared. It only needs a drop of your blood.
