### **Origins**

The Grung were brought into the world by **Prosper**, the god of luck and mischief — though most theologians agree it must have been one of his more _questionable miracles_. Born from the warm rains and laughing streams of the First Jungle, the Grung were Prosper’s attempt to create beings that embodied his paradox: **discipline through chaos**.

According to Grung myth, Prosper didn’t shape them from clay or flame like the other gods shaped their children — he simply dropped seven raindrops from his palm, and each hit the earth a different color. When the rains ceased, the first seven Grung emerged: gold, silver, blue, red, green, orange, and black. Each carried part of his fortune — and part of his curse.

>[!quote|mark] _The Song of Brightwater_
> “We were made from laughter,  <br> but the world did not laugh with us.”  

---

### **The Caste of Color**

Grung society is rigidly stratified — color defines purpose, authority, and destiny. Though outsiders call it cruelty, the Grung call it _balance_. Each color caste excretes distinct toxins, determining not only social rank but spiritual role.

- **Green Grung** – The lowest caste, hunters and gatherers. Their toxin induces mild paralysis, used to preserve prey.
    
- **Blue Grung** – Builders and artisans. Their toxin causes hallucinations — they claim it opens the “Dream Eye,” granting insight from Prosper’s laughter.
    
- **Red Grung** – Warriors and guards. Their venom burns the blood, igniting a feverish rage.
    
- **Orange Grung** – Traders, smugglers, and scouts. Immune to nearly all natural poison, they thrive on risk.
    
- **Silver Grung** – Priests, poets, and singers of fate. Their toxin compels obedience and truth. They claim their words echo Prosper’s own.
    
- **Gold Grung** – Rulers and prophets. Their venom does not kill but _changes_, warping memory and emotion — reshaping loyalty itself.
    
- **Black Grung** – Outcasts and mystics. Rumors claim they can weep poison potent enough to kill gods. Their skin shimmers like oil; few live long enough to test it.
    

Each color regards the others with reverence and fear — even within the same tribe. “To touch another’s skin is to borrow their destiny,” the elders warn.

### **Culture and Faith**

The Grung revere **Prosper** not as a benevolent god, but as a **trickster-patron** — a spirit who tests his children through endless near-misses and impossible luck. Their prayers are less supplications and more _dares_. Shrines are built over pits of snapping frogs and coiled serpents, offerings tossed in with a whispered, _“Catch if you can.”_

To the Grung, every act of chance — a hunt, a leap, a love — is a holy ritual. They believe that death by misfortune is simply Prosper’s way of _taking back his gift_. Luck is both sacred and terrifying.

### **The Lost Empire of Brightwater**

In the swamps south of Togrulyia lie ruins older than any dwarf or elf remembers: vine-choked ziggurats rising from mirror-pools, carved with sigils of frogs wearing crowns. Grung legend speaks of **Brightwater**, a shining empire where the rivers glowed gold and Prosper himself walked among his children.

Brightwater’s fall is debated. Some say the Gold caste sought to trap Prosper’s laughter in a jewel; others whisper that the Silver priests lied to the gods and stole too much of fate. Whatever the truth, the rivers turned black, and the Grung scattered into the wetlands, never to rise again.

Even now, Grung dreams begin with the sound of laughter echoing over still water — Prosper’s or their own, no one can say.

---

### **Modern Grung Society**

Today, Grung settlements hide deep within the **Alesund Valley** and the **Togrulyian marshlands**, their huts woven from reeds and the bones of drowned beasts. Tribes are small — never more than a hundred — ruled by a single Gold or Silver Grung who interprets the will of Prosper.

While other races see savagery, Grung see art. They weave vibrant murals on lily pads using bioluminescent slime, create hypnotic rhythms by striking water with their palms, and tell history through the patterns of croaks and drumbeats that echo through the fog.

Their written language, **Croatal**, is not inscribed but sung — a tonal, rhythmic script that can be read only when recited aloud, making it impossible to steal without learning the melody.

---

### **Grung in Togruylia**

In **Togruylia**, Grung are divided between two faces of fortune.  
Those within **The Horde** serve as **alchemists, poisoners, and scouts**, revered for their unmatched control of toxins. Others, the so-called **Ghost Smugglers of Nahr-Tuul**, operate independently, ferrying contraband beneath the marshes and islands. Their bioluminescent skin trails across the black water like ribbons of ghostlight — the last thing seen by those who try to betray them.

Despite their reputation, the Grung of Togruylia are not inherently cruel. They live by a simple creed:

> “We poison not to kill, but to chase our god.”

The poison, to them, is sacred — memory made physical. Many Grung mark their bodies with scars of venom, reminders of victories, loves, or losses. The more scars a Grung bears, the more “stories” they carry.

### **Regional Variants**

#### **The Vaskari (Marsh Grung)**

_“When the reed bends, the Vaskari leap.”_  
Named after the drowned city of **Vask-Tel**, the Vaskari dwell in the sprawling wetlands of **Togruylia** and **Eophoris’s southern marshes**. Their skin ranges from mottled green to amber, slick with thin toxin that causes brief paralysis.  
Vaskari are pragmatic survivors — ambushers and gatherers who move in silence through knee-high water. They are the keepers of songs carved into the reeds, remembering every drowned god and lost ally. Their villages are invisible until you’re standing in one.

> Appearance: Short and stocky, webbed digits, bright eyes like wet jade. Their bodies often painted with spirals of algae and pollen.  
> Role: Hunters, gatherers, and marsh guardians.

---

#### **The Nahrun (River Grung)**

From _Nahr_, meaning “moving song,” these Grung trace their lineage to the rivers that coil through **Eriwall’s southern delta** and **Osse’s mangrove labyrinths**. Their toxin induces vivid hallucinations, used in rituals of prophecy.  
They believe the river is Prosper’s tongue — that every ripple is a divine syllable. To navigate a Nahrun waterway without permission is considered a theft of voice.

> Appearance: Sleek and long-limbed, turquoise to sapphire-blue skin, with translucent webbing between fingers and limbs. Their eyes glimmer like fish scales in motion.  
> Role: River shamans, ferrymen, smugglers, and truth-seers.

---

#### **The Solok (Jungle Grung)**

Deep in the **Hypnotic Wilds** prowl the Solok — black-skinned Grung with faint bioluminescent streaks running down their spines. Their toxin induces sleep or delirium, which they treat as a sacred state of communion.  
Solok shamans claim to walk between the dreams of mortals and spirits, croaking lullabies that keep the jungle asleep. They decorate themselves in feathers and glowing moss, appearing as living spirits when the moons rise.

> Appearance: Slender but tall for Grung, with pitch-black skin veined by pale green glow, golden eyes that see heat rather than light.  
> Role: Dreamwalkers, druids, poisoners, and jungle seers.

---

#### **The Zok’Teel (Island Grung)**

Across the coral isles of **northern Osse**, the Zok’Teel (“Shell-Blessed”) live in stilt villages painted with crushed coral and bioluminescent slime. Their toxin causes euphoria and mild visions — considered divine gifts of Prosper’s laughter.  
They are joyful and reckless, often performing dangerous feats to test fate. A Zok’Teel will leap from impossible heights into black waters simply to prove they are “watched.” They believe Prosper swims among them as a silver frog during eclipses.

> Appearance: Brilliantly colored — orange, yellow, and pearl-white — with smooth, reflective skin and patterns that resemble coral fans.  
> Role: Divers, singers, traders, and luck-priests.

---

#### **The Kesh’Nar (Deep Grung)**

In the lightless caverns beneath the **Tecumheim Depths**, the Kesh’Nar endure in silence. Pale as ghosts, with eyes clouded to white, they communicate through tremors and deep croaks that echo through the stone. Their poison is the most feared — slow, incurable, and cold.  
They worship **Prosper’s Shadow**, claiming luck and misfortune are the same coin flipped in darkness. To them, every breath is a gamble.

> Appearance: Bone-white or translucent skin, black veins visible beneath the surface, frilled gills that glow faintly blue. Their movements are unnervingly smooth, almost floating.  
> Role: Alchemists, philosophers, and assassins of the deep.

---

#### **The Tlu-Va (Gold Caste / Lost Heirs of Brightwater)**

The rarest of all, the **Tlu-Va** are the gilded descendants of the **Gold Caste of Brightwater** — said to carry traces of Prosper’s original laughter in their blood. Their toxin alters memory, and their presence unsettles even other Grung.  
Few believe they still exist, but stories tell of radiant amphibians crowned in living gold, who wander the wetlands seeking to restore the lost empire. Their song is said to bend luck itself — sometimes granting miracles, sometimes calamity.

> Appearance: Golden or metallic yellow skin that glows faintly under moonlight, long frilled crests, and spiral markings along their limbs.  
> Role: Prophets, wanderers, and lost royalty of the Grung.

---

#### **The Vask-Tel Outcasts (Black Grung)**

Not a tribe but a curse. Born beneath **dead moons** or amidst **poisoned rain**, these black-skinned Grung are shunned even by their kin. Their toxin is both venom and prophecy — those they poison dream their death before it comes.  
The Outcasts live on the fringes of swamps and ruins, tending to the dead and speaking with spirits. Though feared, they are never killed — to slay one is said to erase your name from Prosper’s dice forever.

> Appearance: Iridescent black with oil-slick sheen, eyes deep crimson or reflective silver. Often tattooed with ash and wearing necklaces of shed skins.  
> Role: Oracles, undertakers, and keepers of taboo lore.
    

---

### **Legacy and Reputation**

In the cities of men, the Grung remain half-myth, spoken of in whispers between merchants and mages. Their name carries fear and fascination — equal parts pest and prophet.

Yet among themselves, the Grung know who they are: the children of luck’s cruel joke, who refused to die laughing.

>[!quote|mark] the Gold Priest of Vask-Tel
> “Prosper made us small, <br> so we could slip between the gods’ fingers.”
