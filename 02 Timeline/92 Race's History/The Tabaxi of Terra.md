### Divine Origin — The Thousand Eyes of Truth

The Tabaxi were born not from wrath or vengeance, but from revelation. When **Truth**, god of clarity and divine order, walked Terra after the Cataclysm, He saw that the world had become a labyrinth of lies—gods deceiving mortals, mortals deceiving themselves. To pierce the shroud, He created watchers, not warriors: beings with sharp eyes, quiet steps, and hearts attuned to curiosity.

He called them **His Thousand Eyes**—the first Tabaxi. Each was given a fragment of His perception, allowing them to sense falsehoods, trace hidden paths, and glimpse the secrets behind all things. But when Truth withdrew into silence during the Second Era, His children were left untethered. Without His voice to temper them, their divine curiosity turned chaotic.

Some sought to rediscover Truth’s will, becoming wanderers and philosophers. Others turned their gifts inward, using them to manipulate, deceive, and survive. What was meant as divine revelation fractured into a thousand hungers—and so the race became divided between those who seek the **Hidden Truth** and those who serve the **Silent Lie**.

>[!quote|mark] Tabaxi elder saying
> _“When Truth fell quiet, we learned to listen to the sound of our own claws.”_

### History of the Tabaxi

#### **The Age of Revelation**

In the centuries before the Cataclysm, Tabaxi were revered in select elven and human societies as oracles, truth-seers, and divine messengers. Their ability to sense deception made them invaluable in royal courts and among priesthoods. It was said that when a Tabaxi’s whiskers trembled, lies unraveled.

They served as sacred scribes in the **Hall of Mirrors**, a temple to Truth now lost beneath the Hypnotic Wild. There, they recorded the words of mortals and gods alike, transcribing only what was _true_. But when the Cataclysm broke the Weave, the Hall fell silent—its mirrored walls shattering, scattering fragments of Truth’s reflection across Terra.

#### **The Age of Exile**

After the **Battle of the Continents**, the Tabaxi were cast to Togruylia alongside leonin, rakasta, and felis as Ulfong's Pride. Unlike the others, they did not fight willingly. Their divine purpose had been observation, not conquest. But to survive among the monstrous factions, the Tabaxi adapted—learning to move unseen, to deceive, to kill.

Ulfong the Black used them as scouts and assassins, and soon, “Where the eyes gleam, death follows” became a common Togruylian saying. Yet even then, the Tabaxi recorded everything. Hidden beneath their armor and war paint, they tattooed their histories in secret — whisker-thin lines across their own fur, stories of loss, exile, and unending curiosity.

#### **The Whispering Migration**

When the **Assault of Kninad** shattered the balance once more, many Tabaxi fled Togruylia in the chaos. Some crossed the seas in stolen ships, others hid in caravans or infiltrated trading fleets. These exiles scattered across Eophoris and Osse, forming hidden enclaves within jungles, mountain valleys, and city shadows.

To outsiders, they became myths again — ghostly feline figures who vanished when questioned. Yet among themselves, they formed the **Whispering Migration**, a silent network of informants and historians who preserve the truth of the world’s past through oral tradition and coded songs.

>[!quote|mark] Mrae of the Hidden Step <br> Whispering Migration chronicler
> _“We were never monsters. We were mirrors, showing others what they feared to see.”_

### Culture and Belief

**Tabaxi society** is nomadic and deeply oral. They write little, instead recording history through story, dance, and tattoo. A Tabaxi’s body is a living chronicle — each stripe or mark earned through experience, trial, or revelation.

Their social structure is organized into **Prides**, each following a **Path of Knowing**, a philosophical lens through which they interpret Truth’s silence.

- **Path of the Eye** — Seers and monks who believe Truth still speaks through the natural world. They meditate in storms, winds, and forest whispers.
    
- **Path of the Paw** — Rogues, scouts, and adventurers who believe Truth must be _taken_, hunted, or unearthed. Their claws are their scripture.
    
- **Path of the Tail** — Storytellers, bards, and travelers. They claim that Truth lives through stories, and that every lie eventually reveals a greater meaning.
    

Each Pride guards a **Fragment of the Hall of Mirrors**, a polished shard said to still hold a glimpse of Truth’s divine reflection. When two Prides meet, they touch their fragments together, an act of shared revelation known as _The Joining of Eyes_.

### Regional Variants

The Tabaxi are wanderers and storytellers, the _curious truth-seekers_ among the feline kin. They follow **Truth**, who values the lessons of chaos and curiosity. Their variants are cultural and geographic, marked by the **ways they interpret curiosity** — whether through exploration, art, survival, or thievery.

Their names are descriptive and **reflect emotion or action** — "Silent Paw," "Echo’s Tail," "Candle in Mist."

- **Snowcloaks (Vontrie Snow Plains)** — Compact and thick-furred with white-gray pelts mottled like frost across stone. Their tufted ears and pale eyes make them nearly invisible in snow. They speak little, their silence often mistaken for coldness, though it is reverence for the quiet beauty of the world.  ![[tabaxi.png|right lp|700]]
    _(Inspired by lynx and bobcats.)_
    
- **Dustpads (Todazur Flats)** — Wiry and short-furred, their sandy coats blend with the desert dunes. Narrow faces, wide paws, and long tails keep them steady in shifting sands. Curious scavengers and quick-witted traders, they are known to hide treasures beneath the dunes “for the gods to find later.”  
    _(Inspired by sand cats.)_
    
- **Shadowpads (Underbridge & Eophoris Cities)** — Thin and agile with sleek, soot-black fur and large golden eyes. Their ears twitch constantly, listening for danger or profit. They thrive in the labyrinth of alleys and tunnels, building spy rings and messenger guilds beneath the notice of those they serve.  
    _(Inspired by black-footed cats.)_
    
- **Mistwhiskers (Alesund Valley)** — Striped in gray and rust, with long, sensitive whiskers that sense vibration in fog. Their fur glistens faintly when wet, making them ghostly shapes in the mists. Patient ambushers and dream-readers, they hunt fish and spirits alike.  
    _(Inspired by ocelots and fishing cats.)_
    
- **Highclimbers (Spine Mountains)** — Compact and muscular, thick-necked and broad-pawed. Their coats are silver-brown, speckled with dark spots that vanish against the rock. Fierce climbers and shrine keepers, they guard the high passes and speak the “stone-tongue” of mountain winds.  
    _(Inspired by Pallas’ cats and Andean mountain cats.)_
    
- **Wildhearts (Hypnotic Wilds)** — Vibrant fur of blues, greens, and oranges, faintly iridescent like oil in sunlight. Their markings swirl across their bodies in hypnotic, wave-like patterns. They are jungle wanderers, wild seers, and song-keepers who claim to still hear Truth’s laughter echoing through the canopy.  
    _(Inspired by small rainforest cats like margays and tigrinas.)_
    

Tabaxi are the **wanderers and chroniclers** of the feline kin — the voice of curiosity in a world that fears the unknown.

### Relationships and Reputation

Other races still regard the Tabaxi warily, associating them with theft and treachery, yet scholars of Eriwall recognize their importance as living historians. Dwarves distrust them for their trickery, while elves quietly respect their artistry and natural grace.

But to those who know them well, the Tabaxi are a people of paradox: divine curiosity bound in mortal form, seekers who cannot stop seeking—even when it destroys them.

---

### The Modern Tabaxi

Today, the Tabaxi live scattered across Terra. Some serve as spies for noble houses, others as guides through wildernesses long forgotten. A few rare Prides remain in Togruylia, keeping the oldest traditions alive — fierce, reclusive, and bound by the belief that Truth still watches through them.

In Eriwall and Underbridge, Tabaxi scholars have even begun restoring fragments of the **Hall of Mirrors**, piecing together what may one day reveal Truth’s final lesson:

>[!quote|mark] Inscription on the Mirror of Mrae
> _“There is no greater lie than certainty.”_
