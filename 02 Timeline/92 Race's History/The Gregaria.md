_The Endless Marchers • The Swarm of One Breath • The Line-That-Does-Not-Break_

> [!quote|mark] Saying of the Togruylian arenas  
> _“If you hear the ground beginning to scratch, it is already far too late.”_

The Gregaria are the most numerous and most feared of all Insectoid castes. Where the Zelus kill with precision and the Anisoptera lead with intellect, the Gregaria overwhelm with the oldest truth of the swarm: **numbers, hunger, and unbreakable momentum**.  
Their bodies echo the form of enormous locusts or grasshoppers—long legs built for sudden leaps, plated limbs meant for endless travel, and mandibles sharp enough to tear hide from bone. Among all insectoids, the Gregaria are the closest to “common soldiers,” yet to call them simple is a dangerous mistake. In the Hive, the Gregaria are considered the **Heartbeat of Continuation**, the caste whose deaths are not losses, but investments.

Their story begins not with a creator-god, but with the first Hive’s greatest terror: **the Starving Season**, when food ran thin and the early insectoid tribes devoured themselves to survive. Only one brood endured, not because it was strong, but because it was **willing to become many to preserve one memory**. The Gregaria are their descendants—each generation born knowing it exists to die, so that a future self may walk forward carrying the same mind, sharpened by all previous deaths.

> [!quote|mark] Inscription carved into the Hive-Mother walls  
> _“We are not reborn. We are continued.”_

## **The Flesh of the Swarm**

To call a Gregaria “born” is inaccurate. They **emerge**, fully aware, already carrying ancestral experience. Their bodies are wiry, tense, and constantly flexing—as though even stillness is a waste of precious time. Long, multi-jointed legs end in clawed feet that cling to stone or soil with unsettling silence. Their carapaces range from muted browns to sickly greens, flecked with gold during certain hormonal cycles that mark a hive preparing for war.

Their compound eyes gleam like polished amber, each facet reflecting a different color. Their antennae twitch ceaselessly, tasting pheromones, sensing tremors, and reading the emotional state of the swarm around them. A Gregaria can feel another Gregaria’s heartbeat fifty paces away. ![[Gregaria.webp|right lp|500]]

They speak in hisses, clacks, and pheromonal pulses—language so layered that a single sentence would overwhelm most humanoids with simultaneous meanings: hunger, direction, mood, command, and urgency threaded into one.

## **The Cycle of Breaking and Becoming**

The Gregaria life cycle is infamous even by Insectoid standards. They live a month—sometimes only a few weeks—before collapsing under the strain of their own accelerated metabolism. But death is not an ending.

Shortly after they become full grown, a Gregaria lays a soft, translucent egg under a hinged plate of its thoracic armor. The egg pulses gently, lit from within like a lantern. It remains there for a little over a week as the Gregaria continues to fight, run, or labor, acting as shield and shelter both.

When the egg hatches, the larva emerges not as a helpless creature, but as an immature Gregaria with the **full memories and skills of its progenitor**. The lava grows to fully grown in a couple of days and the two bodies walk side by side for only a handful of days—one dying, the other rising. As soon as the second body is ready, the first willingly collapses. Its corpse is dismantled—carapace, sinew, muscle, bone—into food, weapons, tools, and armor for the hive.

Nothing is wasted. Nothing is mourned. Nothing is forgotten.

> [!quote|mark] Gregarian brood-song  
> _“My shell is yours. Your breath is mine. Forward, ancestor. Forward.”_

## **The Swarm’s Foot Soldiers**

Though outsiders call them “foot soldiers,” the Hive names them **the Forward Stride**. They are not merely fighters, but the front line of existence itself. When the Tournament of Togruylia begins, it is the Gregaria who charge first, leaping in long, arcing trajectories, landing with enough force to shatter brittle bones.

They do not retreat. They do not hesitate. A Gregaria will hurl itself into certain death if doing so improves the chances of victory by a single heartbeat. Sacrifice is their culture. Continuation is their holiness.

Hobs fear their discipline. Orcs fear their numbers. Dwarves fear the way they **consume battlefields** once the fighting ends. Even the other monstrous races of The Nest treat them with wary respect.

The Gregaria do not conquer territory. They simply move through it, stripping, dismantling, and leaving nothing behind but clean bones and scorched earth.

## **The Hive Within**

To understand a Gregaria is to accept that ego means nothing to them. When they speak, it is always as a plurality:

“We march.”  
“We hungered.”  
“We remember.”

Only the Anisoptera think in “I.”  
The Gregaria think in “We always have been.”

Among their kind, they hold the middle rung of the caste-ladder—below the rarefied intellect of the Anisoptera, above the terrifying precision of the Zelus. They do not begrudge hierarchy. They do not envy. They simply fill the role evolution hammered into them: the unstoppable tide.

Within the Hive’s spiraling caverns, they perform every task requiring uncomplaining endurance: maintaining tunnels, clearing nest debris, raising brood-clusters, patrolling perimeters, hunting for Iomachus herds. Their days blur, purpose woven into every heartbeat.

They have no concept of laziness. They do not rest unless commanded.

## **Other Insectoids and Their Place in Gregarian Memory**

The Gregaria alone maintain the hymnal rhythms of the Hive—deep vibrations in their thoraxes that other insectoids instinctively respond to. Because of this, they are the caste most intertwined with the non-playable insectoid species.

**Iomachus (Bush Crickets)**  
The Gregaria raise and harvest them. To a Gregaria, an Iomachus herd is like a breathing orchard; the destruction of a herd is a tragedy, not for morality’s sake, but because it slows the march of continuation.

**Kysolampis (Great Fireflies)**  
Gregaria war-chants trigger their War Call, and Gregaria soldiers will deliberately sacrifice themselves to ignite a chain detonation.

**Nemotocera (Giant Gnats)**  
Gregaria use them as walls of living hunger—sending swarms ahead of the line to soften targets. The Nemotocera recognize Gregarian pheromones and rarely attack them.

**Oryctes (Rhino Beetles)**  
These are the mounts of Gregarian brutes. A Gregaria astride an Oryctes is considered a one-creature siege engine.

**Tinea (War Moths)**  
The Gregaria treat them with reverence bordering on superstition; the dust of a Tinea is considered a blessing of exhaustion upon enemies.

All of these creatures bend instinctively to Gregarian presence. They do not control them through dominance—only through the endless echoes of their ancestral memory.

## **The Arena and the Last Step**

In the Tournament, a Gregaria line fighting at full unity is a terrifying sight: synchronized leaps, bone-cracking landings, mandibles snapping in rhythmic bursts. They swarm up the legs of larger foes, dismantling them joint by joint.

When a Gregaria’s end nears, it seeks the highest vantage point on the battlefield and leaps into the air one final time. The last jump is believed to release the final instinctual map into the pheromone-web of the Hive—a dying signal that tells future generations where dangers lie, what strategies failed, and where the world may still be conquered.

> [!quote|mark] Final leap-saying  
> _“The ground remembers me. Walk where I fell.”_

To a Gregaria, there is no afterlife. The Hive is the only eternity they acknowledge.

---

## **Interactions With Outsiders**

Outside Togruylia, Gregaria are almost unknown. The few who escape the Hive do so only when a brood-line is broken—when a larva hatches too early, without the memories of its predecessors. These “Silent Gregaria” wander the world, confused and aimless, their antennae twitching with the phantom absence of the swarm.

Some become mercenaries.  
Some become prophets of hunger.  
Some become tragedies, starving themselves without ever understanding why.

When encountered, they speak haltingly, like children repeating words they’ve never heard spoken aloud.

Those who face full Hive Gregaria in battle rarely survive. Those who survive rarely sleep again.

## **The Name and the Fear**

Among monstrous races of Togruylia, the Gregaria carry more nicknames than any other caste:

“The Linebreakers.”  
“The Thousand That Are One.”  
“The Hunger-Legion.”  
“The Locust Choir.”  
“Tomorrow’s Bones.”

But the name whispered most often—by hobgoblin generals, by lizardfolk scouts, by hardened orcs who fear nothing—is the simplest:

**“The March.”**

Because once the Gregaria begin marching, nothing in Terra—not gods, not armies, not mountains—has ever proven strong enough to make them stop.
