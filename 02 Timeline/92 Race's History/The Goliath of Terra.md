The Goliaths are children of the mountains—tall, scarred, and proud, walking embodiments of **Qhathos, god of glory**. To meet a goliath is to feel the weight of stone and the blaze of fire: unyielding endurance bound with fierce ambition. While other peoples build walls and kingdoms, goliaths wander the high places, claiming no land but the sky above them.

>[!quote|mark] Proverb carved on a Spine pass marker
>_"The mountain does not kneel, nor does the goliath."_
---

### Origins of the Mountain-Born

Many mistake goliaths for the descendants of giants. The truth is reversed: **goliaths are the forerunners,** born when Firbolgs, guided by Truth, mingled with the ancient forces of the mountain and were reshaped by Qhathos’ breath. Giants arose later, colossal echoes of goliath resilience joined with Firbolg wisdom.

The goliaths themselves speak of a time when Qhathos struck the Spine Mountains with his spear, splitting stone and spilling molten heartfire. From that fire rose the first goliaths, their skin etched with the scars of the mountain, their lungs filled with thin highland air.

>[!quote|mark] From _The Song of Crags_ <br> a goliath tale
> _"When Qhathos breathed on stone, it stood. When he breathed on us, we walked."_ 
---

### The Mark of Glory

For a goliath, life is a ledger of triumphs. Their bodies are canvases of achievement—tattoos, scars, and burns recording every victory, trial, and shame. No two goliath skins tell the same story, and their marks are read like books by other tribes.

- **Tattoos** – honor earned in feats of strength, survival, or leadership.
    
- **Scars** – remembrance of trials survived, worn with pride rather than shame.
    
- **Brands** – declarations of allegiance to tribe or oath.
    

Among themselves, these marks are introductions; a stranger’s body is their history made visible.

>[!quote|mark] Elven chronicler Ralluin Emberstar
>_"Ask not a goliath who they are. Read their scars."_

### Culture of the High Peaks

Goliath tribes are **nomadic**, roaming harsh terrains where few others dare to dwell—jagged ridges, icy passes, volcanic craters. Each tribe numbers no more than a few hundred, bound by shared glory and mutual survival.

Their culture revolves around **three pillars of Qhathos**:

- **Endurance** – to outlast storm, hunger, and pain.
    
- **Strength** – to wield might not for cruelty, but to overcome obstacles.
    
- **Honor** – to seek glory without betrayal or cowardice.
    

Children are raised communally, their worth proven not by birth but by deed. Feats of endurance—climbing a sheer cliff, fasting for days in snow, carrying burdens across miles—are rites of passage.

Goliaths value fairness fiercely. Games, trials, and contests of strength are sacred, and cheating is seen as an unforgivable dishonor.

### Relations with Other Peoples

To outsiders, goliaths often appear intimidating: towering, scarred, their gazes sharp as ridgelines. In cities, they are viewed with suspicion, their tattoos mistaken for criminal marks or their sheer size seen as a threat. Many goliaths hide their markings beneath cloaks when traveling through towns.

Yet those who earn a goliath’s trust discover them to be **loyal allies and impartial judges**. Their reputation for fairness makes them valued mercenaries, mediators, and guardians. Still, their nomadic ways mean they rarely linger long—staying too long in a city feels like suffocation.

### Conflict & Philosophy: The Goliaths and the Dwarves

From the moment dwarves first raised their banners in the **Kazak Barel**, claiming mountains as ancestral thrones, they came into conflict with the goliaths. To the dwarves, a peak was a seat of power—an inheritance of Eaos’ blessing. To the goliaths, the mountain was not a crown but a crucible: a place to test strength, courage, and the will to endure. To carve a mountain into a fortress and bar its slopes from others was, to goliath eyes, the gravest arrogance.

>[!quote|mark] Urdan of the Windscar, goliath elder
> _“No clan may own the sky above them, nor the stone beneath their feet. A mountain bows to no king.”_ 

#### Early Clashes

The first open conflicts were not wars but raids. Goliath tribes, traveling through high passes, would take shelter in peaks that dwarves had begun mining. Dwarves, seeing trespassers, drove them out. These skirmishes were later remembered as the **Stone-Blood Years**, a century of bitter feuds.

A particularly infamous clash was the **Battle of Nine Tunnels**, where the Emberhall Dwarves sealed an entire goliath tribe inside a mountain shaft, collapsing the exits. Only a handful survived, giving rise to the goliath oath: _“Better the storm than the stone’s cage.”_

#### Wars of the Spine

As both peoples grew, these grudges erupted into larger campaigns. The greatest was the **War of Splintered Peaks** (3rd Century Post-Cataclysm), when the goliath Stormpath tribes declared that no dwarf throne would stand on the highest ridges of the Spine. The war raged for decades—dwarves digging into mountains to defend them, goliaths driving warbands across open ridges. Neither side gained clear victory, but whole ranges were left scarred, ridges split by siege fire and avalanches.

During this war, the dwarves coined a slur still remembered: _“Ridge-thieves.”_ Goliaths countered with _“Throne-squatters.”_

#### Cultural Consequences

Despite the bloodshed, neither race truly sought extermination. Goliaths admired dwarves’ resilience, even as they loathed their hoarding. Dwarves, though bitter, respected goliath endurance, and often sang reluctant ballads of goliath champions who had died in battle.

Some clans even exchanged members in uneasy truces. A dwarven smith might wander with a goliath band, while a goliath warrior might swear service in a dwarven hall. These outliers were often mistrusted by their kin but became vital bridges between cultures.

#### Modern Tensions

Today, few outright wars are fought. But memories of the **Stone-Blood Years** linger. Goliaths still refuse to dwell in peaks held by dwarves, forced instead to roam ridges, passes, and borderlands. Dwarves still eye traveling goliath bands with suspicion, worried they may claim mines or sacred halls.

And yet, when orcs press into the Spine, or when dragons descend, it is not unusual to see goliath and dwarf fight back-to-back. The grudges between them are vast—but so too is the respect forged in war.

>[!quote|mark] Toren Skullbrand, goliath war-chief of the Ashfang
> _“We do not forgive them, and they do not forgive us. But when the mountain trembles, we both hold the same stone.”_

### Regional Variants

Generations wandering different landscapes have shaped goliaths into distinct groups, each carrying the marks of their chosen mountains:

- **Stonehearts** – dwellers of granite cliffs and volcanic ridges. Their skin is grey and mottled, like weathered stone. Known for unmatched endurance.
    
- **Frostveins** – tribes of the icy Spine and Vontrie Snow Plains. Pale-skinned, with veins traced blue beneath. They wield cold as kin.
    
- **Ashmanes** – volcanic wanderers with blackened skin and ember-hued eyes. Their tattoos are often burnt into their flesh with fire.
    
- **Skystriders** – nomads of the highest ridges, leaner and lighter than others. They leap and climb with uncanny agility, hair often bleached by sun and wind.
    
- **Ironbrands** – mountain-frontier goliaths who mingle most often with dwarves. Heavily scarred and branded, their strength is tempered by craft and pragmatism.
    

Though tribes may quarrel, all recognize kinship when they meet. Rivalry between variants is fierce, but rarely bloody.

### Closing

Goliaths embody the mountain: immovable, enduring, scarred by storm but never broken. To walk with them is to carry weight—of stone, of history, of honor. They are not conquerors, nor kings, but **wanderers who live for the moment Qhathos himself will notice their glory.**

>[!quote|mark] Varrun Stonescar <br>goliath champion
> _"When the last sun sets, may Qhathos ask of me not my name, but my deeds."_
