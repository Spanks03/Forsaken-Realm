## Origins and Divine Purpose

The **Genasi** are living expressions of the primal elements — not creations of whimsy, but of purpose. **Eaos**, the God of Logic, sought to define perfection in physical law. He gave numbers to fire, symmetry to stone, rhythm to the sea, and silence to the wind — and when those four harmonized, the Genasi were born.

Where the other gods sculpted races in their own likeness, Eaos gave the Genasi the gift of proof — living experiments of balance and structure, each body a theorem of motion, mass, and energy. They were the first beings to fully _understand_ their connection to nature, not merely revere it.

But when Terra fractured in the **Cataclysm**, even logic broke. The elemental planes bled into the mortal world, and the Genasi were torn from their unified purpose. They splintered — Fireborn scattered to volcanic basins, Earthborn buried under collapsing peaks, Airborn flung into endless storms, and Waterborn drowned in flooded valleys.

And from that splintering, diversity — and tragedy — began.

---

## The War of False Prophets

**The Shattering of Balance**

Centuries later, during the **War of False Prophets**, the Genasi were hunted like animals. Human and elven kingdoms, desperate for an endless source of power, discovered that the Genasi’s bodies resonated perfectly with **soul fiber** and the elemental plane, the mystical lattice that powered enchantment and divine magic alike.

Scholars of the **Order of the Algorithm** devised a way to _anchor_ a Genasi’s elemental resonance into machinery — birthing the **Arcane Conduit**, a device that fed off living essence.

What began as “voluntary partnerships” became industrial horror. Thousands were captured, drained, and wired into furnaces, pumps, forges, and flight engines. Cities glowed while the Genasi screamed in silence beneath them.

The war ended only when Sousiterum fell.

## The Fall of Sousiterum — _The Skyheart Cascade_

>[!quote|mark]  Excerpt from “Chronicles of the Ash Docks,” Vol. XII
> _"The sky tore like a wound. For three nights and two days, the stars did not return."_

The greatest catastrophe of the age, the **Skyheart Cascade**, began when an overcharged Airborn core in the Sousey's developmental sky city of Sousiterum’s Grand Engine reached resonance collapse.

Thousands of bound Genasi — mostly **Zephyri** and **Ignari** — were chained in the city’s skyforge towers, keeping Sousiterum’s defense grid afloat. On the third day of the Festival of Sparks, an engineer named **Kal Voress**, himself a Fireborn, reversed the polarity of the core to free his kin.

The explosion leveled half the city. The skies around Sousiterum rippled with an aurora of fire and smoke, and the souls of the bound were seen ascending in whirling lights as the city plummeted.

What remained of the towers fell into the sea — and the empire outlawed conduit technology forever.

Some say **Kal Voress’s** spirit still whispers in storms: _“We rise when the engines fall.”_

## Sub-Variants of the Genasi

Though united in essence, the Genasi evolved through exposure, captivity, and adaptation to Terra’s extremes. Their appearances, cultures, and even temperaments differ wildly across the Realms.

---

### Fireborn (Ignari)

**“The flame remembers who struck the match.”**

- **Appearance:** Skin in tones of ember, bronze, or blackened ash; veins pulse with orange or blue glow; eyes like molten ore. Hair flickers or smokes faintly.
    
- **Nature:** Passionate, impulsive, creative — fire tempered by will. They live as smiths, rebels, and dancers; rarely still, never quiet.
    
- **Sub-Variants:**
    
    - **Forgeborn Ignari** — Hammer-scarred artisans of Byhaven, whose blood can melt iron.
        
    - **Pyrewalkers** — Ash-cloaked ascetics who walk volcanic plains barefoot as prayer.
        
    - **Glassflare** — Crystalline-skinned survivors of conduit experiments, refracting inner fire like prisms.
        

**Region:** Found across the Todazur Flats, the Spine Mountains forges, and ruined cities where magma breaches the surface.

---

### Waterborn (Thalassi)

**“A river remembers every stone it passes.”**

- **Appearance:** Skin in shades of pearl, aquamarine, or obsidian tide; hair flows like kelp, eyes shine like the moon on water.
    
- **Nature:** Adaptive, empathetic, patient — serenity hiding storms. They thrive where trade and tide meet.
    
- **Sub-Variants:**
    
    - **Brineborn Thalassi** — Sea-hardened wanderers, their bodies exude salt and coral sheen.
        
    - **Tideweavers** — Ritualists who control tides through harmonic chant, dwelling near the Tecumheim Depths.
        
    - **Mist-veiled** — Gray and translucent-skinned; their breath becomes fog, and voices leave condensation pearls.
        

**Region:** Tecumheim Depths, the Beressomin Ocean archipelagos, and Eophoris’s inland deltas.

---

### Earthborn (Terral)

**“The mountain grows heavier with memory.”**

- **Appearance:** Stone-textured skin — granite, clay, or quartz; eyes gleam like minerals; hair coarse like moss or shale.
    
- **Nature:** Stoic, grounded, and unmovable in conviction. Terral Genasi are lorekeepers, masons, and guardians of sacred ruins.
    
- **Sub-Variants:**
    
    - **Stoneward** — Towering and broad, flesh like basalt; slow and nearly impervious.
        
    - **Gem-veined** — Skin shot through with luminous mineral veins; voices ring with crystalline tone.
        
    - **Loamspeakers** — Soft-bodied and earthen, capable of coaxing growth from stone.
        

**Region:** The Spine Mountains, Barrdwell Overhange, and Osse’s deep canyons.

---

### Airborn (Zephyri)

**“The sky breathes, and so do we.”**

- **Appearance:** Pale or sky-blue skin, hair like vapor or cirrus, pupils like lightning arcs. Their movement stirs the air; they are never truly still.
    
- **Nature:** Dreamers, explorers, messengers. Airborn are idealists whose laughter carries far but seldom lands.
    
- **Sub-Variants:**
    
    - **Stormbound** — Eyes glow with inner thunder, voice crackles when angered.
        
    - **Skyrunners** — Lightweight scouts and couriers, tattoos inked with flight paths.
        
    - **Zephyrglass** — Hollow-boned and song-voiced, their breath can shatter crystal or open locks.
        

**Region:** The floating ruins of the Skyforge Belt, high valleys of Sousiterum, and northern updraft isles.

## The Black Engine Trade

>[!quote|mark]  Arcanist Thellion’s Last Words
> _“One Genasi could power a city. <br> One city could end the world.”_

Even after the fall of Sousiterum, the practice of capturing and binding elemental beings did not die — it simply went underground.

### Engine Syndicates

Hidden beneath cities like **Underbridge** and **Korrath**, secret guilds known as the **Conduit Houses** maintain the black trade in living energy. They smuggle Genasi across continents, shackle them to new engines, and sell the extracted **Soul Conduits** — small crystalline shards capable of powering magical machinery indefinitely.

Some syndicates even **breed Genasi** in secret, combining elemental bloodlines to produce more volatile hybrids. These so-called **Fluxborn** rarely survive adulthood — but their deaths feed entire machines.

### The Underworld Arms Race

During the **Steam Reformation**, black-market conduit technology was rediscovered. Pirate nations now fit **Windcores** into skyships, while rogue artificers embed **Tide Hearts** into constructs to create living ships.

Among the most feared devices are **Echo Batteries** — portable cores made from condensed Genasi souls. They hum when a lie is told nearby.

## The Conduit Blueprint

**Artifact Class: Arcane Engine (Living Resonance Type)**

>[!quote|mark]  Eaos <br> in the Final Equation
> _“You cannot build balance from bondage.” _

### Design

A Conduit is a cage of runed mithril ribs and arcane crystal, forming a chamber roughly large enough to hold a humanoid. It binds a Genasi (or elemental) by sympathetic resonance — tethering their essence to the core’s runes, translating emotional energy into usable magic.

**Construction Components:**

- 12 Soul-etched Lattices (element-specific)
    
- 1 Runic Heartstone, keyed to the subject’s frequency
    
- 3 pints of quicksilver
    
- Blood (or ichor) of a matching elemental creature
    

**Activation:**  
Upon inscription of the subject’s true name (or divine mark), the Conduit seals and begins outputting **Arcane Joules** proportional to the subject’s vitality. Each minute of operation drains 1% of the bound creature’s life force.

### Traits & Effects

- **Stable Output:** Powers an entire district or war engine.
    
- **Soul Burn:** After 100 hours, the subject begins to lose sentience.
    
- **Resonance Leak:** Improper maintenance may create wild elemental storms.
    
- **Overload (1% chance/hour):** Results in catastrophic cascade, explosion radius 500 ft per Power Tier.
    

### Destruction or Liberation

Destroying a conduit without ritual precision releases chaotic energy — often vaporizing everything nearby.  
However, a **Harmonic Reversal**, performed with a _Crystal of Eaos_ or _Truth’s Sigil_, can redirect the flow and free the spirit intact.

## Legacy & Cultural Impact

Today, Genasi survivors are both revered and pitied. Many wear bands of dull metal around their wrists — the remnants of old conduits, now symbolic of their endurance. They distrust institutions of magic and industry, preferring lives of balance in remote sanctuaries.

In **Sousiterum**, a statue stands at the ruins of the Skyforge Belt:  
_A humanoid form, cracked and half-fused with stone and flame, one hand raised to the sky._  
Its plaque reads only:

> “We lit the world. And the world burned us for it.”
