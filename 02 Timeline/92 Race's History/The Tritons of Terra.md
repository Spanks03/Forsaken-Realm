## Origins and Divine Purpose

When Ogul and Nosduh bled divinity into Terra, they made not only the forests, skies, and mountains but also the seas. From those depths, the tritons were formed: guardians of the waters, heirs to the tides. Where the Harmonians were tasked with shaping the lands and Dragons with nurturing winds, tritons were charged with **safeguarding the oceans**, the hidden arteries of the world.

Nosduh gave them yearning—an eternal pull toward the horizon and mysteries beyond. Ogul gave them suffering and endurance—the strength to survive crushing depths and endless tides. Together, this duality made the tritons ambitious, resilient, and fiercely loyal.

In the early ages, triton emissaries walked openly on land. They taught coastal peoples the arts of navigation, tidal farming, and storm-calming rites. Some were worshipped as sea-gods, their voices carried on the wind like hymns. But after the Cataclysm boiled the oceans and split the Weave, the seas became scarred and treacherous. The tritons withdrew, building hidden cities in the trenches, lagoons, and drifting depths. To surface folk, they became myths—half-remembered saviors turned ghost-stories of the tide.

>[!quote|mark] High Tidecaller Vanyel <br> survivor of the Seventh Leviathan War
> _"The seas are older than stone, older than fire. The seas remember. And so do we."_

## Wars of the Deep

The tritons have waged wars few landwalkers even know occurred. Each conflict reshaped not only their society but their very bodies, driving the development of distinct triton lineages.

- **The Leviathan Wars (First through Seventh)**  
    Enormous sea-beasts, mutated by Void-bleed after the Cataclysm, rose from the Deep of Iropon and Beressomin Ocean. Cities on the backs of living leviathans were shattered, trenches became graveyards, and the tritons fought century after century to preserve the balance. Each war birthed new castes of warriors, new adaptations to pressure, poison, or storm.
    
- **The Battle of Oceri Trench**  
    When a great rift into the Void opened in the trench, horrors unlike any sea-creature emerged. The Abyssals sealed it with blood and song, weaving a shroud of silence over the wound. To this day, they say the rift is not gone—only drowned deep enough to keep the screaming out of mortal ears.
    
- **The Silent Raids**  
    During lean centuries, the Tidecallers turned raider. They struck at coastal towns and merchant ships along the Stambiens Sea, dragging treasures and captives beneath the waves. Though this period ended with the Pact of Tecumheim, sailors still whisper about pale-eyed tritons who appear in storms, demanding offerings of gold or blood.
    
- **The Pact of Tecumheim**  
    In the massive inland sea known as the Tecumheim Depths, tritons forged an alliance with colossal shellback leviathans—beasts the size of mountains that drift across the waters. Upon their armored shells, tritons built entire reef-cities, living fortresses that swam from port to port. To this day, Tecumheim’s tritons are nomads of the deep, their lives tied to the living cities they call home.

## Regional Variants

As Terra reshaped after the Cataclysm, so too did the tritons.

- **Reefborn Tritons**  
    Bright-skinned and coral-marked, these tritons dwell in the **Waters of Coliwe** and the coastal reefs of the **Stambiens Sea**. They are explorers, emissaries, and singers of the tide, with voices said to echo the call of whales. Their scales shimmer in hues of turquoise and gold, blending with coral. They are the most likely to appear in surface harbors, curious and talkative, though their loyalties run as deep as the ocean floor.
    
- **Abyssal Tritons**  
    Found in the **Oceri Trench** and the darkest reaches of the **Deep of Iropon**, Abyssals are secretive and otherworldly. Their skin glows faintly with bioluminescence, their eyes are pale lanterns, and their voices carry the weight of prophecy. They claim to “hear the ocean scream” when Void rifts stir. Land-dwellers see them as oracles; other tritons see them as reminders of the cost of survival. 
    
- **Tidecallers**  
    Masters of the **Beressomin Ocean**, these are the warborn. Broad-shouldered, with storm-grey skin and shark-bite scars, they embody the hunger of the open sea. Once raiders, now navigators, they bond with whales and sea-dragons as companions. Their pale storm-eyes are said to predict tempests, and many human captains carry Tidecaller charms for safe passage.![[Triton.png|left lp|700]]
    
- **Brinekin**  
    Hardy, green-and-brown mottled tritons who live in estuaries, swamps, and marshes. They are pragmatic and close to the land, often working alongside fisherfolk and swamp-dwellers. Their limbs are broad and webbed, their hair like drifting kelp. Though mocked as “mudfish” by other tritons, they are among the most adaptable, able to live comfortably both above and below the water.
    
- **Tecumheim Drifters**  
    Unique to the **Tecumheim Depths**, these tritons are wanderers who live atop shellback leviathans. Their culture is eclectic, borrowing from every port the living cities pass. They are skilled traders, lorekeepers, and illusionists, adorning themselves with driftwood charms and barnacle-etched armor. Their worldview is expansive, seeing land and sea alike as temporary stops on an eternal drift.
    
- **Exiles**  
    Cut off from the sea, either by choice or banishment, exiles lose their fins, their scales dull, and their eyes grow mortal. Neither landfolk nor seafolk fully accept them. They are wanderers who never belong, often drawn to mercenary bands or solitary pilgrimages. Their tragedy is that they are both too much and not enough—too tied to the sea to forget it, too landbound to return.

## Tritons and the Surface

Though much of their history is drowned beneath waves, tritons have never severed ties to the land entirely.

- **Eriwall** – Reefborn tritons were among the first to advise the Council of Eriwall, teaching them storm-magic and hydromancy. Some of Eriwall’s early colleges still keep waterlogged tomes written in triton runes.
    
- **Sousey** – Tidecallers once traded war techniques with Sousey generals, even providing harpoon-guns and whalebone armor. Later, their raiding strained this bond.
    
- **Janbridge** – Tecumheim Drifters send exotic goods to Janbridge’s markets: pearls the size of fists, salt-crystals from leviathan shells, and even bottled deep-sea light.
    
- **Outlaw Ports** – Brinekin often work as smugglers and ferrymen, guiding criminals or refugees through marsh channels where empire ships cannot follow.
    

Surface folk view tritons as **both saviors and raiders**. Farmers thank them for storm-calming rituals, while sailors curse them as pirates. Their mythic duality—half-god, half-menace—persists wherever waves strike the shore.

## Culture and Faith

The tritons still look to **Ogul and Nosduh** as sleeping gods. The closer moon (Ogul) is seen as the “tide that endures,” the farther moon (Nosduh) the “horizon that calls.” The Halo, ring of crystallized souls above Terra, is regarded with sorrow: to the tritons, it is not a celestial wonder but a prison of spirits that should have joined the sea.

Their society prizes loyalty above all. To betray a triton oath is to invite generations of vengeance. Yet to earn a triton’s trust is to gain a bond that does not break, even in death.

They do not measure wealth in gold, but in **names remembered**. To a triton, to forget a comrade is the greatest sin.

## Closing

The tritons are a paradox: withdrawn yet ever-present, alien yet familiar. They carry wars no surface has seen, build cities on the backs of monsters, and speak with gods of tide and silence. Yet they still walk among humans, dwarves, and elves—trading, teaching, sometimes raiding, always watching.

Where there is ocean, there is memory. And where there is memory, there are tritons.
