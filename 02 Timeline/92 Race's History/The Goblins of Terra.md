The goblins were brought into this world by **Vimnera, the goddess of revenge**. Quick, wiry, and born with an instinct for survival in the cruelest corners of the world, goblins are among the most numerous and underestimated of the so-called monstrous races. During the Battle of the Continents, they were herded with orcs, bugbears, and ogres to the war-torn land of Togruylia, where they were forced into the ranks of **The Horde**—a brutal faction designed to cull their numbers through endless warfare in the continent’s savage tournament games.

Despite being treated as expendable cannon fodder, goblins earned a reputation for **cunning sabotage, guerilla tactics, and improvisational ingenuity**. They became a linchpin of Horde strategy, often operating as infiltrators, trap-setters, or siege saboteurs. A single goblin left to prepare a battlefield could turn the tide of war.

Yet goblins have always been more than expendable soldiers. Outside the bloodpits and battlefields, they lived as families, farmers, and traders—quick-fingered survivors who carved hidden lives in the cracks left by larger powers. Their size and speed made them excellent couriers; their nimble hands built traps, yes, but also toys, tools, and clever machines.

>[!quote|mark] Harkk Willow-Ear, Goblin Farmer
> *“Not all of us sharpen knives in the dark. Some sharpen plows, some sharpen fiddles. But a sharp goblin is a goblin who lives.”*

## The Diaspora

After the **Assault of Kninad**, when a rogue Horde army invaded other continents, many goblins slipped free of Togruylia’s grasp. Scattered across Eophoris and Osse, they found freedom but also **relentless prejudice**. In many cities, goblins were subject to kill-on-sight orders, seen as living reminders of the monstrous hordes that had once brought ruin.

Some vanished into wild lands, founding hidden burrows or cliff-villages. Others slipped into human and dwarven settlements, carving niches as tinkers, brewers, ratcatchers, or entertainers. For some, **masks and illusions** became a tool of survival—disguises crafted through salvaged soul-fiber or bought from sympathetic enchanters. But this was never their birthright magic, only a desperate measure to keep a family alive. Unlike changelings, who shift at will, goblins hide only when the world forces them to.

To mistake all goblins for tricksters is to forget the hundreds who labor openly, raising families and keeping hearths while carrying the scars of exile.
  
>[!quote|mark] Goblin Saying
> *“Give us a home, and we’ll guard it like fire. Kick us out, and we’ll follow the smoke until it chokes you.”*

## Culture and Division

Centuries of oppression have left their mark. Goblins are bound by fierce **loyalty to kin and community**—to betray family is the one crime no goblin forgives. Yet this same history makes them deeply suspicious of strangers until bonds are proven.

This gives goblins a wide range of expression. Some rise as loyal friends, neighbors, or guardians. Others turn bitterness into cruelty, embracing sabotage and bloodshed. A goblin thief and a goblin healer may both carry the same ancestral memory of being cast aside, but each chooses differently what to do with that burden.

Among themselves, goblins tell stories in whispered fireside circles. They remember every wrong done to their people, but also every kindness. Their memories stretch back generations, and a goblin child may speak of vengeance and gratitude in the same breath.

## Regional Variants

Centuries of separation and survival in different lands have shaped goblins into distinct groups, each with their own physical traits and cultural quirks:![[Goblins.png|right lp|700]]

- **Horde Goblins** – Broad-shouldered, with longer arms and jutting jaws. Their skin ranges from sickly gray-green to deep swamp black, often scarred or tattooed with clan marks. Tusks are more pronounced, curving slightly outward. Horde goblins are taller than most of their kin, built for war, and carry themselves with an intimidating presence.
    
- **Burrowfolk** – Shorter and sturdier than most goblins, with earth-toned skin in shades of clay brown, moss green, or ashy gray. Their hair tends to be coarse and dark, often worn in practical braids. Burrowfolk eyes are wide and reflective, an adaptation to dim burrows and cliff-homes. They wear thick hides and earthen charms, always smelling faintly of smoke and soil.
    
- **City Shadows** – Slink-thin and wiry, with pale or sallow skin from generations in alleys and sewers. Their features are sharp, with angular noses and quick, darting eyes that rarely stop moving. Many file their teeth into points or wear piercings to appear more threatening in underworld dealings. Their clothing blends with shadows—patched cloaks, muted tones, and pockets full of tricks.
    
- **Tinker-Clans** – Slightly hunched from years bent over contraptions, with long, nimble fingers stained by soot and powder burns. Their skin varies from rust-red to olive, and many shave patches of their hair short to avoid fire accidents. Their eyes gleam unnaturally bright from constant exposure to sparks and flashpowder, and their ears are often pierced with bits of wire, gears, or gems.
    
- **Wilderkin** – Lean and feral, with mottled green or yellow skin patterned like leaves, bark, or reptile scales. Their hair is wild, often streaked with mud dyes or feathers. They paint their faces in bone white or blood red, and their teeth are sharp from gnawing raw meat. Wilderkin move with uncanny silence, their bodies light and quick, and they are often mistaken for woodland spirits or bog demons until it’s too late.

## The Mask-Makers

Of all goblin traditions, one of the strangest is that of the **Mask-Makers**. These artisans claim that goblins once wore no faces of their own, only the guises they chose to craft. In truth, their masks are woven with bits of illusion and soul-fiber, allowing goblins to hide among other races for weeks or months at a time. For some, the masks are protection. For others, they are art. For a few, they are weapons.

The changelings may own the gift of living transformation, but goblins understand the **power of pretending**.

---

Goblins, more than most, embody contradiction: vengeful yet loyal, brutal yet ingenious, feared yet overlooked. They are **survivors first, monsters only if forced to be**.
