_The Tide’s Forgotten Children_

Most believe sea elves are simply a watery cousin of their landborn kin—a minor elven subrace shaped by the sea. They are not. The truth is older, stranger, and far more unsettling. Sea elves are a people of **fractured memory and hidden origins**, born not only of elves and tritons, but of something deeper—something **ancient and patient beneath the waves**.

Sailors fear their name. Scholars dismiss their existence. Tritons name them **_Loreth Sélathar_—Those Who Drift Between._** To most, the sea elves are myth. Yet they are very real—and they are watching.

>[!quote|mark] Captain Lura Voss <br> lost at sea three days later
> _“If you meet one on land, it means they chose to be seen. And if they chose to be seen, tides help you—something is coming.”_  

---

### **Origins: A Song No One Remembers**

The sea elves, originally born from the union of Triton and Elf, do not tell their origin—they claim **they do not know it**. Their oldest tradition, called **The Deep Call**, teaches that memory itself is a tide. Some truths are allowed to surface. Others must be pulled under. They say they were _not born of the sea_—they were **taken by it**, reshaped in ways no mortal magic can achieve.

Some myths claim:

- They were **once elves** who made a pact with ancient **tide spirits**.
    
- Others whisper they were **made by Leviathans**, to serve as messengers between sea and sky.
    
- Tritons tell a darker story—that sea elves were **broken by something that dwells in the Abyssal Trenches**, forever changed by its **dreams**.
    

Sea elves do not refute any version. They simply answer:

> _“We belong to the water now. All else is memory drowned.”_

### **Appearance and Tideshape**

Sea elves are elegant and slender, but carry a faintly alien presence. Their features drift between beautiful and uncanny:

- Skin in shifting sea tones—**storm-gray, tide-blue, kelp-green, coral-pink**
    
- Iridescent **whorled markings** along their arms and neck
    
- Eyes glow like **bioluminescent tide pools**, eerily reflective
    
- Long aquatic hair like flowing kelp or sea-silk
    
- Subtle fin ridges and gill lines along the neck
    
- Webbed fingers and toes, but gracefully so
    

They move as though **their bodies remember water** even on land—smooth, drifting steps, as though resisting invisible currents.

---

### **Culture of the Drowned Memory**

Sea elves do not build kingdoms—they build **currents of influence**. They live in **hidden tide-circles** rather than cities, traveling between secret sanctuaries beneath the sea. Their society is guided by **The Tide Law**, a philosophy that shapes every generation:

|Tide Law|Meaning|
|---|---|
|**What is lost returns**|All things—memories, lives, civilizations—rise and fall like tides|
|**Trust in the undertow**|Do not fear unseen forces; they are part of the cycle|
|**Balance is freedom**|Aid neither tyrant nor conqueror—keep the world in motion|

Sea elves rarely interfere in surface affairs—**unless imbalance threatens the natural rhythm**. When kingdoms grow too large, when tyrants attempt naval dominion, when leviathans stir beneath—**sea elves return**, like a tide reclaiming a drowned shore.

---

### **The Controversial Rumor**

They deny it—but some across Terra whisper:

> **“Sea elves start the leviathan wars.”**

No proof exists—but whenever imperial navies rise, **leviathans awaken**. Entire fleets vanish. Survivors claim to have seen **white-eyed sea elves singing beneath the waves.**

Coincidence… or tide-wrought vengeance?

---

### **Faith – _The Deep Call_**

The sea elves do not worship gods. To them, **faith is a current**—it flows, pulls, returns. They honor:

- **The Moon-Tide Twins** – Nosduhmere and Ogulspire, as wanderers of the eternal sea
    
- **The Deep Ones** – ancient beings, unnamed, sleeping in ocean trenches
    
- **Memory of Water** – every droplet remembers the world before the Cataclysm
    

Their “prayers” are **tidal chants**, rhythmic breaths echoing like whale song. They do not ask their patrons for favor—they **offer witness**.

---

### **Where They Are Found**

- **Gaullinet Islands** – rarely seen, act as sea-watchers and tide oracles
    
- **Underbridge (submerged wards)** – rumored presence, never confirmed
    
- **Salvage Towns near Tecumheim Depths** – seen trading relics for silence
    
- **The Abyssal Trenches** – **never explored**, but cold singing is heard there
    
- **Nomadic currents** – they move more than migrate; they drift in hidden patterns

### **Variants of Sea Elves**

#### **Tideborn Sea Elves** _(Surface-Touched)_

Sleek swimmers and silent travelers, these sea elves linger closest to coastal civilization. Their skin is bright and alive—**coral reds, azure blues, sunlit sea-greens**—and their fins resemble those of dolphins or reef fish. Tideborn serve as **messengers, scouts, and watchers**, studying the land without being seen.

They are the ones most often mistaken for tritons—or called **myth** by sailors who survive encounters with them.

>[!quote|mark] Dockworker in Gallowmere
> _“They wore the ocean like a cloak. Even when they spoke, I swear I heard waves breaking behind their words.”_  

---

#### **Abyssal Sea Elves** _(Trenchborn)_

Rare, rarely spoken of, and **deeply feared**. Their skin is ghost-pale or ink-dark, patterned with glowing bioluminescent runes like deep-sea predators. Their eyes reflect no light. Their voices sound wrong—**hollow, distant, dream-soaked**.

Some call them cursed. Others believe they are **prophets of the unseen deep**. Abyssal sea elves are said to walk between waking and dreaming, bringing visions that are seldom blessings.

>[!quote|mark] Anonymous triton war-cleric
> _“Their gaze does not pierce flesh—it pierces future.”_
