The orcs were brought into this world by **Temp, God of Rage**. Where Nosduh’s children sought reason and Ogul’s children sought truth, Temp’s gift was more primal. He did not weave his people from logic or law, but from the raw surge of emotion that consumes and compels. Orcs were born in fire, in fury, and in grief that burned too deeply to be borne in silence.

>[!quote|mark] Fragment of the Codex Tempest <br> Orc scripture
>*“Rage is not madness. Rage is remembrance. Rage is the wound that refuses to close.”*  
---

### The Flame of Creation

From their first breath, orcs embodied their god’s nature. Their strength was unmatched, their voices carried like thunder, and their tusks grew sharp as if to remind the world of the cost of underestimating them. Temp placed within them not just wrath for war, but passion for kinship, fervor in song, and relentless drive in every struggle.

To other races, rage seemed a curse. To orcs, it was the fire of life itself.

---

### Rage as Philosophy

Unlike elves, who built laws, or dwarves, who built stone, orcs built a culture upon the **discipline of rage**. Anger, they taught, was not blind violence but sharpened will. To rage was to remember loss, to honor the fallen, to resist oppression, to demand dignity.

Young orcs learned from early age that fury untempered was waste, but fury directed was holy. Their shamans taught: _“The fire that devours your kin is folly. The fire that forges them is glory.”_

---

### Early Ages

In the early post-Cataclysm age, orcs surged across Terra. They were feared mercenaries, dreaded raiders, but also respected as defenders of frontier settlements. They built their own tribal strongholds in Osse’s plains and Eophoris’ coasts, carving a place by sheer force of will.

But when the **Battle of the Continents** came, orcs found themselves branded monstrous. Their fury, once honored, was painted as savage chaos. And so they, with goblinoids and serpentkin, were exiled to the forsaken continent of **Togruylia**.

---

### Exile and the Rise of the Horde
![[Orc Hord.png|right lp|350]]
Togruylia was no gift. It was a cage. A continent of hostile storms, jagged volcanoes, and monstrous beasts. Into this crucible orcs were thrown. Many believed they would perish. Instead, they adapted.

Orcs became the spine of **The Horde**, one of the three factions forced into endless bloodsport. Within a generation, the orcs who survived Togruylia grew larger, stronger, their tusks lengthening and their muscles hardening. They became **a foot taller and broader** than their cousins outside, their very bodies reshaped by war and survival.

The Tournament, meant to shame and cull them, became their proving ground. They sang of victories, tattooed their skin with trophies of slain foes, and raised their children to see battle not as punishment, but as purpose.

>[!quote|mark] Ugrah Bloodfang <br> Horde Champion
> *“We are not chained by the Arena. The Arena is chained to us. Every year, we prove that rage endures.”* 
---

### Variants of the Horde
![[Hord Orcs.png|left lp|500]]
Across Togruylia’s brutal landscape, orcs diversified into regional clans:

- **Ashfang Orcs (volcanic plains):** Charcoal-black skin marbled with ember-like scars. Tusks cracked and jagged, resembling cooled obsidian. Their bodies radiate heat from years of living in ash-choked lands.
    
- **Frostmaw Orcs (northern cliffs):** Pale blue-grey skin, tusks sweeping upward like walruses, hair white as snow. Massive frames, draped in furs, said to drink the blood of frost-wolves for strength.
    
- **Junglefang Orcs (southern jungles):** Emerald skin with streaks of darker camo, tusks filed into points, bodies painted with beast-blood tattoos. They hunt as much with silence as with fury.
    
- **Ironhide Orcs (Arena-forged):** Scarred veterans of the Tournament. Missing limbs replaced by crude iron grafts, every scar a mark of honor. Many never live past thirty, but their names echo for centuries.
    

---

### Faith of the Orcs

Temp remained their central god. Rage itself was prayer; the battlefield, their temple. They offered blood to the earth, roars to the sky.

But even among them faith fractured. Some whispered prayers to **Datarr**, god of suffering, seeing kinship in his silent endurance. Others sought **Yearn**, goddess of desire, believing ambition drove their survival. Some even laughed to **Prosper**, calling on luck before battle. Yet all orcs returned to Temp in their dying breath, roaring his name as they fell.

---

### Diaspora Orcs
![[Orcs.png|right lp|500]]
Not all orcs remained caged in Togruylia. Some escaped during the **Assault of Kninad**, when rogue armies broke free and stormed Eophoris and Osse. Though repelled, many scattered across the realms, never returning.

These **diaspora orcs** are smaller than their Horde-born kin, closer to the orcs of old—green-skinned, tusked, broad but approachable. They settled in warbands, mercenary companies, or hidden enclaves. Many filed down their tusks or gilded them, striving to live among humans and elves.

But persecution followed. Cities branded them monsters. Even when they proved themselves loyal, the shadow of Togruylia tainted them. Some embraced it. Others lived in shame.

---

### Diaspora Variants

- **Steppe Orcs (Osse plains):** Tawny-green skin, lighter builds, nomadic riders of horse and aurochs. Known for swift raids and deep loyalty to their bands.
    
- **River Orcs (Eophoris deltas):** Slim, lighter green skin, adept with boats and fishing. Traders, smugglers, and ferrymen—equally comfortable with spear or net.
    
- **City Orcs (integrated):** Tusks filed, polished, or capped with gold. Found as blacksmiths, gladiators, or guards. Many bear tattoos marking both shame and pride.
    
- **Exile Orcs (Underdark):** Grey-skinned, tusks broken as punishment, surviving in caverns. Distrusted even by other orcs, they cling to shadow and secrecy.
    

---

### The Assault of Kninad

The greatest orc incursion into the wider world came during the **Assault of Kninad**, when a rogue warhost of Horde orcs and their monstrous allies surged out of Togruylia. For months, cities burned. Humans whispered of the “Green Tide” that would swallow civilization.

Though repelled, the invasion scattered thousands of orcs across Eophoris and Osse. To some, this was tragedy. To others, it was liberation. From Kninad came mercenary legacies, hidden clans, and orcs who swore never to bow to Tournament chains again.

>[!quote|mark] Grak One-Eye <br> Survivor of Kninad
> “They call it invasion. We call it freedom. They call it defeat. We call it escape.”
---

### Culture and Code

At the heart of orc life is **kin and clan**. Every orc belongs to a band bound by blood and oath. Leadership is earned in battle, but tempered by loyalty. An orc chief who hoards glory without sharing it will be challenged and slain.

Rites of passage define their lives:

- **Tusking:** The filing, carving, or capping of tusks at adulthood, symbolizing one’s chosen path.
    
- **The First Rage:** A trial where young orcs must fight in a frenzy against beast or foe, proving they can harness Temp’s fire.
    
- **Blood Feast:** A funeral rite where kin consume a portion of the fallen’s blood, believing it binds memory and strength into the clan forever.
    

---

### Reputation

To outsiders, orcs are beasts. To elves, they are chaos embodied. To dwarves, blunt instruments of destruction. To humans, an ever-present threat.

But within orc culture, they are more than warriors. They are poets of rage, keepers of song, tellers of tales carved into bone and tusk. In their own words: _“We are not monsters. We are the roar the world tries to silence.”_

---

### Modern Orc Identity

Today, orcs live divided. **Horde-born orcs** in Togruylia embrace their role as champions of rage, proud of every Tournament victory. **Diaspora orcs** struggle to belong, torn between heritage and survival.

But wherever they are found, orcs are marked by the same unyielding truth: they will not be erased. Rage endures. Kin endures. The fire burns on.
