_The Blades Between Heartbeats • The Quiet Mandibles • The Hive’s Cold Mercy_

> [!quote|mark] Whisper overheard in the Underbridge  
> _“You never see a Zelus. You only feel the part of you they decided you didn’t need.”_

If the Gregaria are the endless march, the Zelus are the **pause between breaths**, the silence before a scream, the shadow that moves the instant you think you imagined it. The Zelus caste is born sharp—lean, angular, predatory. They resemble mantises or assassin-wasps in humanoid form, their limbs jointed for precise, elegant violence. Where the Gregaria overwhelm with numbers, the Zelus control the **fate of single targets**: the commander, the caster, the one whose death unravels an army.

Outsiders mistakenly call them assassins. Assassins kill for coin or command. Zelus kill because the Hive needs something removed, and thus it must be removed. There is no cruelty in them, and no kindness. There is only the work.

### _The Silent Venom of the Hive_

> _“You never hear the one that gets you. You hear the others… the ones feeding.”_  
> —Sergeant Kembren of the Osse Vanguard, last report before disappearance

Among all the brood-lines of Togruylia, none inspire such visceral dread as the **Zelus**, the assassin-strain of the Insectoid race. Where the Gregaria embody the unstoppable surge of the swarm and the Anisoptera the cold strategy of command, the Zelus represent something far older and far more terrifying:  ![[Zelus.webp|right lp|500]]
**evolution sharpened into cruelty without malice**.

They do not hate. They do not revel. They simply _do_ what nature sculpted them to do—  
and nature, in this case, sculpted a nightmare.

## **Biology of a Living Weapon**

Zelus stand low and wide, built like predatory traps—compact power at rest, explosive violence in motion. Their carapace darkens to hues that drink the light, and their elongated jaws flex with unsettling intelligence.

From their upper back, twelve rigid spines sit like a folded crown. These are the **death-quills**, grown in clusters during rest cycles. When pulled free, the quills stiffen further, becoming aerodynamic, wickedly barbed projectiles. Each one is coated, naturally and constantly, in the Zelus’ most feared asset:

### **The Neurotoxin of Lost Voices**

The Zelus excrete a transparent, oily venom from small pores around their mandibles. It is not a poison in the traditional sense—no searing pain, no blackening flesh. Instead, it slips between thought and muscle, **silencing the connection**, turning living bodies into locked prisons of their own trapped minds.

Victims often remain conscious as their limbs fail them. Some report hearing their own breathing grow shallow while their bodies refuse to respond. Most survivors—precious few—never shake the tremor the toxin leaves behind.

To the Hive, the neurotoxin is not a weapon of cruelty.  
It is simply the fluid that enables their reproductive cycle.

---

## **The Chamber Within**

Nothing about the Zelus is more horrifying—or more sacred to the Hive—than their method of reproduction. Their entire lifespan, barely a month long, revolves around one inevitable purpose: **finding a living host**.

When a Zelus strikes, it punctures with precision. The neurotoxin collapses the victim’s body, forcing their consciousness inward while keeping the flesh alive. Then comes the injection: an egg sheathed in thick, nutrient-rich fluid that numbs the host’s interior and prevents early death.

For weeks the host continues to breathe, sometimes even to think, trapped in a paralyzed body that slowly becomes a **birthing chamber**. Inside, the larva feeds, gestates, and finally emerges—whether from the abdomen, throat, or any other convenient exit.

The size of the host determines the brood.  
Small creatures birth only the young Zelus.  
Larger ones give rise not only to a single offspring, but to a **storm of hungry Nemotocera**—tiny gnat-like horrors that swarm protectively around the newborn.

In the Hive, this process is regarded with reverence.  
Outside the Hive, it is whispered of as one of Togruylia’s great terrors.

## **The Role of the Zelus in the Hive**

Where the Gregaria are the sword and the Anisoptera the mind, the Zelus are the **shadow**.  
Hives deploy them sparingly, for each Zelus is precious—a spark with only a month to burn. They are often sent ahead of a migrating swarm, slipping through trenches, tunnels, and the high grasses of the tournament grounds. A single Zelus infiltrator can depopulate an entire encampment long before the main force arrives.

Commanders prize them not for brute strength but for reliability. A Zelus does not break formation. A Zelus does not panic. A Zelus does not stop until its biological imperative is complete.

Some cultures outside Togruylia refuse to speak their name, using instead phrases like:

**“The Stillness.”**  
**“The Black Mandibles.”**  
**“The Egg That Walks.”**

To the Insectoids themselves, they are simply:

**“The Silent.”**

## **Zelus Culture (Such As It Exists)**

The Zelus do not sing battle cries like the Gregaria, nor do they strategize like the Anisoptera. Their communication is sparse—subtle clicks of mandibles, brief pheromone bursts, slight shifts of antennae.

But they do possess something akin to ritual.

Before implanting a host, a Zelus often taps its mandibles three times against its victim’s skin. Scholars once believed this a taunt or a ritual of dominance. But studies of captured specimens revealed something else:

It is **a farewell**—  
a final instinctive gesture acknowledging that this host’s life is ending,  
and another is beginning in its place. 

There is no malice in the gesture.  
Only necessity.

## **Legends and Warnings**

> _“If you hear no buzzing, run. You’re already too close.”_  
> —Old Hypnotic Wilds proverb

Stories about Zelus infiltrators are common in the borderlands of Togruylia. Entire villages emptied in a night. Travelers found frozen in place, eyes wide, bodies unmoved for days. Caravan guards who suddenly turn on their own as the Nemotocera burst from their armor seams.

Even hardened tournament champions refuse to face them without fire at their backs. In Togruylia, the only true counter to a Zelus is **obliteration of the entire site**—burning bodies, camps, and earth until nothing remains.

## **To Face a Zelus**

To face a Gregaria is to face a warrior.  
To face an Anisoptera is to face a tactician.  
To face a Zelus is to face **a biological certainty**.

You are not hunted.  
You are **chosen**.

And once you are chosen,  
there is no ceremony, no warning, no mercy—  
only the cold precision of a creature fulfilling its final purpose.
