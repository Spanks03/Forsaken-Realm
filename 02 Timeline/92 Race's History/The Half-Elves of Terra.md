>[!quote|mark] _Alessar Veyndral_ <br> half-elven poet of Osse
> *“We are not halves. We are not fragments. We are what happens when the world refuses to stay divided.”* 

Half-elves are living contradictions and living bridges. Neither bound by the timeless patience of elves nor the restless urgency of humans, they occupy a liminal place between cultures, a people of in-betweens. Yet they are not half of anything—they are wholly themselves, shaped by the bloodlines, cultures, and choices that made them.

---

## History of the Half-Elves

The first half-elves emerged in the wake of the **Cataclysm**, when humans and elves were forced together in shattered refugee camps. Many of these early unions were pragmatic—born of survival, grief, or desperation—but they produced children who were neither one nor the other.

At first, these children were curiosities. Among elves, they were mourned as fragile things destined to fade too quickly. Among humans, they were mistrusted as too long-lived, carrying secrets that might outlast kings. But history proved otherwise.

- During the **Wars of the Dwarven Kings**, half-elven emissaries helped negotiate ceasefires between human mercenary companies and elven auxiliaries to leave dwarven conflict into a internal affair. Their ability to read both sides saved thousands.
    
- In the **Assault of Kninad**, half-elves served as scouts and diplomats, mediating between scattered warbands of orcs, humans, and elves who had little reason to trust each other.
    
- In Osse, one of the **Seven High Magisters**, _Serenya Dawnwater_, was a half-elf whose rulings on arcane law remain foundational.
    

Still, prejudice persisted. The Aelari Court dismissed them as “unfinished.” Human rulers whispered of their disloyalty. But generation after generation, half-elves outlasted such disdain by sheer presence—they survived, they adapted, and they learned.

## Culture of the In-Between

Half-elves do not have kingdoms of their own. Instead, they weave themselves into the societies around them—interpreters of culture, translators of language, negotiators of identity. In markets, they are traders fluent in half a dozen tongues. In cities, they are go-betweens for races that distrust each other. On the road, they are adventurers, always searching for a place that feels like home.

Their spirituality is equally fluid. Some follow **Qhathos**, god of glory, embracing the human drive for ambition. Others revere elven deities tied to memory, craft, or the weave. Many, however, are drawn to **Yearn, goddess of desire**, who represents transformation, change, and becoming something new.

Half-elves also create their own myths. Some say they were secretly blessed by the Elder Gods as proof that unity is possible. Others claim their kind were “woven into existence” by the Weave itself, where human flame and elven thread tangled and refused to separate.

## Regional & Heritage Variants

Because “half-elf” is not one race but many, their features vary depending on their lineage.

- **Human + Elf (_Aelanthir_)** — The most common. The Aelanthir, Elvish-rooted word meaning _the joined blood_, are long-lived (150–200 years), graceful, and adaptable, with sharper features than humans and more warmth than elves.
    
- **Dwarf + Elf (_Stoneborn_)** — Shorter and sturdier, often with stone-grey or copper tones. Their eyes seem to hold patience, their builds carry dwarven endurance with elven grace. Many are rune-carvers and stone-singers.
    
- **Orc + Elf (_Bladeshades_)** — Tall, martial figures with elongated ears, tusks smaller than orcs but sharper than humans, and a natural aura of menace or command. Respected in Eophoris as war-leaders, feared elsewhere as reminders of conquest.
    
- **Halfling + Elf (_Spryfolk_)** — Small, quick, with fey-touched laughter and bright, mischievous eyes. Many lean toward trickery, illusion, or travel.
    
- **Gnome + Elf (_Whispergnomes_)** — Curious and eccentric, with elven elegance mixed with gnomish creativity. Tinkerers, inventors, or illusionists who thrive on discovery.
    
- **Exotic Crossings** — Rare unions between elves and tritons, yuan-ti, or even goblins create striking individuals with features entirely their own. Such half-elves face harsher prejudice but embody the purest form of the “bridge” identity.

## How the World Sees Them

Half-elves exist everywhere—and so do opinions of them.

- **In Elven Courts** — admired for their potential, pitied for their fleeting lives. Many elves see them as “unfinished” or “echoes of eternity.”
    
- **In Human Kingdoms** — respected for their beauty and longevity, feared for their supposed “otherness.” Whispers often accuse them of hidden loyalties.
    
- **In Togruylia** — Rarely seen, rarely respected, and rarely trusted, seen as the children of war.
    
- **In Osse** — half-elves have carved out respected roles in trade, magic, and diplomacy, often rising higher than full-blooded rivals.
    

Despite prejudice, most half-elves use perception to their advantage. They are survivors, diplomats, and cultural chameleons—able to lean into whichever half the moment requires.
