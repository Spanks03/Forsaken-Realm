### **Origin & Divine Creation**

When **Truth** first shaped the world, it is said he sought not a race of warriors or scholars, but of **listeners** — beings who would _remember_ when others forgot. From clay, river silt, and the last breath of an ancient mammoth, he sculpted the **Loxodon**, breathing into them patience and memory. Where elves learned the song of time and men learned the art of ambition, the Loxodon learned _how to bear truth without breaking_.

They were the first to carry the _Echo Words_ — the divine syllables that could repeat a god’s speech without error. For this reason, they became Truth’s living libraries, the keepers of the original language. When the **Cataclysm** fractured Terra, many Loxodon were driven to madness by the loss of divine voices, their sacred memories echoing back upon themselves like thunder in a canyon.

To this day, some say when a Loxodon trumpets across the plains, it is not a call of greeting — but a lament for a god they once could hear.

### **History in Terra**

The Loxodon’s history is one of endurance and silence. Once dwelling across fertile plains and riverlands, they were driven to the **Todazur Flats** and **Alesund Valley** during the _Wars of Ivory_, when humans and dwarves alike hunted them for tusks said to hold fragments of divine resonance.

Each tusk was believed to hum faintly when carved — a superstition that led to centuries of slaughter. Loxodon herds dwindled, retreating into isolation where they turned their grief into philosophy. They began to inscribe their memories into stone, using **earthen grooves and sand-calligraphy** as a way to preserve knowledge without sound.

Where once they roamed open plains, they now travel in **circles of ten to twelve**, called **Herds of Memory**, led by a **Rememberer** — a Loxodon who carries centuries of oral record within their mind.

### **Culture & Beliefs**

To the Loxodon, every act — from eating to speaking — must be performed with intention. They believe falsehood stains the soul and causes _Truth’s light_ to dim within the heart. Many refuse to speak casually, preferring **silence as reverence**, communicating through touch, gesture, and slow, deliberate song.

Loxodon temples are _not_ built of marble or gold but carved from mud and clay, left to crumble naturally so that time can consume false permanence. They teach that “Truth cannot be caged — it must erode.”

Their art takes the form of **sound sculptures**: great hollow stones and wind-formed horns that sing when storms pass through. The Alesund Valley, home to the **Halls of Echoing Sand**, is said to contain the voices of thousands of Loxodon layered into the wind — each tone a memory of the world before the Cataclysm.

---

### **Relations with Other Races**

The Loxodon’s immense size and solemnity often make them seem slow or simple to outsiders. Yet those who share company with them quickly learn that patience is their weapon. A Loxodon’s silence in argument is not surrender — it is _judgment_.

Though they hold no bitterness toward their hunters, they remember. Loxodon never forget a name, a face, or a broken promise. Their forgiveness is not mercy — it is a warning.

In Eophoris and Osse, Loxodon wanderers are sought after as judges, arbiters, and sages. In **Togruylia**, they are viewed as omens — when a herd appears on the horizon, it is said war or revelation follows.

---

### **Rituals & Philosophy**

- **The Rite of Dust:** When a Loxodon dies, their body is covered in river clay, and their herd walks in circles around them for seven nights, whispering memories into the wind until their form dissolves.
    
- **The Listening Hour:** Each dawn, Loxodon sit in complete silence until the first sound of nature breaks it — believing that in that silence, Truth speaks most clearly.
    
- **Ivory Binding:** Some Loxodon carve sigils into their tusks to bind memories they do not wish to recall. When a tusk breaks, those memories flood back, sometimes driving them to spiritual awakening — or madness.

### **Loxodon Variants of Terra**

#### **The Plainsherds (Todazur Loxodon)**

Golden-gray skinned, with tusks that curl inward like crescents. They wear dust-stained wraps and sunstones bound to their foreheads. Their wisdom is earthy and practical; they serve as caravan guides and diplomats between desert tribes.

> _“The horizon is a promise, not a distance.”_

#### **The Riverborn (Alesund Loxodon)**

Ashen-blue skin with faint, swirling markings like river currents. They are excellent swimmers and architects, shaping entire settlements from mud and flowing water. Known for gentle humor and patient craft, they build the “singing bridges” that hum when rain falls.

> _“Truth moves as water does — unbothered by stone.”_

#### **The Stonecallers (Highland Loxodon)**

Dark, slate-colored hide; their tusks often chipped and rough. Living high in the mountain valleys, they are mystics who shape resonant chambers in cliffs, turning the wind itself into memory. Their deep voices can be heard echoing for miles.

> _“If you listen long enough, stone will confess.”_

#### **The Emberveil (Ashlands Loxodon)**

Reddish-brown hide speckled with black soot, descended from those who walked through volcanic plains after the Cataclysm. Their tusks glow faintly when heated by sunlight, and their hides can withstand immense heat. They serve as smith-philosophers, forging relics said to “remember their creator’s hands.”

> _“Every scar is a word the fire would not burn away.”_

#### **The Ivoryless (Broken Herds)**

Rare and pitied, these Loxodon were born without tusks — an omen of silence and loss. Many become hermits, but others travel Terra seeking to _find their voice_ through art, music, or meditation. Their sensitivity to divine resonance is unmatched.

> _“Even without tusks, we still carry the song.”_

### **Modern Role in Terra**

Few Loxodon remain, and fewer still trust the world enough to settle. Yet their herds occasionally appear near ancient ruins or during celestial alignments, as if drawn by echoes of divine truth.

To meet one is to be measured — not by strength or wit, but by the weight of your honesty.  
Those who earn their respect may receive the **Mark of Resonance**: a small shard of ivory engraved with the sigil for “truth kept.”

It is said those who carry such a mark never lose their way — unless they lie.
