Half-Orcs are born of the meeting of strength and survival. They are the children of Orcish fire and another bloodline—human, elf, dwarf, or otherwise. Where others see contradiction, Half-Orcs embody balance: they carry the fury of Temp, god of rage, but tempered by whatever other lineage they inherit. In them is written the story of bridges—bridges between peoples, between survival and belonging, between the scorn of others and the resilience to endure.

Unlike many hybrid races who found homes in liminal spaces, Half-Orcs are rarely welcomed anywhere. Orcs in Togruylia see them as betrayers. Humans in Eophoris see them as monsters. Dwarves call them tainted. Elves treat them as mistakes. And yet, despite the coldness of the world, Half-Orcs endure, building their own stories in hidden corners, mercenary companies, outlaw camps, frontier towns, and broken halls.

>[!quote|mark] attributed to Dahrvak Stonejaw <br> Half-Orc gladiator
> _"We are not halves. We are doubles. Twice the strength, twice the scars, twice the will to live."_

## Origins and Early History

The first Half-Orcs were not born of love. They were born of raids, conquest, and violence during the earliest migrations of Orc warbands into human and dwarven lands. Many were abandoned or killed, but some survived in secret, hidden by mothers who defied shame or fathers who refused to let blood be wasted. These survivors grew into a people defined not by divine creation, but by defiance of rejection.

When the **Battle of the Continents** ended and Orcs were driven into Togruylia, Half-Orcs became symbols of betrayal among the Horde. To Orcs hardened by war, a Half-Orc meant that a clan had either been captured, defiled, or had willingly mingled with outsiders. In Togruylia, such offspring were almost always slain at birth, their existence viewed as dishonor worse than death.

This cruelty forged a brutal truth: Half-Orcs in Togruylia almost never survive unless they flee. The few who escape grow into wanderers, hardened and scarred, often hunted by both Orcs and men.

In the diaspora, however, things unfolded differently. Half-Orcs born in human, elven, or dwarven lands—while still stigmatized—found ways to survive. Some became mercenaries, others smiths or laborers, and still others priests and poets. Over time, Half-Orcs proved that they were more than scars of war. They became bridges in a world that preferred walls.

## Culture and Identity

Half-Orcs carry the weight of **dual heritage**. In some, the Orc side dominates: broad frames, tusks, deep voices, and raw strength. In others, their secondary lineage softens them, giving them finer features, clever wit, or unexpected grace. Many live their entire lives torn between identities—never fully belonging to either side.

But this tension creates resilience. Half-Orcs build their own cultures from fragments: war chants inherited from Orc clans, lullabies sung by human mothers, runes whispered by dwarves, or prayers learned from elven priests.

### Common Threads in Half-Orc Life

- **Names**: Some claim Orcish names to embrace strength. Others take human or elven names to avoid suspicion. Some blend both, creating unique traditions.
    
- **Scars**: Many Half-Orcs scar or tattoo themselves deliberately, reframing marks of shame into marks of pride.
    
- **Beliefs**: While Orcs worship Temp, many Half-Orcs turn instead to Prosper (god of community) or Yearn (goddess of desire). For them, rage is not destiny but a choice.
    
- **Kinship**: Half-Orcs gravitate toward each other. In large cities like Eriwall or Janbridge, small enclaves of Half-Orc families exist, where children grow up without the crushing weight of isolation.
    

_"Every scar I carry is proof that I survived a world that did not want me alive. And every laugh I carry is proof that I did not let it win."_  
— Mirael the Red, Half-Orc tavernkeeper of Janbridge

## Regional Variants

Though united by mixed blood, Half-Orcs vary wildly depending on their second lineage and region.

- **Eophoran Half-Orcs** – Green- or gray-skinned, stocky, and muscular. Commonly children of Orcs and humans, these are the archetypal Half-Orcs found in frontier villages and mercenary bands. Many bear tusks too large to hide.
    
- **Ossean Half-Orcs** – Often slimmer, with darker skin tones influenced by Osse’s coastal peoples. Tusks are smaller, more like pronounced canines. Many excel as sailors, corsairs, and freedom fighters.
    
- **Half-Orc + Elf (Kaerathir)** – Tall and striking, with angular features and sharp eyes. Tusks are delicate, sometimes mistaken for unusual teeth. They are rare and often hunted as abominations by elves, yet some survive as spies or diplomats.
    
- **Half-Orc + Dwarf (Grumbakh)** – Shorter but broad as stone walls, with tusks jutting like chisels. Their frames are compact but immensely strong, and many find homes as smiths, miners, or pit fighters.
    
- **Togruylian Half-Orcs** – Scarce, because most are killed at birth. Those who survive carry grotesque marks: oversized tusks, brutal jawlines, and scars from exile. These are the most feared of Half-Orcs, hardened by rejection from both sides.
    

>[!quote|mark] anonymous Horde soldier’s journal
> _"If you see a Half-Orc in Togruylia, know this: they have already survived what would have killed you a dozen times."_

## Reputation and Treatment

Half-Orcs live under the shadow of prejudice.

- **In Togruylia**: Orcs kill them, humans fear them, and other monstrous races distrust them. Their existence is taboo.
    
- **In Eophoris**: They are tolerated in cities but rarely trusted. Many become mercenaries, blacksmiths, or guards.
    
- **In Osse**: Seen as outsiders, but their strength is valued on ships and in freedom-fighting companies.
    
- **In the Empire**: Propaganda brands Half-Orcs as mongrels of rebellion and treachery, leading to purges and executions.
    

Despite this, Half-Orcs thrive in niches where their strength, cunning, and adaptability cannot be denied. Some of the greatest gladiators, generals, and poets of Terra carry Orcish tusks and human smiles.

## The Path Forward

Half-Orcs are children of contradiction, yet they are also children of promise. Where Orcs embrace rage and humans chase glory, Half-Orcs carry both—rage tempered by reason, glory tempered by survival. They are symbols of resilience in a world that would rather forget them.

In every scar, they carry memory. In every victory, they carry proof. And in every generation, Half-Orcs remind the world that even the most unwanted roots can grow into unbreakable trees.
