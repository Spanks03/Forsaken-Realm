_“We are not the roar nor the hunt — we are the quiet between.”_

The Felis are the smallest and most elusive of Terra’s feline peoples — born, as some legends whisper, from the **curiosity of Truth** and the **warmth of Prosper**. When civilization first lit its hearth-fires and the nights began to thin, something crept close to the flame. Drawn by warmth, by story, by comfort, that something — neither beast nor spirit — became the first Felis: clever, kind, and curious beyond measure.

Where Leonin rule with pride, Rakasta stalk with reverence, and Tabaxi wander with wonder, the Felis dwell in the in-between — in shadowed rafters, under tavern tables, and behind temple altars. They are the **small gods of the hearth**, the quiet guardians of those who dream beside the firelight.

---

### **History and Origin**

The Felis trace their beginnings to the earliest cities of men, when the magic of the gods still bled freely through mortal life. At first, they were thought to be simple familiars — animal companions to hedge-witches, seers, and priests of Prosper. But over the centuries, the lines blurred: familiars grew wise, and Felis learned to speak, until one could scarcely tell which had come from the other.

Their earliest songs — low, rhythmic hums said to echo the pulse of the world — were prayers not to be heard but _felt_. They called themselves the **Kindred of the Quiet Flame**, claiming their duty was not to command or conquer, but to observe, remember, and protect.

Some ancient texts even suggest that the Felis were _never created at all_, but rather _chosen_: mortals and beasts who loved the fire so deeply that the flame loved them back.

---

### **Culture and Nature**

Felis are small, rarely taller than a halfling, with sleek fur and luminous eyes that seem to catch every glimmer of light. Most move upright, but with the ease of slipping to all fours when they run or fight. Their voices carry a melodic hum — purrs threaded with vowels — and they often accent their words with flicks of the tail or ears, as if their bodies still speak languages older than words.

Unlike their proud or warlike cousins, Felis societies have no kings or hierarchies. Instead, they live by a **web of favors, debts, and memory**. A kindness is currency; a promise is sacred. Many travel from home to home, trading stories, spells, or even good fortune for a meal and a night’s rest.

Their philosophy is simple:

> “To warm a hearth is to light the world.”

They favor **Prosper**: the God of Luck or Hestinia: the Keeper of Hearth, above all gods. Every Felis home — even a tent or tavern corner — bears a **Hearthmark**, a small charm or ember that symbolizes Hestinia’s blessing. To extinguish another’s Hearthmark is considered an unforgivable insult.

---

### **Disguises and Misunderstandings**

Perhaps the Felis’ greatest peculiarity is their ability to **mask themselves as ordinary cats**. Through a subtle glamour — part magic, part instinct — they can shift their form into that of a mundane feline. In this guise, they live among mortals unnoticed, studying, protecting, or simply observing the rhythms of daily life.

Many tavern cats, shipboard mousers, and library strays are not what they seem. Felis disguised as house cats listen to gossip, collect secrets, and guide travelers toward — or away from — danger. Some serve as spies for guilds or temples; others simply enjoy the peace of anonymity.

> [!quote|mark] Old Eriwall proverb
> “Never insult a cat in a wizard’s tower, <br> You’ll never know which ones are listening.”

### **Felis in Ulfong’s Pride**

Though small compared to the towering Leonin or fierce Rakasta, the Felis hold a respected place in **Ulfong’s Pride** — the great confederation of feline peoples in Togruylia. Within the Pride, they are the **Chroniclers and Whispers**, serving as historians, lorekeepers, and negotiators.

In a land dominated by strength and dominance, the Felis command influence through intellect and information. A single whispered word from a Felis adviser has turned more than one duel or treaty within the Pride. They are the unseen paw behind strategy — patient, precise, and occasionally ruthless.

Still, their small size often makes them targets of mockery among the younger warriors of the Pride. The Felis answer such taunts with soft smiles and sharper wits, for they know that while claws may draw blood, _knowledge draws obedience_.

> [!quote|mark] Old Togruylian proverb
> “The lions guard the gate, the leopards take the crown,  <br> but the housecat sleeps beside the throne.”

### **Felis Variants**

Each breed of Felis traces its ancestry to a different environment or magical influence. While small and sleek, their eyes, fur, and behavior vary widely across the Forsaken Realms.

- **Hearthfelis** — Short, round, and warm-furred, with large golden eyes and soft paws. Found in **villages, taverns, and city corners**, they are the storytellers and fortune-keepers of mortal hearths. They can sense emotions like temperature, offering comfort or warning with uncanny instinct.  
    _(Inspired by domestic cats.)_
    
- **Mistfelis** — Long-limbed, silvery-furred, with tufted ears and faintly glowing eyes. They haunt **ancient ruins and graveyards**, serving as quiet shepherds of lost souls and protectors of sleeping spirits. Their presence wards off darker entities, though they rarely speak of how.  ![[Felis.png|right lp|700]]
    _(Inspired by ghostly white or blue-eyed cats.)_
    
- **Stormpaws** — Sleek black or gray Felis whose fur crackles faintly during storms. Sailors claim these cats “summon the wind” — and many live aboard ships as mascots and storm wardens. Found along the **Beressomin coastlines** and **Stambiens docks**, they command a superstitious respect.  
    _(Inspired by seafaring cats and storm omens.)_
    
- **Wildwhiskers** — Lean, spotted Felis of the **Hypnotic Wilds**, patterned like ocelots and margays. They are the tricksters and scouts of their kin, quick to laughter and quicker to vanish. Masters of ambush, illusion, and mimicry — if a Felis ever steals from you, it’s because you bored them.  
    _(Inspired by jungle cats.)_
    
- **Snowmanes** — Short-furred, stocky Felis adapted to **Vontrie’s snow plains**. Their fur gleams like frost and their tails are bushy for balance and warmth. Known for stoic humor and a fondness for communal fires, they often serve as guides through frozen lands.  
    _(Inspired by lynx and wildcats.)_
    
- **Shadowtails** — Midnight-coated Felis who make their homes in **Underbridge** and the **depths of Eriwall’s archives**. They see in pure darkness and have an instinct for lies. To them, a secret is a kind of treasure — and they guard theirs with velvet claws.  
    _(Inspired by black and smoke-furred cats.)_

### **Felis in the World**

Despite their humble stature, Felis leave a deep impression on the people they meet. In every land, a Felis means something different:

- To sailors, they are omens of safe passage.
    
- To priests, they are Prosper’s wandering blessings.
    
- To thieves, they are rivals who always seem one step ahead.
    
- To scholars, they are questions that never stop purring.
    

> [!quote|mark] Merrin Crowsleeve <br> Scholar of Eriwall
> _“They say every firelight hides a god.  <br> I say it hides a cat.”_ 


### **Felis in the World**

> [!quote|mark] Old Underbridge proverb
>_ “If you wake in the night and see a pair of eyes gleaming in your firelight <br> do not reach for your blade. Offer them milk. You will sleep easier after.”_  

Felis fill the cracks of civilization: where walls meet wilderness, where the forgotten still matter. They move between worlds — mortal and magical, seen and unseen — with grace and quiet mischief.  
They are not mighty like Leonin, nor swift as Rakasta, nor curious as Tabaxi.  
They are simply _present_ — wherever warmth, mystery, or comfort is needed.

And in the silence between the flames, their purrs carry the heartbeat of Prosper himself.
