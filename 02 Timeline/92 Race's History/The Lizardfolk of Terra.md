## Divine Origin

The Lizardfolk were birthed in vengeance.  
When **Vimnera**, Goddess of Revenge, first set her coils upon the swamps of early Terra, she struck the earth in wrath for being denied creation rights by the Elder Gods. Where her venom touched the mud, it frothed and boiled, giving birth to scaled forms — cold-blooded, merciless, and patient.

Unlike the goblins or orcs, who embodied Temp’s rage in flame and fury, the Lizardfolk were forged from _quiet hatred._ Their vengeance was not loud; it was slow, coiling, and inevitable. To live was to wait — and to strike only when the balance of survival demanded it.

When the **Battle of the Continents** ended and monstrous races were driven into exile, the Lizardfolk were cast into **Togruylia’s fetid wetlands**, a land perfectly suited for their nature. There, they thrived where others drowned — and swore no debt to gods or mortals again.

---

## The Nest and the Age of Exile

In Togruylia, the Lizardfolk became part of **The Nest**, alongside the yuan-ti, grung, and other scaled kin. But unlike the serpent-born or frogfolk, the Lizardfolk avoided the politics of deception and sorcery. They built strength through unity — **tribes as bodies, and tribes together as one scaled beast**.

To the Nest’s greater powers, the Lizardfolk were tools — warriors bred for the **Tournament of Flesh**, where monstrous clans were pitted against one another for population control and spectacle. Yet many tribes rose beyond their imposed role. They learned to fight not for the amusement of others, but as ritual — to decide leadership, earn divine favor, or prevent bloodlines from stagnating.

The greatest of these war-rituals was called **The Drowning Moon**, fought once every seven years beneath the dark of both moons. Tribes would converge in the marshes of **Ka’thalar**, fighting until the mud ran black with venom. Those who survived the drowning were declared “Scales of Vimnera,” believed to be avatars of her patience and wrath.

## Culture and Philosophy

Lizardfolk do not perceive life and death as moral opposites — only as cycles of hunger and balance. They do not kill for cruelty, but for necessity. This dispassionate outlook, alien to most warm-blooded races, has led others to brand them as monsters.

Their spirituality is a reflection of Vimnera’s venom — corrosive, purifying, and inevitable. Shamans claim that each drop of blood spilled feeds the swamp itself, nourishing the cycle. The greatest of their spiritual leaders are called **Bloodbinders**, who can sense the pulse of the earth and command it like a second heart.

They keep few written records, preferring oral traditions carved into bone totems or scales. One of the oldest sayings passed from clutch to clutch reads:

> “The gods gave warmth to the weak. We took the cold and learned not to need them.”

## Relations with Other Races

Outside Togruylia, Lizardfolk are feared but not unknown.  
In **Osse**, small groups of **Marsh-Blood clans** serve as guides and mercenaries for explorers, leading expeditions into uncharted wetlands for steep prices. Their loyalty is transactional but absolute.  
In **Eophoris**, reclusive **Deepscale hunters** haunt flooded ruins — remnants of those who fled the Nest centuries ago, worshipping the memories of predators long extinct.

Despite their fearsome appearance, Lizardfolk can form powerful bonds of trust. Once accepted into a tribe’s circle, outsiders are protected as “broodless siblings.” Betrayal of such trust, however, is met with a vengeance that lasts generations.

## The Poisoned War

A century after the Cataclysm, the Nest fractured into factions — one led by the Yuan-ti Priest-King **Zha’kul of the Venom Crown**, the other by the Lizardfolk warlord **Hruun the Mudfather**.

Zha’kul sought domination through control of arcane toxins, while Hruun preached the purity of fang and scale — survival without deceit. This erupted into the **Poisoned War**, a decades-long conflict fought in choking mists and flooded trenches.

In the end, neither side truly won. Zha’kul vanished into the underworld with his cult, while Hruun was consumed by a swamp said to have become alive with his rage. Yet from their ashes, the tribes learned unity again — through blood, not chains.

## Modern Tribes and Regional Variants

The lizardfolk of Togruylia and beyond have adapted to every environment their cold blood allows. Each tribe bears distinct scale patterns, hunting methods, and cultural marks — yet all share one trait: survival above all.

---

### **Mireborn** (Swamp Lizardfolk – “Children of the Drowning Moon”)

Found in the flooded deltas of Togruylia and Osse. Their scales are dull green and brown, with thick hides resistant to toxins and disease. They move slowly, deliberately, and their amber eyes seem to track heartbeats more than motion.

They are the oldest of their kind, keepers of Vimnera’s oldest rites. They paint their scales with the mud of fallen kin before battle, believing it makes them unseen by death.

>[!quote|mark]  Hisskra <br>Bloodbinder of Ka’thalar
> “We sink to rise again. The swamp remembers who fell.” 

---

### **Duneclaws** (Desert Lizardfolk – “Those Who Hunt the Heat”)

Exiled from Togruylia after the Poisoned War, these tribes made their way to the **Todazur Flats**, where they evolved hardened, scale-plated bodies and pale tan hues. Their tails act as water reservoirs, and their claws are sharpened for digging ambush pits.

They follow the _Trail of Thirst_, an endless migration between oasis and ruin, where they trade venom for metal and survival tales for passage. Their leaders are chosen not by strength but by endurance.

---

### **Clifffangs** (Mountain Lizardfolk – “The Stoneblood”)

Dwellers of Osse’s cliffside wetlands and deep caves near the **Barrdwell Overhange**. Their scales have grown stony ridges, colored slate gray and blue. They believe each rock face has a spirit — a trapped ancestor who whispers through the wind.

They are famed siege fighters and ambushers, descending walls on ropes woven from sinew and vine. Their priests carve ancestral faces into cliffs to honor fallen kin, believing their spirits strengthen the mountain.

---

### **Reedstriders** (River Lizardfolk – “The Quickfang Clans”)

Found near Eophoris’s **Whispering Expanse**, these lithe, long-limbed hunters move across water on crafted reed shoes and blow darts of paralyzing sap. Their scales shimmer with iridescent greens and golds.

Unlike other tribes, the Reedstriders practice trade and diplomacy. They often partner with halfling smugglers or grung traders — though both sides know the truce only lasts as long as the current flows.

---

### **Nightmaws** (Deep Jungle Lizardfolk – “The Venom Dreamers”)

Larger and darker than most, their black and red scales blend with the canopy shadows of the **Hypnotic Wilds**. They worship the ancient predator **Tzasshul the Endless Jaw**, believed to have been a child of Vimnera before her godhood.

Nightmaws consume a ritual venom that induces trances — said to let them speak to the dead. Their breath smells faintly of blood and lotus, and their eyes never blink.

---

## The Legacy of the Scaled Blood

Though scattered and forgotten by much of the world, the Lizardfolk endure.  
They remember the warmth of Vimnera’s venom, the cold mud of exile, and the endless cycle of hunger and rebirth.

In the words of the Nest:

> “The world burns. The swamp waits. When the fire dies, we will crawl from the water again.”
