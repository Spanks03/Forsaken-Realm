## Origins – The Children of the Shattered Storm

Centaurs were not shaped by careful divine design, but ripped into being by fury. When **Temp, the god of rage**, cast his great storm across Terra in the age before the Cataclysm, the land itself buckled under his wrath. Lightning seared grasslands into glass, thunder rolled through cavern and jungle, and winds tore snow from mountain and sea alike. From those storm-struck places, the centaurs emerged—half-human, half-beast, embodying both the fury and the balance of the storm.

Their myth speaks of **The Shattering**, when the storm’s rage splintered into tribes. Each tribe was born from a fragment of that first tempest: some in the whispering jungles, others in the frozen tundra, others still upon the high cliffs or sweeping plains. They were many, but all carried within them the same truth: to rage is divine, but to rage without balance is destruction.

>[!quote|mark] Attributed to Brannagh Stoneshorn <br> elder of the Bramaghorn
> _"We are storm-birthed. We are calm skies and thunderclap both. To forget one is to betray Temp’s gift."_

## The Early Ages – Riders of Fury

In the millennia before the Cataclysm, centaurs roamed Terra in vast herds. They were both feared and respected: fierce hunters who defended their domains with stormlike fury, yet wise mediators who counseled mortal rulers in times of imbalance. Humans saw in them strength unmatched by cavalry; elves heard echoes of primal spirits in their chants; dwarves regarded them with suspicion, wary of creatures who could bridge wisdom with wrath.

Centaurs never built empires of stone. Their herds were their nations, their trails their roads, their oaths their walls. They saw cities as cages—iron-built to still what was meant to run. Instead, they raised temporary camps, shrines of stone spiraled like lightning cracks, and halls of woven hide and timber that moved with the seasons.

The **Great Plains Wars** of Eophoris first revealed their duality to the world. When human kings attempted to seize grazing lands, the Aruvalken (Steppe Centaurs) descended with fury unmatched. But when the bloodshed ended, it was centaur shamans who brokered peace, insisting the land was not owned but borrowed, and that balance must be kept.

>[!quote|mark] Hrovaediir war-chant <br> sung after the Battle of Seventy Spears
> _"Our hooves break your armies. Our words mend your ruins. Both are the storm’s truth."_

## The Cataclysm and the Scattering

When the Cataclysm tore Terra asunder, the herds were broken. Great storms drove them into the wilderness, scattering tribes across jungles, tundras, forests, and mountains. Many saw this as punishment; others, as proof that Temp demanded they carry his storm to every corner of the world.

Some tribes grew insular, clinging to ancient ways. Others adapted, forging alliances with mortals or even standing as mediators in the chaos that followed. Unlike orcs, goblins, and yuan-ti, centaurs were not driven into **Togruylia**. Their people were much too hard to track down and capture. Instead, they remained scattered, each herd bound to its own domain but united in myth.

This scattering created the great **tribal divergence**, giving rise to the many distinct centaur peoples.

## The Tribes of the Storm

### **Aruvalken – The Wind’s Hooves (Steppe Centaurs)**

Born of lightning-struck plains. Tall, bronze-skinned, their horse-halves bred for speed and endurance. They braid their manes with charms of bone and silver, each braid a memory of a hunt or battle. Aruvalken culture values horizon-chasing: they believe no centaur should die without seeing the edge of the world.

_"Run until the sky bends. Then run further."_

---

### **Thyllorin – The Whispering Hooves (Forest Centaurs)**

Dappled and fey-touched, with eyes glowing green as foxfire. Antler-like crests sometimes grow from their brows. They live in glades where spirits whisper, and many serve as illusion-weavers and guides through enchanted forests. Their humor is sharp and often unsettling.

_"The trees lie kinder than men. But their lies cut just as deep."_

---

### **Hrovaediir – The Hearth-Bearers (Tundra Centaurs)**

Shaggy elk-bodied, with pale skin and red or white hair. Known for endurance, they guard sacred flames in tundra camps, singing deep hearth-songs that keep despair at bay. To them, fire is life itself—the defiance of warmth against Temp’s storm.

_"A fire is not owned. It is a burden, and we carry it."_

---

### **Nokarrax – The Silent Hunt (Panther Centaurs)**

Sleek and black-pelted, their golden eyes shine in the dark. They stalk through jungle temples, guardians of hidden ruins. Nokarrax centaurs are feared assassins, believing every strike should be unseen and every death remembered with silence.

_"The loudest hunter is the hungriest corpse."_

---

### **Serakhtai – The Roaring Sons (Lion Centaurs)**

Lion-bodied, tawny-maned, with voices that carry for miles. They are war-chanters, whose roars serve both as rallying cries and thunderous weapons of morale. Serakhtai leadership is decided by ritual combat—not to the death, but until the plains themselves echo with Temp’s name.

_"A leader is the throat the storm chooses to roar from."_

---

### **Bramaghorn – The Horns of Temp (Ram Centaurs)**

Mountain-born, broad-bodied, with curling horns and shaggy coats. They are wall-breakers and pathfinders, climbing cliffs with impossible grace. Their storm rituals involve head-butting sacred stones until blood stains their horns.

_"Every wall breaks. Some beg to be broken."_

---

### **Urvalthuun – The Ragebound (Bear Centaurs)**

Massive, scarred, their lower halves hulking with bear muscle. They embody raw fury, entering storm-rages where friend and foe alike fear their charge. Yet they are also revered as storytellers—their long winters are spent in endless sagas of storm and blood.

_"Do not fear my rage. Fear the silence after."_

---

### **Gorvakai – The Firstborn Storm (Gorilla Centaurs)**

The rarest, towering and grey-skinned, their simian halves crushingly powerful. Legends say they are closest to the first centaurs Temp forged, but their rage is untamable. Gorvakai wander alone or in pairs, more myth than people.

_"The storm breaks us all. I simply break first."_

## Culture and Belief

Centaur culture is defined by the **Three Pillars of the Storm**:

- **Balance.** Rage and wisdom are both sacred. To lean too far to either is dishonor.
    
- **Herd.** Loyalty to herd and tribe outweighs all else. To betray kin is to sever yourself from the storm.
    
- **Hunt.** The chase, the strike, and the feast are holy acts, reflections of Temp’s own storm of destruction and renewal.
    

Centaurs are deeply spiritual. They see Temp not as a distant god but as a living force in every storm, every battle, every heartbeat of fury. Their shamans brew intoxicating herbs that ignite the storm inside them, leading hunting parties into berserk trances that terrify their enemies.

And yet, when not roused, centaurs are calm, even serene. They are often sought as advisors, mediators, and guardians of natural balance. To those who know them only in battle, they are monsters. To those who sit by their fire, they are wise counselors.

## Conflict and the Modern Age

Centaurs have fought in many wars, though always reluctantly. They despise cities and empires that seek to cage them, but they will ride beside mortals if the balance of the world is at stake. Many herds fought against the rogue army of Togruylia during the **Assault of Kninad**, their storm-charges scattering monstrous hosts.

Yet they remain outsiders. Too monstrous for humans, too human for beasts, too unpredictable for elves. They prefer liminality: the borderlands between wild and civilized.

Today, a centaur might be found in the service of Eriwall’s scholars, guarding the wild reaches of the Hypnotic Wilds, or roaming the endless plains of Beressomin’s coast. They remain, as ever, stormborn: keepers of fury, carriers of wisdom.

>[!quote|mark] Centaur proverb
> _"We are not monsters. We are not men. We are the storm’s children. Pray the skies stay clear."_
