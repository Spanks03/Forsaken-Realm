> [!quote|mark] Proverb of the Ashen Legions  
> _“Where others wage war, we practice it.”_

Hobgoblins were created not in passion, nor chaos, nor divine inspiration, but in **retribution**. Vimnera, the Goddess of Revenge, shaped them as her answer to betrayal — a race born with memory in their blood and discipline in their bones. Where goblins embodied spite and gnashing need, hobgoblins embodied precision. Purpose. Reckoning.

From their first breath, they were soldiers.

From their first century, they were conquerors.

From their first mistake, they were punished — not by gods, but by mortals.

---

## **Exile to Togruylia**

When the **Battle of the Continents** erupted, hobgoblins fought fiercely, relentlessly, and with terrifying efficiency. Their war-regiments did not break. They did not falter.  
They simply advanced.

Which is precisely why the united mortal kingdoms feared them.

Togrulyia — a continent engineered into a prison, an arena, and a grave — became their sentence. Hobgoblins were forced into the **Horde**, alongside orcs, ogres, bugbears, and other “irreconcilables.” It was less an exile and more a political purge: removing the tacticians so that the victors would not face them again.

But in exile, hobgoblins did not wither.  
They **organized**.

They built the first great war-towers of Togrulyia.  
They drafted the first treaties between the Horde and the Hive.  
They drilled orcs into ranks, ogres into phalanxes, and monsters into armies.

The tournament — the endless, blood-soaked spectacle meant to keep monstrous races fighting one another — became, for hobgoblins, a classroom.

And they **thrived**.

## **The Doctrine of Scars**

> [!quote|mark] Writ of General Akhor Mourn  
> _“Every scar is a memory. Every memory is a plan. Every plan is a weapon.”_

All hobgoblins bear scars — but not all scars are punishments.

**Birth-scars** mark family lineage and early rank.  
**War-scars** mark battle lessons learned.  
**Shame-scars** mark failures… and the vow to rectify them.  
**Revenge-scars** are carved only after justice is claimed.

A hobgoblin’s skin is their library.  
Their body is their record-keeping.  
Their very blood holds history.

The greatest disgrace among them is to reach death with **no unavenged wound**.

For a hobgoblin, justice is not philosophical.  
It is anatomical.

## **Culture of Command**

Even when scattered — whether in the Underbridge warrens or the moonlit canopy of the Hypnotic Wilds — hobgoblin society follows the same structure:

### **The Line**

A chain of command stretching from the lowest recruit to the commander.  
Obeying the Line is sacred.

### **The Ledger**

Every regiment keeps a written ledger of debts, losses, betrayals, and injustices.  
Revenge is not an impulse — it is an item to be resolved.

### **The Standard**

Every hobgoblin clan keeps a banner representing their founding victory or first vengeance.  
To lose a banner is to lose identity itself.

### **The Crucibles**

Young hobgoblins are trained in physical, emotional, and tactical crucibles.  
Those who fail are not killed — they are **exiled**, a punishment considered worse.

## **Hobgoblins and Vimnera**

Unlike many races tied to deities, hobgoblins do not worship Vimnera as a savior.  
They revere her as a **tutor**.

To them, Vimnera is not a goddess of hatred — she is a goddess of **completion**.

> _“A wound left unanswered is a story unfinished.”_

Their rituals are simple:

- A whisper over an unhealed cut.
    
- A vow spoken over a fallen comrade.
    
- A silent march before battle.
    

They do not pray for mercy.  
They pray for **opportunity**.

## **The Iron Harmony**

Hobgoblins are at their most terrifying when they fight together.

Generals of Osse have admitted — in documents sealed by the Third Culling — that hobgoblin doctrine has been secretly studied, stolen, and adapted.

- Their **Three-Step Pincer** broke the Altus Legions.
    
- Their **Silent Advance** crushed the Netherwood Rebellion.
    
- Their **Togrulyian Wheel** — a circular rotating formation — became infamous for rolling over monsters three times their number.
    

Even the Empire has a saying:

> _“One hobgoblin is a nuisance.  
> Ten are a squad.  
> A hundred are a war you’ve already lost.”_

## **Togrulyia: The Living Battlefield**

The Horde’s arena is a place shaped by violence — but hobgoblins made it into something more:

A proving ground.  
A crucible.  
A new homeland forged in blood and strategy.

### **The Siege-Lines of Kahzul**

Hobgoblins carved trenches into volcanic plains, turning molten rock into battlements.

### **The Tower-Fortresses of Grend Rak**

Built from scrap metal, monster bone, and stolen war machines.

### **The Drumwalk**

A ritual march where entire regiments beat war-drums in perfect sync until the ground vibrates.  
It is said to paralyze beasts and terrify anyone with a heartbeat.

## **Relations with Other Races**

Hobgoblins inspire fear, but also **uncomfortable respect**.

- **Orcs** admire them, even when hating their discipline.
    
- **Goblins** worship them, calling them “Tall Chiefs.”
    
- **Bug bears** fear them (and work for them).
    
- **Humans** copy them.
    
- **Elves** consider them “inelegant but effective.”
    
- **Dwarves** blame them for many sieges.
    
- **Goliaths** believe hobgoblins would conquer the Spine “if they desired the view.”
    

Only **gnomes**, ironically, find hobgoblins fascinating — akin to what they _could_ be, if born crueler.

## **Variants of Hobgoblins in the Forsaken Realms**

### **Ironblood Hobgoblins** — The True-Born

Red-skinned, thick-jawed, and towering.  
Found primarily in Togrulyia.  
Legends say Vimnera forged them from molten iron cooled in blood.

### **Shadewrought Hobgoblins** — The Night Engineers

Dusky-blue skin, reflective eyes, silent footsteps.  
Found in the Hypnotic Wilds.  
Expert ambushers and trap-makers.

### **Underbridge Hobgoblins** — The Ledger-Keepers

Shorter, darker, more cunning.  
Maintain entire bureaucracies of grudges and mercenary contracts.

### **Ashmarrow Hobgoblins** — The Cursed Legion

Grey-ashen skin, bone-white eyes.  
Said to be survivors of an ancient magical catastrophe.  
Their scars glow faintly red when rage ignites.

## **The Dream of Return**

The greatest unspoken truth among hobgoblins is this:

**They remember the lands stolen from them.**  
And they intend to reclaim them.

Not tomorrow.  
Not next season.

But **eventually**.

Hobgoblins have patience.  
They have memory.  
They have ledgers.

And every injustice — from exile to prejudice to the bloody games of Togrulyia — is written there.

Waiting.

> [!quote|mark] The First Law of Vimnera  
> _“Revenge is not swift.  
> Revenge is correct.”_
