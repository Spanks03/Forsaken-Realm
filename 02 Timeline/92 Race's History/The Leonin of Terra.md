_Children of Glory, Kin of the Sun_

>[!quote|mark]  High Roar Kar’eth of Ulfong’s Pride
> _“Our claws are not for cruelty, our fangs not for hunger. We were made to test the worth of the world.”_

### Origins

The Leonin were brought into Terra by **Qhathos**, the God of Glory, in an act of divine rivalry. Where his brother **Truth** created the tabaxi — small, curious, and clever — Qhathos sought to forge their opposite: proud, disciplined, and regal. The Leonin would not _slink_ through the shadows of the world; they would _stride_ through it.

It is said that when the first Leonin stood, Qhathos roared across the heavens and the mountains echoed his pride. “Let the meek hide in trees,” he declared, “my children will walk beneath the sun and meet their foes in the open.”

---

### The Pride Wars and Ulfong’s Bond

When the Battle of the Continents ended and Togruylia became a prison for the “monstrous,” Qhathos’s children were thrown among the Horde. For centuries, they fought beside orcs, ogres, and goblinoids in the blood-soaked tournament games — their roar announcing both victory and death.

Yet among the endless battles, a leader rose — **Ulfong the Black**, a Leonin warlord whose mane was said to burn like molten gold. Ulfong united not only the scattered prides of Leonin, but also their feline cousins — the tabaxi, rakasta, and felis — into one banner: **Ulfong’s Pride**.

Under his rule, the feline races found a fragile kinship born from shared suffering. The tabaxi became scouts and storytellers; the rakasta, their frontline hunters; the felis, swift couriers and spies; and the Leonin, the unwavering core — the heart and the roar of the Pride.

When Ulfong fell during the Third Tournament of the Blood Isles, the Pride fractured — but his name became a title, passed from one great leader to the next. Even now, when a Leonin roars before battle, the phrase “**Ulfong hears me**” echoes across Togruylia like thunder.

### Culture and Code

To the Leonin, strength is not cruelty — it is truth. Weakness is not shameful, only the refusal to grow stronger is. They see the world as a proving ground, where each soul earns its worth through courage and endurance.

Their society revolves around _Prides_ — extended families bound by blood, challenge, and oath. Leadership is not inherited, but won. Every generation, challenges are issued beneath the “Sun-Stone,” a sacred relic said to have been gifted by Qhathos himself. Victors lead for a cycle of ten years, after which they must either step down or defend their right again.

Loyalty is sacred. To betray one’s pride is to sever the soul — and such exiles often shave or burn their manes in penance, wandering as mercenaries or bodyguards across Terra.

>[!quote|mark] Raka the Silent Fang
> _“A Leonin’s loyalty is simple: earn it, and you have it until your last breath.”_  

---

### Faith and the Flame of Qhathos

Most Leonin still worship **Qhathos**, though not as subjects — but as competitors. They see themselves as his challengers, eternally striving to impress their god through glory. Offerings are made not in temples, but on battlefields — the victorious roar that follows a duel is both a prayer and a promise.

Some, however, whisper prayers to **Truth**, the creator of their softer kin, acknowledging that cunning and compassion are strengths too. Such Leonin are rare — and often mistrusted — but they form the quiet philosophical sects known as the _Mane-Bound Scholars_.

---

### Relations with Other Races

Leonin respect those who display confidence and strength — physical or otherwise. A frail wizard who stands his ground earns more honor in their eyes than a cowardly warlord. Among dwarves and orcs, they find common ground in discipline and valor; with elves, admiration for artistry in battle; with humans, a wary curiosity — for humans’ ambition mirrors their own.

Though they share kinship with the tabaxi and other feline races, their relationship is complex. Leonin see themselves as guardians of the Pride’s legacy, while the tabaxi accuse them of arrogance and control. Yet when danger looms, no tabaxi will deny the comfort of a Leonin’s roar in the dark.

### Regional Variants — _Children of the Sun and Shadow_

The Leonin are Qhathos’s attempt at perfection — noble, disciplined, and unyielding. Their variants reflect **dominance through adaptation**, each type marked by its environment and shaped by warfare or rulership. Their naming conventions often carry **territorial or military pride**. ![[Leonin.png|right lp|700]]

- **Ulfari (Savannah Leonin)** — The archetypal golden-maned hunters of the plains, proud and honorable, tracing lineage to Ulfong’s first generals.
    
- **Karrash (Mountain Leonin)** — Hardy gray-furred warriors of the Spine. Endurance is their faith; scars are their scripture.
    
- **Tharashi (Jungle Leonin)** — Shadow-maned predators of the Hypnotic Wilds, where silence and patience are counted as strength.
    
- **Zarath (Desert Leonin)** — Pale, sand-dusted survivors of the Todazur Flats. They practice “sun combat,” blending blinding light and speed in duels.
    
- **Bastari (Urban Leonin)** — Adapted to life among mortals. Sleek, cunning, politically aware — lions who hunt with words.
    
- **Ulfongi (Togruylian Leonin)** — The warbred champions of the Pride. Bronze-maned giants, marked by ritual scars and molten eyes. Living weapons forged by faith and fury.
    

Leonin names often echo **drums and thunder** — short, percussive, and powerful. Every syllable is a challenge or a promise.

---

### Philosophy of the Roar

For the Leonin, every roar carries meaning. It is both art and language — expressing challenge, triumph, mourning, or even love. To a Leonin, silence is the only true sin, for it implies surrender.

It is said that when the world ends, the last sound will not be thunder or the sea — it will be a Leonin’s roar, daring the gods to try again.
>[!quote|mark]  _Ulfong’s Creed_
> _“Glory is not given. It is taken from the jaws of the end.”  _
