> [!quote|mark] Old Gnomic Plea, found etched into a hollow oak  
> _“We took what was not ours. Now we dwell in water ’til the world is still.”_

Most folk believe bullywugs to be simple swamp-scavengers — dim, croaking nuisances with more mud than sense in their skulls. But the truth is older, stranger, and far heavier.

Bullywugs were not created by any god.  
They were **made** — by mistake, by greed, and by vengeance.

Before the curse, they were a single extended gnomish clan: the **Bramblecourt Line**, famed for charm, commerce, and a ruthless talent for “acquisitions.” They wanted land. They wanted power. But above all, they wanted **the Stillwater Hollow**, a wetland village built atop ancient roots of a witch-coven who lived in harmony with it.

The Bramblecourt gnomes wanted the wetlands’ wealth — its alchemical bog-herbs, its hot springs, its rare luminous frogs. They wanted it, and so they took it.

How exactly they razed the village remains contested. The witches say the gnomes poisoned the water. The gnomes say the witches struck first. Both are lies.

But one truth is carved in the heartwood of the coven’s ruins:

> [!quote|mark] Last Curse of the Hollow Sisters  
> _“May your greed bloat you.  
> May your eyes forever watch what you ruined.  
> May your children croak our grief.  
> May you bring peace to the realm you shattered —  
> or drown in failure.”_

The curse hit the entire bloodline at once. Every Bramblecourt gnome — from the youngest infant to the eldest matriarch — changed. Skin grew slick. Eyes bulbous. Voices warped into croaks. Their bones softened, their legs bowed, their height shrank, and their lineage ended…

…and **Bullywugs** began.

## **The Weight of the Stillwater Oath**

Bullywugs are not monstrous by nature — they are cursed by design.  
Small, wide-eyed, eternally damp, and awkward, they are walking reminders of an ancient crime.

Where gnomes were once lively and clever, bullywugs became:

- **quicker to panic, but strangely brave**,
    
- **cowardly in solitude, but heroic in service**,
    
- **simple in speech, but deep in emotion**,
    
- **ridiculous in appearance, but solemn in purpose**.
    

They do not seek glory.  
They seek **absolution**.

Each bullywug feels the tug of the curse — a quiet pull in their marrow urging them to follow someone “favored by fate.” Someone who might bring peace where violence reigns. Someone who might one day fulfill the Stillwater Oath and free them.

This makes bullywugs naturally gravitate toward **adventurers**.  
They call it **“squiring,”** though their ideas of service are… interpretive.

A bullywug squire might:

- carry your gear (incorrectly),
    
- polish your boots (too vigorously),
    
- leap in front of danger (too late),
    
- or loudly announce your stealth mission (always).
    

But what they lack in competence, they compensate with **absolute loyalty**.

> [!quote|mark] Bullywug Squire, overheard in Underbridge  
> _“You will save the world, yes? Good! I shall carry your left shoe!”_

## **Appearance — The Curse Made Flesh**

All bullywugs share markers of their origin:

- **Large moon-round eyes** — a warped echo of gnomish curiosity.
    
- **Soft, sagging cheeks** — where the curse dampened their facial structure.
    
- **Mottled skin** in greens, browns, or muddy gold.
    
- **Long limbs**, good for leaping but terrible for standing still.
    
- **Perpetual dampness** — their bodies absorb and release moisture constantly.
    

And always, a faint sense of melancholy clings to them —  
as though they remember a life they never lived.

## **Culture of the Cursed**

Bullywugs live mostly in **small marsh camps**, **sunken log-cities**, or **makeshift raft-villages**. Their society is built around three truths:

### **1. The Curse Must Be Honored**

Every bullywug is required to choose (or be chosen by) a “Peacebringer” — someone they believe has the potential to bring lasting peace to Terra.

Most adventurers have had a bullywug squire at some point — few intentionally.

### **2. Never Take What Is Not Owed**

The Bramblecourt sin is baked into their history.  
Taking something without exchange or permission causes older bullywugs to weep.

Even if it is a stick.

Especially if it is a stick.

### **3. Stories Must Be Preserved**

Every bullywug camp has a “Mud-Reader” — an elder who draws history in mud and water, reshaping it with each retelling.

They cannot read books.  
But they can read **bogs.**

## **The Ever-Sad Kingdom**

In rare times, bullywugs gather into large communities called **Croakholds**. These gatherings are loud, chaotic, entirely unhelpful, and deeply sacred.

A Croakhold elects a ruler:

### **The Misery King**

A leader chosen not for strength nor wisdom, but for how profoundly the curse afflicted them.  
The Misery King rules for one cycle of Nosduhmere, then steps down by choice or by sorrow.

The title predates written memory.  
Some say the first Misery King was the last Bramblecourt gnome,  
forced to watch as the witches sank into the marsh they died protecting.

## **Faith — The Croak at the End of All Things**

Bullywugs have no gods of their own.  
They fear Vimnera.  
They avoid Truth.  
They distrust Prosper.  
They quietly pray to Guilla for protection against being eaten.

But above all, they honor the **Stillwater Sisters**, the three witches who cursed them.

Not as deities.  
As **teachers.**

They leave offerings of:

- polished stones,
    
- lost buttons,
    
- soggy pastries,
    
- or whatever gift their Peacebringer gives them.
    

They whisper a single prayer into deep water:

> _“Still the world. Still my heart. Still the curse.”_

## **Bullywug Variants**

Their transformation left room for new forms to evolve across regions.

### **Mirelilies** — The Distantly Gentle

Wide-pupiled, pastel-skinned, and plant-scented. Found in flower marshes.  
Known for brewing potent teas and crying at sunsets.

### **Boghoppers** — The Loud and the Brave

Huge legs, booming croaks, and a love for heroics.  
Often mistaken for gremlins wearing frog suits.

### **Stillwater-Born** — The Witchmarked

Slate-blue skin, glowing eyes, strange prophetic murmurs.  
Said to dream the memories of the original Bramblecourts.

## **Relations with Other Races**

Most people treat bullywugs as harmless oddities.  
Some as pests.  
A few as blessings.

- **Gnomes** feel guilt, pity, and sometimes denial.
    
- **Humans** find them strangely endearing.
    
- **Elves** find them tragically poetic.
    
- **Dwarves** find them too damp.
    
- **Grung** try to eat them.
    
- **Tritons** often protect them.
    
- **Locathah** tell stories of how they once saved a prince.
    

But all agree on one thing:

> _“If a bullywug chooses you, your life is about to become complicated.”_

## **The End of the Curse — The Peace They Cannot Define**

The Stillwater Oath states the curse will end only when they aid in bringing **forever peace** to Terra.

What is forever peace?

No bullywug knows.  
Some believe it means ending the Tide War.  
Some think it means reuniting the moons.  
Some think it means fixing the Weave.  
Some believe it means saving all realms from destruction.

But one thing is universally true:

A bullywug will follow the one they believe can achieve it.

To death, to ruin, to glory, to silence.

> [!quote|mark] Bullywug Parting Blessing  
> _“May your path be muddy, and may I hop beside you.”_
