> [!quote|mark] Dayton Copperpot <br> tinker of questionable luck
> _“If it glints, it’s gone. If it chirps at you, check your pockets.”_  

Jergals are tiny gliding mammal-folk found in forests, cities, coastlines, and the strange cracks between worlds. Mischievous, curious, and impossibly quick, jergals are known less for violence and more for their uncanny ability to liberate shiny objects from unsuspecting pockets.

Most regard them as pests.  
Some call them thieves.  
Others—rangers, wanderers, and the occasional adventurer—swear they are **good luck** if befriended.

Whatever the truth, jergals remain one of Terra’s most peculiar and enduring smallfolk.

---

## **Physiology**

Jergals are small, rodent-like gliders with round black eyes, overlong fluffy tails, and skin flaps stretching from wrist to ankle. Their size ranges widely—from 3–6 inches in height for most, to rare elders or unusual offshoots reaching nearly 2 feet tall. Their bones are light and flexible, their paws dexterous enough to untie knots, pick locks, and dismantle backpacks with unnerving precision.

Their lifespan is short by humanoid standards: they reach adulthood by age five and rarely surpass fifteen. Few jergals live past twenty—those that do become revered storytellers who rarely leave the safety of their scurries.

Though tiny and harmless-looking, their agility makes them nearly impossible to catch once airborne. A startled jergal becomes a blur of chirps, fluff, and gliding membrane disappearing through foliage or rafters.

---

## **Behavior & Culture**

Jergals organize themselves into **scurries**—tight-knit family colonies that operate with cheerful chaos. Status is determined not by strength or age, but by cleverness and the ability to find or steal particularly shiny trinkets.

They communicate through rapid chirps, chitters, and tail signals, and though capable of understanding Common, they only rarely speak it aloud. Instead, most jergals listen far more than they talk, observing and mimicking until they understand exactly how to get what they want.

Jergals are not malicious.  
They simply have priorities:

1. Shiny things
    
2. Food
    
3. More shiny things
    
4. Dramatic gliding
    
5. Anything else
    

---

## **The Precious Pouch**

Perhaps the most extraordinary feature of jergals is their **nearly seamless magical pouch**. Hidden in the fur of their belly, it appears flat and invisible until opened by its owner.

Inside is a small extradimensional space—similar to, but distinct from, a bag of holding. A jergal can hide dozens of shiny items within, from buttons to gemstones to entire pastries (their second-favorite treasure). Only the jergal may retrieve items; the only other way to access the pouch’s contents is the creature’s death.

If the pouch is punctured, the jergal bleeds normally—the space does not rupture or spill objects into the world. And placing a jergal inside another extradimensional space causes no dimensional rupture… though it may cause the jergal to steal absolutely everything in reach.

Scholars argue over whether the pouch is a biological trait or a divine joke.  
Most jergals insist it was a **gift from The Flicker**, the small trickster god of sudden whims and shiny things.

# **Jergal Variants**

### _Branches of the Glimmer-Quick Lineage_

Jergals adapt rapidly to their environments, their magic-laced biology reshaping itself in only a few generations. Four major variants are known, each with distinct habits and appearances.

---

## **Canopy Jergals**

### _The Classic Glider-Tricksters_
> [!quote|mark] Halfling proverb
> _“Tree shadows with sticky fingers.”_ 

Canopy jergals are the iconic forest-dwellers. Their brown-green mottled fur blends perfectly with bark and leaves, and their gliding is acrobatic and fearless. They swarm through ancient forests in sprawling scurries, creating nests of woven leaves, stolen scarves, and anything colorful.

Their pouches overflow with beetle shells, buttons, ribbon scraps, and coins.

To canopy jergals, the forest exists purely for delight, exploration, and mischief. They are friendly to druids, horrified by owlbears, and fascinated by elves (whom they see as “tall jergals”).

**Where Found:**  
Eophoris Wilds, Osse’s spine-trees, Ilistair greenroads.

---

## **Underbridge Jergals**

### _Gutter-Gliders and Engine-Thieves_
> [!quote|mark] Ryn Halvek <br> Abyssal Archivist
> _“If something small, furry, and giggling disappears into a vent,  
> do NOT follow it.”_

These jergals have adapted to metal, steam, and shadows. Their fur darkens to greys, their gliding flaps thicken to resist sparks and sharp edges, and their eyes enlarge for low light.

Underbridge jergals glide through piping networks, rafters, broken ducts, old dwarven mines, and arcano-mechanical structures. They are notorious for stealing gears, capacitors, and anything that moves or glimmers.

Their scurries form “spark councils,” who divide scavenging routes, regulate shiny distribution, and maintain uneasy truces with engineers.

**Where Found:**  
Underbridge, Malmsbury lower platforms, abandoned dwarven tunnels.

---

## **Tide-Skip Jergals**

### _Salt-Spark Misrule of the Coast_
> [!quote|mark] Tidewarden Kora Shelltide
> _“If you see a jergal riding a crab, it is sacred nonsense.  
> Do not interfere.”_

Adapted to coasts and mangrove forests, tide-skip jergals have sleek waterproof fur and extra-wide gliding membranes. They cannot swim well—but they can glide down and _skim_ across the water surface, chasing fish or fleeing predators.

Their tails are salt-brushed fluff mops; their pouches carry shells, pearls, and driftglass. They get along shockingly well with tritons and sea elves, who find their antics amusing or deeply irritating.

**Where Found:**  
Osse shores, Ilistair’s driftwood coasts, Tidewall archipelagos.

---

## **Fey-Touched Jergals**

### _Glimmer-Ghosts of the Woods Between_
> [!quote|mark] Old ranger warning
> _“Never chase the glowing ones.”_ 

Fey-touched jergals appear where the Feywild bleeds into the mortal realm. Slightly taller than their kin, they shimmer with spiraled bioluminescent markings, glide in complete silence, and vanish by holding still.

Their pouches behave unpredictably—sometimes pulling out objects lost decades ago, sometimes revealing things not native to this plane. They are gentle, eerie creatures who obey strange rules and follow unseen patterns.

To them, every shiny object is a thread in fate’s loom.

**Where Found:**  
Glimmerline Woods, Eriwall’s cursed thickets, Feywild-scarred regions.

---

# **Unified Cultural Identity**

Despite their many variants, all jergals share:

- **Relentless curiosity**
    
- **Collective scurry loyalty**
    
- **An obsession with shiny objects**
    
- **A lighthearted worldview untouched by malice**
    
- **A quiet belief that they are protected by The Flicker**
    

In cities, they are considered nuisances.  
In forests, guides.  
In coastlines, omens of luck.  
In Underbridge, a force of nature.

Wherever they appear, laughter—and missing jewelry—follows.
