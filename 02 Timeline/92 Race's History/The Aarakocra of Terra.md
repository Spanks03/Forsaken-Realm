### The Divine Origin:

When the Cataclysm tore the heavens and the Weave shuddered, the **twenty-one Keepers** gathered atop the highest peak of Terra to seal the rift between realms. They breathed as one — a symphony of divine exhalations that fused light, wind, music, and storm into a single, radiant tempest.

That storm birthed the **Aarakocra** — the Skyborne Choir.  
Each Keeper’s breath gave rise to a different brood, feathered in their god’s essence, bound to the skies yet destined to scatter.

>[!quote|mark] Elder Kresh of the Ninth Sky
> _“We are not born of one god’s hand but twenty-one voices’ song.”_ 

The Aarakocra were meant to be guardians of the world’s balance, the eyes and voices of the divine winds. But when the Keepers withdrew from mortal sight, the Aarakocra fractured. Each lineage followed the calling of their godly progenitor, forming flocks across the continents — proud, varied, and eternal wanderers of the firmament.

### History of the Aarakocra

#### **The Age of First Flight**

The earliest Aarakocra walked among mortals as envoys of the Keepers. Some bore radiant feathers of Sol and Lunala, guiding day and night; others shimmered with the iridescence of Galxium or the sharp edges of Scabbard’s will.  
Their songs shaped weather. Their dances carved winds. The gods watched their children glide and saw hope.

But after the **Cataclysm’s Second Shudder**, the skies themselves splintered. The divine link weakened. Flocks descended or fled to the highest reaches of Terra, and their songs grew discordant. Many forgot their divine parentage, turning to tribal survival, elemental worship, or mercenary flight.

It was during this era that the first **Kenku** appeared — descendants of Kilinio’s Flock, cursed by their Keeper for stealing divine melodies to sell to mortals. Their voices were stolen, and their wings grounded. The Aarakocra still speak their name in mourning and shame.

>[!quote|mark] from _The Wind’s Dirge_
> _“Kilinio’s laughter became a silence that haunts every sky.”_

### Faith, Society, and Song

Aarakocra faith is built on **air and memory**. They believe the breath that created them never truly left; it flows in every gust and whisper of wind.  
To them, the act of breathing itself is worship — each inhale a remembrance of the Keepers, each exhale a continuation of their divine song.

Their homes, called **Aeries**, are carved into mountain peaks, massive trees, and cloud islands. They are ruled not by kings but **Conductors**, chosen for their harmony of voice, wisdom, and windcraft.

Aarakocra record history not in ink but in _flight_. Long arcs across the sky — swoops, dives, and turns — are a written language called the **Zephyr Script**. When read by those who understand, a flock’s synchronized flight recounts epics, prayers, or omens.

>[!quote|mark] Aarakocra proverb
> _“Ink fades but the wind will blow on.”_

## **Flocks of the Skyborne Choir**

Each Aarakocra traces its lineage to one of the divine Keepers — twenty-one dragons who once exhaled the winds of the world. These flocks differ in feather, form, and faith, yet all share the song of the sky. From the sunlit cliffs of Eophoris to the frozen peaks of Togrulyia, their wings are the shifting prayers of the heavens themselves.

### The Solari - Flock of Sol
*“Children of the First Light,” radiant, proud, noble as eagles or hawks.*
The **Solari**, children of **Sol, Keeper of Day**, gleam like living sunrise. The eagles or golden falcons, their feathers shimmer gold and white, their eyes bright as dawnlight. Found among the high cliffs and open plains where the first rays of sun strike, they are proud guardians and guides, their voices clear as the morning horn.  

### The Noctyrae - Flock of Lunala
*Silver-feathered owls and nightjars who navigate by starlight and dream.*
The **Noctyrae** of **Lunala, Keeper of Night**, are their shadowed kin — owls or nightjars cloaked in starlight, feathers dusted with silver constellations. Silent and introspective, they roost in moonlit forests and whisper prophecies beneath the constellations they map by heart.

### The Veyrani - Flock of Guilla
*Hawk and kestrel hunters, “the breath between heartbeat and strike.”*
From **Guilla, Keeper of the Hunt**, come the **Veyrani**, hawk-blooded predators with rust-red plumage and fierce amber eyes. They rule the tallgrass plains and craggy valleys of southern Togrulyia, hunting in ritualized duels that echo divine pursuit.  

### The Caravels - Flock of Treble
*Macaw-like singers whose plumage bursts with color; name echoes “carol.”*
In contrast, the **Caravels** of **Treble, Keeper of Music**, blaze with color — macaw-bright feathers and lyrebird melodic cries that ripple through the Hypnotic Wilds. To them, every storm is a drumbeat, and every melody a hymn to the winds.

### The Morhallow - Flock of Flavis
*Vulture-priests who believe decay is sacred rebirth; name means “holy rot.”*
The **Morhallow** of **Flavis, Keeper of Decomposition**, are vultures of solemn faith. Their gray, sparse feathers and heavy wings carry them over the scorched lands of Osse, where they sing dirges over the dead, believing decay is merely truth reborn.  

### The Pethrians - Flock of Fertilla
*Penguin-based kin who raise orphaned young in frozen coastal colonies.*
**Fertilla’s Flock**, the **Pethrians**, take after penguins — rounded bodies, black-and-white plumage, and steadfast hearts. Found along icy coasts and the edges of Thelered Crystal Ponds or Myral's Melting Heart, they waddle together in massive colonies, raising young not just of their own kind but of any orphaned creature the sea delivers.

### The Vorthen - Flock of Psion
*Raven-like scholars who study weight, fall, and cosmic balance.*
The **Vorthen**, children of **Psion, Keeper of Gravity**, bear the sleek black feathers of ravens, eyes deep as thought. They dwell among ancient ruins and mountain observatories, reading the pull of stars as scripture.  

###  The Kelari - Flock of Kestella
*Magpie-descended tricksters of cities and sky markets.*
The **Kelari** of **Kestella, Keeper of Theft**, dart through city skylines like magpies. Quick and cunning, their black-and-white plumage gleams as they dive through alleys and towers, stealing both gold and glory.

### The Zuphar - Flock of Zuphinth
*Abyssal-black wanderers whose wings leave trails of violet shimmer.*
Far darker are the **Zuphar**, children of **Zuphinth, Keeper of the Void**, whose wings are black as the abyss and whose flight leaves violet contrails in the dusk. The storm petrel or black albatross-like aarakocra nest along the Oceri Trench, silent wanderers who hear omens in the wind.  

### The Tharnex - Flock of Underthar
*Blind cave-gliders, half-skin and half-feather, living echoes of the deep.*
The **Tharnex** of **Underthar, Keeper of the Underdark**, forsake sunlight altogether. Half-feather, half-skin, they glide through vast caverns, their pale eyes blind but all-seeing in echo and touch like bats or oilbirds.

### The Galaxen - Flock of Galxium
*Celestial crane-kin who map constellations in their plumage.*
Born from **Galxium, Keeper of the Astral Sea**, the **Galaxen** are cranes with feathers like spilled constellations. They drift over mountaintop lakes, ever searching for the mirrored veil between sky and dream. 

### The Talassir - Flock of Scabbard
*Falcon-like warriors, feathers sharp and polished as blades.*
From **Scabbard, Keeper of Weaponry**, come the **Talassir** — falcons with steel-gray feathers and crimson tips, the forgefire of battle in their gaze. They are falcon or gyrafalcon-like soldiers of the Aarakocra, each feather treated as a blade’s edge.

### The Hesari - Flock of Heselo
*Peacock nobility obsessed with beauty and possession.*
The **Hesari**, of **Heselo, Keeper of Greed**, are peacocks with tails glimmering gold and sapphire. They adorn themselves with jewels and metal filaments, nesting atop trade towers and golden domes.  

### The Veylen - Flock of Yvelia
*Snowy-owl predators that vanish into fog and tundra.*
**Yvelia’s Flock**, the **Veylen**, resemble snowy owls, their plumage shifting with the terrain — white in winter, gray in fog. From the tundras of Osse they hunt, unseen until the instant they strike.

### The Lumeir - Flock of Opacitous
*Swanlike beacons of light and healing, glowing faintly even at dusk.*
The **Lumeir**, born of **Opacitous, Keeper of Radiance**, move with swanlike grace, their feathers faintly luminescent. They make their homes along reflective waters, preaching peace and clarity to those who seek them.  

### The Kularen - Flock of Kular
*Kite-eyed sentinels whose gaze is said to pierce the future.*
**Kular’s Flock**, the **Kularen**, appear as brown kites with eyes that pierce horizons. They circle deserts and high mesas, believing that to witness is to serve the truth.

### The Aesari - Flock of Jezebel
*Kingfisher tricksters who speak in wordplay and paradox.*
From **Jezebel, Keeper of Riddles**, came the **Aesari**, plumaged in vivid blues and oranges like kingfishers. Dwelling near rivers and waterfalls, they speak in puzzles whose meanings shift like ripples on water.

### The Myren - Flock of Myral
*Gentle sparrow-folk devoted to peace and companionship.*
The **Myren** of **Myral, Keeper of Friendship**, are gentle sparrows that nest near mortal homes and temples. They act as messengers, peacemakers, and healers — the first to greet, and the last to abandon a fallen friend.

### The Hestari - Flock of Hestinia
*Puffin-like villagers who believe generosity is divine fire.*
**Hestinia’s Flock**, the **Hestari**, resemble puffins and finches, small and warm-hearted, who build nests in coastal villages and docks. They share bread, stories, and laughter freely, saying, “Every meal is a covenant.”  

### The Dezarin- Flock of Dezimone
*Condor-titans who find meaning through endurance; stoic ascetics.*
And lastly, the **Dezarin** of **Dezimone, Keeper of Torture**, rise as massive condor-like titans. Their feathers are darkened at the tips as if singed by divine fire. They dwell among volcanoes and scarred ridges, believing that pain is not cruelty, but proof one still lives.

### The Skybound Legacy

Though scattered, all Aarakocra share a single unspoken prayer — that one day, when every flock sings together again, the wind will remember the unity of the first breath.

And when that happens, they say the sky itself will heal.

>[!quote|mark] Final verse of _The Hymn of the First Flight_
> _“When twenty-one winds blow as one, Terra shall remember how to fly.”_
