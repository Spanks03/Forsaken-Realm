### **Origin & Divine Creation**

When **Truth**, the god of truth and clarity, sought to understand the lies that fester within the minds of mortals, he reached into their dreams — the one realm where deceit could not hide. What he found there was both wondrous and terrible: unfiltered emotion, desire, fear, and guilt.

From these fragments of unspoken honesty, Truth shaped the **Kalashtar** — beings of two souls: one mortal, one dreamborn. Each was born from a mirror of self-reflection, their consciousness intertwined with an echo from the **Plane of Reverie**, the Dream Beyond the Veil.

It is said that every Kalashtar is the dream of someone who never woke.

Their purpose was once sacred — to walk among mortals and whisper the truths that only dreams could reveal. But when the **Cataclysm** shattered the balance between the divine and the dreaming, the mirrors that connected their souls cracked. Now, each Kalashtar hears two minds in one skull: their own and the faint, broken song of the Dream-Echo that clings to them.

---

### **History in Terra**

The earliest Kalashtar walked freely among humanity, revered as prophets and oracles. Their twin-voiced speech — mortal and dream overlaying in harmony — was considered a sign of divine communion. But with time, fear replaced reverence.

The rise of **mirror magic** during the Age of Binding led to exploitation of Kalashtar essence, as mages discovered their souls could be used as **living keys** to breach demi-planes and dream realms. The more that were captured, the deeper their kin retreated — vanishing behind mirrored gateways and glass sanctuaries that shimmer at the edge of sleep.

Now, most Kalashtar live in **dream sanctums** hidden behind enchanted mirrors, existing half in Terra and half in the Plane of Reverie. They observe the waking world like reflections in a still pond — sometimes reaching through to guide mortals, other times watching in sorrow as history repeats itself.

---

### **Culture & Society**

The Kalashtar do not dream as mortals do. Their sleep is a **conversation** — an exchange of memory between body and echo. Because of this, they experience emotions differently: to feel too deeply is to risk merging with their dream-self and losing their sense of individuality.

Thus, Kalashtar suppress outward emotion — not from apathy, but out of fear. Their serenity is a mask; beneath it hums a storm of shared feeling and alien thought.

They see truth not as a moral choice but as a frequency — something that resonates cleanly when unbroken. Falsehoods, to them, sound dissonant, creating ripples through their psychic link. A Kalashtar cannot lie without feeling the _pain of it_ resonate through their soul.

---

### **The Dream-Echo Bond**

Every Kalashtar is tethered to a **Dream-Echo**, a being from the Plane of Reverie that is both guardian and reflection. These echoes are ancient entities — once pure embodiments of Truth’s divine curiosity — but now fragmented.

A Kalashtar’s echo influences their temperament and powers, shaping how they perceive both dreams and reality. When a Kalashtar dies, their echo either dissipates into the dream sea or attaches itself to another sleeping soul, sometimes creating a new Kalashtar generations later.

To awaken the echo fully is called **The Convergence**, a sacred but dangerous act that can grant vast psychic insight — or dissolve the self entirely into dreamlight.

---

### **Faith & Philosophy**

Most Kalashtar still revere **Truth**, seeing him as both creator and jailer. They believe he watches through mirrors, each reflection a fragment of his divine eye. Some sects, however, have turned toward **Lunala**, **Ogul**, or **Nosduh**, believing the moons’ cycles influence dream stability and spiritual resonance.

They hold an unshakable belief that _all lies begin as dreams unspoken_, and thus, to heal the world’s falsehood, one must first mend its dreaming heart.

---

### **Relations with Other Races**

Kalashtar often pass unnoticed in society by maintaining emotional stillness and using illusionary glamours. Only when one peers too long into their eyes — which reflect faint, moving shapes like a mirror in rippling water — does their nature become apparent.

To elves, they are curiosities; to dwarves, unsettling mystics; to humans, prophets or madmen. Few trust them fully, and fewer still understand them. Yet every nation has legends of a _mirror-seer_ or _dream oracle_ who intervened at a crucial moment in history — only to vanish when dawn came.

---

### **The Mirror Sanctums**

Hidden behind enchanted glass, **Mirror Sanctums** are demi-planes that blur the line between waking and dreaming. Within, gravity bends like liquid, and light refracts endlessly. Here the Kalashtar build mirrored spires that reflect impossible skies — each a sanctuary for a few dozen souls.

Some sanctums are isolated, guarded by labyrinths or riddles; others lie in plain sight, disguised as antique mirrors in noble estates or temples. To look into one too long is to risk being seen _from the other side._

> _“Every reflection is a question asked twice — once by you, once by what watches back.”_

---

### **Kalashtar Variants of Terra**

#### **The Mirrorborn**

The most common, tied to **Truth** directly. Their eyes shimmer like polished silver, and faint runes of reflection appear on their skin under moonlight. Known for clarity of thought and piercing empathy, they often serve as judges, mediators, or dream interpreters.

> _“We do not see faces — only layers.”_

#### **The Reverent**

Tied to **Nosduhmere**, the slower moon. They are somber, pale, and deliberate, with faint glowing lines like cracks of starlight beneath their skin. These Kalashtar dream deeply, speaking rarely, their voices echoing faintly even in silence.

> _“The dream that waits is patient, but it never forgets.”_

#### **The Emberdreamers**

Born under **Ogulspire**, their emotions burn bright but dangerously. Their eyes glint like embers, and when they speak, warmth ripples through the air. They are often visionaries, artists, or rebels whose dreams ignite revolutions — or disasters.

> _“Desire is just truth that refused to sleep.”_

#### **The Veiled**

Their echoes are fractured — Dream-Echoes broken during the Cataclysm. They appear ordinary, but their reflections sometimes move independently. Many live among mortals completely disguised, acting as spies or chroniclers of the waking world.

> _“We are the silence between dreams.”_

#### **The Twinhearted**

Rare and feared, these Kalashtar house _two_ Dream-Echoes. Their eyes constantly shift in color, their speech overlapping in harmony or discord. Often mad, often brilliant, they are said to glimpse the raw architecture of the Plane of Reverie itself.

> _“To see too much truth is to lose what you are.”_

---

### **Modern Role in Terra**

Today, the Kalashtar exist mostly in whispers — their kind mistaken for humans until they speak words no human could know. Some live in peace, hidden as scholars, artists, or priests. Others act as wardens of the dream boundary, ensuring that what sleeps beyond does not awaken in Terra.

To encounter a Kalashtar is to feel the weight of unseen eyes behind glass — and to know that every lie you’ve ever told just stirred in its sleep.
