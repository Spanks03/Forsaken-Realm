The Firbolg are a paradox: towering in body, yet soft in step; strong enough to shatter stone, yet preferring to nurture saplings. Brought into Terra by **Truth, god of truth and clarity**, they embody not raw power, but the quiet strength of wisdom and honesty. To most of Terra’s peoples, the Firbolg are whispers in the trees or shadows in the mist—felt more than seen, guardians who prefer to let the world move past them rather than force themselves upon it.

>[!quote|mark] Sivra Tallow <br> ranger of the Briarwind
> _"If you find yourself in a forest and feel you are being watched—do not fear. It is not a predator. It is a Firbolg deciding whether you belong."_  
---

### Origins and Misconceptions

Though their height and strength rival that of ogres or giants, Firbolgs are not of giant descent. The myth persists because of their size, and because it was **the union of Firbolgs and goliaths** in ancient times that gave rise to the rare giant bloodlines that still linger in Terra. Giants, then, are the children of Firbolg wisdom joined with goliath endurance—not the other way around.

The Firbolg themselves claim a far older story. Truth, seeking to anchor his ideals in mortal form, crafted them not as conquerors but as keepers of balance. Their great size was not to dominate, but to withstand—storm, hunger, and time. Their eyes were made keen to pierce lies, and their voices low and resonant to carry honesty into the hearts of others.

>[!quote|mark] Excerpt from _The Clear Tongue_ <br> a Firbolg teaching
> _"Giants boast of mountains they have climbed. Firbolgs speak of roots they have tended. One looks upward; the other looks inward."_

### Culture of Clarity

Firbolg clans are **nomadic and discreet**, moving silently along forgotten paths, living in harmony with wild places. They build little that endures in stone; instead, their mark is left in groves nurtured, streams cleared, and beasts guided.

Their culture centers on **three tenets**, said to be gifts of Truth:

- **Speak honestly**: Deception is poison, for lies cloud clarity. Firbolgs value plain words and distrust riddles, though they respect elven poetry and gnomish wit when it is not used to obscure meaning.
    
- **Guard balance**: Nature must neither be hoarded nor squandered. Firbolgs see themselves as caretakers of cycles, culling when numbers grow too great and healing when land is wounded.
    
- **Walk unseen**: Not every truth must be shouted. Firbolgs believe humility is strength, and that their role is to protect, not to rule.
    

They often adorn themselves with natural paints, charms of wood or bone, and cloaks of moss or fur, blending into the land they traverse.

---

### Roles Among Other Peoples

Firbolgs rarely enter cities, for they find them noisy, dishonest, and suffocating. When they do appear, it is often as:

- **Druids**, tending sacred groves and teaching respect for the land.
    
- **Rangers**, guiding travelers safely through perilous wilds.
    
- **Sages or witnesses**, called upon to settle disputes where impartiality is needed.
    

Their reputation as mediators comes not from politics, but from their incorruptible honesty. In conflicts where other voices are swayed by bribes or threats, a Firbolg’s words are considered binding.

>[!quote|mark] Old saying in Eriwall
> _"A Firbolg’s truth cuts sharper than steel. You can fight a sword; you cannot fight clarity."_  

---

### The Weight of Secrecy

Though Firbolgs embody honesty, they are not reckless with it. They keep many truths guarded—secrets of sacred sites, of lost paths, of rituals whispered by Truth himself. To betray these mysteries is the greatest dishonor a Firbolg can bear.

This has led to tension with humans and elves, who sometimes accuse Firbolgs of arrogance for withholding what they know. To a Firbolg, however, truth is not possession—it is responsibility. Some truths, they say, are too dangerous to give freely.

### Variants of Firbolg

Though Firbolgs are one race, their long wandering lives and bonds with specific wilds have shaped them into distinct groups.

- **Grovenkin** – Forest Firbolgs, with bark-like skin tones and mossy hair. Masters of concealment, often mistaken for treants by travelers.
    
- **Highclads** – Mountain Firbolgs, tall and broad-shouldered, with stone-grey skin and eyes like the sky. Stoic guardians of high passes.
    
- **Riverborn** – Firbolgs tied to lakes and rivers, with skin dappled like pebbled streams and hair the color of reeds. They are gifted in healing and water magic.
    
- **Stormcallers** – Nomads of the coasts and high cliffs, their hair wild as lightning and their eyes storm-bright. Revered as seers of weather and omens.
    
- **Deepwood** – Firbolgs who wander the densest jungles, darker-skinned with broad frames and markings of leaf-green. Often more reclusive than others.
    

These clans seldom meet, but when they do, they greet one another as kin, sharing songs and stories before vanishing again into their chosen wilds.

---

### Firbolgs in History

Firbolgs rarely leave records, but others write of them. They appear in human war-chants as allies against invading beasts; in dwarven chronicles as impartial judges of border disputes; in elven lore as companions to great druids.

Yet they are never at the center of these tales. Firbolgs choose roles at the edges—quiet influences that shape events without dominating them.

During the **Assault of Kninad**, Firbolgs were among the few who sheltered refugees in wild places, refusing to take sides but offering safety. To some, this made them heroes; to others, cowards unwilling to fight openly.

---

### Closing

Firbolgs remain rare, enigmatic, and deeply respected—though often misunderstood. They are neither warriors of conquest nor recluses of fear. They are **keepers of clarity**, guardians who walk unseen, speaking only when words are needed, and shaping Terra in ways subtle but enduring.

>[!quote|mark] Firbolg elder <br> last words
> _"We do not need statues or songs. Let the rivers sing of us. Let the roots remember."_
