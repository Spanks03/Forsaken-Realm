The Dragonborn are not a race of divine intention, but of draconic aftermath. They are the legacy of Terra’s most ancient and incredible creatures—dragons themselves—mingling with mortals in the centuries after the Cataclysm. Born from grief, pride, passion, and survival, Dragonborn are as fractured as their progenitors.

Where elves are tied to logic and dwarves to labor, Dragonborn are tied to struggle. They are the children of Cassierrel’s kin, of wyrms who in mourning or fury took mortal form and bound themselves to humans and others in the aftermath of the Cataclysm. Unlike the dragons who became Keepers, most of these progenitors were not deities but survivors—creatures who needed mortal bloodlines to carry their essence forward. The result was a race forever caught between two worlds: too mortal to be dragons, too draconic to be accepted as mortals.

>[!quote|mark] Merchant of Ichinoki
> _"When you see one, you don’t see a man. You don’t see a woman. You see a dragon with all its teeth hidden behind soft flesh. And you ask yourself—how long until those teeth come out?"_  
 
---

### The Birth of Dragonborn

The first Dragonborn appeared not in a single place, but wherever dragons chose to walk as mortals. Some came from grief-stricken wyrms who sought companionship in their mourning. Others came from dragons who sought to spread their influence in new ways, crafting dynasties of scaled heirs. And some came from darker unions: forced bloodlines meant to bind mortals to a dragon’s will.

In those early centuries, Dragonborn were rare and hunted. To humans, they were reminders of draconic power, terrifying and unwanted. To dwarves, they were unstable half-breeds whose blood could not be trusted. To elves, they were wild things that blurred the order of lineage. Many Dragonborn never survived their childhoods, slain by fearful communities or abandoned to the wilds.

But some endured. They learned to hide their scales beneath armor and cloth, or to survive on the fringes of society where others dared not tread. Slowly, scattered enclaves formed—small villages clinging to mountains, deep caves, or the ruins of dragon lairs.

---

### The Keeper Bloodlines

Though most Dragonborn were born of ordinary dragons, a handful claimed descent from the children of Cassierrel who rose as Keepers. These were rare and fiercely contested lineages, often the center of dynastic wars or brutal purges.

A Dragonborn said to carry the blood of the Keeper of the Hunt or the Keeper of Ambush might command loyalty—or suspicion. Entire villages were razed on rumors of such ancestry, either by enemies who feared their power or by cultists who sought to control it.

But for every claimed Keeper-blood Dragonborn, there were dozens more whose ancestors were common wyrms, forgotten by time. This became the common lot of their people: to carry fragments of a legacy they could never fully claim.
  
>[!quote|mark] Talenir Embermaw <br> Dragonborn exile
> _"We are not gods’ children. We are dragonkind’s mistakes—and their pride. We are proof that even grief leaves something behind."_
---

### Culture of the In-Between

Dragonborn live with one constant: **they do not belong**.

In human lands, they are feared as monsters. In elven courts, they are distrusted as volatile. In dwarven halls, they are treated as curiosities. Among dragons themselves, they are seen as failed echoes of true power.

And yet, Dragonborn endure. They have built cultures around survival, resilience, and pride. Their enclaves often place heavy emphasis on oral history, songs of ancestry, and the teaching of draconic philosophy—lessons of flight, fire, and hunger. Even Dragonborn who reject their draconic heritage cannot escape the weight of it.

This duality shapes them. They are both feared and fascinating, both outsiders and inheritors of some of the oldest bloodlines on Terra.

---

### Faith and Worship

Unlike elves or dwarves, Dragonborn faith is not united. Some worship the Keepers as their patron deities, while others despise them for abandoning dragons to grief. A significant number of Dragonborn honor Cassierrel herself—not as a goddess, but as the great progenitor of all dragonkind.

A few turn to other gods—Datarr for his endurance through suffering, Yearn for the drive to transcend themselves—but these are individual choices, not cultural norms.

>[!quote|mark] Inscription on the Spine
> _"Cassierrel did not die for us, but she made us possible. In her bones, dragons wept, and in their weeping, we were born."_

### Regional Development

The world of Terra is vast, and so Dragonborn diverged into many forms depending on the dragons from which they descended. Over time, these groups became distinct enough to be recognized as cultural and physical variants.

---

#### Chromatic Dragonborn

Descended from the wildest of dragons—red, blue, green, black, and white. Their scales gleam in brilliant colors, their temperaments often mirroring the ferocity of their progenitors. Chromatic Dragonborn are often distrusted for their association with destructive power.

>[!quote|mark] Farmer of Byhaven
> _"A red-scale boy walked into the village once. We didn’t see a boy. We saw a wildfire."_
---

#### Metallic Dragonborn

Gold, silver, bronze, brass, and copper. Often viewed with more tolerance, but just as often burdened with expectations to be noble and protective. Many Metallic Dragonborn chafe under these assumptions, yearning to live for themselves.

---
![[Dragonborn.png|right lp|700]]
#### Gem Dragonborn

Born of crystal-scaled dragons tied to psionic power. Their bodies shimmer with gem-like hues—amethyst, sapphire, emerald, ruby, topaz. They often hear whispers of thought not their own, the lingering echo of their ancestors’ minds.

---

#### Planar Dragonborn

Rare children of dragons touched by the planes—celestial, infernal, or elemental. Their features often glow faintly, or flicker with otherworldly hues. Planar Dragonborn are treated as omens, sometimes revered, sometimes shunned.

---

#### Lung Dragonborn

From the eastern seas and valleys, descended from serpentine lung dragons. They are long-bodied, with graceful scales and thin, trailing whiskers. They are seen as mystics and water-binders, often respected by coastal communities.

---

#### Dragon Turtle Dragonborn

Rare descendants of the great sea-behemoths. Their scales are thicker, their backs ridged like shells. Dragon Turtle Dragonborn are slow to anger but devastating when roused, often serving as sea-warriors or guardians of deep harbors.

---

#### Drakeborn

Children of lesser drakes, often considered “diluted” by true Dragonborn. They lack the breath power of others, but are hardy, stocky, and fiercely loyal to their kin.

---

#### Pseudodragonborn

Born from the blood of small, fey-touched pseudodragons. They are diminutive compared to other Dragonborn, often playful and trickster-like. Their kind are rare and often dismissed as fanciful.

---

#### Wyvernborn

Descended from wyverns, they bear leaner frames and venomous fangs. Often seen as savage, they are mistrusted even among other Dragonborn.

---

#### Deep Dragonborn

Spawned from the shadowed wyrms of the Underdark. Their scales are dull grey or purple-black, their eyes glowing faintly. They thrive in darkness, often becoming assassins, scouts, or seers of hidden paths.

---

#### Faerie Dragonborn

Sprightly and rare, touched by whimsy. Their scales shimmer in prismatic colors, and many have faint wings. Often mistaken for illusions, they are beloved in folklore but rare in fact.

---

#### Shadow Dragonborn

Children of dragons corrupted by the Void. Their scales are smoky black, their eyes hollow-glowing. They are feared as omens of betrayal, often driven to exile even from Dragonborn enclaves.

---

### Conflict and Struggle

Dragonborn history is written in fire and exile. They fought in the margins of wars, often as mercenaries or scapegoats. When the Battle of the Continents tore monstrous races into Togruylia, Dragonborn were not included—neither fully monstrous nor fully mortal. Instead, they wandered, caught in-between.

This left them fractured. They either fled to the center of Eophoris, to Osse, or the Depths. A few carved out their own enclaves, hidden and fiercely independent.

---

### Cultural Divergence

Despite their variety, Dragonborn culture shares a common thread: **the struggle for identity**.

Some lean into dragon pride, calling themselves the heirs of a greater age. Others deny it, living as close to mortals as possible. And many walk the line, neither ashamed nor exalted, but pragmatic about what their heritage means.

They honor ancestry through **drums and chants**, echoing the heartbeats and roars of dragons. They forge their own weapons, often engraved with dragon-marks. And they raise their children to never forget that they are both feared and needed.

---

### Closing

Dragonborn are not gods’ children. They are not dragons. They are not mortals. They are **the scar left by dragonkind on Terra**—a scar that endures, adapts, and refuses to fade.

>[!quote|mark] Eryndral Steelfang, Dragonborn chieftain
> _"We are not accepted, and we may never be. But when the fires come, when the mountains fall, when the seas rise—look to us. For in our blood is survival. In our scales is memory. And in our breath is the will to live."_
