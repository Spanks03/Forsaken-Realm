## The Age of Reach and Iron

The centuries after the Cataclysm had been marked by rebuilding, slow healing, and the cautious return of hope. Nations grew from sparks into flames, cultures reasserted themselves, and the fragile beginnings of a new order took shape. Yet with recovery came ambition, and with ambition came the restless desire to push further than any had dared before.

Two endeavors would define this age — one reaching outward into the **Void** that had scarred their world, the other binding Terra together with iron, wheels, and unthinkable magic.

###  The Eriwall Expedition

It was the **College of Eriwall** that first voiced the dream. Their halls of learning, rising tall in the rebuilt city of Eriwall, became the new beacon of scholarship after the darkness. Within their vaulted libraries, scholars whispered of ancient wounds and unanswered questions: _What was the Void? Could it be measured, touched, perhaps even controlled?_

And so, with pride and trepidation, they declared the **Exploration of the Void**. A council of academics handpicked explorers, mages, and warriors to brave the fracture where Deep Oak had fallen.

> [!quote|mark] The Proclamation etched upon the Eriwallian Gates
>_"Knowledge must be sought, even when it hungers to devour us,"_ 

At first, the expedition was hailed as glorious. Songs were sung of their bravery, children carved their names into play-scripts, and priests blessed them at every turn. But history remembers them not for triumph, but for what followed.

For of those who marched into the abyss, few — if any — returned.

###  Fragments of a Lost Journal

All accounts of what happened are fractured, contradictory, or lost. Some say a single survivor staggered back, mad and broken. Others claim a handful emerged, twisted by shadow, only to vanish again. But through every retelling, one artifact remains consistent: the **Journal of Errich Boggenlow**.

The halfling scholar’s words became the only surviving record. Preserved in trembling script, they describe horrors that no historian agrees on fully — and perhaps none _can_.

> [!quote|mark] Errich Boggenlow <br> Journal of Falling Skies
> _“We thought the air merely thin. But it pressed, alive, against our chests. The Void is not emptiness. It is hunger given shape.”_ 
> _<br>“They had no eyes, yet they watched. No mouths, yet they screamed. And when they touched Dalen, he was unmade, not killed. His name fell out of my memory like sand through fingers.”_

The journal ends abruptly, pages torn and scorched. Whether Errich himself escaped, or whether his writings were carried out by some other hand, none can say.

The College of Eriwall, humiliated yet undeterred, sealed away the surviving fragments under lock and ward. Yet rumors of what they found spread nonetheless, fueling both fear and fascination for generations.

### The Rolling Capital: Veriwind

But the true heartbeat of this age was not a sword nor a book — it was a promise. A family of wealth, whose name has since been worn away by history, swore they could protect all of Eophoris from the creeping darkness. In their hands, they unveiled a marvel no one had dared to dream: **Veriwind, the Train**.

At first, Veriwind was merely a railway linking cities, bridging distances that once took months to traverse. Its great engines, arcane-driven and glimmering with soulfire, pulled carriages filled with goods, soldiers, scholars, and dreamers.

In time, the Train became more than a wonder of travel — it became the **spine of an Empire**. Cities reshaped themselves around its tracks. Outposts grew into towns, towns into cities, and cities into metropolises bound together by the great silver rail.

>[!quote|mark]  Common Proverb along the Veriwind Line
>_“Where the rail goes, so too goes fortune. Where it does not, silence lingers.”_  

Yet Veriwind was never only what it seemed. Though its creators spoke of unity and protection, whispers persisted — quiet and unnerving — that the Train carried a deeper power. Some who worked its engines spoke of spells layered upon spells, a constant hum of magic not merely to move wheels but to wrap the world itself.

> [!quote|mark] Fragment of an engineer’s journal<br> Sealed in the restricted archives of Eriwall
> _“It is not a train. It is a spell, vast beyond comprehension. Each mile we lay is a rune, each wheel a circle, each station a word in a language meant to silence the gods.”_ 

The wealthy family, reborn as the **Council of Light**, made Veriwind their throne. Its capital was no longer a fixed city but a **moving fortress** — a capital-on-wheels that crossed the continent like a wandering heart. Wherever it stopped, people gathered, swearing loyalty to the Council who had bound their fates to the rails.

> [!quote|mark] Inscription on the Veriwind banners of the Council of Light
> _“The Train protects us, the Train unites us. The Council guides us. What need have we of gods, when the rail does not fail?”_  

Stretching nearly **seventy-five miles long**, Veriwind was no mere vehicle, but a moving fortress-city. Its iron spine could encircle any settlement it ported at, transforming towns into instant fortresses, sealed from outside threat. What began as an ambitious means of connecting distant cities soon grew into something far greater: a **rolling capital**, the seat of the growing empire that spread across Eophoris.

>[!quote|mark] Common Poster found on the Rail
> _“It is not merely transport, It is the backbone of a nation, the chain that binds us together.”_

Eophoris blossomed. Trade exploded, carrying silks, gems, and spices along the line. Colleges multiplied, some dedicated to practical arts, others obsessed with the mysteries of the Weave and the Void. New weapons were forged, airships began to rise, and for a generation it seemed as though Terra itself had entered an age of peace gilded with wonders.

And yet — beneath the gold, cracks spread. The Train consumed unimaginable amounts of arcane energy, and though its engines gleamed, the skies began to change. Storms of unnatural color swirled across the plains. Entire harvests grew twisted under the weight of unstable magic. Some spoke of rain that burned like acid, or of winds that carried whispers.

> [!quote|mark]  Diary of a farmer outside Karroweld
> _“I have seen lightning fall like chains, binding the horizon. The world itself is crying out, and still the rails press on.”_ 

In these years appeared the first **UnForsaken** — mortals oversaturated with magic until it clung to their very blood, their bodies sparking with arcane brilliance. And on the opposite end, the **Forsaken** — those cut off from magic altogether, walking in silence through a world they could no longer touch. They were living proof that Terra was changing, and not all welcomed what they represented.

The Lesser Gods, especially the dragon Keepers, took note of this shift. Their domains — the Hunt, Hospitality, Ambush, Friendship, and countless others — remained intact, but their worshippers now mingled within great cities that no longer belonged solely to one faith. Their voices grew quieter as mortals turned more to councils, colleges, and nations than to divine will.

It was a paradoxical age: faith weakened, yet wonders abounded. Nations formed identities, but the rail bound them into one. Magic lifted civilization higher, even as it gnawed away at the very foundations of the world. And always, Veriwind thundered across the land, both savior and executioner, too vast for mortals to comprehend.

>[!quote|mark] Chronicler Merin Dathil, “Annals of the First Veriwind Era”
> _“We believed ourselves builders of an eternal order. But even as the cities grew, I could not shake the sense that we had planted seeds whose roots would one day split the stone beneath our feet.”_  
### Historical Reflections


> [!quote|mark]  ‘The March of Iron’, Anonymous Chronicler <br>Year 412 Post-Cataclysm
> _“Sous had its militia, hardened and fierce, but they bent knee when Veriwind came. Eriwall had its scholars, proud and aloof, but their halls opened to its engines. Veriwind was not asked into cities. It consumed them.”_ 

>[!quote|mark] Diary of Lenora Vale, 2nd Age Farmer
> _“I stood on the ridges and saw it arrive. Not a train, no. A serpent of fire and thunder, its breath shaking the mountains, its body swallowing the horizon. Children cheered, but my bones knew fear.”_ 

For the common folk, Veriwind was both savior and tyrant. It brought food where famine had lingered, soldiers where monsters prowled, and trade where isolation had starved nations. But it also imposed the will of the Council of Light, whose hand grew ever heavier as the empire swelled.

### Two Faces of Ambition

Thus, the Age of Reach and Iron was shaped by two great movements. The Eriwall Expedition pushed toward the unknown, and was swallowed by it. Veriwind pulled the world inward, forging empire through fear and awe.

Some saw this duality as inevitable — the human (and in truth, mortal) drive to stretch hands both outward and inward, to seek the unseeable even as they bind themselves in chains of their own making.

###  Closing the Section

By its end, the world of Terra stood on the cusp of something new. The **Sous Militia**, once a proud band of defenders, grew into the sprawling city of **Sousey**, capital of the empire’s martial arm. The **Eriwall Council**, shaken but resolute, formalized its halls into the **College of Eriwall**, the greatest bastion of magical study and scholarship in history. And the **Council of Light**, hidden behind veils of wealth and secrecy, solidified their rule through the Veriwind — the beating iron heart of empire.

>[!quote|mark] ‘On the Dawn of the Third Age’ <br> Historian Ilmar the Younger
> _“We explored the stars and found them hostile. We chained our world in iron and called it safety. Between those choices lies our age — an age of wonder, fear, and ambition unbridled.”_ 

And so Terra marched forward, carried by wheels of steel and the whispers of the Void, toward a future none could fully grasp — but all would be forced to live.
