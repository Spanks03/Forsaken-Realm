### The Will to Create

> [!quote|mark] Book of Wynne, Verse XV
> _"From the hands that had held only void, there came the shape of worlds."_ 

At the farthest edge of the Astral Sea, where no other gods dared to look, Nosduh and Ogul began their great work.

They did not begin with Terra. Creation is a craft, and craft must be tested. Nosduh shaped a world of reason, each mountain placed in geometric harmony, each river bending to measured law. Ogul answered with a world of spirit and fortune, where winds carried voices of ancestors and the soil bloomed at the touch of chance. These were not failures — they were lessons.

> [!quote|mark] Book of Wynne, Verse XVI_
> _"One made a world of order, the other of dream. Neither was whole, yet each was true."_  

Over long ages, they studied these worlds as if they were sacred scrolls, taking from each what was worthy and casting aside what was flawed. Nosduh learned that beauty lay not only in precision. Ogul learned that chance without form withers. And so they came together to shape a world greater than either could alone.

They called it **Terra**.![[ChatGPT Image Aug 4, 2025, 01_18_37 PM.png|left lp|500]]

They bled into its making — not from wounds, but from will. Stone rose from the marrow of Nosduh’s discipline. Winds curled from the breath of Ogul’s memory. Seas filled with the mingled tears of both. They set a star in the heavens, not as a god to be worshiped, but as a gift to warm and light the world below.

> [!quote|mark] Book of Wynne, Verse XVIII_
> _"And the first light shone not to reveal, but to remember."_ 

The Forsaken Realm — once a barren expanse of the Astral Sea — was reshaped around Terra. They stretched time so it would flow like a great river, neither rushing nor stagnant. They mended magic so that it would weave through every stone, wave, and breath — not a hoarded treasure, but a living current.

Everything was placed with intention. There would be no careless gods here, no idle conflicts. If strife arose, it would be like the contests between Nosduh and Ogul — sharp, but bound by respect and necessity.

They sculpted mountains to anchor memory, carved valleys to cradle life. Forests were painted to sing in the wind; oceans were taught to remember the shape of the moons. In the heart of Terra, they left silent places — sanctuaries where the Weave lay bare, unshielded, for those who might seek to know its truth.

> [!quote|mark]  Book of Wynne, Verse XX
> _"They did not make for power. They made that it should not be forgotten."_  

But the shaping of Terra did not end with stone, sea, and sky. Nosduh and Ogul knew a world without life is only an echo, so they seeded their realm with breath and motion.

They filled the soil with creatures that roamed and grazed, some fierce, others gentle. In the waters, they released fish and leviathans, currents and coral gardens, crafting a living tapestry beneath the waves. In the skies, flocks rose — some swift as thought, others vast enough to cast valleys into shadow.

Yet they were not content with beasts alone. To walk the land, they made the **Ancient Harmonians** — a people rooted in both song and stone, bound to the heartbeat of Terra itself. In the oceans, they gave rise to the **Tritons**, regal as tide and current, bearing the grace of the deep and the voice of the storm. And to rule the skies, they shaped **Cassierrel**, the first and only dragon — vast as mountain ranges, her wings spanning the heavens, a guardian born not of loyalty but of her own fierce code.


> [!quote|mark]  Book of Wynne, Verse XIX
> _"Thus the seas had rulers, the skies a sentinel, and the land a song."_ 

Life now moved across Terra. For a time, there was balance — the kind Nosduh and Ogul had once believed impossible. But as they watched from the heights of their realm, they felt something still missing. The creatures they had shaped lived and died without reverence. They sang no hymns to the hands that had made them. They remembered not the exile nor the will that had forged their world.

The Elder Gods understood then: to anchor Terra in memory, they would need more than mortal life.

They would need children of their own essence.
