###  In The Time Before Memory

> [!quote|mark] Book of Wynne, Verse I
>  *"In the beginning, there was silence. And in silence, two voices rose that no god could silence."* 

_As first transcribed from the visions of Sage Wynne, Sightseer of the Past, and recorded by Elianora V. Scolenruh._

> [!quote|mark] Sage Wynne <br>First Vision
> *"To start the long story that is the Forsaken Realm, we must start with the creators..."* 

There was no light, no name, no song — only the **Void Between All Things**, and within it, silence eternal. No sound, no wind, no Weave — only potential and the ache of possibility.

From that silence emerged two beings — not born, but _beaten into existence_ by suffering and ascension: **Nosduh** and **Ogul**.

Nosduh, once a mortal **Yuan-ti pureblood**, was a creature of coiled thought and cold logic, his eyes slit with purpose and his mind sharpened by endless contemplation. He had learned patience in fire, discipline in defeat, and control through chaos.

Ogul, a winged **Aarakocra of the Phoenix bloodline**, carried upon his back an urn of ancestral ashes that smoldered without flame. He was a bearer of memory, a spirit of longing, and a creature whose fire never died, only transformed.

They were born of forgotten lives in the **Forgotten Realms**, but they did not rise easily.

> [!quote|mark] Book of Wynne, Verse III
> *"From dust and blood they rose, clawing toward the light the old gods had hoarded. Not gifted. Not chosen. Only earned."* 

Through impossible trials, wars of will, and triumphs in isolation, they transcended flesh and reached the brink of godhood. In their hunger, they chased divinity like children grasping at fire — not knowing it would burn.

As mortals, they had clawed their way through mortality’s veil not by divine favor, but by force of will alone. The path they walked left scars across reality. And when they reached godhood, they looked upon the divine with awe—and then, with envy.

In their hubris, they believed themselves ready to stand among the pantheon of old. They were no longer mortal, yet still burning with mortal ambition.

They were wrong.

> [!quote|mark] Book of Wynne, Verse V 
> *"And so the thrones of heaven shook, not from fear, but from pride that would not yield."*  

The gods of old saw their rise not as triumph, but **transgression**. Nosduh and Ogul, proud and ascending, dared to challenge the heavens. Their voices demanded place. Their eyes shone too brightly. And so the gods, in wrath cloaked as judgment, tore them from the tapestry of divinity and **cast them down** into the lowest and most distant edge of the **Astral Sea**.

> [!quote|mark] Sage Wynne, Second Vision
> *"Seen as impure gods by the divine beings of the realm in which they were born, they were cast into a hole of the universe where they were forsaken from the gods they called their own..."*  

They fell through lightless waters where even starlight could not follow.

 > [!quote|mark] Sage Wynne <br>Second Vision Book of Wynne, Verse VIII 
> *"And the sky swallowed them, as prophecy swallowed time. In exile, divinity tasted despair."* 

The Astral Sea was not a haven. It was not a void of rest — it was a tomb of potential. Time unraveled. Identity blurred.

And still they fell.

Ogul's wings grew heavy, burned by the weight of unfulfilled memory. Nosduh coiled inward, withdrawing into logic to shield himself from madness. They cursed each other. They cursed the gods. They cursed themselves.

But they were not destroyed. And in time, silence became thought. Thought became longing. Longing became a spark.

They drifted to a place beyond distance, beyond comprehension — a nowhere at the edge of everything.

 > [!quote|mark] Book of Wynne, Verse X 
> *"Where light could not follow, thought did. Where gods dared not look, will became creation."*

There, the exiled brothers remembered who they were. Not gods. Not mortals. Something else. And together, with no one left to worship or stop them, they whispered the first words of their own making:

 > [!quote|mark] Sage Wynne <br>Vision of the First Words
> _“Let there be that which has never been.”_

And the dark began to listen.
### The Exile in the Stars
  
> [!quote|mark] Book of Wynne, Verse IX
> *"And they fell through the veil, beyond the stars, past even the Weave — into nothing. Where silence thinks and light forgets."*

There is no measure for the time they fell. Some say they plummeted through eternity; others believe they never truly stopped falling. The Astral Sea, for all its vastness, became a single prison — a hollow stretch of starlit nothing where even the laws of thought withered.

Here, **Nosduh** and **Ogul** were flung to the furthest edge of existence — where the forgotten rot and the remembered flicker and vanish. The gods had not killed them, but worse: they had left them **incomplete**.

They hovered between states — alive, but unraveling; divine, but fractured. On the edge of death, they did not burn or scream. They **dimmed**.

For the first centuries, perhaps millennia, there was no speech between them. **Ogul** curled his wings inward, pressing the urn of his ancestors against his chest like a wound he could not close. He dreamed of flame but woke only to cold. **Nosduh** twisted into loops of thought, calculating how far they had fallen, how long they would drift, until even the numbers betrayed him.
 
> [!quote|mark] Book of Wynne, Verse X
> _"And they were silent, not from peace, but because blame is sharpest when spoken aloud."_ 

Their mortal forms dissolved — peeled away by time, by starlight, by divine decay. What remained was not flesh, not even soul, but **concept**. Essence stripped to its core. **Nosduh became will. Ogul became memory.**

In their disembodied state, they quarreled without sound. Thought clashed with vision. Rage echoed in memory. Each blamed the other for their fall: for the challenge, for the hubris, for the silence they now endured.

But blame is a fire that burns fuel. And eventually, they were cold.

It was necessity that brought them back together — not forgiveness. Not affection. They felt the ache of power with no altar, of godhood with no worship. Something inside them whispered, _If we do not make, we will vanish._

But they had learned. They had seen what gods do with power when untempered by pain. The gods of the Forgotten Realms had thrown them away without thought. So Nosduh and Ogul decided, for the first time, to do **what those gods never had**.

They would wait.

They would plan.

They would create — not from dominance, but from understanding.

> [!quote|mark] Book of Wynne, Verse XIII
> _"And so the silence was broken not by sound, but by intention. Two wills aligned, not in harmony — but in purpose."_ 

At the edge of the Astral Sea, where nothing stirs and all things are lost, **the first act of true creation** was born.
