### Origins of the Moonborn

The Rakasta were born not from the pride of **Qhathos** nor the curiosity of **Truth**, but from a forgotten pact between the two. When their divine rivalry over the perfection of the feline races turned to ruin, both gods sought a compromise. In the quiet shadow between glory and truth, under a full silver moon, their shared essence gave rise to the Rakasta — beings of fierce instinct and strange grace, neither wholly divine nor entirely mortal.

Legends say the first Rakasta walked beneath a blood-red moon. Their pelts shimmered with starlight, their eyes reflecting the phases of the heavens. The gods gave them a single gift: _freedom._ And a single command: _never kneel._

Thus, the Rakasta became wanderers of moonlight and dusk — hunters who walk the boundary between civilization and wilderness, between beast and soul.

---

### The Way of the Hunt

Every Rakasta believes they are born from a _Hunt._ It is not a metaphor. Each birth is said to mirror a divine chase — a spirit’s journey through the celestial jungle before descending into flesh. With this you find a lot of Rakasta worshiping the Keeper of the Hunt. From this belief stems their most sacred tradition: the **Five Hunts** — rites that mark life’s great thresholds.

1. **The First Paw:** The hunt of youth — a solo kill that proves survival instinct.
    
2. **The Blood Moon Hunt:** The trial of adulthood — a battle fought not for survival, but for meaning.
    
3. **The Twin Hunt:** The bonding rite — shared between lovers or chosen kin, forging their souls as one.
    
4. **The Silent Hunt:** The trial of loss — a spiritual pilgrimage after the death of kin, undertaken alone.
    
5. **The Final Hunt:** The death ritual — a last journey into the wilderness, never to return.
    

Rakasta never bury their dead. They let the wild reclaim the fallen, believing that to feed the earth is to return one’s spirit to the Hunt above.

### Culture and Philosophy

The Rakasta live in tight _Claw-Clans_ of 20 to 40 kin, led by a **Bloodseer** — a mystic who reads omens in the patterns of wounds, claw marks, and blood trails. To outsiders, their ways may seem savage, but within the clan, they are deeply spiritual. Every hunt, battle, and dance is part of their living religion — the worship of motion, instinct, and rhythm.

Rakasta architecture is minimalistic, often built into cliffs or canopies. Homes are circular and woven from bone and hide, symbolizing the cycle of life and death. Music, drums, and chants echo through their gatherings — all guided by the heartbeat of the Hunt.

> [!quote|mark] High Huntress Shar’ka of the Crescent Claw
> _“The Rakasta do not speak to gods. We make the gods _listen._” _

### Relationship with the Pride

In **Togruylia**, the Rakasta form the _wild vanguard_ of **Ulfong’s Pride**. Where the Leonin hold the line and the Tabaxi scout the paths, the Rakasta _break the enemy_. Their claws are sharpest under moonlight, and their frenzied war dances terrify even orcs.

Yet, unlike the disciplined Leonin, Rakasta fight not from duty — but from instinct. The Pride sees them as unpredictable but invaluable, a storm of fur and fury unleashed only when chaos is necessary.

Outside Togruylia, some Rakasta have settled in remote regions — deep in the **Hypnotic Wilds**, among the steaming jungles of the **Waters of Coliwe**, or near the **Todazur Flats**, where they hunt sand beasts and moon-lizards. Others roam as mercenaries, monster-hunters, or performers whose martial dances tell ancient stories.

### Faith and the Moon Spirits

Where you will find plenty worship Guilla, the Keeper of the Hunt, Rakasta do not worship gods in temples. Their faith is tactile — bound to the _Lunar Elder Spirits_, the reflections of Qhathos and Truth through the faces of Ogul and Nosduh that still wander the night sky. The Rakasta are a nocturnal and deeply spiritual feline race whose lives are governed by the rhythm of the two moons. Born beneath the silent glow of **Ogulspire** and **Nosduhmere**, they believe themselves to be children of twilight — the hour between fury and peace, between fire and memory. Where the **Leonin** embody pride, and the **Tabaxi** curiosity, the **Rakasta** are the inheritors of reflection — seekers of the truth found only in stillness and shadow.

They are lithe and long-limbed, their fur patterns echoing the celestial light under which they were born: streaks like burning embers, or mottled patches like moonstone and ash. Their eyes shimmer differently depending on which moon governs their birth — the amber flame of Ogulspire’s children, or the cool silver of Nosduhmere’s. They walk with deliberate grace, every motion measured as though guided by unseen constellations.

---

### **The Lunar Doctrine**

Rakasta spirituality is built upon a belief called the **Doctrine of Dual Light**, which teaches that every soul carries both moons within — the quick and the patient, the burning and the enduring. A Rakasta’s duty is to keep those forces in balance.

> [!quote|mark] High Seer Tahl of the Crescent Claw
> _“Too much Ogul and the spirit burns away.  <br> Too much Nosduh and it grows cold to life.”_  

In their theology, Ogulspire represents **the Hunt, the Heart, and the Flame** — instinct, passion, and courage — while Nosduhmere stands for **the Mind, the Memory, and the Coil** — patience, truth, and restraint.  
To live by one moon while denying the other is considered spiritual blindness.

### **The Moonborn Clans**

Rakasta are divided into **seven great clans**, each tracing its lineage to a particular **phase and pairing** of the moons. A Rakasta’s birth alignment defines their temperament, appearance, and often, their social role.

- **Emberclaws** — Born beneath a full **Ogulspire** and new **Nosduhmere**. Massive, flame-furred Rakasta with blackened rosettes and molten amber eyes. They roam the **southern plains and Todazur borders**, proud and relentless. Their blood burns with the lunar flame of Ogul’s passion.  
    _(Inspired by cheetahs and leopards.)_
    
- **Pale Fangs** — Born beneath a full **Nosduhmere**. Coated in thick, pale silver fur dusted with faint gray rosettes. Their eyes gleam like moonstone, their voices quiet as falling snow. Found in the **Vontrie Snow Plains** and frozen ridges, they embody patience and silent judgment.  ![[Rakasta.png|right lp|700]]
    _(Inspired by snow leopards.)_
    
- **Duskstalkers** — Born beneath twin crescents. Sleek and black-coated with a faint indigo sheen that mirrors the night sky. They dwell in the **Hypnotic Wilds** and **Alesund jungles**, moving like shadows through vine and mist. Predators by nature, philosophers by moonlight.  
    _(Inspired by black panthers and jaguars.)_
    
- **Tideclaws** — Born during the eclipse of **Ogulspire**, when **Nosduhmere** reigns alone. Smooth, dark fur marbled with blue-gray waves, tails longer and broader than most. They live along **mangrove deltas and coastal ruins**, hunting both above and below the tide. Their purrs echo like the sea’s pull.  
    _(Inspired by fishing cats and coastal leopards.)_
    
- **Sunmanes** — Born beneath twin full moons. Golden or copper-furred, with radiant eyes and proud bearing. Their manes—shorter and more refined than leonin—shimmer like sunset flame. Found in **open savannas and rolling plateaus**, they are teachers, storytellers, and keepers of peace between prides.  
    _(Inspired by lions, but fully integrated into Rakasta culture.)_
    
- **Voidtails** — Born under the dark of both moons. Coats of midnight, eyes pale and opalescent. These solemn Rakasta serve as priests and oracles, guiding souls through the liminal paths between light and dark. Found near **lunar ruins and sacred burial grounds**, they are rarely seen but deeply respected.  
    _(Inspired by melanistic leopards and spirit folklore.)_
    
- **Stormstripes** — Born beneath an _Ogulspire waxing red_ and _Nosduhmere waning gray_ — a time of celestial imbalance. Broad-shouldered and massive, their orange coats streaked with black like lightning through flame. They rule the **dense jungles and rainforests of southern Eophoris**, fierce and honorable, known for their thunderous roars and rigid warrior codes. To defy a Stormstripe is to stand against the storm itself.  
    _(Inspired by tigers — often regarded as moon’s enforcers or divine duelists among Rakasta legend.)_

### **Culture and Philosophy**

Rakasta culture is deeply **ritualistic**. Every phase of the moon has its songs, dances, and shadow rites. They measure time not in days, but in “glows” — the length between an Ogulspire waxing and fading. They say life itself is a glow, brief and burning, yet part of a greater cycle.

Music and rhythm guide their lives. Their songs are low, percussive, and hypnotic — often accompanied by drums made from stone and hollowed bone, and chimes carved from moon-touched minerals. The Rakasta believe rhythm itself is divine speech — a form of prayer that bridges mortal emotion and celestial memory.

> [!quote|mark] Rakasta Proverb
> _“We do not speak to the gods with words.  <br> We move until the moons remember us.”_  

---

### **The Lunar Trials**

When a Rakasta reaches adulthood, they undergo the **Trial of the Twin Shadows** — a sacred pilgrimage where they must journey under both moons’ light, from dusk till dawn, without food or fire. Along the way, they are said to be visited by “their other self” — a reflection of what they would become if they gave fully to one moon’s pull. Those who survive return changed, often bearing new markings, scars, or visions that guide their place in the clan.

---

### The Great Betrayal of the Skyfire

Rakasta history holds one unhealed scar: the **Skyfire Betrayal.** When the gods of light first descended upon Terra to mark the beginning of the Cataclysm’s end, the Leonin rallied to Qhathos’s banners. The Rakasta, believing themselves free from divine rule, refused to kneel. Qhathos, enraged, withdrew his favor — and the Rakasta were struck by searing meteors said to have been “the tears of the sun.”

The survivors’ fur turned from golden to black, marking the birth of the **Voidtail**, a lineage of Rakasta who vowed to never serve god or king again. Their descendants still bear jet fur and eyes like molten gold, and are said to dance with spirits unseen.
