## The Cataclysm

> [!quote|mark] Book of Wynne, Dawn Verse 42  
> *"And lo, the Elder Gods returned, and their steps were like the coming of dawn. But the light they bore was not the light we remembered, for it was the light that shows all the dust in the corners of the soul."*

When Ogul and Nosduh returned to Terra, the skies blazed with auroras of every color, shimmering over mountains and seas alike. The Ideal Gods, who had not seen their creators in an age, greeted them with open arms and proud hearts. For a time, there was laughter in the courts of the divine. There were toasts to old triumphs. There was the fragile hope that the family of gods might stand whole once more.

But as the Elders looked upon the world, their joy turned to sorrow. Terra was vast and alive, yet the harmony they had shaped with such care had withered. Petty wars marred the land. Mortal nations mirrored the rivalries of their patrons. The Ideal Gods had turned the sacred gift of creation into a theater for their own contests.

Ogul’s voice was a storm. Nosduh’s words were iron. Together, they delivered their judgment: the Ideal Gods would step aside, relinquishing their grip upon mortals, so the Elders could mend what had been broken.

That was the moment the hope died.

---

> [!quote|mark] Book of Wynne, Shattered Verse 7  
> _"It is not the hand of the enemy that cuts deepest, but the hand of kin, when it will not let go."_

The Ideal Gods did not see rebuke; they saw betrayal.  
Truth accused Logic of whispering this doom into the Elders’ ears. Rage roared that Glory had bowed to tyranny. Luck laughed bitterly and said Revenge had always been their downfall. The heavens trembled as words gave way to blows.

The first blow fell not in shadow, but in light. Qhathos, god of Glory, met Ogul’s gaze and would not bow. Temp’s rage shook the oceans. Vimnera’s revenge hissed through the clouds. The Elders, unwilling to yield, struck back.

The Godwar had begun.

From the mortal lands, it was as if the sky itself shattered. Farmers in the fields saw sunlight split into jagged arcs, rivers boiled in their beds, and mountains cracked under the weight of divine strikes. A shepherd boy from the hills of Myr recalls:  

> [!quote|mark] The Mortal Fragments <br>Testimony of the Lost Shepherd Boy
>_"I looked up and saw not clouds, but wings of fire and coils of shadow. And in their clash, the stars screamed."_

---
Qhathos, Temp, and Vimnera took to the battlefield in physical form, hurling storms, quaking continents, and flooding the coasts. The Elder Gods met them with relentless force. Cities such as Turael-on-the-Sea and Brath’thoran, once marvels of mortal craft, vanished beneath waves or crumbled into chasms.

Above, Eaos, Truth, and Prosper wielded thought, law, and fortune as weapons — binding the Weave and twisting fate against their makers. But the Elders pressed on, their blows shaking even the bones of the world.

> [!quote|mark] The Mortal Fragments <br>Testimony of Ellis Marr, mason of the city of Veydrath  
> _"We saw the sky burn, and in its light, the mountains moved. Some say they walked; I only know that their shadows fell over the city before the walls began to crumble. The air turned thick and wrong, and the river boiled at its mouth. My wife screamed my name, but the sound was carried away like ash."_

---

It was then that Cassierrel, the First Dragon, took flight. Her twenty-one children soared with her, but she alone understood what must be done. She saw the war’s pattern, saw that in days, not years, Terra would be reduced to dust drifting in the Astral Sea. ![[Pasted image 20250804134548.png|right lp|500]]

She rose higher than she ever had, until the curve of the world lay beneath her. Her wings spread, vast enough to darken oceans and forests alike. With all her strength, she cast herself down, wings enfolding the heartlands of Terra. The sky split with the thunder of her landing. Mountains rose from her bones. Rivers formed from the blood in her veins.

For three days and nights, she shielded the world. On the dawn of the fourth, her heart stilled. Her sacrifice cost her life. When the final blow struck her, she fell still, her titanic body becoming the mountain range that would one day be called the Spine of the World. In that moment, the mortals who saw her fall began to name her not just dragon, but **God of Sacrifice**.

---

> [!quote|mark] Book of Wynne, Sacrifice Verse 1  
> _"She laid her body across the realm, and her bones became the ridges of the earth, and her breath the mist over the rivers. And we learned that the greatest wings are those that shelter, not strike."_

---

Beyond her shield, the land was scoured. Seas boiled into steam. Continents cracked and shifted. The sun was struck from its place and flung far into the void, dimming the light for centuries to come. The Weave itself, the great current of magic, frayed and tore, spilling into a new plane of raw sorcery. Opposite it, the absence of magic gathered into the Void, cold and hungry.

As millions died, their soul-threads — the unseen fibers tying life to magic — tore free and drifted upward, seeking to knit the wound in the world. But there were too many. The threads tangled, crystallized, and joined into a vast ring encircling Terra.

At first, it hung above the world like a scar — cold, silent, and strange. But when the sun’s light touched it for the first time, the ring burned with brilliance. Its shards refracted the dawn into waves of opal, lavender, and gold, rippling across sky and sea.

> [!quote|mark] Book of Wynne, Halo Verse 4  
> _"And the sky was crowned with sorrow made beautiful, and the light of the dead bathed the living, that they might remember."_

Now called the **Halo of the Fallen**, it turns endlessly in the heavens, its glow shifting with the passing hours. At sunrise, its pale shimmer blooms slowly in the east, scattering rainbows across mountain and plain. By midday, it burns in white fire, casting sharp shadows and prismatic auras on all beneath it. At dusk, it fades into a tapestry of deep blues and ghostly golds, until night swallows it — only for the stars to dance through its crystalline gaps.

Each shard is said to hold the essence of a soul lost in the Cataclysm, preserved in eternal stillness. The Halo is not merely a sight to behold; it is a cosmic reliquary, an unblinking reminder of the cost of godly pride. To some, it is a promise of protection. To others, it is a grave marker large enough for a world.

> [!quote|mark] The Mortal Fragments <br>Testimony of Elder Sarwen, Keeper of the Eastwatch Observatory  
> *"When the sun’s first light kisses the Halo, it is as if the dead speak. Not in words, but in color — and in that color is memory. We who remain are painted with their passing."*

---
The Elder Gods, though victorious, were battered and bleeding from the war. They had barely overcome their own children. Qhathos, Temp, and Vimnera were bound together into a single monstrous form, Khomos, the Apex Predator, and cast to guard the heart of the Void. Eaos, Truth, and Prosper were fused into Pruthaos, the Omniscient, chained to the center of the Weave.

But still, the realm wept, and the Elders knew they could not remain.

---

Before they withdrew, Ogul and Nosduh joined what remained of their power. From Nosduh’s final breath came **Datarr**, god of Suffering, to remind mortals of the cost of survival. From Ogul’s last flame came **Yearn**, god of Desire, to inspire mortals to strive beyond the ashes.

And in their last act, the Elders took the worlds they had made before Terra, worlds abandoned in their youth, and hurled them into Terra’s sky. They became two moons, silver and crimson, to watch over the realm when no god could.

---

> [!quote|mark] Book of Wynne, Forsaking Verse 13
>_"The moons were their eyes, but their gaze had grown weary. So they closed them, and the realm was left to dream without its keepers."_

The Elder Gods then turned from the world, their power spent. They fell into a slumber deep as death, leaving Terra without the guidance of Elder or Ideal. The Halo burned faintly in the dark, a reminder of the price paid.

And so the Age of the Forsaking began.
