# **The Age of Ignorance**

The Cataclysm and the forging of the Empire had left Terra scarred, yet in its scars, new growth bloomed. The Council of Light’s vision had stabilized Eophoris, and Veriwind still thundered its great arcs across the continent, carrying goods, soldiers, and promises of security. To most who lived beneath its shadow, this was peace — a peace so convincing, so carefully cultivated, that its flaws were easily overlooked.

Cities prospered. Sousey, with its ever-churning forges of soldiers, filled its barracks and drilled its troops to perfection. Eriwall, with its luminous college halls, deepened its studies into magic and history, producing scholars who would one day be cited across generations. Malmsbury expanded its docks until it became the crown jewel of shipwrights, crafting both vessels of sea and vessels of sky. Each city was a piece of the great machine, and the machine ran well.

But peace, in truth, was not shared by all.

Beyond the Mythal of Veriwind, the Legion under General Tal Honcese carried the Empire’s torch into Osse, yet without the Council’s light to temper them, they twisted that flame into a pyre. And in Togruylia, where the “monstrous” races had been cast, the games of blood and sport consumed entire generations. For those who lived within the Empire’s borders, these stories became little more than background noise, painted over with propaganda and silence.

---

### **The Fog of Comfort**

For the ordinary citizen of Empire, life was better than it had been in centuries. Food was plentiful, jobs were steady, and rebellion was something spoken of only in hushed tones, as if it belonged to another age. A generation after the Battle of the Continents, the propaganda of the Council had taken root so deeply that few questioned it. Children grew up knowing the train not merely as transport, but as the backbone of civilization.

>[!quote|mark] Common saying engraved on a Sousey barracks wall
> *"Veriwind carries our bread, our steel, our hope. Without it, we are nothing."*

Cities abandoned during the early rebellions — Deep Oak, Netherfield, Sage Wynne — were spoken of in hushed tones, their ruins a reminder of what happened to those who defied Veriwind’s rhythm. To the average farmer in Byhaven or fisher in Merrimarsh, the logic was simple: better to live in peace than risk becoming ash.

---

### **The Tournament of Togruylia**

The Tournament was born from necessity, or so the Empire claimed. With entire races exiled to the fractured continent of Togruylia, the problem of survival was rebranded as entertainment. The Horde — orcs, goblinoids, and minotaur — raised vast warbands. The Hive — insectoid tribes of all shapes — engineered labyrinthine nests and honed swarms. The Nest — yuan-ti, lizardfolk, kobolds — steeped themselves in ritual and venom.

Each year, these factions sent champions to a blood-soaked island. There, under the watch of enchanted Scrying Orbs, they tore each other apart while the rest of the world watched from safe seats in taverns and theaters.

>[!quote|mark] Council of Light propaganda poster
> *"Glory through Games! Strength through Strife!"*

To the masses, it was just a spectacle — a brutal one, yes, but no different than the coliseums of old. Few questioned what it meant to live an entire life preparing only for slaughter. Few asked how children raised in the Hive or the Nest viewed the world.

---

### **A Fighter’s Whisper**

From the private journal of Irklash, a goblin from the Horde:

*“We sharpen spears not to hunt, but to kill men we will never know. The drums of war beat each season, and our children march to them. The orbs will watch us die, and the people across the sea will cheer. Sometimes, I wonder if they know we are real.”*

His words, smuggled through an abandoned orb and later copied by sympathetic scribes in Bracitos, would remain one of the few firsthand accounts to survive the games.

---

### **The Legion in Osse**

If Togruylia was a prison masked as sport, then Osse was a conquest masked as a frontier. When Tal Honcese’s Legion marched beyond the Mythal, it carried with it the rhetoric of protection: to purge magic, to cleanse corruption, to extend the Empire’s safety net. At first, they were hailed as heroes, soldiers bringing law to the wild.

But isolation changed them. The Mythal’s severance meant no whispers of correction from the Council, no guiding hand to temper their zeal. In its absence, suspicion festered. Magic, once regulated, became feared outright. A single spark of spellcraft was enough to condemn an entire village.

>[!quote|mark] Decree of Tal Honcese, 640 AC
> *"Burn the witch, break the heretic, scour the land clean."*
  
Their engineers turned their craft to detection — great machines designed to sense the fibers of magic within souls. At first used against suspected weave-touched, soon these devices were turned upon children, mothers, even livestock. In their minds, safety was only possible when the world was free of magic altogether.

---

### **The Empire’s Blind Eye**

Back in Eophoris, news of the Legion’s excesses rarely surfaced. When it did, it was dismissed as rumor or twisted into justification.

>[!quote|mark] Council of Light statement
> *"Do not mistake their zeal for cruelty. The Legion does what must be done, so we may sleep in peace."*

And so the people believed, because to doubt was to risk unraveling the peace they enjoyed. Ignorance was easier, safer.

---

### **Cracks Beneath the Surface**

Though Veriwind still ran, the weight of its hidden task — the eternal Mythal that barred the gods — strained the Weave further. Magical weather swept the skies in strange storms, rivers ran backward for days at a time, and some children were born marked with the glow of soul fibers: the first UnForsaken. Others, born hollow of magic entirely, became Forsaken.

These omens were whispered about in taverns and universities, yet never spoken of too loudly. For most, the tournament was a more entertaining distraction, and the Legion’s crusades a distant tale.

---

### **Closing: The Calm Before the Fracture**

To live in this age was to live in a paradox. On one hand, the Empire shone brighter than any age before: cities thrived, airships dotted the skies, and Veriwind’s fortress-wheels kept nations connected. On the other, suffering bled quietly at the margins — in the coliseums of Togruylia, in the burning villages of Osse, in the strained fabric of the Weave itself.

But the people of Terra did not yet see the fault lines. To them, this was peace. To them, this was progress.

And so they cheered at the games, ignored the screams from Osse, and praised the train as it thundered by.

>[!quote|mark] Attributed to an Ichinoki merchant
> *"Let the world turn. The gods are gone, the monsters caged, the witches burning, and we — we are safe. What more could be asked of an age?"*
