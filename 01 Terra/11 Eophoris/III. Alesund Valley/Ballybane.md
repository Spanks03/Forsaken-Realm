### Background

A large city with a wealthy working class. The Gem mining city is a sharp-edged city of people that have a different definition of "work-hard, play-hard". The major families do not have a family crest but a family gem cut. The members of the city always show their wealth (even if they don’t have it) and judge a person by the roughness of their hands. The nicer jewelry and the rougher the hands, the higher regard in this society.

Finding work is nearly impossible in Ballybane. The people are greedy and hard to trust individuals. The people mine their own gems and work like dogs in their own mines out of sight of others but act like it is effortless to show face.

The large known families do not have a family sigil but a type of gem-cut that signifies lineage, loyalty, and strength. So a stranger that comes to town with a ring on their finger could unintentionally represent a family and could help or hurt relations around town.

In Ballybane, the clarity of a gem can directly correlate with a person’s status and long working lineage. Flawless gems are revered and signify ultimate wealth, while inclusions or imperfections may indicate lower social standing. The constant clarity in their products shows the wisdom that is seeded in the mountain and a family tree whose roots are most likely deeper than their mines. The phrase “clear as the truth” could reflect this belief, where clarity in gems is metaphorically linked to honesty in people.

### Appearance

Ballybane is a striking circular city that ascends gracefully, with each ring cascading upward like the layers of a finely cut gem. The Lesser Outskirts form a rugged perimeter, where modest homes and workshops cling to the outer edges, often surrounded by the remnants of abandoned mines and the echoes of weary workers. Here, a thick smog hangs in the air, a byproduct of relentless mining drills, compelling residents to wear masks to shield themselves from the toxic haze. Moving inward, the Common Ring teems with life; vibrant markets burst with color, and the air is filled with the chatter of traders showcasing their wares, from rough-hewn tools to sparkling jewelry, each reflecting the city's obsession with clarity and wealth. Yet, even here, the air remains tainted, and masks are a common sight. Beyond that, the Capitol Outskirts introduce a sense of grandeur, with stately mansions and lush gardens, where the elite flaunt their gem-cut family crests and host extravagant gatherings. At the heart of the city lies the Capitol Circle, an opulent hub where the most prestigious families converge, its ornate architecture adorned with shimmering gemstones that catch the sunlight, casting colorful reflections across the plaza—a symbol of both wealth and the city's relentless ambition.

### History

Ballybane’s history is steeped in conflict and transformation, beginning with the aftermath of the War of the Broken Mountain. This devastating conflict fractured the dwarven clans, particularly affecting the house that once held the mighty fortress of Khazaduum. The war forced many clans to traverse the treacherous ridge of the Spine, eventually leading them down to the split of the Qonoa’s Talons. There, they descended into the depths below, marking a significant cultural divide among the dwarven populace. Those who remained on the ground would be known as Ground Dwarves or Ekonum Dwarves, who placed a new emphasis on gem craftsmanship as a measure of worth and prestige. In stark contrast, the Ascended Dwarves, also known as the Thrummaz Dwarves, held onto their lofty traditions.

A century later, the city faced another upheaval during the War of the Drills. Initially dismissed by the dwarves as a foolish notion, the idea of drilling for gems took root among the Lizardfolk, who introduced advanced drilling technology to the Ekonum Dwarves. In secret, two massive drills were constructed, one made for the Goldbanks Mining Company and the other to the Gempacked Expanse. The drilling revolution spurred a surge in gem extraction but also ignited fierce competition between mining companies, leading to violent disputes among the various races involved—primarily dwarves, humans, and elves. The Dwarven clans, feeling threatened and betrayed, declared war against all non-dwarves, aligning themselves with Orc mercenaries in a desperate bid to reclaim their dominion over the mines.

The conflict was brutal and chaotic, pitting kin against kin and driving a deeper wedge between the dwarves and their neighbors. Ultimately, the dwarves faced defeat, leading to a significant loss of territory and influence in the city. As the dust settled, the once-unified clans splintered further, leaving behind a fractured society where mistrust and rivalry simmered beneath the surface. The consequences of this war reshaped the fabric of Ballybane, reinforcing the importance of gem craftsmanship while also giving rise to a new social hierarchy driven by the intricate politics of wealth and status. As Ballybane continues to thrive, the echoes of its tumultuous past remain, shaping its future and the lives of its inhabitants.
