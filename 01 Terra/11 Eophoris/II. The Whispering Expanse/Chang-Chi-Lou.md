### Background

A large city full of people in it for personal gain. A purple haze wafts through tightly woven streets. Beautiful dancing lights are everywhere in different shapes and colors that seem to pull you in and out of stalls and gambling halls. Seductive women on every corner looking for new faces. For the people who live long-term in the city, the magic can only last so long as immunities develop and the true nature of the city is unveiled. 

Chang-Chi-Lou is a dazzling yet deceptive metropolis, where the streets pulse with the allure of personal gain and illusion. A thick purple haze wafts through its tightly woven streets, a byproduct of magical pollution that tourists believe originates from the city’s numerous arcane industries. In reality, this haze is meticulously pumped into the city by the Lou family, a sinister tactic designed to lower defenses and lull newcomers into a false sense of security. The haze creates an atmosphere thick with intrigue, making residents and visitors alike more susceptible to charms and illusions. Brightly colored lights dance across the facades of gambling halls and stalls, pulling newcomers into a vibrant carnival of excess, where seductive performers and enticing games beckon at every corner.

However, beneath this theatrical facade lies a darker truth. For the locals who have learned to navigate the city’s underbelly, secret entrances and hidden elevators lead to a sprawling network below—an underground slum far removed from the glittering chaos above. Here, in the shadows, the true nature of Chang-Chi-Lou reveals itself. While tourists play their parts in the grand show, the locals live behind the scenes, protected from the predatory gaze of the crime families that dominate the surface.

On the Train's arrival, the city hosts the Festival of Illusions, a mesmerizing event that celebrates its magical lights and tricks, drawing in crowds eager for spectacle. But amid the festivities, a hidden game of power plays out between the three crime families: the Chang family, with their gambling empire; the Chi family, who gather secrets through seduction; and the Lou family, whose dark dealings run deep. As the festival unfolds, tensions mount, and the boundary between entertainment and danger blurs, setting the stage for intrigue and conflict in this city where every shadow hides a story waiting to be told.

### Appearance

The Crimson Districts, is a bustling hub of activity centered around lavish gambling halls and opulent establishments. Streets are lined with shops adorned in rich crimson hues, signaling allegiance to the Chang family. The air is filled with the sounds of dice clattering and laughter, as well-dressed patrons indulge in games of chance. Lavish neon signs beckon visitors into extravagant casinos, where the promise of wealth dances just out of reach. However, behind this facade lies an undercurrent of tension, as the Changs fiercely protect their economic dominance.

The Azure Quarters, the territory of the Chi family. This area is marked by vibrant blue awnings and intimate establishments, such as lounges and theaters that cater to desires both subtle and overt. Here, the atmosphere is sultry and inviting, with entertainers performing on street corners, captivating passersby with charm and wit. The Chi family’s spies are ever-present, blending in with the crowd, gathering secrets and whispers. While the surface is lively, the underbelly thrives on intrigue, with hidden alcoves where information is exchanged for favors.

The Grey Wards, a couple zones that reflects the shifting alliances and neutral grounds of Chang-Chi-Lou. Businesses operate without overt allegiance to any family, often painted in muted colors. It’s a chaotic marketplace, where shady deals and mercenary contracts are struck under the watchful eyes of the families. The Grey Wards serves as a battleground for conflict resolution, negotiations, and clandestine meetings, where the lines between loyalty and survival blur.

Beneath the bustling streets of Chang-Chi-Lou lies the Obsidian Depths, the expansive undercity dominated by the Lou family. This shadowy realm serves as the true heart of the city’s darker dealings, where the rich hues of black and purple signal allegiance to the Lou family. The streets here are labyrinthine and dimly lit, filled with clandestine operations, smuggling routes, and the echoes of whispered threats. In this slum-like environment, locals navigate a network of secret passages and hidden entrances, providing a stark contrast to the opulence above.

The Obsidian Depths thrive on danger and deception, housing those who have learned to survive in the shadows. While tourists and newcomers remain oblivious to this underbelly, the residents below are acutely aware of the power plays at hand. This hidden city pulsates with the energy of illicit trade, heists, and mercenary contracts, where every deal could mean life or death. Here, loyalty is bought and sold, and the stakes are as high as the towering structures above.

### The Families

The city of Chang-Chi-Lou is the crime underbelly of Eophoris. Three crime families run the city, and the battle for territory is an everyday struggle. 

The Chang family is the major gambling syndicate. They control the economic flow of the city. The Changs made the city run on its currency to make people feel richer and have to come back to spend. If you are hurting for money, you find the Changs.

The Chi family owns the sex work in the city. The men and women that work for Chi family's sole job is to make the newcomers happy, but also collect information when people's guards are down. If you are looking for information, you can find the Chi’s.

The Lou family is the darker and strongest of the three. Lou runs the smuggling, trafficking, heist, and mercenary work. If you need someone to be taken care of, you find the Lou’s.

The territory lines of the family roll in and out like a tide with the leaving and arrival of the train. The Chang’s are a steady threat, The gambling halls and the money of the city are a good shield when the train is or isn’t in the city. The Chi’s grow to incredible strength when the train is around the city and slowly dwindles until the train comes back. The Lou’s are the scariest right before the train comes but they have to crawl in their holes when it comes.

On top of the power struggle in the city between the three major cities, there is a much more secretive and volatile game above Chang-Chi-Lou. Hidden above the steam, smoke, and fog of the city is a clash of 5 pirate aeronaut groups. Initially starting with a mercenary group that was under the pay of the Lou family and was their secret guarantee for smuggling and trafficking. The other families started financing balloon crews for personal gain and to keep the other families from taking over. Quickly the crews broke away from their connection with their families seeing that they could do the best work contracting out. 

### The Aeronaut Pirates

The Silent Corsairs was the first crew. Known for their impossible completed jobs (getting in and out of places without detection). Much is a mystery about how they do it but the only thing that is known is that their ship travels quieter than a whisper, it can go higher than all the other balloons.

The Bandits of the Silver Eye was second to the skies put in by the Chang family. The head of the family’s advisor, a divination cult leader, lets the Changs know of the Lou’s advantage and captains a crew of radical cultists that are ten steps ahead of everyone in the skies; Stealing, plundering, and teaching the religion of the Silver eye. The Chi family aggressively retaliates by hiring two groups. The Red Rose Raiders and The Eternal Smiles.

The Red Rose Raiders, a group of all females, is a powerful rage that is out for blood. The family took a group of girls who were fueled by hate due to the maltreatment from the other families and is a cruel execution squad for the protection of the people who can't protect themselves. They are known to send a red rose to the families of the victims before the mission.

The Eternal Smiles is the cover crew for the wrecking ball that is the Red Rose Raiders. An incredibly charismatic group made up of sex workers, bards, and storytellers. They not only steal from people and get thanked for it but also get the people to forget and forgive the aggressive group.

The Pillagers of the Horizon came out of nowhere and no one knows who is backing them but seems to the pirate of pirates. They don't come around often but when they do they only target the other four pirate groups.

### Pirate Captains

Captain Verla Lucius: The Head of The Silent Corsairs. Few have seen her balloon let alone her face. No one truly knows how the crew aligns but all that know of their existence are amazed by how discrete their practice is.  

Bishop Ogden Shade: The Head of The Bandits of the Silver Eye. A Cult leader with a cloudy left eye who seems to have divination powers. He claims he is a demi-god and that all the gods that the realm claims don't exist. He is beyond the label of crazy but the success that he has and the following that he has for what it's worth are surprising.

Captain Rae Krome: The Head of The Red Rose Raiders. An angry redhead with a scarred past and a powerful voice strikes almost as much fear as her psychological torture. A past worker for the Chi family, the personal hit list is long but she always finds time for the reckoning of masses.

Captain Blackwell Kellum: The Head of The Eternal Smiles and legend of the highest caliber. He is known for slaying the meanest monsters, stealing from the most corrupt politicians, and bedding the wives of said politicians. A loud man does not enter the room but steals it. The man's story amplifies through the echoes of time.

Captain Kingsley Carmine: The Head of The Pillagers of the Horizon. A man who has a vendetta against pirates. A man is hired by the Council of Light and he will eliminate any pirate aeronaut if possible but protection of the train is more important. A man of mission focused even on the small tasks.
