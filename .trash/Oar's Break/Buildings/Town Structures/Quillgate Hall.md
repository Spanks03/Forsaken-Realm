The heavy oak door of **Quillgate Hall** creaks open to reveal a cool, orderly room lined with well-worn desks and shelves stacked with parchment, ink pots, and neatly folded maps. The faint scent of beeswax and dried ink hangs in the air, mingling with the crispness of freshly swept floors. Light filters softly through tall, narrow windows, casting long shadows that stretch across the polished wooden planks like whispered warnings.

At the front stands **[[Rilda Venn]]**, the hall’s strict but patient teacher, her gaze as sharp and unyielding as the brass bell she rings to call her students to attention. The bell’s clear, commanding chime echoes off the walls and down the hallway, sending a ripple of apprehension through the children—an almost tangible mix of respect and fear. Yet beneath her stern exterior, there is a surprising warmth in Rilda’s dedication, especially when she unfolds a new map, tracing river bends and mountain ridges with careful precision. Her voice, though firm, carries the steady cadence of someone who understands the importance of knowledge and discipline in this small river town.

The murmurs of scribbling quills and rustling pages fill the quiet space, occasionally punctuated by the sharp ring of the bell or Rilda’s crisp instructions. It’s a place where patience molds young minds, where the discipline of the classroom is balanced by the boundless curiosity sparked by the secrets hidden in every fold of a map. For the children of Oar’s Break, Quillgate Hall is both a place of challenge and the first gateway to the wider world beyond the river’s bend.
