From the outside, it appears no different than any other Market Row support building—modest, square-cut stone with iron-banded shutters, sanded steps worn by decades of foot traffic, and a painted sign so faded it’s barely legible. Locals call it simply _“the office.”_

A casual traveler might assume it’s just another ledger-keeping stop for merchant stalls, maybe a permit office or customs post. That illusion is intentional.

But look longer, and things don't quite sit right. **A pair of guards always loiter nearby**, too alert for a posting this dull. The front door boasts **three locks**, each distinct and maintained. Inside, the air is cool and dry, and the polished floors swallow footsteps like velvet.

Behind a high desk sits **[[Penra Hollow]]**, the vaultclerk. **Thin-lipped, sleepless-eyed, and methodical to the second**, she has memorized the value of every secured coin, writ, deed, and dowry note entrusted here. Her records are flawless. No one audits her—they don’t dare.

The building houses the **entire town’s treasury**: taxes collected, guild shares stored, and collateral locked behind walls **thicker than they look**. There are vault rooms buried deeper than the foundation, accessible only by Penra’s iron key and three witnesses.

Most townsfolk pretend the office is exactly what it looks like. The less said, the better. But ask the right person after the right number of drinks, and they’ll whisper about **a time the vault bled through the cracks**, or how **Penra once killed a man with a ledger spine and called it “clerical error.”**

One thing is true—**nothing leaves that vault without a mark in her book.**
