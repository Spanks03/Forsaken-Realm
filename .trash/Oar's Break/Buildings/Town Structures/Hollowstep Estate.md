Just past the edge of town, where the cobbled path gives way to overgrown flagstone and the woods lean a little too close to the fences, the **Hollowstep Estate** sits in dignified decay. Its once-proud frame—timber-boned and stone-skinned—is slowly being reclaimed by the wilds. Ivy curls up the sides like skeletal fingers, choking window frames and prying at the roof tiles. Shutters hang crooked like closed eyelids refusing to wake.

The estate is silent, but not empty.

Wind whistles through broken slats beneath the eaves, carrying with it the subtle scent of mildew, damp wood, and something faintly metallic—blood, or perhaps rust. The front gate creaks on rust-welded hinges, though no one is ever seen opening it. Even animals seem to avoid the grounds. Birds don’t nest in the rafters. No rats in the cellar. Just stillness, like the house is holding its breath.

Inside—though few have dared trespass—the manor is frozen in time. Dust coats every surface like snow. A sword still rests against the mantle above the cold fireplace. Mismatched boots sit beside the door, one toppled over. An unfinished letter lies on a desk in the study, sealed with wax but never posted. In the right light, footprints appear in the dust and vanish just as quickly. Some say they belong to **the adventurer** who once lived here. Others say _something else_ walks these halls now.

That adventurer was known simply as **Edran Hollowstep**—a name once spoken with pride. No one knows what drove him into the deep woods beyond the ridge, but everyone knows he never came back. Some whisper he found a ruin older than the kingdom itself. Others say he made a pact, and the price is now being paid by the house that remembers him.

Children dare each other to touch the doorknob. Some claim they’ve heard it click open when no one was near. The bravest might peek through the window, only to swear they saw someone—something—move in the drawing room.

The mayor has long since ordered the place left alone. "Let the past rest," he says.

But Hollowstep Estate doesn’t rest. It watches. It waits.
