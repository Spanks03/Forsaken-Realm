The soft scent of herbs and burning incense greets visitors as they step through the worn wooden door of **Whisperwillow Clinic**, a humble refuge nestled just off the town’s quieter streets. Inside, sunlight filters through lace-curtained windows, casting a gentle glow over shelves lined with jars of dried flowers, tinctures, and carefully wrapped bundles of medicinal roots. The air hums with quiet purpose, punctuated by the low murmur of prayers and the occasional creak of a rocking chair.

At the center of the clinic moves **[[Brother Elgyn]]**, a gentle, aging healer whose calm hands and steady gaze carry the weight of years spent tending wounds both physical and spiritual. His voice is soft but firm, mixing measured bedside manner with a quiet determination born from years serving in far-off wars. Poultices are applied with careful precision, prayers whispered over the injured, and small comforts offered to those in pain. Despite the clinic’s modest size, there is a warmth here that soothes even the most restless souls.

When the ward fills and the need grows too great, **[[Sawbones Varrick]]** sometimes stumbles through the door—disheveled, slurring, and heavy with drink. Once a skilled field medic, Varrick’s hands still remember their work even if his mind sometimes falters. When the clinic is busy and the ale flows, his sharp knowledge emerges like a flickering flame, steadying the care given even as he grumbles and jokes in a haze. The townsfolk treat his visits as both a blessing and a bittersweet reminder of battles past, grateful for his help while wary of the man who drinks to forget.
