The warm glow of oil lamps spills out from **The Frothing Barrel**, casting flickering shadows over rough-hewn tables stained with countless spills and stories. Inside, the air is thick with the mingling scents of honeyed ale, tangy sour rye, and the hearty aroma of thick stews simmering over the hearth. The wooden floorboards creak beneath boots, while low murmurs, laughter, and the occasional clatter of mugs create a comforting, lively hum that feels like the heartbeat of Oar’s Break after sundown.

Behind the bar stands **[[Wyla Froth]]**, a stout woman with a rosy complexion and eyes that gleam with mischief and exhaustion in equal measure. Her hands are never still—always wiping, pouring, or reaching for another mug, though it’s whispered among regulars that she drinks nearly as much as she sells. Her laughter is loud and contagious, cutting through the smoky haze like a sharp blade, and her quick wit keeps patrons both entertained and on their toes. “Best ale in the Brilliant Run, or my name isn’t Froth,” she’ll boast with a wink, even if the ale’s potency sometimes gets the better of her.

The crackling fire warms the cramped, well-worn room, where fishermen swap tales of river monsters, travelers seek respite, and the town’s familiar faces gather for a brief escape from the day’s hard work. The Frothing Barrel is more than just a tavern—it’s a place where warmth, stories, and the sweet sting of strong drink blend into a rough but welcoming home for all who pass through.
