Stepping into **Thread & Tannin** feels like entering a quiet sanctuary of craft and care, where the rich, earthy scent of cured leather mingles with the faint tang of vegetable dyes and warm wool. The walls are lined with sturdy leather armor pieces—each scarred and softened by use—alongside vibrant bolts of dyed wool that ripple gently on wooden shelves. The soft flicker of candlelight dances across the worn workbench, cluttered with spools of thread, needles, and tools of the trade, while the faint hum of [[Daria Ulven]]’s voice weaves through the air like a comforting melody.

Daria’s rough, calloused hands move with surprising grace as she carefully stitches and shapes leather, her focus unwavering even as she hums softly to herself, lost in the rhythm of her work. Though her exterior is weathered and strong, her touch is gentle—a delicate balance between toughness and tenderness that shows in every piece she creates. The soft thud of her hammer tapping leather echoes through the room, punctuated by the occasional satisfied sigh as a seam holds tight or a color turns just right.

Regular customers come not only for protection but for the care sewn into every stitch. Daria’s shop is more than a place of trade; it’s a refuge where rough hands craft armor with a softer heart, weaving strength and warmth into each garment for those who venture beyond Oar’s Break’s calm shores.
