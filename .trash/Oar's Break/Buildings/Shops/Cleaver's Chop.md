The sharp tang of iron and freshly cut meat greets you as you step into **Cleaver’s Chop**, a modest but impeccably kept butcher’s shop nestled close to the river’s edge. The walls are lined with polished wooden racks displaying rows of gleaming knives, each meticulously sharpened and hung like prized instruments. The faint chill of the stone floor mixes with the warm, smoky aroma of cured meats hanging from the ceiling beams, while a steady rhythm of chopping echoes through the room like a practiced heartbeat.

[[Tressa Cleaver]] stands behind a sturdy block of oaken wood, her movements swift and exact—each slice precise, measured, and economical. She wastes nothing; scraps that others might toss are transformed into savory stocks and hearty sausages, filling the shop with a rich, mouthwatering scent that lingers in the air. Her eyes are sharp and unyielding, reflecting a no-nonsense spirit as keen as the knives she wields. Her voice carries a hint of sarcasm, delivered with the kind of dry wit that keeps customers both amused and on their toes. “You want the best cut or just the scraps?” she’ll ask, a razor-sharp smile tugging at the corner of her mouth.

Despite the crisp efficiency of the shop and its owner, there’s a warm undercurrent—a place where the honest labor of food preparation is respected and where every meal begins with care and skill honed over years. The occasional clink of a cleaver resting against the block punctuates the low murmur of conversation, as regulars share quick greetings and Tressa offers advice on the freshest catch or the best way to roast a river trout.
