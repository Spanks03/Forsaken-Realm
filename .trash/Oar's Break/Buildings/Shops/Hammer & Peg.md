The sharp scent of freshly hewn timber and pine resin greets you as you step into **Hammer & Peg**, Oar’s Break’s dedicated woodworking shop. Sunlight filters through dust-speckled windows, casting warm golden slants across piles of precisely cut beams, planks, and joints, each arranged with an almost reverent care. The steady rhythm of a hammer tapping nails echoes softly alongside the occasional creak of bending wood, forming a quiet symphony of craft and precision.

[[Carp Benslow]], a broad-shouldered man with a streak of gray in his calloused hands, moves through his workshop with deliberate pride. His eyes narrow critically at every beam and joint, muttering exacting assessments—“Not quite square,” “Needs a tighter peg here,” “That rafter’s got a lean”—in a voice tinged with both frustration and devotion. He is obsessed with beams being “just so,” a phrase he repeats like a mantra as he measures and levels, never satisfied until every structure in town meets his exacting standards. Carp knows every house in Oar’s Break and carries strong opinions on their craftsmanship, freely offering unsolicited advice to anyone who’ll listen.

Despite his meticulousness, there’s warmth beneath his gruff exterior—pride not just in wood and nails, but in the homes that shelter the town’s people. Visitors often leave his shop with more than just carefully crafted beams; they take away a sense of enduring care woven into every corner of Oar’s Break’s wooden heart.
