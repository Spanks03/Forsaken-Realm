Tucked just beside the town square fountain is a colorful stretch of hanging cloth and stacked crates known as the **Trinket Stalls**—though everyone really means **Ma’s corner** when they say it.

[[Maevi (Ma) Dunnet]], a soft-spoken woman with creased eyes and thimble-calloused fingers, **has sold dreams sewn into thread for over fifty years.** Her little stall bursts with **embroidered satchels**, **carved wooden animals**, **button-eyed dolls**, and the occasional charm on a string that _might_ just be lucky.

She never rushes. She sits behind her patchwork table humming lullabies from old sailors' tales and half-forgotten nursery rhymes. Her voice **soothes the market like incense**, drifting through the clatter of haggling merchants and stomping boots.

Locals say **every child in Oar’s Break owns something Ma Dunnet made**—a toy, a stitched keepsake, or a handkerchief with their name delicately sewn into the hem. Even grizzled adventurers often stop by to tuck a token from Ma into their pouch, _"for protection,"_ they claim. Not that they’d admit how often they hold it before sleep.

Her prices are never written down, and somehow always affordable—though if she likes you, she might **trade for a story, a song, or a bit of gossip.** The carved toys of wolves, dragons, and serpents are _strangely accurate_, as though **she’s seen the real thing.**

Around her stall are a few others: a young weaver apprenticing under her eye, a potter who once claimed they were her grandson (though she denies having any children), and a cheerful broom maker who _clearly adores her._

If Maevi ever misses a day at the stalls, **half the town comes looking.** But she never has.

###  **Common Trinkets on Display**

Ma keeps a rotating stock of handmade items, lovingly crafted, a little magical-feeling even when they aren’t:

|Item|Description|Price|
|---|---|---|
|**Button-Eyed Doll**|Stuffed cloth doll with glass or wooden buttons for eyes. Different expressions and sometimes matching outfits to local children.|**2 sp**|
|**Carved Wooden Animals**|Wolves, owls, bears, and occasionally monsters from dreams. Great children's toys or lucky tokens.|**5 sp**|
|**Embroidered Satchel**|Holds small items, embroidered with initials or little motifs (e.g. moons, sparrows, fireflies).|**8 sp**|
|**Herbal Dream Sachet**|A linen pouch filled with dried lavender, sage, and something she won’t name. Helps ward off nightmares.|**1 gp**|
|**Stitched Handkerchief**|Personalized with initials or lucky charms sewn into the corners. Always clean. Always slightly warm.|**1 gp**|
|**Charm on a String**|Bits of colored glass, feathers, bone, and coin strung in a protective pattern. They jingle softly.|**5 sp**|

###  **Barter Options**

If you can’t afford something, or she takes a liking to you, Ma might ask for:

- A story she hasn’t heard before (especially with a sad ending).

- A local secret (she never writes them down).

- A melody hummed by your mother or father.

- A favor: _“Bring me back a stone from the bottom of the ravine,”_ etc.
