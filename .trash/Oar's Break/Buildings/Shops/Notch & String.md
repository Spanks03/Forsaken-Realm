The scent of freshly carved wood and taut bowstrings fills the air as you step into **Notch & String**, a small but well-organized shop nestled near the riverbank. Walls are adorned with rows of meticulously crafted arrows, each fletched with colorful feathers and balanced with care. A rack of bows—longbows, shortbows, and the occasional crossbow—rests nearby, their polished limbs gleaming softly in the afternoon light filtering through the open windows. The faint twang of a drawn bowstring seems to linger here, a testament to the shop’s purpose.

Behind the counter stands **[[Davos Brin]]**, his wiry frame hunched over a half-finished arrow shaft, hands deft and sure despite his constant stream of bad jokes that echo off the wooden walls. His sharp eyes, however, betray a steady focus, the same precision that sends his arrows flying true in the nearby woods. Perched proudly on a sturdy beam overhead is **Shriek**, his pet hawk, whose piercing cry occasionally cuts through the chatter, adding a wild note to the shop’s atmosphere.

Davos’s voice carries a dry humor, “If my arrows missed as much as my jokes, I’d be out of business,” he’ll say with a crooked grin, not quite managing to pull off the punchline. Yet there’s an undeniable warmth here—between the archer, his hawk, and the carefully tended gear—that draws travelers and townsfolk alike. The soft rustle of feathers, the scent of pine resin from bowstrings, and the gentle clatter of tools on workbenches all come together to make **Notch & String** a place of craft, skill, and subtle companionship.
