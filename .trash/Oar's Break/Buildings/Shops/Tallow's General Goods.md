Step inside **Tallow’s General Goods**, and you’re immediately greeted by a jumble of wares stacked from floor to ceiling—everything from rusty nails and fishing hooks to lengths of rope, worn boots, and dusty tins of preserved food. The air carries a faint mix of cedar wood, aged leather, and a whisper of dust disturbed by the slow shuffle of customers and the creak of floorboards. Narrow aisles wind through piles of crates and barrels, making every step feel like an adventure in itself, as if the perfect tool or forgotten trinket might be hiding just out of sight.

Behind a cluttered counter stands [[Old Mirk Tallow]], whose wiry frame is hunched beneath a threadbare coat. His sharp eyes twinkle with mischief, though his memory often slips—prices blur, and deals are spun on the spot with an impish grin. “Looking for a fair price, or just a story to go with it?” he’ll say, his voice rough but friendly, before launching into a half-remembered tale that somehow makes you want to buy even the most questionable of goods. The bell above the door jingles softly as visitors come and go, while Mirk shuffles through piles of ledgers and receipts, mumbling names and numbers that don’t always add up.

The shop smells of well-worn wood and a faint hint of tar, a reminder of river journeys and patchwork repairs. Despite the chaos, there’s an undeniable charm here—a place where a quick barter and a shared laugh are as valuable as the goods on the shelves.

###  Mirk’s Mystery Crate

> _“One coin, one reach.”_  
> Mirk keeps a crate near the counter labeled **“LOST & FOUND & NOT CLAIMED”**.  
> For 1 gp (or a really good story), players can draw a random item from the crate, such as:

- A halfling-sized single boot

- A brass token with a lion on one side

- A small, locked tin labeled "DO NOT OPEN"

- A rusted compass that spins wildly

- A ring of keys for unknown doors


DMs can roll or improvise from a small table of trinkets or minor magic items.
