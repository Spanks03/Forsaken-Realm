The heavy scent of dust, crushed stone, and cold iron hangs thick inside **Stonekeep Depot**, where towering stacks of weathered crates loom like silent sentinels. The wooden floorboards creak underfoot, coated in a fine layer of gray grit that swirls up with every step. Faint echoes of clinking metal and the dull thud of shifting cargo fill the air, punctuated by the occasional cough from someone who’s been breathing in the depot’s perpetual dust too long. The place feels less like a shop and more like a cavern of stone and timber, where raw materials wait to be carved, shaped, or traded.

[[Joss Flintblight]] darts between piles of ore and timber with a wiry, restless energy, his angular face streaked with gray dust that seems permanently baked into his skin and clothes. His sharp tongue is as quick as his hands, often launching sarcastic remarks at anyone slow enough to catch, though there’s a grudging respect beneath the barbs. He leans over crates, tapping and shaking them to test their contents, always on the lookout for something valuable or out of place. “Careful where you step,” he warns with a half-smile, “or you’ll be mining your own feet before you leave.”

Despite the grit and grime, the depot is vital—a nexus for miners, builders, and traders who depend on Joss’s keen eye and uncanny knack for finding just the right stone or ore for their needs. The low hum of distant river currents slips in through the open doors, blending with the smell of cold earth and oiled wood, reminding everyone that even in a place so rough and ready, the pulse of Oar’s Break never stops.
