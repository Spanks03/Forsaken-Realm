The scent of fresh hay and earth rises thick from the wide-open stalls of **Willowmane Stables**, where horses flick their tails and stamp soft hooves against the dirt floor. Mules stand patiently, their large eyes blinking slowly, while the occasional bleat of a goat echoes from a shaded pen at the back, mingling with the faint rustle of straw. Weathered beams and rough-hewn planks frame the stable like a well-worn coat, its wooden walls still faintly smelling of cedar and sweat.

[[Orven Willowmane]] moves through the rows with quiet assurance, hands steady and practiced as he checks bridles and pats necks. His connection to the animals is evident: horses nicker softly when he approaches, mules lean into his touch, and even the goats seem calmed by his presence. Unlike the bustling town square or the lively tavern, here the noise softens to the rhythmic clinking of harnesses and the low murmur of contented beasts. Orven’s words are sparse, often mumbled under his breath, but in the warmth of the stable’s dim light, his care speaks louder than conversation.

Outside, the soft clip-clop of hooves on cobblestones drifts through the open doors, mixing with the sweet scent of river reeds carried on a gentle breeze. It’s a place where both man and beast find a rare moment of peace—anchored by the steady hands of the town’s quietest guardian of animals.
