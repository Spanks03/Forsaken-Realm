Descending the stone steps into **Chillcellar Stores**, a sudden rush of cool, crisp air washes over you—a welcome respite from the warm riverside sun. The cavernous cellar is neatly organized, its thick stone walls and vaulted ceiling keeping the temperature steady and the air fresh. The faint scent of cured meats, salted fish, and earth-rich root vegetables lingers, mingling with a subtle hint of cedar from the storage racks. Soft echoes bounce gently through the space, but notably absent is the usual scurry or squeak of rats—a point of pride for the town-monitored storage.

At the far end, **[[Thresher Morn]]** stands tall, his muscular orcish frame a striking contrast to the calm, almost meditative way he surveys the stores. His hands rest lightly on the handle of a heavy spear, but his dark eyes reflect a quiet wisdom and patience that few would expect beneath his formidable presence. Often, he can be heard quietly musing to himself or to trusted visitors about the flow of the river, the balance of strength and peace, and the fleeting nature of time—philosophies that seem at odds with his role as a guard but reveal the layers beneath.

The chill in the air sharpens the senses, making each breath feel clean and invigorating. Lanterns cast warm pools of light on stacked barrels and crates, and the occasional creak of wood or faint drip of water from the ceiling reminds visitors that beneath the town, life quietly hums on. In this cool refuge, where goods are kept safe and fresh, Thresher’s steady watch ensures that Oar’s Break’s provisions remain secure and plentiful—even when the river’s moods turn unpredictable.
